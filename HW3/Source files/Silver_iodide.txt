max-height:1px; border-top:2px solid transparent; border-right:1px solid transparent;"></td>\n<td style="width:60%; max-height:1px; border-top:2px solid transparent; border-left:1px solid transparent;"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Silver iodide</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Inorganic_compound" title="Inorganic compound">inorganic compound</a> with the formula <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">Ag</a><a href="/wiki/Iodide" title="Iodide">I</a>. The compound is a bright yellow solid, but samples almost always contain impurities of metallic silver that give a gray coloration. The silver contamination arises because AgI is highly <a href="/wiki/Photosensitivity" title="Photosensitivity">photosensitive</a>. This property is exploited in silver-based <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photography</a>. Silver iodide is also used as an <a href="/wiki/Antiseptic" title="Antiseptic">antiseptic</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Cloud_seeding" title="Cloud seeding">cloud seeding</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Structure"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Structure</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Preparation_and_properties"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Preparation and properties</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Cloud_seeding"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Cloud seeding</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Safety"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Safety</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_iodide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The structure adopted by silver iodide is temperature dependent:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<ul>\n<li>Below 420\xc2\xa0K, the \xce\xb2-phase of AgI, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wurtzite_(crystal_structure)" title="Wurtzite (crystal structure)">wurtzite</a> structure, is most stable. This phase is encountered in nature as the mineral <a href="/wiki/Iodargyrite" title="Iodargyrite">iodargyrite</a>.</li>\n<li>Above 420\xc2\xa0K, the \xce\xb1-phase becomes more stable. This motif is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Body-centered_cubic" title="Body-centered cubic">body-centered cubic</a> structure which has the silver centers distributed randomly between 6 octahedral, 12 tetrahedral and 24 trigonal sites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> At this temperature, Ag<sup>+</sup> ions can move rapidly through the solid, allowing <a href="/wiki/Fast_ion_conductor" title="Fast ion conductor">fast ion conduction</a>. The transition between the \xce\xb2 and \xce\xb1 forms represents the melting of the silver (cation) sublattice. The <a href="/wiki/Entropy_of_fusion" title="Entropy of fusion">entropy of fusion</a> for \xce\xb1-AgI is approximately half that for <a href="/wiki/Sodium_chloride" title="Sodium chloride">sodium chloride</a> (a typical ionic solid). This can be rationalized by considering the AgI crystalline lattice to have already "partly melted" in the transition between \xce\xb1 and \xce\xb2 polymorphs.</li>\n<li>A metastable \xce\xb3-phase also exists below 420\xc2\xa0K with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zincblende_(crystal_structure)" title="Zincblende (crystal structure)">zinc blende structure</a>.</li>\n</ul>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Iodargyrite-263859.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="568" data-file-width="710" height="192" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Iodargyrite-263859.jpg/240px-Iodargyrite-263859.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Iodargyrite-263859.jpg/360px-Iodargyrite-263859.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Iodargyrite-263859.jpg/480px-Iodargyrite-263859.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Iodargyrite-263859.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nThe golden-yellow crystals on this mineral sample are <a href="/wiki/Iodargyrite" title="Iodargyrite">iodargyrite</a>, a naturally occurring form of \xce\xb2-AgI.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation_and_properties">Preparation and properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_iodide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparation and properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Silver iodide is prepared by reaction of an iodide solution (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Potassium_iodide" title="Potassium iodide">potassium iodide</a>) with a solution of silver ions (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Silver_nitrate" title="Silver nitrate">silver nitrate</a>). A yellowish solid quickly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitate" title="Precipitate">precipitates</a>. The solid is a mixture of the two principal phases. Dissolution of the AgI in <a href="/wiki/Hydroiodic_acid" title="Hydroiodic acid">hydroiodic acid</a>, followed by dilution with water precipitates \xce\xb2-AgI. Alternatively, dissolution of AgI in a solution of concentrated silver nitrate followed by dilution affords \xce\xb1-AgI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> If the preparation is not conducted in the absence of sunlight, the solid darkens rapidly, the light causing the reduction of ionic silver to metallic. The photosensitivity varies with sample purity.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cloud_seeding">Cloud seeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_iodide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cloud seeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cessna_210_Hagelflieger_Detail.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1954" data-file-width="2605" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/Cessna_210_Hagelflieger_Detail.jpg/220px-Cessna_210_Hagelflieger_Detail.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/Cessna_210_Hagelflieger_Detail.jpg/330px-Cessna_210_Hagelflieger_Detail.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/Cessna_210_Hagelflieger_Detail.jpg/440px-Cessna_210_Hagelflieger_Detail.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cessna_210_Hagelflieger_Detail.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\n<a href="/wiki/Cessna_210" title="Cessna 210">Cessna 210</a> equipped with a silver iodide generator for cloud seeding</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crystalline_structure" title="Crystalline structure">crystalline structure</a> of \xce\xb2-AgI is similar to that of ice, allowing it to induce <a href="/wiki/Freezing" title="Freezing">freezing</a> by the process known as heterogeneous <a href="/wiki/Nucleation" title="Nucleation">nucleation</a>. Approximately 50,000\xc2\xa0kg are used for <a href="/wiki/Cloud_seeding" title="Cloud seeding">cloud seeding</a> annually, each seeding experiment consuming 10\xe2\x80\x9350\xc2\xa0grams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ullmann_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ullmann-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Safety">Safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_iodide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Extreme exposure can lead to <a href="/wiki/Argyria" title="Argyria">argyria</a>, characterized by localized discoloration of body tissue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NLM-toxnet_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NLM-toxnet-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_iodide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-b1-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-b1_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-b1_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">Zumdahl, Steven S.
