colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Tropical Storm Bill</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Tropical_storm" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical storm</a> that affected the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="Gulf Coast of the United States">Gulf Coast of the United States</a> in the summer of 2003. The second storm of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Bill developed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on June 29 to the north of the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</a>. It slowly organized as it moved northward, and reached a peak of 60 miles per hour (97\xc2\xa0km/h) shortly before making <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> in south-central <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>. Bill quickly weakened over land, and as it accelerated to the northeast, moisture from the storm, combined with cold air from an approaching <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">cold front</a>, produced an outbreak of 34\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornadoes</a>. Bill became extratropical on July 2, and was absorbed by the cold front later that day.</p>\n<p>Upon making landfall on Louisiana, the storm produced a moderate <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a>, causing tidal flooding. In a city in the northeastern portion of the state, the surge breached a <a href="/wiki/Levee" title="Levee">levee</a>, which flooded many homes in the town. Moderate winds combined with wet soil knocked down trees, which then hit a few houses and power lines, and left hundreds of thousands without <a href="/wiki/Electricity_sector_of_the_United_States" title="Electricity sector of the United States">electric power</a>. Two people drowned from rough <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocean_surface_wave" title="Ocean surface wave">surf</a> in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. Further inland, tornadoes from the storm produced localized moderate damage. Throughout its path, Tropical Storm Bill caused around $50\xc2\xa0million in damage (2003\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>) and four deaths.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Meteorological_history"><span id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Bill_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bill_2003_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Bill_2003_track.png/220px-Bill_2003_track.png" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Bill_2003_track.png/440px-Bill_2003_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bill_2003_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> developed scattered convection in the central <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> on June 24, while interacting with an upper-level low. It moved slowly northwestward,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-june24two_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-june24two-1">[1]</a></sup> and remained disorganized due to strong upper level <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-june25two_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-june25two-2">[2]</a></sup> Late on June 27, the convection became slightly better organized around a broad <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a>, though land interaction prevented further development as it moved towards the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-june27two_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-june27two-3">[3]</a></sup> The area of low pressure became better defined over the central Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula, and after the system turned to the northwest, convection quickly organized while located over the warm waters of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. On June 29, following the development of a closed circulation, the system organized into Tropical Depression Three while located about 40 miles (64\xc2\xa0km) north of <a href="/wiki/Progreso,_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Progreso, Yucat\xc3\xa1n">Progreso, Yucat\xc3\xa1n</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TSBill03LARadarLandfall.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="455" data-file-width="598" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/TSBill03LARadarLandfall.png/220px-TSBill03LARadarLandfall.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/TSBill03LARadarLandfall.png/330px-TSBill03LARadarLandfall.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/TSBill03LARadarLandfall.png/440px-TSBill03LARadarLandfall.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TSBill03LARadarLandfall.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRadar image of Tropical Storm Bill\'s landfall in Louisiana</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The depression quickly strengthened to become Tropical Storm Bill later on June 29. Operationally, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> did not begin issuing advisories until it attained tropical storm status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup> Initially, the system resembled a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical cyclone</a>, with the strongest winds and deep convection located far from the center, though it was classified <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical</a> due to its tropical origins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bill1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-bill1-5">[5]</a></sup> The storm steadily intensified as wind shear decreased,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup> and early forecasts from the National Hurricane Center mentioned the possibility of Bill intensifying to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">hurricane status</a> if the low level circulation organized beneath the area of deepest convection. Bill turned to the north-northwest, and later to the north, as it moved around the periphery of a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge of high pressure</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bill3_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-bill3-6">[6]</a></sup> On June 30, Tropical Storm Bill reached a peak intensity of 60 miles per hour (97\xc2\xa0km/h), one hour before making <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Terrebonne_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Terrebonne Parish, Louisiana">Terrebonne Parish, Louisiana</a>. It moved onshore at peak intensity, and quickly weakened to a depression over land as it accelerated to the northeast. Bill remained a tropical cyclone as it moved through the southeast United States,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup> until it became attached to an approaching <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">cold front</a> on July 2 near the <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> border.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc13_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-hpc13-7">[7]</a></sup> The remnant <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical storm</a> was absorbed by the cold front on July 3 near central Virginia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup> while the remnant low pressure area continued northeastward until reaching the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> later on July 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bill17_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-bill17-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Bill_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Shortly after the storm formed, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches#Tropical_Storm_Watch" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm watch</a> from the southern end of <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Island" title="Galveston Island">Galveston Island</a> to <a href="/wiki/Morgan_City,_Louisiana" title="Morgan City, Louisiana">Morgan City, Louisiana</a>. As a more northward motion occurred, the watch was canceled and replaced with a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches#Tropical_Storm_Warning" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm warning</a> from <a href="/wiki/High_Island,_Texas" title="High Island, Texas">High Island, Texas</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Pascagoula,_Mississippi" title="Pascagoula, Mississippi">Pascagoula, Mississippi</a>. Shortly before the storm made landfall, the warning was discontinued between High Island and <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">Cameron, Louisiana</a>. The National Hurricane Center briefly issued a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Watch" title="Hurricane Watch">hurricane watch</a> from <a href="/wiki/Intracoastal_City,_Louisiana" title="Intracoastal City, Louisiana">Intracoastal City</a> to Morgan City, but it was discontinued when Tropical Storm Bill failed to strengthen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup> Prior the storm making landfall, local <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> offices issued <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">flash flood</a> watches and a <a href="/wiki/Tornado_watch" title="Tornado watch">tornado watch</a> for large portions of the Gulf Coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-redcross-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The threat of Tropical Storm Bill caused 41\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Oil_platform" title="Oil platform">oil platforms</a> and 11\xc2\xa0oil rigs to evacuate. This resulted in a loss of production of over 71 thousand barrels (11,300\xc2\xa0m<sup>3</sup>) of oil and 610\xc2\xa0million cubic feet (17,000,000\xc2\xa0m<sup>3</sup>) of gas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oil_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-oil-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> mobilized workers and brought food, water, and other supplies to Louisiana prior to the storm\'s arrival. At the request of Louisiana <a href="/wiki/Emergency_management" title="Emergency management">emergency management</a> officials, the organization opened two shelters for residents in low-lying areas. Several floodgates in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">New Orleans</a> were closed before the storm made landfall, and many universities and government offices were closed as well. <a href="/wiki/List_of_parishes_in_Louisiana" title="List of parishes in Louisiana">Parishes</a> along the coastline closed summer camps and prepared sand bags, boats, and high-wheeled vehicles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-redcross-9">[9]</a></sup> Officials declared a voluntary evacuation for <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">Grand Isle</a>, though few residents heeded the recommendation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apbill_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-apbill-11">[11]</a></sup> Louisiana Governor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Murphy_J._Foster,_Jr." title="Murphy J. Foster, Jr.">Mike Foster</a> declared a statewide <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> to easily make state resources available, and <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a> Governor <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Musgrove" title="Ronnie Musgrove">Ronnie Musgrove</a> made a similar declaration for <a href="/wiki/Harrison_County,_Mississippi" title="Harrison County, Mississippi">Harrison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hancock_County,_Mississippi" title="Hancock County, Mississippi">Hancock</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jackson_County,_Mississippi" title="Jackson County, Mississippi">Jackson Counties</a>, where shelters were also opened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-12">[12]</a></sup> Officials in Mississippi ordered the evacuation of flood-prone areas in anticipation for a moderate storm surge and above-normal tides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-redcross-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Bill_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bill caused four direct deaths along its path, as well as minor to moderate damage. Damage estimates totaled to over $50\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $65.1\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD), primarily as a result of flooding or tornadic damage. Throughout its path, Bill spawned 34 <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornadoes</a>, ranking it fourteenth in the list of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricanes</a> generating the most tornadoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top25_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-top25-13">[13]</a></sup> The tornado outbreak was caused by <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, moist air from the storm, and cool air from an approaching <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">cold front</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tornado_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-tornado-14">[14]</a></sup> In spite of the large numbers of tornadoes, most were weak and short-lived.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-georgiatorn_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-georgiatorn-15">[15]</a></sup> Prior to forming, the storm produced rainfall along coastal areas of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> along the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a>, peaking at nearly 4 inches (100\xc2\xa0mm) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_(state)" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n (state)">Yucat\xc3\xa1n</a>, and over 3 inches (76\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Campeche" title="Campeche">Campeche</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smn_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-smn-16">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_Gulf_Coast">Western Gulf Coast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Bill_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Western Gulf Coast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bill_2003_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="853" data-file-width="708" height="265" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Bill_2003_rainfall.gif/440px-Bill_2003_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bill_2003_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRainfall totals from Tropical Storm Bill</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The outer <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">bands</a> of Bill dropped light rain across southeastern <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, peaking at 1.07 inches (27\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Beach,_Texas" title="Jamaica Beach, Texas">Jamaica Beach</a>. Sustained winds from the storm remained weak, and peak wind gusts were 20 miles per hour (32\xc2\xa0km/h) in eastern <a href="/wiki/Galveston_County,_Texas" title="Galveston County, Texas">Galveston County</a>. Upon making landfall, Bill caused a storm surge of up to 3.81 feet (1.16\xc2\xa0m) at <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">Pleasure Pier</a>. Effects in Texas were minimal, limited to minor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">beach erosion</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolivar_Peninsula" title="Bolivar Peninsula">Bolivar Peninsula</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Texas_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Texas-17">[17]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A moderate storm surge accompanied Tropical Storm Bill as it made landfall on <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>. In the state, the maximum reported surge was 5.8 feet (1.8\xc2\xa0m), and it occurred at the Louisiana Universities Marine Consortium facility in <a href="/wiki/Chauvin,_Louisiana" title="Chauvin, Louisiana">Chauvin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcla_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcla-18">[18]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Montegut,_Louisiana" title="Montegut, Louisiana">Montegut</a>, the surge breached a <a href="/wiki/Levee" title="Levee">levee</a> which was still damaged from the effects of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lili" title="Hurricane Lili">Hurricane Lili</a> 9\xc2\xa0months before. The breach flooded many homes in the town,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lasurge_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-lasurge-19">[19]</a></sup> forcing the evacuation of an entire neighborhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdsu_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-wdsu-20">[20]</a></sup> As a result, 150\xc2\xa0homes in the town were damaged, with half of them severely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amredcross_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-amredcross-21">[21]</a></sup> The storm surge affected numerous low-lying cities in southeastern Louisiana by flooding roadways,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lasurge_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-lasurge-19">[19]</a></sup> including the only road to <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">Grand Isle</a>, stranding residents and visitors. The road was opened a day after the storm as floodwaters receded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-22">[22]</a></sup> The floodwaters entered a few homes and businesses in <a href="/wiki/St._Tammany_Parish,_Louisiana" title="St. Tammany Parish, Louisiana">St. Tammany Parish</a>. Damage from the storm surge totaled $4.1\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lasurge_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-lasurge-19">[19]</a></sup> Rough waves sank two boats offshore; their occupants were rescued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-22">[22]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The tornado outbreak associated with the storm began with an <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F0</a> in <a href="/wiki/St._Bernard_Parish,_Louisiana" title="St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana">St. Bernard Parish</a> that destroyed a boat house.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latorn1_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-latorn1-23">[23]</a></sup> A short-lived <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F1</a> tornado touched down in <a href="/wiki/Reserve,_Louisiana" title="Reserve, Louisiana">Reserve</a>, striking a private high school, where it destroyed half of one classroom and damaged several others. Later, the F1 tornado passed through a trailer park and severely damaged or destroyed 20\xc2\xa0trailers. One trailer with a woman and three children was lifted into the air and dropped 30 feet (9.1\xc2\xa0m) away; four occupants were injured, none of them seriously. Damage from the tornado amounted to $2\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $2.6\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latorn2_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-latorn2-24">[24]</a></sup> A third tornado, rated an F0, struck <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orleans_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Orleans Parish, Louisiana">Orleans Parish</a>, damaging a car and a portion of a roof.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latorn3_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-latorn3-25">[25]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Winds of 35\xc2\xa0to 45\xc2\xa0mph (56\xe2\x80\x9372\xc2\xa0km/h) were common across southeastern Louisiana, with the highest sustained wind being 53 miles per hour (85\xc2\xa0km/h) in Chauvin and a peak gust of 62 miles per hour (100\xc2\xa0km/h) on the northern end of the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Pontchartrain_Causeway" title="Lake Pontchartrain Causeway">Lake Pontchartrain Causeway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcla_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcla-18">[18]</a></sup> The winds knocked trees and tree branches into power lines,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-nws-26">[26]</a></sup> leaving 224,000\xc2\xa0residents without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-22">[22]</a></sup> The storm dropped moderate to heavy amounts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a>, peaking at 10.2 inches (260\xc2\xa0mm) at a location 6 miles (9.7\xc2\xa0km) south of <a href="/wiki/Folsom,_Louisiana" title="Folsom, Louisiana">Folsom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCMAX_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPCMAX-27">[27]</a></sup> The rainfall led to <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">flash flooding</a>, which inundated roads and cars and overwhelmed local drainage capacities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdctam_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdctam-28">[28]</a></sup> The rainfall also led to overflown rivers and creeks, resulting in flooding along the <a href="/wiki/Tangipahoa_River" title="Tangipahoa River">Tangipahoa River</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Tangipahoa_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Tangipahoa Parish, Louisiana">Tangipahoa Parish</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Bogue_Falaya" title="Bogue Falaya">Bogue Falaya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tchefuncte_River" title="Tchefuncte River">Tchefuncte River</a> in St. Tammany Parish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lancdcriv_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-lancdcriv-29">[29]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bogue_Falaya_River" title="Bogue Falaya River">Bogue Falaya River</a> crested at 57 feet (17\xc2\xa0m) on July 1, 12 feet (3.7\xc2\xa0m) above <a href="/wiki/Flood_stage" title="Flood stage">flood stage</a>, which became a record for the recording station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-nws-26">[26]</a></sup> The flooding damaged several structures and roadways. Less severe river flooding occurred in <a href="/wiki/Washington_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Washington Parish, Louisiana">Washington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Livingston_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Livingston Parish, Louisiana">Livingston Parishes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lancdcriv_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-lancdcriv-29">[29]</a></sup> In all, damage in Louisiana totaled to $44\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $57.3\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcla_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcla-18">[18]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_Gulf_Coast">Eastern Gulf Coast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Bill_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Eastern Gulf Coast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table align="right" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="background:transparent; margin: .1em .1em .1em 1em; text-align:center">\n<tr>\n<td colspan="7" nowrap="nowrap"><small><a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">Fujita scale</a></small></td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td style="background-color:#00faf4;width:1.8em" title="F0: Light damage">F0</td>\n<td style="background-color:#ffffcc;width:1.8em" title="F1: Moderate damage">F1</td>\n<td style="background-color:#ffe775;width:1.8em" title="F2: Considerable damage">F2</td>\n<td style="background-color:#ffc140;width:1.8em" title="F3: Severe damage">F3</td>\n<td style="background-color:#ff8f20;width:1.8em" title="F4: Devastating damage">F4</td>\n<td style="background-color:#ff6060;width:1.8em" title="F5: Incredible damage">F5</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>Upon making landfall, Bill produced a peak <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> of 4.99 feet (1.52\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/Waveland,_Mississippi" title="Waveland, Mississippi">Waveland, Mississippi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcms_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcms-30">[30]</a></sup> The storm surge led to beach erosion, damage to piers, and flooded roadways, with damage from the surge amounting to around $1\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $1.3\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcms2_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcms2-31">[31]</a></sup> The maximum sustained wind from the storm recorded in Mississippi was 43 miles per hour (69\xc2\xa0km/h), at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulfport-Biloxi_International_Airport" title="Gulfport-Biloxi International Airport">Gulfport-Biloxi International Airport</a>, while the airport and <a href="/wiki/Keesler_Air_Force_Base" title="Keesler Air Force Base">Keesler Air Force Base</a> both reported a peak wind gust of 52 miles per hour (84\xc2\xa0km/h). Tropical Storm Bill dropped moderate to heavy rainfall across the state, peaking at 9.49 inches (241\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Jackson_County,_Mississippi" title="Jackson County, Mississippi">Van Cleave</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcms_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcms-30">[30]</a></sup> The moderate wind gusts, along with the <a href="/wiki/Water_content" title="Water content">saturated</a> ground, downed trees in several locations. 34\xc2\xa0roads in <a href="/wiki/Pike_County,_Mississippi" title="Pike County, Mississippi">Pike</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walthall_County,_Mississippi" title="Walthall County, Mississippi">Walthall Counties</a> were blocked by fallen trees, and two homes were damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ms3_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-ms3-32">[32]</a></sup> Additionally, power outages were reported near the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup> The rainfall flooded streets in various portions of the state<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcfloodroad_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcfloodroad-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcflodroad_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcflodroad-34">[34]</a></sup> and led to overflown rivers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcms_30-2"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcms-30">[30]</a></sup> An overflown creek in <a href="/wiki/Pearl_River_County,_Mississippi" title="Pearl River County, Mississippi">Pearl River County</a> flooded structures and roadways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc5_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc5-35">[35]</a></sup> The outer rainbands of Bill produced a weak tornado that touched down briefly in Waveland, blowing down several trees which resulted in minor damage to roofs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mstorn_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-mstorn-36">[36]</a></sup> Statewide damage accrued to $5\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $6.51\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD), primarily from flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcms_30-3"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcms-30">[30]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As Tropical Storm Bill made landfall on Louisiana, its effects were felt in the <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a> coast as well, as heavy surf and tidal flooding pounded the coastline of the state. High waters closed a road to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dauphin_Island" title="Dauphin Island">Dauphin Island</a> and portions of a road along <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Bay" title="Mobile Bay">Mobile Bay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcal_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcal-37">[37]</a></sup> Bill dropped over 3 inches (76\xc2\xa0mm) of rain across the southern half of Alabama,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-rain-38">[38]</a></sup> with isolated locations receiving over 8 inches (200\xc2\xa0mm). Due to wet conditions for months preceding the storm, rainfall from Bill led to flash flooding in many counties. The deluge led to overflown rivers and streams, and left several roadways temporarily impassable from high floodwaters. Saturated grounds and wind gusts of 30\xc2\xa0to 35\xc2\xa0mph (48\xe2\x80\x9356\xc2\xa0km/h) downed numerous trees. Many downed trees landed on power lines, which caused power outages for around 19,000\xc2\xa0people. One downed tree destroyed a car, and another damaged a roof of a house. In <a href="/wiki/Lee_County,_Alabama" title="Lee County, Alabama">Lee County</a>, a man was required to be rescued after driving through high flood waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alncdc_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-alncdc-39">[39]</a></sup> Roadway flooding resulted in a few minor traffic accidents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-redcross-9">[9]</a></sup> Also, the outer bands of the storm spawned an <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F1 tornado</a> in <a href="/wiki/Crenshaw_County,_Alabama" title="Crenshaw County, Alabama">Crenshaw County</a>. Early in its path, it was narrow, and damage was limited to downed trees, two destroyed sheds, and a few houses experiencing light shingle damage or damage from fallen trees. Later, it expanded to reach a width of 1,800 feet (550\xc2\xa0m) as it moved northwestward. The tornado destroyed the roofs of two houses, one of which experienced damage to its walls. The tornado dissipated eight minutes after its 3 miles (4.8\xc2\xa0km) path began, resulting in $200,000 in damage (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $260\xc2\xa0thousand 2017\xc2\xa0USD) and only slight injuries<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altorn1_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-altorn1-40">[40]</a></sup> A second tornado, rated F0 in the <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">Fujita scale</a>, occurred in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_County,_Alabama" title="Montgomery County, Alabama">Montgomery County</a>. A small tornado with a width of only 180 feet (55\xc2\xa0m), it moved to the northwest and tore down a few trees that fell onto a mobile home, a house, and two cars. The tornado dissipated six minutes after its 5 miles (8.0\xc2\xa0km) path began.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altorn2_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-altorn2-41">[41]</a></sup> Throughout Alabama, Tropical Storm Bill caused around $300,000 in damage (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $391\xc2\xa0thousand 2017\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alncdc_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-alncdc-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altorn1_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-altorn1-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altorn2_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-altorn2-41">[41]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Rainfall from the storm began affecting <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> a few days before the storm formed, and locations in the southern portion of the state received over 3 inches (76\xc2\xa0mm) of rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-rain-38">[38]</a></sup> Along the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">Florida Panhandle</a>, Bill dropped over 8 inches (200\xc2\xa0mm) of rain as it made landfall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flancdc_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-flancdc-42">[42]</a></sup> closing several roads<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcfla_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcfla-43">[43]</a></sup> or leaving them impassable due to flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flancdc_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-flancdc-42">[42]</a></sup> A stationary line of thunderstorms in <a href="/wiki/Okaloosa_County,_Florida" title="Okaloosa County, Florida">Okaloosa County</a> produced downpours of up to 6 inches (150\xc2\xa0mm) in one hour, resulting in flash flooding which washed out a portion of a bridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcfla2_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcfla2-44">[44]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Bay_County,_Florida" title="Bay County, Florida">Bay County</a>, heavy rainfall and flooding damaged 40\xc2\xa0homes, while several residents in an apartment in <a href="/wiki/Parker,_Florida" title="Parker, Florida">Parker</a> needed to be rescued by boat from the floodwaters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcfla3_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcfla3-45">[45]</a></sup> Rough surf produced by the storm killed two swimmers at <a href="/wiki/Panama_City_Beach,_Florida" title="Panama City Beach, Florida">Panama City Beach</a>, while a dozen had to be rescued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flancdc2_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-flancdc2-46">[46]</a></sup> Part of the tornado outbreak spawned by the storm extended into northern Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torn_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-torn-47">[47]</a></sup> Damage in Florida totaled to around $1\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $1.3\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcfla2_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcfla2-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcfla3_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcfla3-45">[45]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southeastern_U.S.">Southeastern U.S.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Bill_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Southeastern U.S.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Morgbark.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="336" data-file-width="448" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Morgbark.jpg/220px-Morgbark.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Morgbark.jpg/330px-Morgbark.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Morgbark.jpg/440px-Morgbark.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Morgbark.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDamaged trees from tornado in <a href="/wiki/Morgan_County,_Georgia" title="Morgan County, Georgia">Morgan County</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Tropical Storm Bill dropped light rainfall of around 1 inch (25\xc2\xa0mm) in northeastern <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a> and southeastern <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, and over 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm) in isolated parts of eastern <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_38-2"><a href="#cite_note-rain-38">[38]</a></sup> The storm also produced moderate rainfall in northwestern <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, which peaked at 7.1 inches (180\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Monroe,_Georgia" title="Monroe, Georgia">Monroe</a>. Areas in southeastern Georgia received generally around 1 inch (25\xc2\xa0mm); some coastal areas did not receive any precipitation from the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pcganws_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-pcganws-48">[48]</a></sup> Rainfall resulted in flooding in numerous locations around the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_metropolitan_area" title="Atlanta metropolitan area">Atlanta metropolitan area</a>, leaving some roads impassable or closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcga_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcga-49">[49]</a></sup> The combination of moist air from the south, cool temperatures from a cold front to the north, and low pressures led to the development of <a href="/wiki/Supercell" title="Supercell">supercells</a> throughout Georgia and <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, several of which produced tornadoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tornado_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-tornado-14">[14]</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F1</a> tornado touched down 3 miles (4.8\xc2\xa0km) north-northeast of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pennington,_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pennington, Georgia (page does not exist)">Pennington</a>; first it passed through a farm, causing severe damage to two dairy sheds, a <a href="/wiki/John_Deere" title="John Deere">John Deere</a> tractor, and three metal storage buildings. The tornado destroyed a hay barn, a carport, and a car inside the carport as well, while also causing a tree to fall and kill one cow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcgatorn_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcgatorn-50">[50]</a></sup> The tornado passed through a forested area, where it toppled or sheared off hundreds of trees. As it entered a more urban area, it downed 30\xc2\xa0isolated trees, some of which fell on a portion of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_20" title="Interstate 20">Interstate 20</a>, temporarily closing the roadway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ptcgatorn_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-ptcgatorn-51">[51]</a></sup> The tornado damaged seven houses, primarily to roof damage, although one experienced damage to several windows, while another had a utility trailer and a car damaged by fallen trees; a commercial building was damaged as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcgatorn_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcgatorn-50">[50]</a></sup> An F2 tornado was reported in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Clito,_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Clito, Georgia (page does not exist)">Clito</a>, which knocked down trees and damaged mobile homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcgatorn2_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcgatorn2-52">[52]</a></sup> Severe thunderstorms from the remnants of Bill caused considerable damage to a house near <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Georgia" title="Louisville, Georgia">Louisville</a> and knocked down several trees. Moreover, a tornado was briefly associated with the storms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncgastorm_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncgastorm-53">[53]</a></sup> Bill also caused thunderstorms in <a href="/wiki/Kite,_Georgia" title="Kite, Georgia">Kite</a> which uprooted several trees onto a car and a house.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcgastorm2_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcgastorm2-54">[54]</a></sup> One man in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta</a> died due to a falling tree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup> Damage in Georgia totaled to $244,500 (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $318\xc2\xa0thousand 2017\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcgatorn_50-2"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcgatorn-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncgastorm_53-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncgastorm-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcgastorm2_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcgastorm2-54">[54]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hampton,_SC_tornado_damage.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="390" data-file-width="673" height="127" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Hampton%2C_SC_tornado_damage.JPG/220px-Hampton%2C_SC_tornado_damage.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Hampton%2C_SC_tornado_damage.JPG/330px-Hampton%2C_SC_tornado_damage.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Hampton%2C_SC_tornado_damage.JPG/440px-Hampton%2C_SC_tornado_damage.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hampton,_SC_tornado_damage.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTornado damage in <a href="/wiki/Hampton,_South_Carolina" title="Hampton, South Carolina">Hampton, South Carolina</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The tornado outbreak spawned by Bill was the greatest in the <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> area since the outbreak provoked by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Earl_(1998)" title="Hurricane Earl (1998)">Hurricane Earl</a> in 1998. One such tornado was an <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F1</a> that struck <a href="/wiki/Hampton,_South_Carolina" title="Hampton, South Carolina">Hampton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tornado_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-tornado-14">[14]</a></sup> which uprooted trees and downed power lines. The tornado severely damaged a <a href="/wiki/Dollar_General" title="Dollar General">Dollar General</a> store, while several houses were damaged from fallen trees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sctorn_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-sctorn-55">[55]</a></sup> An F1 tornado also touched down near <a href="/wiki/Smoaks,_South_Carolina" title="Smoaks, South Carolina">Smoaks</a>, which uprooted several trees, resulted in a crush car due to a fallen tree, caused severe roof damage to a mobile home, and killed one dog.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sctorn2_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-sctorn2-56">[56]</a></sup> The storm produced heavy rainfall in the northern portion of the state, with some locations reporting over 7 inches (180\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_38-3"><a href="#cite_note-rain-38">[38]</a></sup> The rainfall resulted in flash flooding in areas, which caused some damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-screview_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-screview-57">[57]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, the remnants of the storm dropped around 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm) in the east-central part of the state, while the southwestern portion of the state received totals of over 7 inches (180\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_38-4"><a href="#cite_note-rain-38">[38]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">Raleigh</a>, a boy drowned from the floodwaters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">[4]</a></sup> The tornado outbreak from the storm extended into North Carolina, though specifics are unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torn_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-torn-47">[47]</a></sup> Bill produced light to moderate precipitation in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" title="Mid-Atlantic States">Mid-Atlantic</a>, including over 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm) in central <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_38-5"><a href="#cite_note-rain-38">[38]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, a line of thunderstorms from the system produced small amounts of hail in <a href="/wiki/Falls_Church,_Virginia" title="Falls Church, Virginia">Falls Church</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-acon_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-acon-58">[58]</a></sup> The tornado outbreak associated with Bill ended in New Jersey;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torn_47-2"><a href="#cite_note-torn-47">[47]</a></sup> a narrow <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F0</a> tornado briefly touched down in a marsh near <a href="/wiki/Goshen,_New_Jersey" title="Goshen, New Jersey">Goshen</a>. The tornado remained away from the city, and caused no damage or injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-njtorn_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-njtorn-59">[59]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Bill_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>By one day after the storm, power companies restored electricity to 151,000 customers. Citizens in <a href="/wiki/Montegut,_Louisiana" title="Montegut, Louisiana">Montegut</a> circulated a petition for a class-action lawsuit in response to the levee failure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-22">[22]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> set up a church in <a href="/wiki/Reserve,_Louisiana" title="Reserve, Louisiana">Reserve</a> as an emergency shelter. Only five people stayed the first night, resulting in the organization to convert it to a family services center. Over 100\xc2\xa0families asked for food assistance. The Red Cross also set up a shelter in <a href="/wiki/Houma,_Louisiana" title="Houma, Louisiana">Houma</a>, where 14\xc2\xa0people stayed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amredcross_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-amredcross-21">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
