class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lewes,_Delaware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Lewes was the site of the first European settlement in <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Whaling" title="Whaling">whaling</a> and trading post that <a href="/wiki/Dutch_people" title="Dutch people">Dutch</a> settlers founded on June 3, 1631 and named <a href="/wiki/Zwaanendael_Colony" title="Zwaanendael Colony">Zwaanendael</a> (Swan Valley).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-COLONIAL_DELAWARE_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-COLONIAL_DELAWARE-7">[7]</a></sup> The colony had a short existence, as a local tribe of <a href="/wiki/Lenape" title="Lenape">Lenape</a> Native Americans wiped out the 32 settlers in 1632.</p>\n<p>The area remained rather neglected by the Dutch until, under the threat of annexation from the English colony of Maryland, the city of Amsterdam made a grant of land at the Hoernkills (the area around Cape Henlopen, near the current town of Lewes) to a group of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mennonites" title="Mennonites">Mennonites</a> for settlement in 1662. A total of 35 men were to be included in the settlement, led by a <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Corneliszoon_Plockhoy" title="Pieter Corneliszoon Plockhoy">Pieter Cornelisz Plockhoy</a> of Zierikzee and funded by a sizable loan from the city to get them established. The settlement was established in 1663, but the timing of the settlement was terrible: In 1664, the English wrested New Netherland from the Dutch, and they had the settlement destroyed with British reports indicating that \xe2\x80\x9cnot even a nail\xe2\x80\x9d was left there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The area was slow to resettle, but a new settlement gradually regrew around the Hoernkills. In late December 1673, when the area was briefly held again by the Dutch, the settlement was attacked and burned down again by soldiers from the English colony of Maryland. In 1680, under the authority of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Stuart,_Duke_of_York" title="James Stuart, Duke of York">James Stuart, Duke of York</a>, who had been granted such authority by his brother, <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">King Charles II</a>, the village (and county) was reorganized and known for two years as New Deale, Deale County, Delaware. A log courthouse was authorized to be built at this time. A Church of England congregation was established by 1681 and a Presbyterian church was built in 1682.</p>\n<p>In 1682, the Delaware colonies were given to William Penn by English King Charles II in payment of a family debt. When Penn arrived in the New World later that year, he renamed the county as Sussex and the Hoernkills settlement as Lewes, in commemoration of sites back in England. Lewes became and remained the county seat of Sussex County until 1791, when it was moved to a more west-central county location, the current town of <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Delaware" title="Georgetown, Delaware">Georgetown</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On April 5 and 6, 1813, during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>, British naval vessels led by <a href="/wiki/HMS_Poictiers_(1809)" title="HMS Poictiers (1809)">HMS <i>Poictiers</i></a> under the command of Captain Sir <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Beresford,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir John Beresford, 1st Baronet">John Beresford</a> briefly and ineffectually bombarded the town. A cannonball from the bombardment is lodged in the foundation of Cannonball House, which now serves as the town\'s maritime museum.</p>\n<p>Lewes was incorporated by an act of the state assembly on Feb. 2, 1818. The act provided for five persons to be chosen as commissioners to be known as "Trustees of the Town of Lewes." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Lewes Beach itself was an important stop on the <a href="/wiki/Underground_Railroad" title="Underground Railroad">Underground Railroad</a> in the years leading up to the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. As a "border state," Delaware was not part of the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederacy</a>, but was still quite dangerous for fugitive slaves. Several houses in Lewes thus housed escaping slaves; these "safe houses" were identified by the residents placing a single candle in the top window of the house. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-City_of_Lewes_Delaware_Website_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-City_of_Lewes_Delaware_Website-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In 1941, the United States built <a href="/wiki/Fort_Miles" title="Fort Miles">Fort Miles</a> on <a href="/wiki/Cape_Henlopen" title="Cape Henlopen">Cape Henlopen</a>, immediately south of Lewes, to defend <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Bay" title="Delaware Bay">Delaware Bay</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_River" title="Delaware River">Delaware River</a> and the oil refineries and factories on its shores, as well as the city of <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>. It was one of the largest and most heavily armed coastal fortifications ever built.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<p>Fort Miles never saw any major action; except for range practice, it fired its guns only once between its establishment and the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Fort Miles ceased operation altogether in 1991 and was deeded to the State of Delaware.</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lightshipoverfalls.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2748" data-file-width="3664" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Lightshipoverfalls.jpg/220px-Lightshipoverfalls.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Lightshipoverfalls.jpg/330px-Lightshipoverfalls.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Lightshipoverfalls.jpg/440px-Lightshipoverfalls.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lightshipoverfalls.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nLightship Overfalls, preserved as a tourist attraction.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In addition to Fort Miles, the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Henlopen_Archeological_District" title="Cape Henlopen Archeological District">Cape Henlopen Archeological District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coleman_House_(Lewes,_Delaware)" title="Coleman House (Lewes, Delaware)">Coleman House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cool_Spring_Presbyterian_Church" title="Cool Spring Presbyterian Church">Cool Spring Presbyterian Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/De_Vries_Palisade" title="De Vries Palisade">De Vries Palisade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Breakwater" title="Delaware Breakwater">Delaware Breakwater and Lewes Harbor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fisher_Homestead_(Lewes,_Delaware)" title="Fisher Homestead (Lewes, Delaware)">Fisher Homestead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fisher%27s_Paradise" title="Fisher\'s Paradise">Fisher\'s Paradise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Col._David_Hall_House" title="Col. Three of the men lived on Lewes\' Second Street. Brothers Daniel and Caleb Rodney, descendants of <a href="/wiki/Caesar_Rodney" title="Caesar Rodney">Caesar Rodney</a> each served as governor of Delaware. Each a member of the <a href="/wiki/Federalist_Party" title="Federalist Party">Federalist Party</a>, Daniel served from 1814\xe2\x80\x931817 while Caleb served as acting governor from 1822\xe2\x80\x931823. Ebe Walters Tunnell moved to Lewes in 1873 to enter the drug and hardware business in part of the old Caleb Rodney House on Second Street. Tunnell worked his way up the state government hierarchy before unsuccessfully running for governor in 1892. Four years later, the Democrat won the election, and served from 1897\xe2\x80\x931901. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lewes,_Delaware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" Lewes has a mild subtropical climate consisting of hot, humid summers and mild winters. The average daytime high in July is 87\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (30.6\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C) and a low of 70\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (21\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C); in January, the average high is 45\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (7\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C) with an average low of 30\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (-1\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weather.com_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-weather.com-19">[19]</a></sup> The month of highest average rainfall is July with 4.78 inches of rain, while February is historically the driest month, receiving an average of only 3.23\xc2\xa0inches (80.5\xc2\xa0mm) of rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weather.com_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-weather.com-19">[19]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The highest official temperature ever recorded in Lewes was 102\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (38.8\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C) in 1997. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 801.5 people per square mile (309.3/km\xc2\xb2). There were 2,368 housing units at an average density of 647.3 per square mile (249.8/km\xc2\xb2). The racial makeup of the city was 87.31% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 9.89% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.14% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 1.02% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.03% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.72% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 0.89% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.67% of the population.</p>\n<p>There were 1,338 households out of which 15.1% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 49.3% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 40.4% were non-families. 35.1% of all households were made up of individuals and 14.4% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 1.99 and the average family size was 2.53.</p>\n<p>In the city, the population was spread out with 13.6% under the age of 18, 3.7% from 18 to 24, 18.0% from 25 to 44, 31.5% from 45 to 64, and 33.1% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 55 years. For every 100 females, there were 78.2 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 76.1 males.</p>\n<p>The median income for a household in the city was $66,387, and the median income for a family was $72,605. Males had a median income of $39,500 versus $35,227 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $36,685. About 3.1% of families and 6.0% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 11.3% of those under age 18 and 5.4% of those age 65 or over.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lewes,_Delaware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:UD_Windmill.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1840" data-file-width="3264" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/UD_Windmill.jpg/220px-UD_Windmill.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/UD_Windmill.jpg/330px-UD_Windmill.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/UD_Windmill.jpg/440px-UD_Windmill.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:UD_Windmill.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\n<a href="/wiki/University_of_Delaware" title="University of Delaware">University of Delaware</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">wind turbine</a> seen from Canary Creek</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Lewes is served by the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Henlopen_School_District" title="Cape Henlopen School District">Cape Henlopen School District</a>. Smith Park</li>\n<li>Canalfront Park &amp; Marina</li>\n<li>Lewes Beach</li>\n<li>Great Marsh Park <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>\n</ul>\n<p><a the city limits along the <a href="/wiki/Broadkill_River" title="Broadkill River">Broadkill River</a>, adjacent to the Roosevelt Inlet.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lewes,_Delaware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lewes_Ferry_port.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2200" data-file-width="3301" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Lewes_Ferry_port.JPG/220px-Lewes_Ferry_port.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Lewes_Ferry_port.JPG/330px-Lewes_Ferry_port.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Lewes_Ferry_port.JPG/440px-Lewes_Ferry_port.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lewes_Ferry_port.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nThe Lewes terminal of the Cape May\xe2\x80\x93Lewes Ferry</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Delaware_Route_1" title="Delaware Route 1">Delaware Route 1</a> (DE 1) passes just outside city limits at <a href="/wiki/Five_Points,_Delaware" title="Five Points, Delaware">Five Points</a> where DE 1, <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_9_in_Delaware" title="U.S. Route 9 in Delaware">U.S. Route 9</a> (US 9), <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Route_404" title="Delaware Route 404">DE 404</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Route_23" title="Delaware Route 23">DE 23</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delaware_Route_1D" title="Delaware Route 1D">DE 1D</a> (Plantation Road) intersect. There are three main arterial roads that connect Lewes to DE 1: New Road, Savannah Road (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Route_9_Business_(Lewes,_Delaware)" title="U.S. Route 9 Business (Lewes, Delaware)">US 9 Business</a>) and King\'s Highway (US 9). US 9 passes to the southeast of the city on the Theodore C. Freeman Memorial Highway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DE_2008_map_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-DE_2008_map-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Parking_meter" title="Parking meter">Parking meters</a> are in effect for on-street parking and parking lots in the downtown area between May 1 and October 14 and at parking lots at Lewes Beach between May 1 and September 30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The southern terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_May%E2%80%93Lewes_Ferry" title="Cape May\xe2\x80\x93Lewes Ferry">Cape May\xe2\x80\x93Lewes Ferry</a> is located in Lewes. The ferry provides passenger and automobile ferry service between southern Delaware and <a href="/wiki/South_Jersey" title="South Jersey">southern New Jersey</a>, crossing the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Bay" title="Delaware Bay">Delaware Bay</a> to <a href="/wiki/North_Cape_May,_New_Jersey" title="North Cape May, New Jersey">North Cape May, New Jersey</a>, and serves as part of US 9. The ferry crossing is 17 miles (27\xc2\xa0km) long and takes 85 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DE_2008_map_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-DE_2008_map-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/DART_First_State" title="DART First State">DART First State</a> operates the year-round Route 206 bus, which connects Lewes to <a href="/wiki/Rehoboth_Beach,_Delaware" title="Rehoboth Beach, Delaware">Rehoboth Beach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Delaware" title="Georgetown, Delaware">Georgetown</a>. Between September and May, the bus provides service into Lewes while in the summer months bus service stops at the Lewes Transit Center outside the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beachbus_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-beachbus-32">[32]</a></sup> During the summer months, DART First State operates <a href="/wiki/DART_First_State_Sussex_County_bus_routes" title="DART First State Sussex County bus routes">Beach Bus</a> service out of the Lewes Transit Center Park and Ride just outside Lewes along DE 1. This park and ride serves the Route 201 bus to the Rehoboth Beach Boardwalk, the Route 203 bus to <a href="/wiki/Dewey_Beach,_Delaware" title="Dewey Beach, Delaware">Dewey Beach</a>, and the Route 204 bus which heads along Savannah Road into Lewes to Cape Henlopen Drive. The Route 305 "Beach Connection" bus provides service on weekends and holidays in the summer to the Lewes Transit Center Park and Ride from <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_Delaware" title="Wilmington, Delaware">Wilmington</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Christiana_Mall" title="Christiana Mall">Christiana Mall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middletown,_Delaware" title="Middletown, Delaware">Middletown</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dover,_Delaware" title="Dover, Delaware">Dover</a>, with service continuing south to Rehoboth Beach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beachbus_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-beachbus-32">[32]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Department_of_Transportation" title="Delaware Department of Transportation">Delaware Department of Transportation</a> built the Lewes Transit Center Park and Ride, with groundbreaking taking place on March 9, 2016 and the park and ride opening in May 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Delaware_River_and_Bay_Authority" title="Delaware River and Bay Authority">Delaware River and Bay Authority</a> operates shuttle bus routes in the summer months that connect the Cape May\xe2\x80\x93Lewes Ferry to downtown Lewes, Cape Henlopen State Park, the Lewes Transit Center, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tanger_Outlets" title="Tanger Outlets">Tanger Outlets</a>, and Rehoboth Beach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Lewes is served by a branch of the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Coast_Line_Railroad" title="Delaware Coast Line Railroad">Delaware Coast Line Railroad</a> that originates in Georgetown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DE_2008_map_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-DE_2008_map-28">[28]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Utilities">Utilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lewes,_Delaware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Utilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Lewes Board of Public Works (BPW) provides electricity, water, and sewer service to the city. The BPW was established by an act of the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_General_Assembly" title="Delaware General Assembly">Delaware General Assembly</a> on March 15, 1901.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Lewes formerly had a power plant that generated electricity for the city, but the plant\'s usage was reduced as the city brought in power from outside and the plant was shut down in the 1970s due to rising fuel costs. Lewes currently purchases power from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constellation_Energy" title="Constellation Energy">Constellation Energy</a> which is transmitted to the city over <a href="/wiki/Delmarva_Power" title="Delmarva Power">Delmarva Power</a> lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The BPW is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Municipal_Electric_Corporation" title="Delaware Municipal Electric Corporation">Delaware Municipal Electric Corporation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Trash collection is provided by the city while recycling collection is provided under contract by <a href="/wiki/Republic_Services" title="Republic Services">Republic Services</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Natural gas service in Lewes is provided by <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Utilities" title="Chesapeake Utilities">Chesapeake Utilities</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_care">Health care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lewes,_Delaware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Health care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beebe_Healthcare_Medical_Center" title="Beebe Healthcare Medical Center">Beebe Healthcare Medical Center</a> is located in Lewes, founded in 1916 by the brothers, Drs. James Beebe and Richard C. Beebe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Because of Beebe Healthcare Medical Center, there is a growing healthcare industry in and around the city. The hospital\'s name was changed to Beebe Healthcare in 2013, and 2016 marked its 100th anniversary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_events">Notable events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lewes,_Delaware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Notable events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On August 21, 2013, a helicopter reportedly dumped $10,000 in multiple dollar bill denominations over Lewes Harbor in the fulfillment of a deceased local resident\'s last wish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lewes,_Delaware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-City_of_Lewes_Delaware_Website-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-City_of_Lewes_Delaware_Website_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-City_of_Lewes_Delaware_Website_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-City_of_Lewes_Delaware_Website_1-2"><sup><i><b>c</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.ci.lewes.de.us/" rel="nofollow">"City of Lewes Delaware Website"</a>.
