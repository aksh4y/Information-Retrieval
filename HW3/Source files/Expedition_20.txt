21</a>\xc2\xa0\xe2\x86\x92</div>\n</div>\n</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Expedition 20</b> was the 20th long-duration flight to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>. The expedition marked the first time a six-member crew inhabited the station. Because each <a href="/wiki/Soyuz-TMA" title="Soyuz-TMA">Soyuz-TMA</a> spacecraft could hold only three people, two separate launches were necessary: <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-14" title="Soyuz TMA-14">Soyuz TMA-14</a> launched on 26 March 2009, and <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-15" title="Soyuz TMA-15">Soyuz TMA-15</a> followed on 27 May 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launch_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-launch-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Soyuz TMA-15 launched from <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> at 10:34 UTC on 27 May 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launch_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-launch-1">[1]</a></sup> The vehicle docked with the station on 29 May 2009, officially changing the Soyuz TMA-14 crew from <a href="/wiki/Expedition_19" title="Expedition 19">Expedition 19</a> to Expedition 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dock_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dock-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Gennady_Padalka" title="Gennady Padalka">Gennady Padalka</a> was the first commander of a six-member station crew, and the first commander of two consecutive expeditions (Expedition 19 and 20). <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Stott" title="Nicole Stott">Nicole Stott</a> was the final expedition astronaut to be launched on the shuttle.</p>\n<p>During the expedition, Koichi Wakata performed a special experiment wherein he did not change his underpants for one month, in order to test a specially-designed underwear without washing or changing; he reportedly did not develop body odor due to the effects of the special garment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The station would not be permanently occupied by six crew members all year. For example, when the Expedition 20 crew (<a href="/wiki/Roman_Romanenko" title="Roman Romanenko">Roman Romanenko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frank_De_Winne" title="Frank De Winne">Frank De Winne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Thirsk" title="Robert Thirsk">Bob Thirsk</a>) returned to Earth in November 2009, for a period of about two weeks only two crew members (<a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Williams_(astronaut)" title="Jeffrey Williams (astronaut)">Jeff Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maksim_Surayev" title="Maksim Surayev">Max Surayev</a>) were aboard. This increased to five in early December, when <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Kotov" title="Oleg Kotov">Oleg Kotov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Creamer" title="Timothy Creamer">Timothy Creamer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soichi_Noguchi" title="Soichi Noguchi">Soichi Noguchi</a> arrived on <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-17" title="Soyuz TMA-17">Soyuz TMA-17</a>. It decreased to three when Williams and Surayev departed in March 2010, and finally returned to six in April 2010 with the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-18" title="Soyuz TMA-18">Soyuz TMA-18</a>, carrying <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Skvortsov_(cosmonaut)" title="Aleksandr Skvortsov (cosmonaut)">Aleksandr Skvortsov</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mikhail_Korniyenko" title="Mikhail Korniyenko">Mikhail Korniyenko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tracy_Caldwell_Dyson" title="Tracy Caldwell Dyson">Tracy Caldwell Dyson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISSEx_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISSEx-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-current_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-current-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div
