border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/1991_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1991 Atlantic hurricane season">1991 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Bob</b> was one of the costliest hurricanes in <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> history. The second <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> and first hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1991_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1991 Atlantic hurricane season">1991 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Bob developed from an <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">area of low pressure</a> near <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a> on August 16. The depression steadily intensified, and became Tropical Storm Bob late on August 16. Bob curved north-northwestward as a tropical storm, but re-curved to the north-northeast after becoming a hurricane on August 17. As such, it brushed the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> on August 18 and August 19, and subsequently intensified into a major hurricane (Category 3 or greater on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>). After peaking in intensity with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_winds" title="Maximum sustained winds">maximum sustained winds</a> of 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h), Bob weakened slightly as it approached the coast of New England.</p>\n<p>Bob made landfall twice in <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> as a Category 2 hurricane on August 19, first on <a href="/wiki/Block_Island" title="Block Island">Block Island</a> and then in <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport</a>. Upon doing so, it became the only hurricane to make <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> in the contiguous United States during the 1991 season. Moving further inland, Bob rapidly weakened, and deteriorated to a tropical storm while emerging into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Maine" title="Gulf of Maine">Gulf of Maine</a>. Shortly thereafter, Bob made landfall in <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> as a strong tropical storm early on August 20. Bob entered the Canadian province of <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> a few hours later, where it transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a>. By August 21, the remnants of Bob crossed <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a> and re-emerged into the open Atlantic Ocean. The remnants traveled a long distance across the northern <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, and finally dissipated west of Portugal on August 29.</p>\n<p>Bob left extensive damage throughout New England in its wake, totaling approximately $1.5\xc2\xa0billion (1991 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>, $2.64\xc2\xa0billion 2017\xc2\xa0USD). This made it one of the costliest United States hurricanes at the time; as of 2013, it ranked thirty-second in the category. In addition, seventeen fatalities were reported in association with Bob. The loss of life and most of the damage occurred as a result of high winds and rough seas. There were six confirmed tornadoes during its passage. Bob is the most recent hurricane to hit the New England states directly as a hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bob_1991_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" href="/wiki/File:Bob_1991_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Bob originated from the remnants of a <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">frontal</a> <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> to the southeast of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on August\xc2\xa012. The system tracked towards the southwest and later west towards the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>. By August\xc2\xa015th, satellite analysis of the system found a weak <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> a couple hundred miles east of the Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR1-2">[2]</a></sup> Operationally, the system was not declared a tropical depression until 0600\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa016 after a reconnaissance mission into the storm found a closed circulation and flight level winds of 37\xc2\xa0mph (60\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-D1-3">[3]</a></sup> After post-storm analysis, it was determined that the low had developed into a depression around 0000\xc2\xa0UTC. Several hours after being designated, the system began to develop <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">convective</a> <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">banding features</a>. Roughly 18\xc2\xa0hours after being declared a depression, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) upgraded it to a tropical storm, giving it the name <i>Bob</i>. At this time, Bob was situated roughly 140\xc2\xa0mi (225\xc2\xa0km) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau, Bahamas</a>. The storm tracked slowly towards the northwest in response to the deep layer mean flow it was embedded within.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A deepening <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> over the eastern United States was forecast to turn the storm toward the north on August\xc2\xa016. This turn took place earlier than forecasters anticipated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The storm slowly intensified as convection was displaced from the <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">center of circulation</a>; however, upper-level <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> was well-defined and intensification of the storm was expected as it tracked over the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Later that day, Bob began to consolidate and a reconnaissance plane recorded hurricane-force winds at 1719\xc2\xa0UTC, following this reading, the NHC upgraded the storm to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_1" title="SSHS">Category 1 hurricane</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR1_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR1-2">[2]</a></sup> Shortly after, the hurricane began to turn towards the north-northeast in response to a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical</a> <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> over the Atlantic and the trough over the southeastern United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>By August\xc2\xa018, the NHC noted that the hurricane was asymmetrical, having uneven distribution of the wind radii.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Later that day, deep convection continued to form and an eye later appeared on satellite imagery. Early the next day, the eye became increasingly defined as the center of Bob passed roughly 35\xc2\xa0mi (55\xc2\xa0km) from the North Carolina coastline. By 0600\xc2\xa0UTC, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> recorded flight level winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (225\xc2\xa0km/h), corresponding to surface winds of 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h). At this time, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of the storm also decreased to 950\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 28.05\xc2\xa0inHg), the lowest pressure recorded during the storm. After attaining this intensity, the hurricane tracked quickly northeast at 25\xc2\xa0mph (35\xc2\xa0km/h), steered by the trough over the southeast United States, an upper-level cutoff low over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Lakes_region_(North_America)" title="Great Lakes region (North America)">Great Lakes Region</a> and the subtropical ridge over the Atlantic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_bob_1991.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1134" data-file-width="1161" height="215" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Hurricane_bob_1991.png/220px-Hurricane_bob_1991.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Hurricane_bob_1991.png/330px-Hurricane_bob_1991.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Hurricane_bob_1991.png/440px-Hurricane_bob_1991.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_bob_1991.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Bob near peak intensity</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The track of Hurricane Bob by August\xc2\xa019 was similar to that of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Carol" title="Hurricane Carol">Hurricane Carol</a> in <a href="/wiki/1954_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1954 Atlantic hurricane season">1954</a>, another major hurricane that impacted New England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR2-6">[6]</a></sup> Significantly cooler <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperatures" title="Sea surface temperatures">sea surface temperatures</a> in the path of the hurricane resulted in weakening, leading to the eye becoming cloud-filled. Later on August\xc2\xa019, the western portion of the eyewall brushed the eastern tip of <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>. Around 1800\xc2\xa0UTC, the center of Bob made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport, Rhode Island</a> with winds of 100\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h), making it a Category 2 hurricane. The storm quickly weakened as it tracked through <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> before entering the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Maine" title="Gulf of Maine">Gulf of Maine</a>. Around 0130\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa020, the now weakened Tropical Storm Bob made another landfall near <a href="/wiki/Rockport,_Maine" title="Rockport, Maine">Rockport, Maine</a> with winds of 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Later that day, Bob had crossed through <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> and part of <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and entered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_of_St._Lawrence" title="Gulf of St. Lawrence">Gulf of St. Lawrence</a>. Around 1800\xc2\xa0UTC, the former hurricane transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a>. Early the next day, the storm passed over northern <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a> before re-entering the Atlantic Ocean. Rapidly tracking eastward, the storm briefly weakened to the equivalent of a tropical depression on August\xc2\xa022. After restrengthening to tropical storm-force winds, the remnants of Bob turned towards the southeast and slowed. Once more, the extratropical system weakened to the equivalent of a tropical depression; however, it did not re-intensify. The storm slowly tracked towards the east before dissipating off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> on August\xc2\xa029.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bob_1991_rainfall.png"><img alt="" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/Bob_1991_rainfall.png/440px-Bob_1991_rainfall.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bob_1991_rainfall.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRainfall from Hurricane Bob in the United States</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Watches_and_warnings">Watches and warnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Watches and warnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Several hours after the declaration of Tropical Storm Bob on August\xc2\xa016, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Bahamas" title="Government of the Bahamas">Government of the Bahamas</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm warning</a> for the northwestern Bahamas, between the islands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andros_Island" title="Andros Island">Andros</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eleuthera_Island" title="Eleuthera Island">Eleuthera</a>. After the storm turned northward, this warning was discontinued as tropical storm-force winds were no longer expected to affect the islands. Shortly before Bob was upgraded to a hurricane on August\xc2\xa017, the NHC issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane watch</a> for coastal areas of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> between <a href="/wiki/Little_River_(Horry_County,_South_Carolina)" title="Little River (Horry County, South Carolina)">Little River Inlet</a> northward to <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach, Virginia</a>. Roughly four hours after this watch, it was upgraded to a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane warning</a>. A new hurricane watch was issued late on August\xc2\xa017, encompassing areas between Virginia Beach northward to <a href="/wiki/Cape_Henlopen" title="Cape Henlopen">Cape Henlopen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>. This watch was also upgraded to a warning on August\xc2\xa018 as Bob paralleled the Mid-Atlantic coastline. During the afternoon hours, a tropical storm warning was declared for areas in the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">lower Chesapeake Bay</a> area, including <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk, Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR17_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR17-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A new, extensive hurricane warning was issued late on August\xc2\xa018 as forecasts showed Bob moving directly over southern New England. The warning covered areas between Cape Henlopen, Delaware to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth,_Massachusetts" title="Plymouth, Massachusetts">Plymouth, Massachusetts</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Sound" title="Long Island Sound">Long Island Sound</a>. Early on August\xc2\xa019, all watches and warnings south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Lookout,_North_Carolina" title="Cape Lookout, North Carolina">Cape Lookout, North Carolina</a> were discontinued and the hurricane warning was extended northward to encompass areas south of <a href="/wiki/Eastport,_Maine" title="Eastport, Maine">Eastport, Maine</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a> began issuing warnings for <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a> later that day. <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> were placed under wind and heavy rain warnings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR17_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR17-8">[8]</a></sup> By the morning of August\xc2\xa020, all watches and warnings in the United States were discontinued; however, a brief tropical storm warning was declared for coastal Maine between <a href="/wiki/Rockland,_Maine" title="Rockland, Maine">Rockland</a> and Eastport before the remnants of Bob moved through eastern Canada. The advisories for <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a> were later discontinued after the system moved out of the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR18_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR18-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mid-Atlantic_states_and_the_Carolinas">Mid-Atlantic states and the Carolinas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mid-Atlantic states and the Carolinas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Following the issuance of a hurricane warning in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> on August\xc2\xa017,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR17_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR17-8">[8]</a></sup> mandatory evacuation orders were declared for the outermost islands in the state. Local police assisted significant traffic as an estimated 50,000 to 100,000 people left the region. At the height of the evacuations, delays in some places exceeded three hours. Despite the large number of evacuees, an additional 50,000 people were estimated to have stayed home and wait out the storm. Several bridges in the area were shut down prior to Bob\'s arrival as hurricane-force winds were likely to create life-threatening conditions on them. The <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a> in the state also shut down campgrounds across the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GBT1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-GBT1-10">[10]</a></sup> An evacuation shelter was also opened in <a href="/wiki/Carteret_County,_North_Carolina" title="Carteret County, North Carolina">Carteret County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KD1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-KD1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, 125 planes were relocated from <a href="/wiki/Langley_Air_Force_Base" title="Langley Air Force Base">Langley Air Force Base</a> to <a href="/wiki/Wright-Patterson_Air_Force_Base" title="Wright-Patterson Air Force Base">Wright-Patterson Air Force Base</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dayton,_Ohio" title="Dayton, Ohio">Dayton, Ohio</a>. Roughly 400 people evacuated from coastal areas in <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, and stores in <a href="/wiki/Ocean_City,_Maryland" title="Ocean City, Maryland">Ocean City</a> reported a substantial increase in sales related to storm preparation. Further north in <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, casinos prepared sandbags to protect the structures and tape to cover windows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KD1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-KD1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County,_New_York" title="Suffolk County, New York">Suffolk County, New York</a>, emergency officials were unsure of whether or not to issue evacuation orders for residents along coastal areas. This contemplation led to "mixed messages" on the ability of the government being sent to the public. By the evening of August\xc2\xa019, the evacuation order was not issued; however, the county was placed under a state of emergency and disaster centers were opened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> opened four shelters on Long Island that housed 800\xc2\xa0people during the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-fema1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_England_and_Canada">New England and Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New England and Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Prior to Bob\'s arrival in <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>, officials in <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> declared a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> in anticipation of significant damage. All state workers in the area were told to go home early and prepare for the storm. The <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_General_Assembly" title="Connecticut General Assembly">Connecticut Legislature</a> canceled a session on the state\'s budget and Governor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lowell_P._Weicker,_Jr." title="Lowell P. Weicker, Jr.">Lowell P. Weicker, Jr.</a> activated the emergency operations center. Submarines stationed in <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton</a> left port and waited out the storm under water. Airplane and train service was disrupted throughout the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_states" title="Mid-Atlantic states">Mid-Atlantic states</a> and New England in relation to Bob.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TDR1_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-TDR1-14">[14]</a></sup> The American Red Cross opened 23\xc2\xa0shelters in 16\xc2\xa0communities in Connecticut. In neighboring Rhode Island, the agency set up 40\xc2\xa0shelters,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-fema1-13">[13]</a></sup> and there were an additional 25\xc2\xa0shelters unaffiliated with the agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema2_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-fema2-15">[15]</a></sup> About 3,500\xc2\xa0people statewide evacuated, including 2,000 on Block Island. Several people injured themselves while preparing for the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ri_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-ri-16">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, thousands of residents evacuated <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a>, leading to an 11\xc2\xa0mi (18\xc2\xa0km) backup on the <a href="/wiki/Sagamore_Bridge" title="Sagamore Bridge">Sagamore Bridge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wp820_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-wp820-17">[17]</a></sup> There were nine shelters set up in the region,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema2_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-fema2-15">[15]</a></sup> which became overcrowded with evacuees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wp820_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-wp820-17">[17]</a></sup> There were about 50,000\xc2\xa0people who left their homes statewide, of which 6,500\xc2\xa0went to shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chant_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-chant-18">[18]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Martha%27s_Vineyard" title="Martha\'s Vineyard">Martha\'s Vineyard</a>, electronic stores reported their best business sales in years, with supplies of D-batteries being cleaned off the shelves. Many residents purchased these as well as battery-powered radios and flashlights. President <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a>, staying at his home in <a href="/wiki/Kennebunkport,_Maine" title="Kennebunkport, Maine">Kennebunkport, Maine</a>, evacuated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pease_Air_Force_Base" title="Pease Air Force Base">Pease Air Force Base</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>. To avoid possible risks by using helicopter, he drove by <a href="/wiki/Motorcade" title="Motorcade">motorcade</a> to the base. During this time, portions of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_95" title="Interstate 95">Interstate 95</a> were closed to allow him direct access to the Air Force base; however, this created many miles of backup as thousands of residents moved inland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wp820_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-wp820-17">[17]</a></sup> Eight shelters were opened in New Hampshire,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema2_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-fema2-15">[15]</a></sup> and about 5,200\xc2\xa0people \xe2\x80\x93 mostly tourists \xe2\x80\x93 evacuated the coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nh_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-nh-19">[19]</a></sup> There were 49\xc2\xa0shelters opened in neighboring Maine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema2_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-fema2-15">[15]</a></sup> and about 8,600\xc2\xa0people evacuated along the coastline in <a href="/wiki/York_County,_Maine" title="York County, Maine">York County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wcm_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-wcm-20">[20]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Fundy" title="Bay of Fundy">Bay of Fundy</a>, fishermen were urged to return to port in fears of large swells from Hurricane Bob.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_coast_of_the_United_States" title="East coast of the United States">east coast of the United States</a>, Hurricane Bob produced moderate rainfall and substantial damage. Damage totaled about $1.5\xc2\xa0billion (1991\xc2\xa0USD). That included about $700\xc2\xa0million through cleanup costs, uninsured losses, and food claims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-23">[23]</a></sup> That made it, at the time, one of the costliest United States hurricanes, although the total was due to the storm passing through a densely populated region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> As of 2010, Hurricane Bob ranked as the 22nd costliest tropical cyclone in the United States mainland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Across its track, the hurricane left 2.1\xc2\xa0million people without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> There were six confirmed tornadoes, along with thirteen unconfirmed tornadoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Carolinas_and_Mid-Atlantic">Carolinas and Mid-Atlantic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Carolinas and Mid-Atlantic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The center of Hurricane Bob passed several hundred miles east of South Carolina with only slight effects on the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR2-6">[6]</a></sup> However, the storm produced large <a href="/wiki/Swell_(ocean)" title="Swell (ocean)">swells</a> that caught a swimmer in <a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Beach,_South_Carolina" title="Myrtle Beach, South Carolina">Myrtle Beach</a> in strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undertow_(wave_action)" title="Undertow (wave action)">undertow</a>; this swimmer drowned to death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TDR1_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-TDR1-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-23">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A crew of three sailed from <a href="/wiki/Little_River_(Horry_County,_South_Carolina)" title="Little River (Horry County, South Carolina)">Little River (Horry County, South Carolina)</a> en route to Rhode Island. The hurricane destroyed the mast, leaving the crew stranded over the open ocean in shark-infested waters. After 12\xc2\xa0days, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> rescued the three.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On 18 August 1991, Hurricane Bob brushed the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, bringing strong winds and heavy rains. Although a maximum of 5.30 inches (135\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell at the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> office building at <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">Cape Hatteras</a>, little heavy rain fell inland of the Outer Banks. One person was killed in the state in relation to Bob, and damage from the storm was estimated at $8\xc2\xa0million (1991\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-23">[23]</a></sup> The community of <a href="/wiki/Duck,_North_Carolina" title="Duck, North Carolina">Duck, North Carolina</a>, received the highest recorded onshore sustained winds in the state, 62 miles per hour (100\xc2\xa0km/h), whereas the highest gusts reached 74 miles per hour (119\xc2\xa0km/h) at Cape Hatteras.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR9_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR9-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Shoal_Light" title="Diamond Shoal Light">Diamond Shoal Light</a> recorded a sustained wind of 85.0 knots (157.4\xc2\xa0km/h; 97.8\xc2\xa0mph) with a gust of 106.7 knots (197.6\xc2\xa0km/h; 122.8\xc2\xa0mph) and a minimum pressure of 962.1 millibars (962.1\xc2\xa0hPa) on the evening of 18/19 August 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Cape Hatteras also recorded a <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> of 2.6 to 4.6\xc2\xa0ft (0.79 to 1.40\xc2\xa0m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR9_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR9-29">[29]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Flooding was reported throughout the Outer Banks in relation to the heavy rains and storm surge of the storm. The storm required the shutdown of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Highway_12" title="North Carolina Highway 12">North Carolina Highway 12</a>, the only highway connecting the area to the mainland, isolating those who decided to stay behind. Many residents lost power as numerous power lines fell from strong winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GBT1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-GBT1-10">[10]</a></sup> Six brief tornadoes, ranging between F0 and F1 on the <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">Fujita scale</a>, struck the state within <a href="/wiki/Squall_line" title="Squall line">squall lines</a> in the outer <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">rainbands</a> of Hurricane Bob.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-23">[23]</a></sup> Four tornadoes in <a href="/wiki/Dare_County,_North_Carolina" title="Dare County, North Carolina">Dare County, North Carolina</a>, caused $256,000 in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-NCDC-31">[31]</a></sup> Near Cape Hatteras, wind severely damaged one structure, which lost its roof and some siding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TDR1_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-TDR1-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As the hurricane passed east of Virginia, it produced minimal rainfall and wind gusts of up to 40 miles per hour (64\xc2\xa0km/h). No one reported damage beyond some minor beach erosion in <a href="/wiki/Sandbridge,_Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Sandbridge, Virginia Beach, Virginia">Sandbridge, Virginia Beach, Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Ocean_City,_Maryland" title="Ocean City, Maryland">Ocean City, Maryland</a>, a brief period of heavy rainfall caused street flooding. High waves caused minimal beach erosion and <a href="/wiki/Overwash" title="Overwash">overwash</a> along the coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>\n<p>High tides to 5.9 feet (1.8\xc2\xa0m) occurred along the Delaware coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Farther north, the storm caused widespread beach erosion along the <a href="/wiki/Jersey_Shore" title="Jersey Shore">Jersey Shore</a>, while heavy rains caused street flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The heaviest rains outside New England fell in <a href="/wiki/Bridgehampton,_New_York" title="Bridgehampton, New York">Bridgehampton, New York</a>, where 7.18 inches (182\xc2\xa0mm) fell during the passage of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Two reported tornadoes struck <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup> High winds destroyed apple, corn, and peach fields along <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> After a dry summer, the sudden deluge wrecked potato crops, resulting in $20\xc2\xa0million in crop damage;,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-38">[38]</a></sup> about one-sixth of the agricultural output of the region. High winds caused power outages for 477,765 <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Lighting_Company" title="Long Island Lighting Company">Long Island Lighting Company</a> customers for a period of nearly five days. Most power outages occurred in the <a href="/wiki/East_End_(Long_Island)" title="East End (Long Island)">East End</a>. One person died in a car accident while a traffic light was offline,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-suf_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-suf-39">[39]</a></sup> and another person died when a falling tree struck a train conductor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema1_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-fema1-13">[13]</a></sup> The high waves destroyed fish nets offshore <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-38">[38]</a></sup> and caused beach erosion and the widespread destruction of boats along the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-suf_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-suf-39">[39]</a></sup> Statewide damage totaled over $75\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_England">New England</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: New England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Bob_19_aug_1991_1818Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1800" data-file-width="1600" height="248" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Hurricane_Bob_19_aug_1991_1818Z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Bob_19_aug_1991_1818Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Hurricane_Bob_19_aug_1991_1818Z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Bob_19_aug_1991_1818Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Hurricane_Bob_19_aug_1991_1818Z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Bob_19_aug_1991_1818Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Bob_19_aug_1991_1818Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Bob making landfall at <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Before Bob made landfall in New England, it passed directly over Block Island, Rhode Island. Stations on the island recorded gusts to 105\xc2\xa0mph (169\xc2\xa0km/h) near the upper end of their range, indicating that the winds were likely stronger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup> On the mainland, winds peaked at 90\xc2\xa0mph (144\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Narragansett,_Rhode_Island" title="Narragansett, Rhode Island">Narragansett</a>. In the state, the <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a>\xe2\x80\x94the rise of water above the normal tide\xe2\x80\x94was 6.6\xc2\xa0ft (2.0\xc2\xa0m) in the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence</a>, while the peak storm tide\xe2\x80\x94the rise of water including the normal high tide\xe2\x80\x94was 16.5\xc2\xa0ft (5.0\xc2\xa0m) at the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Sakonnet_River" title="Sakonnet River">Sakonnet River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR12_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR12-40">[40]</a></sup> At the latter location, the estimate was based on high-water marks, which included wave effects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup> The high waves resulted in extensive beach erosion along the state\'s coastline, which washed out several roads in <a href="/wiki/Coventry,_Rhode_Island" title="Coventry, Rhode Island">Coventry</a>. There was flooding reported along the <a href="/wiki/Pocasset_River_(Rhode_Island)" title="Pocasset River (Rhode Island)">Pocasset River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ri_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-ri-16">[16]</a></sup> Rainfall in the state peaked at 7.13\xc2\xa0in (181\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Foster,_Rhode_Island" title="Foster, Rhode Island">North Foster</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Eng_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Eng-41">[41]</a></sup> In Rhode Island, where the hurricane made landfall, Bob caused about 200,000 power outages;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema2_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-fema2-15">[15]</a></sup> about 60% of residents in the state and southeastern Massachusetts were left without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-box_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-box-42">[42]</a></sup> During the storm, treatment plants spilled over 100\xc2\xa0million tons of sewage due to overflow into Narragansett Bay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap826_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap826-43">[43]</a></sup> Damage totaled over $115\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>While moving over Rhode Island, the strong winds of Bob extended westward into Connecticut, peaking at 75\xc2\xa0mph (120\xc2\xa0km/h) near <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton</a>; there, a gust of 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h) was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR12_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR12-40">[40]</a></sup> High winds downed trees in every county in the state, although wind damage was heaviest in the southeastern portion near the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hart_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-hart-44">[44]</a></sup> Overall there were about 315,000\xc2\xa0people left without power in Connecticut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema1_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-fema1-13">[13]</a></sup> A man in <a href="/wiki/Sterling,_Connecticut" title="Sterling, Connecticut">Sterling</a> died after being struck by a falling tree limb.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hart2_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-hart2-45">[45]</a></sup> The highest storm surge was 5\xc2\xa0ft (1.5\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/New_London,_Connecticut" title="New London, Connecticut">New London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR12_40-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR12-40">[40]</a></sup> Coastal flooding was limited to New London County, although coastal damage was minimal. One woman died while on a capsized sailboat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hart2_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-hart2-45">[45]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Norwich,_Connecticut" title="Norwich, Connecticut">Norwich</a>, a rainfall total of 6.22\xc2\xa0in (158\xc2\xa0mm) was reported, which was the statewide peak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Eng_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-New_Eng-41">[41]</a></sup> Flooding was minor, limited to streams and small rivers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hart_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-hart-44">[44]</a></sup> Total damage in the state was estimated around $49\xc2\xa0million, including $4.5\xc2\xa0million in crop damage. There was one death due to a fire that occurred during the storm\'s passage,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hart_44-2"><a href="#cite_note-hart-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hwso_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-hwso-46">[46]</a></sup> and there were five other deaths across the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_23-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-23">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The most significant area affected by Bob was Massachusetts, where over $1\xc2\xa0billion in damage occurred.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-end-47">[47]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Coastal-Marine_Automated_Network" title="Coastal-Marine Automated Network">C-MAN</a> station in <a href="/wiki/Buzzards_Bay" title="Buzzards Bay">Buzzards Bay</a> recorded winds every hour and observed peak sustained winds of 77\xc2\xa0mph (124\xc2\xa0km/h), along with gusts to 89\xc2\xa0mph (143\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-6"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup> Sustained winds in the state peaked at 100\xc2\xa0mph (161\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Provincetown,_Massachusetts" title="Provincetown, Massachusetts">Provincetown</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR13_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR13-48">[48]</a></sup> and there was an unofficial report of a gust of 125\xc2\xa0mph (201\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brewster,_MA" title="Brewster, MA">Brewster</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> The highest rainfall total in the state was 7.06\xc2\xa0in (179\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Westfield,_Massachusetts" title="Westfield, Massachusetts">Westfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Eng_41-2"><a href="#cite_note-New_Eng-41">[41]</a></sup> Storm surges in the state were most significant along Buzzards Bay, peaking at 5.8\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford,_Massachusetts" title="New Bedford, Massachusetts">New Bedford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woods_Hole,_Massachusetts" title="Woods Hole, Massachusetts">Woods Hole</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR13_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR13-48">[48]</a></sup> High waves eroded the beach around <a href="/wiki/Chatham_Light" title="Chatham Light">Chatham Light</a>, leaving behind a 5\xc2\xa0ft (1.5\xc2\xa0m) cliff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chant_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-chant-18">[18]</a></sup> Other locations, including southward-facing shores along <a href="/wiki/Martha%27s_Vineyard" title="Martha\'s Vineyard">Martha\'s Vineyard</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nantucket" title="Nantucket">Nantucket</a>, lost 50\xc2\xa0ft (15\xc2\xa0m) of beach from erosion. Boat damage was significant in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-box_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-box-42">[42]</a></sup> The hurricane left over 500,000\xc2\xa0people without power, including all of Cape Cod.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema2_15-5"><a href="#cite_note-fema2-15">[15]</a></sup> Damage was heaviest from Buzzards Bay eastward to Cape Cod, and at least 61\xc2\xa0houses were destroyed. Damage was also extensive to apple and peach <a href="/wiki/Orchard" title="Orchard">orchards</a> across these areas,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-box_42-2"><a href="#cite_note-box-42">[42]</a></sup> and agricultural damage was estimated around $10\xc2\xa0million. The state also suffered $69\xc2\xa0million in damage to public property.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap826_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap826-43">[43]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Although it remained offshore of the state, Bob produced strong winds in New Hampshire, with gusts reaching 60\xc2\xa0mph (97\xc2\xa0km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Pease_Air_National_Guard_Base" title="Pease Air National Guard Base">Pease Air National Guard Base</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mount_Washington_(New_Hampshire)" title="Mount Washington (New Hampshire)">Mount Washington</a> experienced 7.46\xc2\xa0in (189\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation during the storm\'s passage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Eng_41-3"><a href="#cite_note-New_Eng-41">[41]</a></sup> Both the <a href="/wiki/Lovell_River" title="Lovell River">Lovell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contoocook_River" title="Contoocook River">Contoocook</a> rivers experienced flooding due to the storm, and widespread flooding occurred in urban areas in the south and central portions of the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nh_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-nh-19">[19]</a></sup> There were two deaths in the state, one from an automobile accident and the other due to a capsized boat. High winds downed widespread trees and power lines,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nh_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-nh-19">[19]</a></sup> causing 30,000\xc2\xa0people to lose power statewide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema2_15-6"><a href="#cite_note-fema2-15">[15]</a></sup> Damage was estimated around $2\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-7"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup> In neighboring Vermont, the highest rainfall report was 4.27\xc2\xa0in (108\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Vernon,_Vermont" title="Vernon, Vermont">Vernon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Eng_41-4"><a href="#cite_note-New_Eng-41">[41]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Although moving across Maine as a tropical storm, Bob still maintained strong winds, producing gusts to 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-port_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-port-51">[51]</a></sup> A station in <a href="/wiki/Wiscasset,_Maine" title="Wiscasset, Maine">Wiscasset</a> reported a gust of 92\xc2\xa0mph (148\xc2\xa0km/h) before it was blown away, and another station recorded a gust of 93\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-port2_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-port2-52">[52]</a></sup> A total of 169,200\xc2\xa0customers lost power during the hurricane, some of whom remaining without electricity for a week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-port_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-port-51">[51]</a></sup> There was a tornado reported in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Albans,_Maine" title="Saint Albans, Maine">St. Albans</a>, which downed multiple trees, damaged a few houses, and moved a boathouse three blocks away;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-port2_52-1"><a href="#cite_note-port2-52">[52]</a></sup> its status as a tornado was not confirmed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR3_27-8"><a href="#cite_note-TCR3-27">[27]</a></sup> As it passed the region, the storm produced a 2.77\xc2\xa0ft (0.84\xc2\xa0m) storm tide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-port_51-2"><a href="#cite_note-port-51">[51]</a></sup> The heaviest rainfall nationwide from the hurricane fell at the <a href="/wiki/Portland_International_Jetport" title="Portland International Jetport">Portland International Jetport</a>, where 8.24\xc2\xa0in (209\xc2\xa0mm) fell during its passage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brain_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-brain-53">[53]</a></sup> This contributed to August 1991 being the wettest month on record in Portland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> At the time, its 24\xe2\x80\x91hour rainfall total of 7.83\xc2\xa0in (199\xc2\xa0mm) was the highest on record, although it was surpassed in October 1996 by an extratropical system fueled by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lili_(1996)" title="Hurricane Lili (1996)">Hurricane Lili</a>. The rains from Bob led to a record <a href="/wiki/Discharge_(hydrology)" title="Discharge (hydrology)">flow rate</a> along the <a href="/wiki/Presumpscot_River" title="Presumpscot River">Presumpscot River</a>, although that record was also surpassed by the 1996 system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> River flooding washed out five bridges and roads across southwestern Maine. One man died due to being swept away by floodwaters, and another died in the ocean while on a life raft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wcm_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-wcm-20">[20]</a></sup> A total of 700\xc2\xa0houses were affected by the storm, including one that was destroyed and three that sustained major damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maap_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-maap-56">[56]</a></sup> Damage in the area around Portland totaled over $25.7\xc2\xa0million, and there were three deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-port_51-3"><a href="#cite_note-port-51">[51]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_Canada">Atlantic Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Atlantic Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After causing severe damage in the United States, the remnants of Hurricane Bob moved into <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_23-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-23">[23]</a></sup> The highest rainfall amount in the country was 4.37\xc2\xa0in (111\xc2\xa0mm) in northern New Brunswick. <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John\'s, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. John\'s, Newfoundland and Labrador</a> recorded a record 1\xc2\xa0inch (26\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall during the storm\'s passage. Gusty winds were observed across the region, and winds reached 68\xc2\xa0mph (109\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Digby,_Nova_Scotia" title="Digby, Nova Scotia">Digby, Nova Scotia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-chc-57">[57]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, two 17-year-old girls were washed out to sea near <a href="/wiki/Cape_Forchu,_Nova_Scotia" title="Cape Forchu, Nova Scotia">Cape Forchu</a> after being caught in rough swells produced by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TS2_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-TS2-58">[58]</a></sup> Rescue officials quickly began searching operations;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TS1_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-TS1-59">[59]</a></sup> however, it was later confirmed that the two had drowned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR4_23-6"><a href="#cite_note-TCR4-23">[23]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Fredericton" title="Fredericton">Fredericton</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, tropical storm-force winds downed trees and power lines, resulting in scattered power outages. <a href="/wiki/Lightning" title="Lightning">Lightning</a> associated with strong storms also reportedly struck some trees in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TS1_59-1"><a href="#cite_note-TS1-59">[59]</a></sup> A two-story home in <a href="/wiki/Pointe-Verte,_New_Brunswick" title="Pointe-Verte, New Brunswick">Pointe-Verte</a> collapsed due to high winds. In <a href="/wiki/Petit-Rocher,_New_Brunswick" title="Petit-Rocher, New Brunswick">Petit-Rocher</a>, five fishing boats sank after being overwhelmed by large swells at port. Firefighters in the area were surveying the town all night on August\xc2\xa020, checking for downed power lines and trees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Power outages were also reported on Prince Edward Island. The storm left about $1\xc2\xa0million in damage (1991\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">CAD</a>) on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Manan_Island" title="Grand Manan Island">Grand Manan Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Fundy" title="Bay of Fundy">Bay of Fundy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-chc-57">[57]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p>Following the storm\'s passage, the Long Island Lighting Company used 165\xc2\xa0workers to restore the power outages on Long Island. In Connecticut, state officials deployed about 200\xc2\xa0trucks to remove debris on roads. The Massachusetts National Guard was activated to assist in relief efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema1_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-fema1-13">[13]</a></sup> In Rhode Island, a man was killed while repairing damage caused by Bob.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Beaches in the state re-opened by five days after the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rj_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-rj-62">[62]</a></sup> The hurricane mixed the waters in <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Sound" title="Long Island Sound">Long Island Sound</a> and prevented the algae bloom that happened in previous summers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> In the days after Bob, the <a href="/wiki/NSTAR_(company)" title="NSTAR (company)">Commonwealth Electric Company</a> brought electricians from outside the company and put their own workers on 16\xe2\x80\x91hour shifts to restore power across Massachusetts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap826_43-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap826-43">[43]</a></sup> Most Massachusetts residents, excluding those living on Cape Cod, had their power restored within five days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rj_62-1"><a href="#cite_note-rj-62">[62]</a></sup> The hurricane struck toward the end of the summer, which significantly impacted the tourism season already in the midst of a <a href="/wiki/Early_1990s_recession" title="Early 1990s recession">recession</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap826_43-3"><a href="#cite_note-ap826-43">[43]</a></sup> The New England coast was affected by a powerful nor\'easter known as the <a href="/wiki/1991_Perfect_Storm" title="1991 Perfect Storm">Perfect Storm</a> about two months after the hurricane struck the region. Some locations experienced worse damage from the October storm than from Bob.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maine_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-maine-64">[64]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to the high damage from Hurricane Bob, president George H.W. Bush declared the following states as disaster (in order): Rhode Island, Massachusetts, Maine, Connecticut, New Hampshire, and New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> Damage in Maine was insufficient to qualify for individual family assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maap_56-1"><a href="#cite_note-maap-56">[56]</a></sup> Instead, the declaration allocated federal funding to reimburse cities for debris removal, repairing damaged public buildings, and other municipal expenses for each of the states. Despite the declaration, the federal government did not immediately provide aid to the affected region. This was partially due to President Bush considering the storm a "disaster but not an emergency", which therefore required cuts from other budgets to offset the relief aid. This was in opposition to the Democratic-controlled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/101st_U.S._Congress" title="101st U.S. Congress">Congress</a>, who requested an expedited process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> The name Bob was subsequently retired by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>\'s hurricane committee, and will never be used again for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHC_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-67">[67]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
