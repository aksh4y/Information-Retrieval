title="Expedition 43">Expedition 43</a> crew celebrate a birthday in Zvezda module, 2015</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p><i><b>Zvezda</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">\xd0\x97\xd0\xb2\xd0\xb5\xd0\xb7\xd0\xb4\xd0\xb0\xcc\x81</span>, meaning "star"), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salyut_program" title="Salyut program">DOS-8</a>, also known as the <i><b>Zvezda Service Module</b></i>, is a component of the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS). It was the third module launched to the station, and provides all of the station\'s <a href="/wiki/Life_support_system" title="Life support system">life support systems</a>, some of which are supplemented in the <a href="/wiki/US_Orbital_Segment" title="US Orbital Segment">USOS</a>, as well as living quarters for two crew members. It is the structural and functional center of the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> portion of the station - the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orbital_Segment" title="Russian Orbital Segment">Russian Orbital Segment</a>.</p>\n<p>The module was manufactured by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S.P._Korolev_Rocket_and_Space_Corporation_Energia" title="S.P. Korolev Rocket and Space Corporation Energia">RKK Energia</a>, with major sub-contracting work by GKNPTs Khrunichev.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruspace_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ruspace-6">[6]</a></sup> <i>Zvezda</i> was launched on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_(rocket)" title="Proton (rocket)">Proton rocket</a> on July 12, 2000 and docked with the <i><a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">Zarya</a></i> module on July 26.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zvezda_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mir-2" title="Mir-2">Mir-2</a></div>\n<p>The basic structural frame of <i>Zvezda</i>, known as "DOS-8", was initially built in the mid-1980s to be the core of the <i><a href="/wiki/Mir-2" title="Mir-2">Mir-2</a></i> space station. This means that <i>Zvezda</i> is similar in layout to the <a href="/wiki/Mir_Core_Module" title="Mir Core Module">core module (DOS-7)</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">Mir</a></i> space station. It was in fact labeled as "<i>Mir</i>-2" for quite some time in the factory. Its design lineage thus extends back to the original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salyut" title="Salyut">Salyut</a> stations. The space frame was completed in February 1985 and major internal equipment was installed by October 1986.</p>\n<p>The MIR-2 space station was redesigned after the failure of the <a href="/wiki/Polyus_(spacecraft)" title="Polyus (spacecraft)">Polyus</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orbital_weapons_platform" title="Orbital weapons platform">orbital weapons platform</a> core module to reach orbit. Zvezda is around 1/4 the size of Polyus, and has no armaments.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zvezda_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>Zvezda</i> consists of the cylindrical "Work Compartment" where the crews work and live (and which makes up the bulk of the modules volume), the small spherical "Transfer Compartment" located at the front (with three docking ports), and at the aft end the cylindrical "Transfer Chamber" (with one docking port) which is surrounded by the unpressurized "Assembly Compartment" \xe2\x80\x93 this gives <i>Zvezda</i> four docking ports in total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruspace_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-ruspace-6">[6]</a></sup> The component weighs about 18,051\xc2\xa0kg (39,796\xc2\xa0lb) and had a length of 13.1 metres (43\xc2\xa0ft). The solar panels extend 29.7 metres (97\xc2\xa0ft).</p>\n<p>The "Transfer Compartment" attaches to the <i>Zarya</i> module, and has docking ports intended for the <a href="/wiki/Science_Power_Platform" title="Science Power Platform">Science Power Platform</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Docking_Module" title="Universal Docking Module">Universal Docking Module</a>. As in the early days of <i>Mir</i>, the transfer compartment provides a suitable EVA airlock where spacewalkers in Orlan suits removed a hatch after closing a few that connected the compartment to the rest of the station. It was used only during <a href="/wiki/Expedition_2" title="Expedition 2">Expedition 2</a>, where two men put a docking cone on the nadir port. The lower port connects to <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)"><i>Pirs</i></a> and the top port connects to <a href="/wiki/Poisk_(ISS_module)" title="Poisk (ISS module)"><i>Poisk</i></a>. Eventually, the plan for Pirs is for it to be deorbited and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Nauka_(ISS_module)" title="Nauka (ISS module)">Nauka</a>(Multipurpose Laboratory Module).</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ISS-06_Sprouts_on_the_Russian_plant_growth_experiment.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="680" data-file-width="1024" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/ISS-06_Sprouts_on_the_Russian_plant_growth_experiment.jpg/220px-ISS-06_Sprouts_on_the_Russian_plant_growth_experiment.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/ISS-06_Sprouts_on_the_Russian_plant_growth_experiment.jpg/330px-ISS-06_Sprouts_on_the_Russian_plant_growth_experiment.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/ISS-06_Sprouts_on_the_Russian_plant_growth_experiment.jpg/440px-ISS-06_Sprouts_on_the_Russian_plant_growth_experiment.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ISS-06_Sprouts_on_the_Russian_plant_growth_experiment.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSprouts in the BIO-5 Rasteniya-2/Lada-2 (Plants-2) experiment aboard Zvezda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The "Assembly Compartment" holds external equipment such as thrusters, thermometers, antennas, and propellant tanks. The "Transfer Chamber" is equipped with automatic docking equipment and is used to service <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyuz_spacecraft" title="Soyuz spacecraft">Soyuz</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progress_spacecraft" title="Progress spacecraft">Progress spacecraft</a>.</p>\n<p><i>Zvezda</i> can support up to six crew<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruspace_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-ruspace-6">[6]</a></sup> including separate sleeping quarters for two cosmonauts at a time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruspace_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-ruspace-6">[6]</a></sup> It also has a <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>-provided <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treadmill_with_Vibration_Isolation_System" title="Treadmill with Vibration Isolation System">Treadmill with Vibration Isolation System</a> and a bicycle for exercise, a toilet and other hygiene facilities and a galley with a refrigerator and freezer. The module contains the primary Russian computers for guidance and navigation.</p>\n<p><i>Zvezda</i> has 14 windows<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruspace_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-ruspace-6">[6]</a></sup>\xe2\x80\x94 There are two 9-inch-diameter (230\xc2\xa0mm) windows, one in each of the two crew sleep compartments (windows No. 1 and 2). Six 9-inch-diameter (230\xc2\xa0mm) windows (No. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 and 8) on the forward Transfer Compartment earth facing floor. As well a 16-inch-diameter (410\xc2\xa0mm) window in the main Working Compartment (No. 9) and one 3-inch-diameter (76\xc2\xa0mm) window in the aft transfer compartment (No. 10). There are a further three 9-inch-diameter (230\xc2\xa0mm) windows in the forward end of the forward transfer compartment (No. 12, 13 and 14), for observing approaching craft. Note: Window No. 11 is unaccounted for in all available sources.</p>\n<p><i>Zvezda</i> also contains the <a href="/wiki/ISS_ECLSS#Elektron" title="ISS ECLSS">Elektron</a> system that electrolyzes condensed humidity and waste water to provide <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>. The hydrogen is expelled into space and the oxygen is used for breathing air. The condensed <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> and the waste water can be used for drinking in an emergency, but ordinarily fresh water from Earth is used. There are 16 small thrusters and two large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S5.79" title="S5.79">S5.79</a> thrusters for propulsion. The oxidizer used for the propulsion system is <a href="/wiki/Dinitrogen_tetroxide" title="Dinitrogen tetroxide">dinitrogen tetroxide</a> and the fuel is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UDMH" title="UDMH">UDMH</a>, the supply tanks being pressurised with nitrogen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russianspaceweb_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-russianspaceweb-8">[8]</a></sup> Additionally, it has eight batteries for storing power. The Elektron system has required significant maintenance work, having failed several times and requiring the crew to use the <a href="/wiki/Vika_oxygen_generator" title="Vika oxygen generator">Solid Fuel Oxygen Generator</a> canisters (also called <a href="/wiki/Chemical_oxygen_generator#Oxygen_candle" title="Chemical oxygen generator">"Oxygen Candles"</a>, which were the cause of a fire on <i>Mir</i>) when it has been broken for extended amounts of time. It also contains the Vozdukh, a system which removes <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> from the air based on the use of regenerable absorbers of carbon dioxide gas. <i>Zvezda</i> has been criticized for being excessively noisy and the crew has been observed wearing earplugs inside it.</p>\n<p><i>Zvezda</i> contains the ESA\'s Digital Management System, a computer that controls ISS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p><i>Zvezda</i> is also the home of the Lada Greenhouse, which is a test for growing plants in space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Connection_to_the_ISS">Connection to the ISS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zvezda_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Connection to the ISS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Zvezda_rear.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1984" data-file-width="2968" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Zvezda_rear.jpg/220px-Zvezda_rear.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Zvezda_rear.jpg/330px-Zvezda_rear.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Zvezda_rear.jpg/440px-Zvezda_rear.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Zvezda_rear.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nProgress docked to Zvezda (<i>aft view</i>)</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The rocket used for launch to the ISS carried <a href="/wiki/Space_advertising" title="Space advertising">advertising</a>; it was emblazoned with the <a href="/wiki/Logo" title="Logo">logo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pizza_Hut" title="Pizza Hut">Pizza Hut</a> restaurants,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pizza-hut_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-pizza-hut-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pizza-hut2_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-pizza-hut2-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> for which they are reported to have paid more than <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The money helped support <a href="/wiki/Khrunichev_State_Research_and_Production_Space_Center" title="Khrunichev State Research and Production Space Center">Khrunichev State Research and Production Space Center</a> and the Russian advertising agencies that orchestrated the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On July 26, 2000, <i>Zvezda</i> became the third component of the ISS when it docked at the aft port of <i>Zarya</i>. (<i>Zarya</i> had already been attached to the U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unity_Module" title="Unity Module"><i>Unity</i> module</a>.) Later in July, the computers aboard <i>Zarya</i> handed over ISS commanding functions to computers on <i>Zvezda</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On September 11, 2000, two members of the <a href="/wiki/STS-106" title="STS-106">STS-106</a> <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> crew completed final connections between <i>Zvezda</i> and <i>Zarya</i>; during a 6-hour, 14 minute <a href="/wiki/Extravehicular_activity" title="Extravehicular activity">EVA</a>, astronaut Ed Lu and cosmonaut Yuri Malenchenko connected nine cables between <i>Zvezda</i> and <i>Zarya</i>, including four power cables, four video and data cables and a fiber-optic telemetry cable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The next day, STS-106 crew members floated into <i>Zvezda</i> for the first time, at 05:20 UTC on September 12, 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>\n<p><i>Zvezda</i> provided early living quarters, a life support system, a communication system (<i>Zvezda</i> introduced a 10\xc2\xa0Mbit/s Ethernet network to the ISS<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>), electrical power distribution, a data processing system, a flight control system, and a propulsion system. These quarters and some, but not all, systems have since been supplemented by additional ISS components.</p>\n<p>The two main engines on <i>Zvezda</i> can be used to raise the station\'s altitude. This was done on April 25, 2007. This was the first time the engines had been fired since <i>Zvezda</i> arrived in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Launch_risks">Launch risks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zvezda_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Launch risks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Due to Russian financial problems, <i>Zvezda</i> was launched with no backup and no insurance. Due to this risk, NASA had constructed an <a href="/wiki/Interim_Control_Module" title="Interim Control Module">Interim Control Module</a> in case it was delayed significantly or destroyed on launch.</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Interior">Interior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
