class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Springfield,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Established on December 16,1790 at the "Cross Roads" of Hampshire County on the property of William and Samuel Abernethy by an act of the Virginia General Assembly,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Springfield was named in commemoration of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Springfield_(1780)" title="Battle of Springfield (1780)">Battle of Springfield (1780)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> first visited the Springfield area in 1748 as a member of a party that surveyed the land holdings of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Fairfax,_6th_Lord_Fairfax_of_Cameron" title="Thomas Fairfax, 6th Lord Fairfax of Cameron">Thomas Fairfax, 6th Lord Fairfax of Cameron</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River#South_Branch_Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">South Branch Potomac River</a> Valley.</p>\n<p>In June 1861, the town organized a <a href="/wiki/Company_(military_unit)" title="Company (military unit)">company</a> of men known as the "Potomac Guards" in support of the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederacy</a>. The company was under the command of Captain Philip T. Grace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On August 23, 1861, Springfield played host to an <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> skirmish between the Unionists and the Confederates.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_Sites">Historic Sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Springfield,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historic Sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Today, Springfield is the site of a number of historic private residences dating from the 18th and 19th Centuries.</p>\n<ul>\n<li><b>63 Springfield Pike</b> (1860), <a href="/wiki/Springfield_Grade_Road" title="Springfield Grade Road">Springfield Pike</a> (CR 3)</li>\n<li><b><a href="/wiki/Washington_Bottom_Farm" title="Washington Bottom Farm">Ridgedale (George W.
