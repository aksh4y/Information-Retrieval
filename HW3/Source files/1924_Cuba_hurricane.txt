colspan="2" style="text-align:center; border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/1924_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1924 Atlantic hurricane season">1924 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The <b>1924 Cuba hurricane</b> is the earliest officially classified <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a>, and one of two hurricanes to make landfall on <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> at Category 5 intensity, the other being <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irma" title="Hurricane Irma">Hurricane Irma</a> in 2017. It formed on October\xc2\xa014 in the western Caribbean, slowly organizing as it tracked northwestward. By October\xc2\xa016, it attained hurricane status to the east of the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</a>, and subsequently executed a small counterclockwise loop. On October\xc2\xa018, the hurricane began undergoing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid deepening</a>, and the next day it reached an estimated peak intensity of 165\xc2\xa0mph (270\xc2\xa0km/h). Shortly thereafter, it struck extreme western Cuba at peak intensity, becoming the strongest hurricane on record to hit the country. Later the hurricane weakened greatly, striking southwestern Florida with winds of 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h) in a sparsely populated region. While crossing the state it weakened to tropical storm status, and after accelerating east-northeastward, it was absorbed by a <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">cold front</a> on October\xc2\xa023 south of Bermuda.</p>\n<p>Across the western Caribbean Sea, the developing storm produced heavy rainfall and increased winds. Strong winds in western Cuba caused severe damage, with two small towns nearly destroyed. About 90\xc2\xa0people were killed in the country, all in <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province</a>. Later, the hurricane brought heavy rainfall to southern Florida, which caused flooding and crop damage. Damage was light in the state, and there were no casualties.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1924_Cuba_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1924_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/1924_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png/220px-1924_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/1924_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png/330px-1924_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/1924_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png/440px-1924_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1924_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On October\xc2\xa014, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical depression</a> was first observed over the western Caribbean Sea, just off the eastern Honduras coast. It was a large and weak tropical cyclone, moving slowly northwestward and gradually intensifying. On October\xc2\xa015, it is estimated the depression attained tropical storm status, and its strengthening became more steady. The next day, the storm reached hurricane status about 130\xc2\xa0mi (215\xc2\xa0km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Cozumel" title="Cozumel">Cozumel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>. Around that time, it began to execute a small counterclockwise loop off the east coast of the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula. By October\xc2\xa018 the hurricane completed the loop, during which its winds increased to 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h); this is the equivalence of a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">major hurricane</a>, or a Category\xc2\xa03 on the Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale. The estimation of its strength at this point was based on subsequent analysis of peripheral recordings of <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressure</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_winds" title="Maximum sustained winds">maximum sustained winds</a> by ships and land stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-meta-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Beginning late on October\xc2\xa018 as it tracked north-northeastward toward Cuba, the hurricane underwent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid deepening</a>, evidenced by a ship wind report of 120\xc2\xa0mph (193\xc2\xa0km/h). This wind report was initially thought to be the peak intensity of the cyclone; however, subsequent research confirmed further deepening, based on very low pressures recorded across the region. A ship in the <a href="/wiki/Radius_of_maximum_wind" title="Radius of maximum wind">radius of maximum winds</a> reported a reading of 922\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a>; the barometer on the ship was found to be 5\xc2\xa0mbar too high, resulting in a pressure of 917\xc2\xa0mbar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-meta-1">[1]</a></sup> Additionally, a station on land reported a pressure of 932\xc2\xa0mbar (27.52\xc2\xa0inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Based on the readings, the <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Research_Division" title="Hurricane Research Division">Hurricane Research Division</a> estimated the hurricane attained a minimum central pressure of 910\xc2\xa0mbar very near the western coast of Cuba; this suggested peak winds of 165\xc2\xa0mph (270\xc2\xa0km/h). Late on October\xc2\xa019, the hurricane made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> in extreme western Cuba in <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-meta-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Mill%C3%A1s" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Carlos Mill\xc3\xa1s">Jos\xc3\xa9 Carlos Mill\xc3\xa1s</a>, director of the National Observatory at Havana, believed that "this hurricane [was] one of the most severe ever experienced in our latitudes."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After exiting Cuba into the Gulf of Mexico, the hurricane weakened greatly. On October\xc2\xa020 it passed a short distance west of <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">Key West, Florida</a>, and very early on October\xc2\xa021 the hurricane moved over <a href="/wiki/Marco_Island,_Florida" title="Marco Island, Florida">Marco Island</a> with winds of 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h). The cyclone weakened further as it turned eastward through the state, deteriorating to tropical storm status as it passed near or over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">Miami</a>. It accelerated east-northeastward, moving over the <a href="/wiki/Abaco_Islands" title="Abaco Islands">Abaco Islands</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a>. Gradually weakening, the storm interacted with an approaching cold front; late on October\xc2\xa023 it transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a>, and shortly thereafter it was absorbed by the front.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-meta-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_and_records">Impact and records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1924_Cuba_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact and records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As a developing tropical cyclone, the storm produced increased winds and lower pressures in the <a href="/wiki/Swan_Islands,_Honduras" title="Swan Islands, Honduras">Swan Islands</a>, off the coast of Honduras.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-meta-1">[1]</a></sup> Heavy rainfall occurred throughout Jamaica, causing street flooding and several mudslides, but little damage. No disruptions were reported to communications or railway travel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The storm brushed eastern Belize while located off the coast, producing 3.62\xc2\xa0inches (21.9\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall and light winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In extreme western Cuba, damage was very severe from the strong winds, likened to the impact of a tornado. Severe damage was reported in Los Arroyos and Arroyos de Mantua. In the latter location, around a dozen people were killed, 50 were injured, and nearly every building in the town was severely damaged; heavy losses also occurred to the tobacco crop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Across western <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province</a>, the hurricane destroyed all communication links.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-his3_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-his3-5">[5]</a></sup> Further from the center, the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a> recorded southerly winds of 72\xc2\xa0mph (116\xc2\xa0km/h), as well as a minimum pressure of about 999\xc2\xa0mbar (29.50\xc2\xa0inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Around the country, the hurricane capsized several ships, primarily fishing vessels. The death toll in the country was estimated at around 90.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-his3_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-his3-5">[5]</a></sup> In the days after the storm, Cuban <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Zayas_y_Alfonso" title="Alfredo Zayas y Alfonso">President Zayas</a> authorized about $30,000 in relief aid to send to hurricane victims in Pinar del R\xc3\xado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Several days prior to striking Florida, the outer circulation began producing rainfall across the state. <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">Storm warnings</a> were issued along the east and west coastlines northward to <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Key,_Florida" title="Cedar Key, Florida">Cedar Key</a> and <a href="/wiki/Titusville,_Florida" title="Titusville, Florida">Titusville</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Later, hurricane warnings were issued for much of the same area,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and schools in the <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa</a> area were closed as the storm was expected to move ashore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The hurricane first affected Florida when it passed west of <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">Key West</a>, where sustained winds of 66\xc2\xa0mph (107\xc2\xa0km/h), along with gusts to 74\xc2\xa0mph (120\xc2\xa0km/h), were reported. Little damage occurred in the region, limited to downed trees; this was due to advance warning by the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">U.S. Weather Bureau</a>, which advised ships to remain at port and for residents to secure property.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Later, the hurricane moved ashore in a sparsely populated region of southwestern Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Damage was reported in Fort Myers and Punta Gorda and communications were temporarily cut, although no deaths were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Heavy rainfall was reported along its path, and one location accumulated 23.22\xc2\xa0inches (590\xc2\xa0mm) in a 24\xe2\x80\x91hour period; this established a new one-day rainfall record in the state. A station in Miami recorded 12.18\xc2\xa0inches (309\xc2\xa0mm), and wind gusts in the area approached hurricane force. The combination of winds and rain damaged 5% of the local citrus and avocado crop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-meta-1">[1]</a></sup> The rainfall flooded streets, homes, and commercial buildings in the Miami area, and hundreds of people were left without telephone access.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> No impact was reported in the Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-meta-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After a reanalysis of hurricanes between 1921 and 1925, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Centers</a> Atlantic reanalysis project determined this hurricane attained winds of 165\xc2\xa0mph (270\xc2\xa0km/h), making it a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5</a> on the Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale. The hurricane is the earliest known to have attained the intensity, besting the <a href="/wiki/1928_Okeechobee_hurricane" title="1928 Okeechobee hurricane">1928 Okeechobee hurricane</a>, which was previously thought to be the earliest storm of this intensity. It is also one of only two on record to make <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> in Cuba at Category\xc2\xa05 status, with the other being <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irma" title="Hurricane Irma">Hurricane Irma</a> of <a href="/wiki/2017_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2017 Atlantic hurricane season">2017</a>, which was slightly weaker at landfall, having maximum sustained winds of 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-meta-1">[1]</a></sup> A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Havana_Hurricane_of_1846" title="Great Havana Hurricane of 1846">hurricane in 1846</a> that hit the country was also thought to have struck at Category\xc2\xa05 status, although the storm existed prior to the start of the Atlantic hurricane database.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> When the steamship "Toledo" recorded an atmospheric pressure of 922\xc2\xa0mbar (27.22\xc2\xa0inHg) in the storm, it was the lowest pressure recorded in an Atlantic hurricane, breaking the previous record of 924 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mbar" title="Mbar">mbar</a> (27.28 inHg) in the Atlantic hurricane of 1853. The record during this storm lasted until the <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">1932 Cuba hurricane</a>, when a pressure of 915\xc2\xa0mbar (27.02\xc2\xa0inHg) was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1846pres_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-1846pres-14">[14]</a></sup> The reading of 932 mbar (27.52 inHg) at Los Arroyos in <a href="/wiki/Mantua,_Cuba" title="Mantua, Cuba">Mantua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">Pinar del R\xc3\xado</a> remains the lowest pressure recorded on land in Cuba.<sup
