title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/\xcb\x88/: primary stress follows">\xcb\x88</span><span title="/\xca\x92/: \'s\' in \'pleasure\'">\xca\x92</span><span title="/\xc9\x94\xcb\x90r/: \'ar\' in \'war\'">\xc9\x94\xcb\x90r</span><span title="/\xca\x92/: \'s\' in \'pleasure\'">\xca\x92</span></span>/</a></span></span> was a powerful and long-lived <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde-type_hurricane" title="Cape Verde-type hurricane">Cape Verde-type</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_4_hurricane" title="Category 4 hurricane">Category 4 hurricane</a> which caused severe destruction as it traversed the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> in September 1998, making eight landfalls along its path. Georges was the seventh tropical storm, fourth hurricane, and second major hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1998_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1998 Atlantic hurricane season">1998 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. It became the most destructive storm of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="Atlantic hurricane season">season</a> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_costliest_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of costliest Atlantic hurricanes">costliest Atlantic hurricane</a> since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">Hurricane Andrew</a> in 1992, and the deadliest since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Gordon_(1994)" title="Hurricane Gordon (1994)">Hurricane Gordon</a> in 1994. Georges killed 604\xc2\xa0people, mainly on the island of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>, and caused extensive damage resulting at just under $10\xc2\xa0billion (1998 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_dollars" title="US dollars">US dollars</a>, equivalent to $17\xc2\xa0billion in 2016) in damages mostly in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Kitts_and_Nevis" title="St. Kitts and Nevis">St. Kitts and Nevis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>.</p>\n<p>The hurricane made landfall in at least seven different countries (<a href="/wiki/Antigua_and_Barbuda" title="Antigua and Barbuda">Antigua and Barbuda</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Kitts_and_Nevis" title="St. Kitts and Nevis">St. Kitts and Nevis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> and the United States) and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, a territory of the United States\xe2\x80\x94more than any other hurricane since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Inez" title="Hurricane Inez">Hurricane Inez</a> of the <a href="/wiki/1966_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1966 title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> exited the coast of Africa on September 13. Moving westward, the large system quickly developed a closed circulation, and was classified Tropical Depression Seven on September 15. A strong upper-level ridge forced the depression to the west-northwest, where warm water temperatures allowed it to strengthen to a tropical storm on September 16. Georges\'s circulation developed strong banding features around a well-organized <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Structure_and_classification" title="Tropical cyclone">Central Dense Overcast</a>, and with the aid of a developing anti-cyclone, Georges attained hurricane status late on September 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Georges_25_sept_1998_1935Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1240" data-file-width="1240" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Hurricane_Georges_25_sept_1998_1935Z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Georges_25_sept_1998_1935Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Hurricane_Georges_25_sept_1998_1935Z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Georges_25_sept_1998_1935Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Hurricane_Georges_25_sept_1998_1935Z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Georges_25_sept_1998_1935Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Georges_25_sept_1998_1935Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nGeorges near the Florida Keys</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Conditions became nearly ideal for continued development, including warm water temperatures, low-level inflow to the hurricane\'s north, and good upper-level outflow. A banding eye developed, and Georges reached major hurricane strength on September 19 while 675\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Mile" title="Mile">mi</a> (1085\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Kilometre" title="Kilometre">km</a>) east-southeast of <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>. By September 19, an upper-level anticyclone was well established over Georges and satellite pictures suggested that the hurricane was beginning to strengthen rapidly, as indicated by the cooling cloud tops, increased symmetry of the deep convection, and the warming and contracting of the well-defined 40\xc2\xa0mi (63\xc2\xa0km) wide eye as rapid intensification continued, and Georges peaked at a very dangerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_4_hurricane" title="Category 4 hurricane">Category 4</a> storm with 155\xc2\xa0mph (250\xc2\xa0km/h) wind and a 937 minimal pressure late on September 19 and early September 20. At that time, Georges was the most intense, strongest storm since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">Hurricane Hugo</a> and alongside <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Luis" title="Hurricane Luis">Hurricane Luis</a>, it is one of the largest major hurricanes in the Atlantic with hurricane force windfields extending more than 150\xc2\xa0mi (250\xc2\xa0km) from the center and with a more than 300\xc2\xa0mi (490\xc2\xa0km) wide tropical storm force windfield. Shortly after peaking, upper-level wind shear from the development of an upper-level low weakened the hurricane on September 20 in the afternoon, as the central pressure had risen 26 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millibar" title="Millibar">mb</a> as Georges approached the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On September 21, after weakening considerably, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_3_hurricane" title="Category 3 hurricane">Category 3</a> Georges made landfall directly on <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a> and three hours later in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Kitts" title="St. Kitts">St. Kitts</a>, though its 175 \xc2\xa0mi (280\xc2\xa0km) wide tropical storm force windfield affected all the Leeward Islands. After weakening to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 2 hurricane</a> over the Caribbean, upper-level shear decreased, and Georges strengthened a bit before making landfall near <a href="/wiki/Fajardo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Fajardo, Puerto Rico">Fajardo, Puerto Rico</a> as a 115\xc2\xa0mph (175\xc2\xa0km/h) Category 3 hurricane early in the morning. Over the mountainous terrain of the island, the hurricane weakened again, but over the <a href="/wiki/Mona_Passage" title="Mona Passage">Mona Passage</a> it again re-intensified to hit eastern <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> with winds of 120\xc2\xa0mph (195\xc2\xa0km/h) on September 22. Like in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, Georges was greatly weakened by the mountainous terrain, and after crossing the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Passage" title="Windward Passage">Windward Passage</a>, it struck 30\xc2\xa0mi (48\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Bay" title="Guant\xc3\xa1namo Bay">Guant\xc3\xa1namo Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> on September 23. Well-defined upper-level outflow allowed the hurricane to remain well organized, and while paralleling the northern coastline of the island Georges retained minimal hurricane status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Hurricane Georges reached the <a href="/wiki/Straits_of_Florida" title="Straits of Florida">Straits of Florida</a> on September 24, and as it had done earlier in its lifetime, quickly restrengthened to Category\xc2\xa02 status on the September 25 due to warm water temperatures and little upper-level shear. It continued to the west-northwest, and struck <a href="/wiki/Key_West" title="Key West">Key West</a> later on September 25 with winds of 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h). Despite moving over warmer water, Georges only managed to peak at 110\xc2\xa0mph (175\xc2\xa0km/h) in the Gulf of Mexico, likely due to its disrupted inner core. A mid-tropospheric anticyclone pushed the hurricane slowly north-northwestward, forcing Georges to make its seventh and final landfall near <a href="/wiki/Biloxi,_Mississippi" title="Biloxi, Mississippi">Biloxi, Mississippi</a> on September 28. Within 24\xc2\xa0hours, Georges had weakened to a tropical depression, and due to weak steering currents the storm looped over southern <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, then drifted to the east. The weak circulation moved eastward over the interior of the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">Florida Panhandle</a>, and dissipated on October 1 near the <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>/<a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> border.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saba" title="Saba">Saba</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a>; it was extended to include the <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">British</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">Virgin Islands</a> on the following day. Later on September\xc2\xa019, a hurricane warning was put into effect for islands between <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a> and Anguilla, as well as <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">Saint Martin</a>, but excluding <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint Barth\xc3\xa9lemy">Saint Barth\xc3\xa9lemy</a>. Early on the following day, a tropical storm warning was issued for Saint Lucia and <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>. About six hours later, the hurricane warning issued on September\xc2\xa019 was extended on September\xc2\xa020 to include islands north and west of Dominica until Puerto Rico. Simultaneously, the tropical storm warning in effect for Saint Lucia and Martinique was discontinued. By later on September\xc2\xa021, the hurricane warning was canceled for all islands east of the Virgin Islands including <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbuda</a>, St. Barthelemy, St. Martin. At 0300\xc2\xa0UTC on the following day, the hurricane warning was extended to include the British and United States Virgin Islands, though six hours later, it was also discontinued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Several hundred people on the island of <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">Montserrat</a> went into twelve hurricane shelters as Georges passed by with winds of 110\xc2\xa0mph (175\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Montserrat_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Montserrat-2">[2]</a></sup> On September\xc2\xa018, the National Disaster Preparedness Committee in Dominica began meetings to prepare for possible impacts from Georges. Residents began stocking up on supplies by this time. For the following two days, the island was placed under a state of high-alert as direct impact from a Category 4 hurricane was anticipated. By the following morning, most businesses had boarded up their windows and roads were quiet. Officials declared that schools would be closed on September\xc2\xa021 and shelters across the island were opened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dominica_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dominica-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greater_Antilles">Greater Antilles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a 21:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa021 with a hurricane watch for Puerto Rico, the NHC and national governments in the Greater Antilles and the Bahamas issued a number of tropical cyclone watches and warnings in anticipation of the hurricane. As the storm progressed across the islands, the warnings were gradually canceled, until the Government of Cuba discontinued a hurricane watch at 03:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa026.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the days prior to the hurricane\'s arrival, thousands of citizens in the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> prepared for the major hurricane by boarding windows and purchasing supplies. Puerto Rican governor <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rossell%C3%B3" title="Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3">Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3</a> activated the island\'s National Guard, opened 416\xc2\xa0shelters, and enacted a temporary prohibition on alcohol sales. More than 28,000\xc2\xa0people across the island evacuated their homes to the shelters in the northern portion of the island. Both the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">Federal Emergency Management Agency</a> (FEMA) and the <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> deployed workers there with supplies for a potentially deadly event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puertoprep_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-puertoprep-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puertorico_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-puertorico-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to initial forecasts of the hurricane brushing the northern portion of the country, the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> was caught off guard. Instead, like in Puerto Rico, Georges traversed the entire country, and passed close to <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>. Neighboring <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> expected the worst, opening shelters and evacuating vulnerable people from low-lying coastal areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hisprep_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-hisprep-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Prior to making landfall, more than 200,000\xc2\xa0people were evacuated from coastal areas in eastern <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. In the potentially impacted area, Cuba\'s revolutionary army was sent to farm lands to harvest crops that could be destroyed during the storm. Members of the Cuban government travelled door-to-door to alert everyone of the hurricane. In addition to this, President <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">Fidel Castro</a> spoke live on national television to explain the country\'s plans to withstand the hurricane, as well as ensuring a quick recovery effort by using all of the nation\'s resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cubaprep_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-cubaprep-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span evacuate, including much of the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Florida Keys</a>. Despite the mandatory evacuation order, 20,000\xc2\xa0people, including over 7,000\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Key_West" title="Key West">Key West</a> citizens, refused to leave. Some of those who remained to ride out the storm were shrimpers, whose boats were their entire livelihood. Insurance companies refused to insure some of the older shrimp boats, leading shrimpers to ride it out with all they had left.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-keys_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-keys-8">[8]</a></sup> Due to lack of law enforcement, those who stayed in Key West went through red lights, double-parked, and disobeyed traffic laws. Long-time Florida Keys citizens noted the solitude of the time and enjoyed the island for how it once was, rather than the large crowds of tourists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-keyss_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-keyss-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the northern <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>, Georges was forecast to attain major hurricane status and make landfall in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>. Because of this, portions of the state were evacuated, including <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>. There, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louisiana_Superdome" title="Louisiana Superdome">Louisiana Superdome</a> was, for the first time in its history, used as a refuge of last resort for those unable to evacuate New Orleans. More than 14,000\xc2\xa0citizens rode out the storm in the facility, causing difficulties to supply necessities. The building had no problems related to the weather, though evacuees looted the building, stole furniture, and damaged property. However, the damage was much less than in the aftermath of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a> in 2005. Many citizens in southern <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a> were told to leave due to a mandatory or recommended evacuation. Of those in the evacuation area, 60% actually left. Most of those who stayed remained because they believed their house was safe enough for the storm. Of those who left, most went to a relative\'s house in their own county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-storn1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-storn1-10">[10]</a></sup> Prior to making landfall, Georges\'s track was very uncertain. This forced for the mandatory evacuations of <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>\'s two coastal counties, <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_County,_Alabama" title="Baldwin County, Alabama">Baldwin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mobile_County,_Alabama" title="Mobile County, Alabama">Mobile</a> Counties, with a combined population of over 500,000\xc2\xa0people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carib_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-carib-11">[11]</a></sup> Despite the order, only 67% of the area actually left to a safer place. Most of those who remained stayed because they believed their house would be able to withstand the hurricane. The majority of those who did leave went to a relative\'s house in a safer portion of the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-storn1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-storn1-10">[10]</a></sup> In the days before making landfall, only 22% of the population in recommended evacuation areas along the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">Florida Panhandle</a> actually left. However, most of them were prepared to leave if the situation became worse. Those who did leave were concerned about the severity of the storm, while those who stayed felt their home was safe enough for the hurricane\'s effects. Floridians who evacuated typically left for a friend or relative\'s house, and only went to another area of their county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-storn1_10-2"><a mudslides along much of its path through the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a>. In all, the hurricane caused $9.72\xc2\xa0billion in damage and 604\xc2\xa0fatalities. In the two months after Georges\'s final landfall, the <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> spent $104\xc2\xa0million on relief aid through <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">United States Virgin Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, making Georges the costliest disaster aid in the program\'s 125-year history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leeward_Islands">Leeward Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Leeward Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Hurricane_Georges_in_the_Lesser_Antilles" title="Effects of Hurricane Georges in the Lesser Antilles">Effects of Hurricane Georges in the Lesser Antilles</a></div>\n<p>Upon moving through the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a>, Georges brought strong winds and heavy rainfall, amounting to a maximum of 7.5\xc2\xa0inches (190\xc2\xa0mm) at <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Antigua_and_Barbuda" title="St. John\'s, Antigua and Barbuda">St. John</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Antigua">Antigua</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Antigua">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>, strong winds caused severe property damage, mostly caused to roofs. 10\xe2\x80\x9320% of houses were greatly impacted, including three schools. High winds during the passage of the hurricane downed telephone and power lines, causing loss of communication and power across much of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lees_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-lees-18">[18]</a></sup> An unofficial wind observation from Antigua reported winds at 94\xc2\xa0mph with gusts to 116\xc2\xa0mph.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Between <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbuda</a> and Antigua, Georges killed 3 people, left 3,800 homeless and resulting at $160 million damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-overall_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-overall-20">[20]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Guadeloupe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The weakening hurricane spared the island as it passed 25 miles (40\xc2\xa0km) to the north, causing moderate damage (houses and roofs, uprooted trees, power lines and outages, beach erosion) especially in <a href="/wiki/Grande-Terre" title="Grande-Terre">Grande-Terre</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Basse-Terre" title="Basse-Terre">Basse-Terre</a>, minor to moderate damage was common; the worst damage was to the banana crops, 85% to 100% devastated, with a cost of 100 millions of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francs" title="Francs">francs</a> (22 millions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USD" title="USD">USD</a>). The maximal rainfall was 5 to 6\xc2\xa0inches in this area.</p>\n<p>The Met office in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Desirade" title="Desirade">Desirade</a>, east of <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a> had a 75 miles per hour (121\xc2\xa0km/h) wind and a 88 miles per hour (142\xc2\xa0km/h) sustained gust. In Raizet, they experienced a 42 miles per hour (68\xc2\xa0km/h) wind and a maximal gust 66 miles per hour (106\xc2\xa0km/h). The minimal pressure fell to 1000 mb (29.54 IHg) for several hours.</p>\n<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%A9t%C3%A9o_France" title="M\xc3\xa9t\xc3\xa9o France">M\xc3\xa9t\xc3\xa9o France</a> forecast 12\xe2\x80\x9324 hours before a 65\xc2\xa0mph winds with gust near 100\xc2\xa0mph and a minimal pressure around 980 mb, meaning the worst has been avoid.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="St._Kitts_and_Nevis">St. Kitts and Nevis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: St. Kitts and Nevis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After passing through Antigua, Georges produced strong winds of up to 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) while passing over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Kitts" title="St. Kitts">St. Kitts</a>, Georges caused catastrophic damage downing power lines, telephone lines, and trees across the island. Lack of electricity resulted in damage to water facilities, as well. Georges\'s high winds caused extensive property damage, damaging 80\xe2\x80\x9385% of the houses on the island, and destroying 20\xe2\x80\x9325% of homes. Many schools, businesses, hospitals, and government buildings lost their roofs, while the airport experienced severe damage to its main terminal and control tower, limiting flights to the daytime. St. Kitts\' economy was disrupted from severe agricultural losses, including the devastation of 50% of their sugar crop. In addition, damaged hotels and piers created a long-term impact through lack of tourism\xe2\x80\x94an industry the island relies on. In all, Hurricane Georges caused 5\xc2\xa0fatalities, left 3,000\xc2\xa0homeless, and resulted in $458\xc2\xa0million (1998\xc2\xa0USD) in damage on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kittnevis_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-kittnevis-21">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the other part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Kitts_and_Nevis" title="St. Kitts and Nevis">the country</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevis" title="Nevis">Nevis</a> fared better. Like on St. Kitts, high winds downed power and telephone lines, damaging the water system there. 35% of homes on the island were damaged, though none were destroyed. Rainfall and debris killed several hundred livestock and seriously damaged coconut trees, amounting to $2.5\xc2\xa0million (1998\xc2\xa0USD) in agricultural damage. There were no casualties reported on the island, and damaged amounted to $39\xc2\xa0million (1998\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kittnevis_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-kittnevis-21">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Damage in St. Kitts totaled EC$1.2\xc2\xa0billion (US$458 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OCHA_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-OCHA-22">[22]</a></sup> Total damage on Nevis amounted to $39\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Damages_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Damages-23">[23]</a></sup> The total damage from the storm was nearly twice the country\'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gross_Domestic_Product" title="Gross Domestic Product">Gross Domestic Product</a> of US$271\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GovernmentLetter_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-GovernmentLetter-24">[24]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Virgin_Islands">British Virgin Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: British Virgin Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>No major damage was reported to public buildings in the <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">British Virgin Islands</a>. Some of the islands\' homes had roofs blown off. The environment suffered major damage. There were many reports of eroded soil in areas where construction was in progress. Some of the soils were planted on roads in <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove farms</a> and in the sea, which could have potentially killed sea life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Impact_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Impact-12">[12]</a></sup> National Parks around the islands suffered minor damage except for Queen Elizabeth Park, which had many fallen trees. None of the schools in the area suffered any damage and opened again four days after Georges had passed. There were no fatalities in the islands and one minor injury was reported. There was no major damage to the islands\' medical buildings. Pipe damage was found in two areas, but there was no damage to the sewage systems. The total damage in the British Virgin Islands was valued at US$9.404 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Impact_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-Impact-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._Virgin_Islands">U.S. Virgin Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: U.S. Virgin Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_9272_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_09-24-1998_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="188" data-file-width="288" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/FEMA_-_9272_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_09-24-1998_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg/220px-FEMA_-_9272_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_09-24-1998_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fd/FEMA_-_9272_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_09-24-1998_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_9272_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_09-24-1998_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDirector of the Virgin Islands Water and Power Authority showing the islands\' improved power grid structures</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>As Georges moved through the northern end of the Lesser Antilles, it produced significant rainfall and strong winds over the United States Virgin Islands. Maximum rainfalls reached 6.79 inches (172\xc2\xa0mm) at the airport in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Croix" title="St. Croix">St. Croix</a> and 5.26 inches (134\xc2\xa0mm) at St. Thomas Airport. The strongest sustained winds and gusts were recorded on St. Croix as well, measuring 74\xc2\xa0mph (119\xc2\xa0km/h) and 91\xc2\xa0mph (146\xc2\xa0km/h) respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDCUSVI1_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-NCDCUSVI1-25">[25]</a></sup> A total of 20 homes were destroyed and 50 others sustained damage. Most of the losses were confined to agriculture and livestock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAA_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA-26">[26]</a></sup> Fifty-five boats were sunk across the islands. Several power lines were downed throughout St. Croix by high winds, leaving some residences without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDCUSVI1_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-NCDCUSVI1-25">[25]</a></sup> However, compared to the intensity of Georges during its passage of the islands, relatively few people, 15% of the island\'s customers, lost power. This was due to the improved power grid set up across the island for this type of event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Total losses on the island were estimated at $2\xc2\xa0million (1998 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In other nearby islands, Georges impact was relatively minor to moderate. Power outages, flooding, and minor to moderate structural damage was common.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carib_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-carib-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Puerto_Rico">Puerto Rico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Puerto Rico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Hurricane_Georges_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Effects of Hurricane Georges in Puerto Rico">Effects of Hurricane Georges in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Damaged_Earthflow_from_Georges.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="177" data-file-width="295" height="132" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Damaged_Earthflow_from_Georges.JPG/220px-Damaged_Earthflow_from_Georges.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b1/Damaged_Earthflow_from_Georges.JPG 1.5x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Damaged_Earthflow_from_Georges.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDamage from mudslides</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Georges was the first hurricane to cross the entire island of Puerto Rico since the <a href="/wiki/1932_San_Ciprian_hurricane" title="1932 San Ciprian hurricane">San Ciprian hurricane</a> in 1932.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puertovirgin_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-puertovirgin-29">[29]</a></sup> The hurricane brought storm surge peaking at an estimated 10-ft (3\xc2\xa0m) in height in <a href="/wiki/Fajardo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Fajardo, Puerto Rico">Fajardo</a>, along with tides up to 20\xc2\xa0ft (6\xc2\xa0m) above normal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wave_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-wave-30">[30]</a></sup> The hurricane spawned two <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F2</a> tornadoes on the island, though they caused little damage. Georges dropped immense precipitation in the mountain regions, peaking at 30.51\xc2\xa0in (775\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico" title="Jayuya, Puerto Rico">Jayuya</a> with many other locations reporting at least 1\xc2\xa0ft (300\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-31">[31]</a></sup> Hurricane-force winds were observed throughout the island. Sustained winds and gusts reached 87\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h) and 107\xc2\xa0mph (172\xc2\xa0km/h), respectively, at the <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_Roads_Naval_Station" title="Roosevelt Roads Naval Station">Roosevelt Roads Naval Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ceiba,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ceiba, Puerto Rico">Ceiba</a>. Unofficially, a sustained wind speed of 102\xc2\xa0mph (164\xc2\xa0km/h) and a gust of 164\xc2\xa0mph (264\xc2\xa0km/h) were observed in <a href="/wiki/Isabela,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isabela, Puerto Rico">Isabela</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The mountain flooding drained off in the island\'s rivers, causing every river to overflow its banks. Near the coast, the surfeit of water carved new channels from the record discharge rate. The storm\'s strong winds caused <a href="/wiki/Coastal_erosion" title="Coastal erosion">beach erosion</a> in many places along the coastline. Eroded beaches, flooding, and debris left many roads impassable or destroyed, isolating some areas on the western portion of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puertovirgin_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-puertovirgin-29">[29]</a></sup> Georges\'s deluge of rainfall caused significant damage to the agricultural industry, including the loss of 75% of its coffee crop, 95% of its banana or plantain crop, and 65% of its live poultry.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Georges_PR_Damage.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="150" data-file-width="225" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Georges_PR_Damage.jpg/220px-Georges_PR_Damage.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4d/Georges_PR_Damage.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Georges_PR_Damage.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nPuerto Rico damage</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Its large circulation brought fierce winds to the entire island, damaging 72,605\xc2\xa0houses and destroying 28,005\xc2\xa0others. This left tens of thousands homeless after the storm\'s passage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Over 22,000\xc2\xa0people were sheltered in 139\xc2\xa0shelters in cities throughout the island. All shelters experienced power outages, and after the storm passed through, lack of water and sewer systems became a serious problem.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-storn1_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-storn1-10">[10]</a></sup> High winds downed nearly half of the island\'s electric and telephone lines, leaving 96% of the population powerless and 8.4% of telephone customers without service. Lack of electricity greatly damaged the water system, resulting in the loss of water and sewer service for 75% of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puertovirgin_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-puertovirgin-29">[29]</a></sup> In the nearby small island of <a href="/wiki/Culebra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Culebra, Puerto Rico">Culebra</a>, Georges destroyed 74\xc2\xa0houses and damaged 89\xc2\xa0others, although damage estimates are not available there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puertovirgin_29-3"><a href="#cite_note-puertovirgin-29">[29]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In all, Georges caused $3.6\xc2\xa0billion in damage and there were no direct casualties due to well-executed warnings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> However, Georges indirectly caused eight deaths in Puerto Rico. Two deaths occurred due to <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide_poisoning" title="Carbon monoxide poisoning">carbon monoxide poisoning</a> from operating a gasoline-powered generator indoors, while two other people were hospitalized for the same issue. Four other fatalities occurred after a lit candle started a house fire. Additionally, there was one death each from head trauma and electrocution in the aftermath of the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-puerto_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-puerto-32">[32]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Hispaniola">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Though there are no recorded amounts, satellite-derived rainfall estimates show up to 39\xc2\xa0inches (990\xc2\xa0mm) of rain falling in the mountainous terrain of the countries. This heavy rainfall resulted in mudslides and flooding, killing a total of 589\xc2\xa0people across the island and leaving more than 350.000 homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Dominican_Republic">Dominican Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Dominican Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Hurricane_Georges_in_the_Dominican_Republic" title="Effects of Hurricane Georges in the Dominican Republic">Effects of Hurricane Georges in the Dominican Republic</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GeorgesDR1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="268" data-file-width="437" height="135" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/GeorgesDR1.jpg/220px-GeorgesDR1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/GeorgesDR1.jpg/330px-GeorgesDR1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f2/GeorgesDR1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GeorgesDR1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nToppled trees blocked hundreds of streets in Santo Domingo</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, Georges brought strong winds and very heavy rains, along with a 7-foot (2\xc2\xa0m) storm surge. Nearly 10 \xc2\xa0hours of continuous rainfall resulted in mudslides and overflown rivers across the mountainous country, damaging many cities along the southern coastline, including the capital. 120\xc2\xa0mph (193\xc2\xa0km/h) winds downed and uprooted trees across much of the country, littering streets with debris and mud. Thousands of houses were destroyed, while many were completely destroyed from the flooding and winds. The entire country was without electricity during the aftermath of the storm, damaging water and communication systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-domrep_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-domrep-33">[33]</a></sup> Heavy wind damage and flooding caused extensive damage to the airport in <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>, restricting usage to military and non-commercial flights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-car_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-car-34">[34]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Most impacted by Hurricane Georges was the agricultural industry. The areas hardest hit by the hurricane coincided with the country\'s main crop-growing areas, including the provinces around Santo Domingo. After a severe drought in 1997, extreme rainfall damaged around 470,000 acres (190,202\xc2\xa0ha) of food crops, including various types of vegetables, fruits, and roots\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x94 some of the country\'s main diet food. Substantial amounts of tobacco and sugar plantations, the country\'s most important export crop, were severely damaged. The extreme flooding caused great losses in the poultry industry, an important economy in the area. The Dominican Republic had to import significant amounts of rice and other crops to compensate for the losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crop_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-crop-35">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Death toll reports were slow in the wake of the storm, but a total of 380\xc2\xa0people died from Hurricane Georges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-14"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Damage in the Dominican Republic amounted to $1.2\xc2\xa0billion (1998\xc2\xa0USD, $1.8\xc2\xa0billion 2009\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dpdamage_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-dpdamage-36">[36]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Haiti">Haiti</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Haiti">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Hurricane_Georges_in_Haiti" title="Effects of Hurricane Georges in Haiti">Effects of Hurricane Georges in Haiti</a></div>\n<p>Upon reaching <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>, Georges was a weakened hurricane, but it still brought heavy rainfall across the entire country. The capital city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince,_Haiti" title="Port-au-Prince, Haiti">Port-au-Prince</a> was largely unharmed, with the exception of flooding in low-lying coastal areas, damaging the main commercial port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-car_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-car-34">[34]</a></sup> The rest of the country, however, experienced a significant number of mudslides due to deforestation along the mountains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hait_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-hait-37">[37]</a></sup> These mudslides destroyed or severely damaged many houses, leaving 167,332\xc2\xa0homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-15"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Damage was greatest along the northern coastline from <a href="/wiki/Cap-Ha%C3%AFtien" title="Cap-Ha\xc3\xaftien">Cap-Ha\xc3\xaftien</a> to <a href="/wiki/Gona%C3%AFves" title="Gona\xc3\xafves">Gona\xc3\xafves</a> due to the flooding and mudslides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hispd_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-hispd-38">[38]</a></sup> On the southern coast, the head of a U.S.-based medical team, stranded for several days by flooding in the remote town of Belle Anse, anticipated a rise in malnutrition, disease, homelessness and poverty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haiti_docs_stranded_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Haiti_docs_stranded-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lluberas_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lluberas-40">[40]</a></sup> Lack of electricity led to a total disruption of Haiti\'s water supply system, causing a decrease in sanitary conditions across the poorest country in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haiti_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-haiti-41">[41]</a></sup> In all, 209\xc2\xa0people died in <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-16"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Like in the Dominican Republic, the agricultural sector suffered extreme damage. After a severe drought in 1997, Georges\'s severe flooding stopped any chances of recovering quickly. Most of the country\'s significant crop land, including <a href="/wiki/Artibonite_Valley" title="Artibonite Valley">Artibonite Valley</a>, suffered total losses. Up to 80% of banana plantations were lost, while vegetable, roots, tubers, and other food crops were ruined. In addition, thousands of small farm animals were either killed or lost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crop_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-crop-35">[35]</a></sup> Total agricultural losses amounted to $179\xc2\xa0million (1998\xc2\xa0USD, $250\xc2\xa0million 2009\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caribd_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-caribd-42">[42]</a></sup> The country requested food assistance in the aftermath of the hurricane to alleviate the serious losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crop_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-crop-35">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Hurricane_Georges_in_Cuba" title="Effects of Hurricane Georges in Cuba">Effects of Hurricane Georges in Cuba</a></div>\n<p>Upon making landfall, Hurricane Georges produced torrential rainfall, amounting to a maximum of 24.41\xc2\xa0inches (620\xc2\xa0mm) at Limonar in the province of <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Province" title="Guant\xc3\xa1namo Province">Guant\xc3\xa1namo</a>. Several other locations reported over a foot (300\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-17"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Storm surge of 4\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93 6\xc2\xa0feet (1\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93 2\xc2\xa0m) was expected along the eastern coastline, along with dangerous waves on top of the surge. Though winds were reduced by the time Georges hit Cuba, it still retained winds of 75\xc2\xa0mph (121\xc2\xa0km/h), along with stronger gusts in squalls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pubadvcuba_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-pubadvcuba-43">[43]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The hurricane\'s heavy rainfall resulted in mudslides along the mountainous terrain. This, combined with strong winds, damaged 60,475\xc2\xa0homes, of which 3,481 were completely destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-18"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> In the country, 100,000 were left homeless due to Hurricane Georges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seasum_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-seasum-44">[44]</a></sup> High winds downed power lines, trees, and telephone poles, leaving many in eastern Cuba without electricity in the aftermath of the storm. Along the coast, severe flooding washed out railroad and highway bridges. Though eastern Cuba was the area most affected, the central and western portion of the island, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Havana,_Cuba" title="Havana, Cuba">Havana</a>, experienced torrential rains and strong wind gusts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cubad_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-cubad-45">[45]</a></sup> There, strong waves broke over the <a href="/wiki/Seawall" title="Seawall">seawall</a>, and caused heavy flood damage to some of the town\'s old buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-havana_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-havana-46">[46]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Like in Puerto Rico and Hispaniola, the severe drought during the <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o">El Ni\xc3\xb1o</a> of 1997 exacerbated the flood\'s disruption to crops in eastern Cuba. The heavy rainfall from Georges damaged the crops greatly, despite the effort to harvest them prior to its arrival. Up to 70% of the plantain crop, a chief food in the country\'s diet, was destroyed. The sugarcane crop fared badly as well, limiting one of the country\'s important export crops. The coffee and cocoa plantations also suffered from the hurricane, further damaging the country\'s food supply.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crop_35-3"><a href="#cite_note-crop-35">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Well-executed evacuations and warnings limited the death toll to six,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-19"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> while damage amounted to $305\xc2\xa0million (1998\xc2\xa0USD, $500\xc2\xa0million 2009\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cubdam_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-cubdam-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bahamas">Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Though Georges was forecast to move through <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">the Bahamas</a>, it passed to the south of the archipelago. It brought 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) winds to <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Andros" title="South Andros">South Andros</a>, as well as precipitation in the storm\'s outer bands. Though damage was minimal, one person died in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-20"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_2">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Georges_tornado_outbreak" title="Hurricane Georges tornado outbreak">Hurricane Georges tornado outbreak</a></div>\n<p>Throughout the mainland areas of the United States, Georges left approximately $2.765\xc2\xa0billion in damage. The hurricane ranked as the country\'s fifth costliest hurricane at the time of its occurrence, but has since fallen to the twentieth costliest. Due to extensive preparations, only one direct death was attributed to the storm. A tornado outbreak produced 47\xc2\xa0tornadoes\xe2\x80\x9320 in <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, 17 in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, and 10 in <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, leaving 36\xc2\xa0injuries and about $9\xc2\xa0million in damage.</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" brought a storm surge of up to 12\xc2\xa0ft (3.6\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/Tavernier,_Florida" title="Tavernier, Florida">Tavernier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-keys_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-keyss-9">[9]</a></sup> Tides reaching about 10\xc2\xa0ft (3\xc2\xa0m) above normal inundated many parts of the <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Highway" title="Overseas Highway">Overseas Highway</a>. Strong winds downed palm trees and power lines, leaving all of the Keys without power. Georges\'s waves overturned two boats in Key West,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-keys_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-keys-8">[8]</a></sup> damaged 1,536\xc2\xa0houses, and destroyed 173\xc2\xa0homes, many of which were mobile homes. Rainfall amounted to a maximum of 8.41\xc2\xa0in (210\xc2\xa0mm) in Tavernier, while other locations reported lesser amounts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-21"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Damage in the Florida Keys amounted to $200\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-after_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-after-47">[47]</a></sup> Strong winds knocked down power lines, leaving 200,000\xc2\xa0without power in the <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-keys_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-keys-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Georges produced a storm surge of up to 10\xc2\xa0ft (3\xc2\xa0m) in the Florida Panhandle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-22"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Some roads were completely washed away in <a href="/wiki/Pensacola_Beach,_Florida" title="Pensacola Beach, Florida">Pensacola Beach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Navarre_Beach,_Florida" title="Navarre Beach, Florida">Navarre Beach</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwsmobile_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-nwsmobile-48">[48]</a></sup> The storm surge and waves flattened about 1,125\xc2\xa0ft (343\xc2\xa0m) of dunes. Between <a href="/wiki/Panama_City_Beach,_Florida" title="Panama City Beach, Florida">Panama City Beach</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perdido_Key" title="Perdido Key">Perdido Key</a>, most of the beach access stairs and walkways suffered damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> As the storm moved slowly through the northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast" title="Gulf Coast">Gulf Coast</a>, it produced torrential rainfall amounting to a maximum of 38.46\xc2\xa0in (976.88\xc2\xa0mm) in Munson, causing extensive inland flooding. href="#cite_note-after-47">[47]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Throughout the state, the storm spawned 17\xc2\xa0tornadoes \xe2\x80\x93 the largest outbreak in the state since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Agnes" 7\xc2\xa0homes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-sd-54">[54]</a></sup> and 12\xc2\xa0cars,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gs_53-1"><a The storm surge caused extensive beach erosion, with nearly 1,200\xc2\xa0ft (370\xc2\xa0m) of sand was lost, including 6\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m) sand dunes, leaving boardwalks formerly situated atop the dunes suspended near the water\'s surface. The storm surge also inundated the <a href="/wiki/Chandeleur_Islands" title="Chandeleur Islands">Chandeleur Islands</a>, the first line of protection for the coasts of Louisiana and <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>. The long island chain was reduced to a few banks of sand in the Gulf of Mexico. Grand Gosier, the home to a flock of <a href="/wiki/Brown_pelican" title="Brown pelican">brown pelicans</a>, experienced severe flooding, destroying their habitats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-after_47-2"><a href="#cite_note-after-47">[47]</a></sup> Located on the weaker side of the storm, rainfall totals in Louisiana were low, with a maximum amount of 2.98\xc2\xa0in (76\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Bogalusa,_Louisiana" title="Bogalusa, Louisiana">Bogalusa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-24"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Sustained winds in the state reached 48\xc2\xa0mph (77\xc2\xa0km/h) and gusts peaked at 68\xc2\xa0mph (109\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-25"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> High winds downed power lines, leaving 160,000\xc2\xa0without electricity across the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-power_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-power-57">[57]</a></sup> At least 50\xc2\xa0homes located outside the levee system were flooded by the storm surge, while 85\xc2\xa0fishing camps on the banks of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Pontchartrain" title="Lake Pontchartrain">Lake Pontchartrain</a> were destroyed. Overall, impact from the hurricane was fairly minor, with damages estimated at approximately $30.1\xc2\xa0million, but no direct deaths due to well-executed evacuations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-26"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> However, there were three indirect fatalities in the state. Two men collapsed and died due to medical conditions aggravated by the stress of the evacuation; the other death occurred as the result of a house fire ignited by an emergency candle that was tipped over.</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mississippi">Mississippi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Mississippi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Hurricane_Georges_in_Mississippi" title="Effects of Hurricane Georges in Mississippi">Effects of Hurricane Georges in Mississippi</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TchoutacabouffaRiver1998.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="592" data-file-width="875" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/TchoutacabouffaRiver1998.jpg/220px-TchoutacabouffaRiver1998.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/TchoutacabouffaRiver1998.jpg/330px-TchoutacabouffaRiver1998.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/TchoutacabouffaRiver1998.jpg/440px-TchoutacabouffaRiver1998.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TchoutacabouffaRiver1998.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nFlooding at the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_Highway_67" title="Mississippi Highway 67">Old Hwy 67</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_Highway_15" title="Mississippi Highway 15">MS 15</a> near D\'Iberville after the <a href="/wiki/Tchoutacabouffa_River" title="Tchoutacabouffa River">Tchoutacabouffa River</a> overflowed</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Upon making landfall, Hurricane Georges brought a storm surge of up to 8.9\xc2\xa0ft (2.7\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/Biloxi,_Mississippi" title="Biloxi, Mississippi">Biloxi</a>. Beach erosion occurred along the coastline, resulting in some property damage on beach houses. However, around Biloxi, coastal casinos and the shipyards experienced little damage from the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miss_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-miss-58">[58]</a></sup> While stalling over the southern portion of the state, it produced torrential rainfall, amounting to 16.7\xc2\xa0in (420\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Pascagoula,_Mississippi" title="Pascagoula, Mississippi">Pascagoula</a>. The heavy rainfall contributed to significant river overflowing, including the <a href="/wiki/Tchoutacabouffa_River" title="Tchoutacabouffa River">Tchoutacabouffa River</a> at <a href="/wiki/D%27Iberville,_Mississippi" title="D\'Iberville, Mississippi">D\'Iberville</a>, which set a record crest of 19\xc2\xa0ft (5.7\xc2\xa0m). The overflown rivers in the southern portion of the state flooded homes and forced more to evacuate just days after the hurricane came through.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-27"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Along the coast of Mississippi, more than 1,000 homes were flooded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> One of the worst impacted areas inland was <a href="/wiki/Stone_County,_Mississippi" title="Stone County, Mississippi">Stone County</a>, where 54\xc2\xa0homes had minor damage, 26\xc2\xa0suffered major damage, and 5\xc2\xa0were destroyed. In addition, squall lines spawned multiple tornadoes, damaging evacuation shelters in Pascagoula and Gautier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-power_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-power-57">[57]</a></sup></p>\n<p>One location observed hurricane-force winds, with a sustained wind speed of 75\xc2\xa0mph (121\xc2\xa0km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Keesler_Air_Force_Base" title="Keesler Air Force Base">Keesler Air Force Base</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Harrison_County,_Mississippi" title="Harrison County, Mississippi">Harrison County</a>, winds downed power lines, signs, and traffic light, while damaging some roofs. Impact was similar but more severe in <a href="/wiki/Jackson_County,_Mississippi" title="Jackson County, Mississippi">Jackson County</a>, with more considerable damage to the roofs of some buildings, while a few mobile homes were damaged or overturned. Throughout the state, winds left approximately 230,000\xc2\xa0people without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carib_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-carib-11">[11]</a></sup> After the storm, over 6,800\xc2\xa0people stayed in 49\xc2\xa0different shelters. One shelter in <a href="/wiki/Forrest_County,_Mississippi" title="Forrest County, Mississippi">Forrest County</a> was damaged, forcing the citizens to another camp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-storn1_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-storn1-10">[10]</a></sup> Overall, Hurricane Georges caused about $676.8 million in damage, though no deaths were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-28"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Alabama">Alabama</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Alabama">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_760_-_Photograph_by_John_Pilger_taken_on_10-08-1998_in_Alabama.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="224" data-file-width="300" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/FEMA_-_760_-_Photograph_by_John_Pilger_taken_on_10-08-1998_in_Alabama.jpg/220px-FEMA_-_760_-_Photograph_by_John_Pilger_taken_on_10-08-1998_in_Alabama.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d6/FEMA_-_760_-_Photograph_by_John_Pilger_taken_on_10-08-1998_in_Alabama.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_760_-_Photograph_by_John_Pilger_taken_on_10-08-1998_in_Alabama.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nA home destroyed by storm surge</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Upon making landfall, storm surge heights along the coast of Alabama were estimated to have ranged from 7 to 12\xc2\xa0ft (2.1 to 3.7\xc2\xa0m), with a peak of 11.9\xc2\xa0ft (3.6\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Morgan,_Alabama" title="Fort Morgan, Alabama">Fort Morgan</a>, along with 25\xc2\xa0ft (7.6\xc2\xa0m) waves on top of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-29"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carib1_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-carib1-60">[60]</a></sup> Along the coastline, heavy rainfall and strong waves caused extensive property damage. In <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Shores,_Alabama" title="Gulf Shores, Alabama">Gulf Shores</a>, 251\xc2\xa0houses, 16\xc2\xa0apartment buildings, and 70\xc2\xa0businesses experienced significant damage. On <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dauphin_Island" title="Dauphin Island">Dauphin Island</a>, the hurricane damaged 80\xc2\xa0houses and left around 40\xc2\xa0uninhabitable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-after_47-3"><a href="#cite_note-after-47">[47]</a></sup> Many waterfront homes on the western side of the island were pushed into other homes or strewn into pieces across the sand. Some roads were completely washed away. In <a href="/wiki/Orange_Beach,_Alabama" title="Orange Beach, Alabama">Orange Beach</a>, two condominiums were heavily damaged, one by fire and the other by high waves. Extensive storm surge flooding also occurred along <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Bay" title="Mobile Bay">Mobile Bay</a>. With storm surge of 8.0\xc2\xa0ft (2.4\xc2\xa0m) at Mobile Bay Causeway, a number of businesses near the bridge were damaged.</p>\n<p>Sustained winds in Alabama reached 54\xc2\xa0mph (87\xc2\xa0km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Aeroplex_at_Brookley" title="Mobile Aeroplex at Brookley">Mobile Aeroplex at Brookley</a>, while gusts peaked at 64\xc2\xa0mph (103\xc2\xa0km/h) at an agricultural station in <a href="/wiki/Fairhope,_Alabama" title="Fairhope, Alabama">Fairhope</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-30"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> High winds downed power lines and trees, leaving 177,000\xc2\xa0people without power after the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carib1_60-1"><a href="#cite_note-carib1-60">[60]</a></sup> A total of 17\xc2\xa0shelters housed 4,977\xc2\xa0people in the aftermath of the storm. Wind damage to the buildings was minimal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-storn1_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-storn1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> The storm spawned 20\xc2\xa0tornadoes throughout Alabama. The most devastating tornado touched-down in <a href="/wiki/Enterprise,_Alabama" title="Enterprise, Alabama">Enterprise</a>, causing severe damage to Camp Wiregrass and a few homes, while many residences in the city were left without electricity after numerous power lines were downed. Damages from the tornado amounted to roughly $1.5\xc2\xa0million.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Georges_1998_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="910" data-file-width="1143" height="175" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Georges_1998_rainfall.gif/220px-Georges_1998_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Georges_1998_rainfall.gif/330px-Georges_1998_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Georges_1998_rainfall.gif/440px-Georges_1998_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Georges_1998_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTotal rainfall in the United States from Georges</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>While moving slowly through the state, it dropped torrential rainfall, peaking at 29.66\xc2\xa0in (75\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Bay_Minette,_Alabama" title="Bay Minette, Alabama">Bay Minette</a>. Outer squalls spawned tornadoes in the southeast portion of the state, though damage from them was minimal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-31"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Several rivers in southern Alabama crested at record or near record heights at some locations, including the <a href="/wiki/Conecuh_River" title="Conecuh River">Conecuh River</a> at <a href="/wiki/Brewton,_Alabama" title="Brewton, Alabama">Brewton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fish_River_(Alabama)" title="Fish River (Alabama)">Fish River</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perdido_River" title="Perdido River">Perdido River</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Styx_River_(Alabama)" title="Styx River (Alabama)">Styx River</a>. Many homes situated near the banks of these rivers were destroyed or suffered extensive damage. Flooding the shutdown many bridges, highways, and secondary roads in <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_County,_Alabama" title="Baldwin County, Alabama">Baldwin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Escambia_County,_Alabama" title="Escambia County, Alabama">Escambia</a> counties, including <a href="/wiki/Interstate_10" title="Interstate 10">Interstate 10</a> near the Alabama\xe2\x80\x93Florida state line; <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_29" title="U.S. Route 29">U.S. Route 29</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alabama_State_Route_113" title="Alabama State Route 113">Alabama State Route 113</a> in <a href="/wiki/Flomaton,_Alabama" title="Flomaton, Alabama">Flomaton</a>; and US 31 in Brewton and Flomaton. Overall, damage in Alabama amounted to $125\xc2\xa0million. Freshwater flooding in Mobile resulted in one death, the only direct death in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-32"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Georgia">Georgia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Georges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Georgia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>In Georgia, the remnants of Georges dropped 4\xc2\xa0in (100\xc2\xa0mm) of rain across <a href="/wiki/Franklin_County,_Georgia" title="Franklin County, Georgia">Franklin County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Appling_County,_Georgia" title="Appling County, Georgia">Appling County</a>, however, rainfall of 5\xe2\x80\x937\xc2\xa0in (130\xe2\x80\x93180\xc2\xa0mm) closed several roads and left $10,000 in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Atkinson_County,_Georgia" title="Atkinson County, Georgia">Atkinson County</a> also reported flood damage of $15,000 in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Stewart County, received over 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm) of rain which caused extensive flooding that left several roads impassable. Damage from the storm totaled to $33,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> In the town of Lumpkin, a <a href="/wiki/Funnel_cloud" title="Funnel cloud">funnel cloud</a> was reported but there were no damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> In addition to the flooding, the remnants of Georges spawned numerous tornadoes across the state of Georgia. In Randolph County, an F1 tornado uprooted several trees and injured one person. Damage from the tornado totaled $500,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span
