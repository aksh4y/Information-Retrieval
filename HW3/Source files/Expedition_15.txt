NASA<br/>\nFirst spaceflight</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crew_Notes">Crew Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_15&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Crew Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Flight Engineer <a href="/wiki/Sunita_Williams" title="Sunita Williams">Sunita Williams</a> was the first Expedition 15 crew member to arrive. She participated in <a href="/wiki/Expedition_14" title="Expedition 14">Expedition 14</a>, until Expedition 15 Commander <a href="/wiki/Fyodor_Yurchikhin" title="Fyodor Yurchikhin">Fyodor Yurchikhin</a> assumed command of the station. Williams arrived at the station on 11 December 2006, aboard the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Discovery" title="Space Shuttle Discovery"><i>Discovery</i></a> flight <a href="/wiki/STS-116" title="STS-116">STS-116</a>. Yurchikhin and Flight Engineer <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Kotov" title="Oleg Kotov">Oleg Kotov</a> arrived the station on 9 April 2007 aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-10" title="Soyuz TMA-10">Soyuz TMA-10</a>.</p>\n<p>On 26 April 2007, NASA announced that Williams would return to Earth on <a href="/wiki/STS-117" title="STS-117">STS-117</a>, flown by Space Shuttle <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Atlantis" title="Space Shuttle Atlantis"><i>Atlantis</i></a>, instead of <a href="/wiki/STS-118" title="STS-118">STS-118</a> as originally planned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Williams was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Clayton_Anderson" title="Clayton Anderson">Clayton Anderson</a>, who arrived at the station aboard <i>Atlantis</i>, which docked on 10 June 2007.</p>\n<p>Expedition 15 ended officially after <a href="/wiki/Expedition_16" title="Expedition 16">Expedition 16</a> Commander <a href="/wiki/Peggy_Whitson" title="Peggy Whitson">Peggy Whitson</a> arrived at the station aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-11" title="Soyuz TMA-11">Soyuz TMA-11</a>, and the official change of command ceremony took place on 19 October 2007.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Backup_crew">Backup crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a 7 April 2007 17:31 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a></li>\n<li><b>Docking</b>: 9 April 2007 07:10 UTC</li>\n<li><b>Undocking</b>: 21 October 2007 07:14 UTC</li>\n<li><b>Landing</b>: 21 October 2007 10:46 UTC</li>\n<li><b>LandingSite</b>: Ballistic Trajectory Landing Site northwest of <a href="/wiki/Arkalyk" title="Arkalyk">Arkalyk</a></li>\n</ul>\n<p>On 21 October 2007, after the separation of the Soyuz TMA-10 capsule, Moscow Mission Control reported that the Soyuz had entered into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheric_reentry" title="Atmospheric reentry">ballistic trajectory</a>, which resulted in a landing that was 340 kilometres (210\xc2\xa0mi) short of the intended <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> landing site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ballistic_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ballistic-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-landing_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-landing-4">[4]</a></sup> Landing occurred without incident, and by 10:55 UTC, all crew members were out of the capsule, and the vehicle was secured. Until then, the only other time a Soyuz landing had resulted in a ballistic trajectory was the landing of <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-1" title="Soyuz TMA-1">Soyuz TMA-1</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Expedition_6" title="Expedition 6">Expedition 6</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ballistic_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ballistic-3">[3]</a></sup> Another ballistic trajectory occurred
