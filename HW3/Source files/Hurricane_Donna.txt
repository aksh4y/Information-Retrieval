title="1960 Atlantic hurricane season">1960 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Donna</b> was the strongest hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1960_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1960 Atlantic hurricane season">1960 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, and caused severe damage to the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, in August\xe2\x80\x93September. The fifth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>, third hurricane, and first major hurricane of the season, Donna developed south of Cape Verde on August\xc2\xa029, spawned by a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> to which 63\xc2\xa0deaths from a plane crash in <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> were attributed. The depression strengthened into Tropical Storm Donna by the following day. Donna moved west-northwestward at roughly 20\xc2\xa0mph (32\xc2\xa0km/h) and by September\xc2\xa01, it reached hurricane status. Donna deepened significantly and reached its peak intensity early on September\xc2\xa04, with maximum sustained winds of 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">[1]</a></sup>. Thereafter, it weakened slightly as it brushed the Lesser Antilles later that day. On <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a>, the storm left a quarter of the island\'s population homeless and killed seven people. An additional five deaths were reported in <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>, and there were seven other fatalities throughout the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">Virgin Islands</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, severe flash flooding led to 107\xc2\xa0fatalities, 85\xc2\xa0of them in <a href="/wiki/Humacao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Humacao, Puerto Rico">Humacao</a> alone. Donna further weakened to a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane late on September\xc2\xa05, but eventually became a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane again. While passing through The Bahamas, several small island communities in the central regions of the country were leveled, but no damage total or fatalities were reported.</p>\n<p>Early on September\xc2\xa010, Donna made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Marathon,_Florida" title="Marathon, Florida">Marathon, Florida</a> with winds of 130\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h), hours before another landfall south of <a href="/wiki/Naples,_Florida" title="Naples, Florida">Naples</a> at the same intensity. Florida bore the brunt of Hurricane Donna. In the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Florida Keys</a>, coastal flooding severely damaged 75% of buildings, destroyed several subdivisions in Marathon. On the mainland, 5,200\xc2\xa0houses were damaged, which does not include the 75% of homes damaged at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Myers_Beach,_Florida" title="Fort Myers Beach, Florida">Fort Myers Beach</a>; 50% of buildings were also destroyed in the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Everglades,_Florida" title="Everglades, Florida">Everglades</a>. Crop losses were also extensive. A total of 50% of <a href="/wiki/Grapefruit" title="Grapefruit">grapefruit</a> crop was lost, 10% of the <a href="/wiki/Orange_(fruit)" title="Orange (fruit)">orange</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tangerine" title="Tangerine">tangerine</a> crop was lost, and the <a href="/wiki/Avocado" title="Avocado">avocado</a> crop was almost destroyed. In the state of Florida alone, there were 13\xc2\xa0deaths and $300\xc2\xa0million in losses. Donna weakened over Florida and was a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane when it re-emerged into the Atlantic from <a href="/wiki/North_Florida" title="North Florida">North Florida</a>. By early on September\xc2\xa012, the storm made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Topsail_Beach,_North_Carolina" title="Topsail Beach, North Carolina">Topsail Beach, North Carolina</a> as a strong Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane. Donna brought tornadoes and wind gusts up to 100\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h), damaging or destroying several buildings in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_North_Carolina" title="Eastern North Carolina">Eastern North Carolina</a>, while crops were damaged as far as 50 miles (80\xc2\xa0km) inland. Additionally, storm surge caused significant beach erosion and structural damage at <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">Wilmington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nags_Head,_North_Carolina" title="Nags Head, North Carolina">Nags Head</a>. Eight people were killed and there were over 100\xc2\xa0injuries. Later on September\xc2\xa012, Donna reemerged into the Atlantic Ocean and continued to move northeastward. The storm struck <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> late on September\xc2\xa012 and rapidly weakened inland. On the following day, Donna became <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> over <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div west coast of Africa near <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">Dakar</a>. That day, it is estimated a tropical depression developed along the wave southeast of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>. There was a lack of data for several days, but it is estimated that the system gradually intensified. On September\xc2\xa02, ships in the region suggested there was a tropical storm after reporting winds of over 50\xc2\xa0mph (80\xc2\xa0km/h). That day, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> flew into the system and observed a well-defined <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>, along with winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Based on the data, the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">United States Weather Bureau</a> office in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a> initiated advisories on Hurricane Donna at 2200\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on September\xc2\xa02,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> about 700 miles (1,100\xc2\xa0km) east of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-prelim1-4">[4]</a></sup> It is estimated that the storm attained hurricane status a day prior. The <a href="/wiki/Azores_High" title="Azores High">Azores High</a> to the north was unusually powerful, which caused Donna to move to the west-northwest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> When advisories began, Donna was a major hurricane, which is the equivalent of a Category\xc2\xa03 or higher on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale</a>; it would ultimately maintain this status for nine days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Continuing to the west-northwest, Donna strengthened further, and on September\xc2\xa04, Donna reached its peak intensity, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_winds" title="Maximum sustained winds">maximum sustained winds</a> of 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WU_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">[1]</a></sup> (it was reported at the time as 145\xc2\xa0mph (233\xc2\xa0km/h) by the Weather Bureau<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>). After maintaining peak winds for about 12\xc2\xa0hours, the hurricane weakened slightly as it approached the Lesser Antilles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[5]</a></sup> Late on September\xc2\xa04, the eye of Donna moved over <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbuda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint Barth\xc3\xa9lemy">Saint Barth\xc3\xa9lemy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">Saint Martin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>, and passed just south of <a href="/wiki/Anegada" title="Anegada">Anegada</a>. Despite having weakened, Donna remained well-organized, described in the <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Weather_Review" title="Monthly Weather Review">Monthly Weather Review</a></i> as akin to "an intense, idealized hurricane." A weakening <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> to the north turned the hurricane more northwesterly, bringing it within 85 miles (137\xc2\xa0km) of the north coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. By September\xc2\xa07, Donna had turned more to the west after the ridge built to the north. Over the next few days, the intense hurricane moved slowly through the southern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> without defined steering currents, and the eye passed near or over <a href="/wiki/Mayaguana" title="Mayaguana">Mayaguana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acklins" title="Acklins">Acklins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Long_Cay" title="Long Cay">Fortune Island</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ragged_Island,_Bahamas" title="Ragged Island, Bahamas">Ragged Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>While passing through the <a href="/wiki/Straits_of_Florida" title="Straits of Florida">Straits of Florida</a>, Donna brushed the northern coast of Cuba on September\xc2\xa09 with gale-force winds. Subsequently, a <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">cold front</a> moved eastward through the United States and weakened the ridge, causing the hurricane to turn more to the northwest. It re-intensified over warm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperatures" title="Sea surface temperatures">sea surface temperatures</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> and the hurricane\'s minimum <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">barometric pressure</a> dropped to 932\xc2\xa0mbar (27.5\xc2\xa0inHg) on September\xc2\xa010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[5]</a></sup> Between 0200 and 0300\xc2\xa0UTC that day, the 21-mile-wide (34\xc2\xa0km) eye of Donna crossed through the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Florida Keys</a> just northeast of <a href="/wiki/Marathon,_Florida" title="Marathon, Florida">Marathon</a>, with sustained winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h) and gusts to 178\xc2\xa0mph (286\xc2\xa0km/h). The hurricane continued to the northwest along the southwest coast of Florida, passing over <a href="/wiki/Naples,_Florida" title="Naples, Florida">Naples</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fort_Myers,_Florida" title="Fort Myers, Florida">Fort Myers</a> before turning inland to the northeast. At 0800\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa011, Donna exited <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach,_Florida" title="Daytona Beach, Florida">Daytona Beach</a> into the western Atlantic with winds of about 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h), still as an organized hurricane. Accelerating to the northeast due to an approaching trough, the hurricane re-intensified slightly before making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">Wilmington, North Carolina</a>, early on September\xc2\xa012. At 0900\xc2\xa0UTC that day, Donna again emerged over open waters near Virginia, although it had weakened, and the eye expanded to over 50 miles (80\xc2\xa0km) in diameter. Late on September\xc2\xa012, the hurricane made landfall in <a href="/wiki/Westhampton,_New_York" title="Westhampton, New York">Westhampton, New York</a> and crossed <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, later moving into <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> On September\xc2\xa013, Donna became <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> over northern Maine before entering eastern Canada, having become associated with the approaching cold front. After moving across <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> and <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a>, Donna reached the <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Sea" title="Labrador Sea">Labrador Sea</a> and dissipated early on September\xc2\xa014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>At noon on September 3, a hurricane watch was issued for the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a>, which at 6 p.m. was upgraded to a warning. Also at 6 p.m., hurricane watches were raised for <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">Virgin Islands</a>, which at 6 a.m. on September 4, were upgraded to warnings. By 6 a.m. on September 5, hurricane warnings were dropped for the Leeward Islands, and at 9 a.m., southwest Puerto Rico and the Virgin Island\'s hurricane warnings were downgraded to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gale_warnings" title="Gale warnings">gale warnings</a>. By noon, all remaining hurricane warnings for Puerto Rico were changed to gale warnings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watchwarnings_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-watchwarnings-7">[7]</a></sup> In Puerto Rico, flood warnings were issued on September\xc2\xa05, although some residents in the region did not heed the notice; many returned to their homes after the hurricane passed to the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Vieques,_Puerto_Rico" title="Vieques, Puerto Rico">Vieques Island</a>, about 1,700\xc2\xa0<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Marines" title="United States Marines">United States Marines</a> evacuated to naval ships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap952_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap952-8">[8]</a></sup> Officials advised small boats to remain at port, and thousands of residents evacuated to schools set up as Red Cross shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap95_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap95-9">[9]</a></sup> Along the Cuban coast, about 3,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated inland or to churches and schools;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi99_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-upi99-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ws99_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ws99-11">[11]</a></sup> while in the Bahamas, stores closed and boats were sent to port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi98_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-upi98-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n<div class="PopUpMediaTransform" id="mwe_player_0" style="width:220px;" videopayload=\'&lt;div class="mediaContainer" style="width:640px"&gt;&lt;video id="mwe_player_1" poster="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Donna_1960_Miami_Radar_Animation.ogv/640px--Donna_1960_Miami_Radar_Animation.ogv.jpg" controls="" preload="none" autoplay="" style="width:640px;height:480px" class="kskin" data-durationhint="83.84" data-startoffset="0" data-mwtitle="Donna_1960_Miami_Radar_Animation.ogv" data-mwprovider="wikimediacommons"&gt;&lt;source src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/f/f2/Donna_1960_Miami_Radar_Animation.ogv/Donna_1960_Miami_Radar_Animation.ogv.480p.webm" type="video/webm; The next day, the watches were upgraded to hurricane warnings from Key West to Key Largo, with hurricane watches raised on the west coast northward to Fort Myers, and gale warnings issued from Key Largo to Vero Beach. By September\xc2\xa011, hurricane warnings were in effect for southern Florida from Daytona Beach on the east coast to Cedar Key on the west coast, including Lake Okeechobee. Gale warnings were in place northward from Cedar Key to St. Marks, as well as from Daytona Beach northward to Savannah, Georgia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watchwarnings_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-watchwarnings-7">[7]</a></sup> Evacuations in the Florida Keys disrupted traffic along the <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Highway" title="Overseas Highway">Overseas Highway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi99_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-upi99-10">[10]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">Air Force</a> evacuated 90\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Boeing_B-47_Stratojet" title="Boeing B-47 Stratojet">Boeing B-47 Stratojets</a> from <a href="/wiki/Homestead_Air_Reserve_Base" title="Homestead Air Reserve Base">Homestead Air Reserve Base</a>. At <a href="/wiki/Cape_Canaveral" title="Cape Canaveral">Cape Canaveral</a>, the threat of the storm caused the launching of two missiles to be postponed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi98_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-upi98-12">[12]</a></sup> Most flights out of <a href="/wiki/Miami_International_Airport" title="Miami International Airport">Miami International Airport</a> were canceled during the storm\'s approach. Officials closed schools in Miami and the Florida Keys,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ws99_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ws99-11">[11]</a></sup> and recommended residents in low-lying areas of the Florida Keys and southwestern Florida to evacuate. Ultimately, about 12,000\xc2\xa0people in southern Florida sought refuge in storm shelters, two of which were damaged during the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap911_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap911-13">[13]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Miami-Dade_County,_Florida" title="Miami-Dade County, Florida">Miami-Dade County</a> alone, there were 77\xc2\xa0storm shelters housing 10,000\xc2\xa0people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<p>At 5 p.m. on September 10, gale warnings were extended northward to Myrtle Beach. At 11 p.m., hurricane warnings were lowered in the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Florida Keys</a> but extended northward from Daytona Beach to <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watchwarnings_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-watchwarnings-7">[7]</a></sup> At 11 a.m. on September 11, all warnings were lowered south of Vero Beach and along the Florida west coast, while hurricane warnings were extended northward from Savannah to Myrtle Beach. At 5 p.m., hurricane warnings were lowered south of Fernandina Beach, while they were extended northward to include the entire <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> coast. Gale warnings were issued northward to Cape May. At 9 p.m., hurricane warnings were extended northward to <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_New_Hampshire" title="Portsmouth, New Hampshire">Portsmouth, New Hampshire</a>, while gale warnings and a hurricane watch were issued northward to <a href="/wiki/Eastport,_Maine" title="Eastport, Maine">Eastport, Maine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watchwarnings2_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-watchwarnings2-15">[15]</a></sup> Ships at dock in <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport, Rhode Island</a> were towed out into the bay to weather the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On September 12 at 5 a.m., hurricane warnings were extended northward to Eastport, and dropped south of Cape Hatteras. At 7 a.m., hurricane warnings were lowered south of Cape Charles. At 2 p.m., hurricane warnings were dropped south of Cape May. At 5 p.m., hurricane warnings were discontinued south of Manasquan, New Jersey. At 8 p.m., hurricane warnings expired south of Block Island. By 11 p.m. on September 12, all hurricane warnings had been lowered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watchwarnings2_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-watchwarnings2-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Donna_1960_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="705" data-file-width="842" height="184" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Donna_1960_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.png/220px-Donna_1960_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Donna_1960_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.png/330px-Donna_1960_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Donna_1960_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.png/440px-Donna_1960_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Donna_1960_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDonna\'s Rainfall around Puerto Rico</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Donna was a very destructive hurricane that caused extensive damage from the Lesser Antilles to New England. At least 364 people were killed by the hurricane and property damage was estimated at $900 million (1960 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DonnaDeaths_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-DonnaDeaths-17">[17]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Africa_and_Caribbean">West Africa and Caribbean</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: West Africa and Caribbean">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The precursor to Hurricane Donna brought severe weather to the <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">Dakar</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-valate_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-valate-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Air_France" title="Air France">Air France</a> Flight AF343, which was flying from <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> to <a href="/wiki/Abidjan" title="Abidjan">Abidjan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory Coast</a>, attempted to land at the <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9opold_S%C3%A9dar_Senghor_International_Airport" title="L\xc3\xa9opold S\xc3\xa9dar Senghor International Airport">L\xc3\xa9opold S\xc3\xa9dar Senghor International Airport</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Layover" title="Layover">layover</a>. However, due to squally weather, the plane instead crashed into the Atlantic Ocean, killing all 63\xc2\xa0people on board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asn_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-asn-19">[19]</a></sup> Heavy rainfall was also reported in Cape Verde on August\xc2\xa030.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Hurricane Donna caused very extensive damage on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint-Martin" title="Saint-Martin">Saint-Martin</a>, killed 7 and left at least a quarter of the island\'s population homeless. A weather station in <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a> reported sustained wind gusts of 120\xc2\xa0mph (195\xc2\xa0km/h) and a 952\xc2\xa0mbar (28.1\xc2\xa0inHg) pressure reading in the main airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Donna killed two people on Antigua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap96_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap96-20">[20]</a></sup> During the passing of hurricane Donna, <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a> recorded five deaths, including a woman who died when the roof of her house collapsed.</p>\n<p>Despite passing only 35\xc2\xa0mi (56\xc2\xa0km) north of the island, Donna caused only minor damage on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">St. Thomas</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">United States Virgin Islands</a>. A station there reported a wind gust of 60\xc2\xa0mph (97\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Some fences were toppled, while several houses were reported to have been damaged or destroyed. Electrical and telephone services were also disrupted. The highest daily rainfall total on the island was 8.78 inches (223\xc2\xa0mm), causing minor local flooding. On <a href="/wiki/Saint_John,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint John, U.S. Virgin Islands">Saint John</a>, several small boats capsized.</p>\n<p>While passing to the north of Puerto Rico, Donna produced winds of 38\xc2\xa0mph (61\xc2\xa0km/h) in San Juan. Along the north coast of the island, high tides of around 6\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m) and strong waves caused coastal flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> The hurricane dropped torrential rainfall, peaking at 16.23\xc2\xa0in (412\xc2\xa0mm) at <a href="/wiki/Naguabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Naguabo, Puerto Rico">Naguabo</a> in the central portion of the island. Large areas of eastern Puerto Rico received over 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-rain-21">[21]</a></sup> The hurricane left about 2,500\xc2\xa0people homeless on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi98_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-upi98-12">[12]</a></sup> Despite advanced warning of the floods, the hurricane killed 107\xc2\xa0people on the island, of which 84 were in <a href="/wiki/Humacao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Humacao, Puerto Rico">Humacao</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Haiti, the southern periphery of the hurricane killed three people in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_au_Prince" title="Port au Prince">Port au Prince</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap96_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap96-20">[20]</a></sup> Later, Donna brushed the north coast of Cuba with strong winds and heavy rainfall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> causing damage along much of the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi99_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-upi99-10">[10]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Gibara" title="Gibara">Gibara</a>, the storm wrecked 80\xc2\xa0houses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ws99_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-ws99-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Turks_and_Caicos_and_Bahamas">Turks and Caicos and Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Turks and Caicos and Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On <a href="/wiki/Grand_Turk_Island" title="Grand Turk Island">Grand Turk</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos" title="Turks and Caicos">Turks and Caicos</a>, Donna produced winds of 58\xc2\xa0mph (93\xc2\xa0km/h), as the strongest winds remained north of the island. However, the storm dropped heavy rainfall of over 20\xc2\xa0in (510\xc2\xa0mm), much of which fell in a 12\xe2\x80\x91hour period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Despite the rains, damage there was minor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi98_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-upi98-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the Bahamas, the <a href="/wiki/Anemometer" title="Anemometer">anemometer</a> at Ragged Island blew away after registering a 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h) wind gust. At Mayaguana, where residents evacuated to a missile tracking base, hurricane-force winds raged for 13\xc2\xa0hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-14"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> The winds largely destroyed the village of <a href="/wiki/Abraham%27s_Bay" title="Abraham\'s Bay">Abraham\'s Bay</a> on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap99_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap99-22">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Andros,_Bahamas" title="Andros, Bahamas">Andros</a> experienced hurricane-force winds for a few hours, and winds on Fortune Island were estimated at 173\xc2\xa0mph (278\xc2\xa0km/h) before the anemometer blew away. The strongest winds remained south of the northwestern Bahamas, which limited damage there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-15"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Donna cut communications between several islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi98_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-upi98-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Several small island communities in the central Bahamas were leveled. North Caicos reported 20 inches (510\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall in 24 hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Fifty people were reported dead in the United States, with damages totaling to $3.35\xc2\xa0billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Donna crossed directly over <a href="/wiki/Texas_Tower_4" title="Texas Tower 4">Texas Tower 4</a>, causing severe damage to the structure and leading to its eventual loss in January 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Donna was the only hurricane to affect every state along the East Coast with hurricane-force winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Biscayneblvd1960donna_flooding.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1594" data-file-width="1043" height="336" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Biscayneblvd1960donna_flooding.jpg/330px-Biscayneblvd1960donna_flooding.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Biscayneblvd1960donna_flooding.jpg/440px-Biscayneblvd1960donna_flooding.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Biscayneblvd1960donna_flooding.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nFlooding along <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_1_in_Florida#Miami-Dade_County" title="U.S. Route 1 in Florida">Biscayne Boulevard</a> in Miami, Florida</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The U.S. state of Florida received the most damage from Hurricane Donna. Portions of southern and western Florida received over 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall from the hurricane, peaking at 13.24\xc2\xa0in (336\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-rain-21">[21]</a></sup> Strong winds were observed in the state, with a sustained wind speed of 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h) in Tavernier and a gusts up to 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Sombrero_Key_Light" title="Sombrero Key Light">Sombrero Key Light</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> In Miami, winds reached 97\xc2\xa0mph (156\xc2\xa0km/h). Southeast of the city, high waves washed a 104-foot (32\xc2\xa0m) freighter onshore an island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap911_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap911-13">[13]</a></sup> The highest observed <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> of 13\xc2\xa0ft (4.0\xc2\xa0m) was reported at Marathon. The hurricane also lashed <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Florida" title="Southwest Florida">Southwest Florida</a>, where tides were 4 to 7 feet (1.2 to 2.1\xc2\xa0m) above normal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Miami-Dade_County,_Florida" title="Miami-Dade County, Florida">Miami-Dade County</a>, thousands of low-lying homes in the <a href="/wiki/Homestead,_Florida" title="Homestead, Florida">Homestead area</a> were flooded. Overall, 857\xc2\xa0houses in the county were destroyed, while about 2,317\xc2\xa0others suffered damage. Significant agricultural losses were also reported. Donna was the first hurricane to affect Miami, Florida, since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_King" title="Hurricane King">Hurricane King</a> in October 1950.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC2_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-NCDC2-28">[28]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the Florida Keys, some areas experienced "almost complete destruction".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> Further north between Marathon and Tavernier, an estimated 75% of buildings were extensively damaged. In the former, tides inundated the city and destroyed several subdivisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-3"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">Key West</a>, one death was confirmed, and 71\xc2\xa0people were injured. About 564\xc2\xa0homes were demolished, and an additional 1,382 were damaged, 583 of them severely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Storm surge inundated parts of the <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Highway" title="Overseas Highway">Overseas Highway</a> and washed out several portions near bridges. Many boats and docks were severely damaged or destroyed. Additionally, the pipeline supplying water to the Florida Keys was wrecked in three places.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-4"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Large tracts of <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove</a> forest were lost in the western portion of <a href="/wiki/Everglades_National_Park" title="Everglades National Park">Everglades National Park</a>, while at least 35% of the white heron population in the park were killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dunion_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dunion-30">[30]</a></sup> In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Everglades,_Florida" title="Everglades, Florida">Everglades, FL</a>, about 50% of buildings were destroyed due to strong winds and coastal flooding. Late on September\xc2\xa011, 2 to 3 feet (0.61 to 0.91\xc2\xa0m) of water was reported throughout the area. The city briefly became inaccessible due to inundated roads. Many small buildings were destroyed, and roofs were blown off or damaged. Thousands of trees were toppled,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-5"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> blocking portions of the <a href="/wiki/Tamiami_Trail" title="Tamiami Trail">Tamiami Trail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap911_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap911-13">[13]</a></sup> Throughout <a href="/wiki/Collier_County,_Florida" title="Collier County, Florida">Collier County</a>, strong winds and coastal flooding combined destroyed 153\xc2\xa0homes, inflicted major impact on an additional 409, and 1,049 others suffered minor damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The turn into southern Florida lessened damage in the Tampa area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Throughout the state of Florida, the storm destroyed 2,156\xc2\xa0homes and trailers, severely damaged 3,903, and inflicted minor impact on 30,524 others. Approximately 391\xc2\xa0farm buildings were destroyed, an additional 989\xc2\xa0suffered extensive impact, and 2,499 others received minor damage. Roughly 174\xc2\xa0buildings were demolished, 1,029 received major impact, and 4,254 suffered minor damage. Additionally, 281\xc2\xa0boats were destroyed or severely damaged. A total of 50% of grapefruit crop was lost, 10% of the orange and tangerine crops were ruined, and the avocado crop was almost destroyed. With at least $350\xc2\xa0million in damage in Florida alone, Donna was the costliest hurricane to impact the state, at the time. Additionally, there were 14\xc2\xa0confirmed fatalities: six from drowning, four from heart attacks, two from automobile accidents, and two from electrocution. About 1,188 others were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Southeastern_United_States_and_Mid-Atlantic">Southeastern United States and Mid-Atlantic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Southeastern United States and Mid-Atlantic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:212px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bennett-rice-mill-charleston-sc1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2553" data-file-width="3365" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Bennett-rice-mill-charleston-sc1.jpg/210px-Bennett-rice-mill-charleston-sc1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Bennett-rice-mill-charleston-sc1.jpg/315px-Bennett-rice-mill-charleston-sc1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Bennett-rice-mill-charleston-sc1.jpg/420px-Bennett-rice-mill-charleston-sc1.jpg 2x" width="210"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bennett-rice-mill-charleston-sc1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nThe facade of the 1840s-era Bennett\'s Rice Mill in <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a>; much of the structure was destroyed by a <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a>.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The storm brought minor impact to <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>. Wind gusts of 50\xc2\xa0mph (80\xc2\xa0km/h) along the coast felled trees and tree limbs, resulting in electrical and telephone-service outages. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brunswick,_GA" title="Brunswick, GA">Brunswick, GA</a>, a power outage at the power plant caused a minor explosion. Heavy rainfall temporarily flooded some streets in the city. Further north in <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, gale-force winds were reported along the coast, but caused little damage. A tornado spawned in the <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston area</a> destroyed several houses and severely damaged a number of others, and injured a few people by flying glass. Damage from this tornado was over $500,000. Another tornado touched down in <a href="/wiki/Garden_City,_South_Carolina" title="Garden City, South Carolina">Garden City, SC</a> and destroyed or extensively damaged six buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-6"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_County,_South_Carolina" title="Beaufort County, South Carolina">Beaufort County, SC</a>, many trees were uprooted, power lines were downed, homes were unroofed, piers were destroyed, and there was significant damage to corn and soybean crops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bccc_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-bccc-34">[34]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In North Carolina, Donna brought two tornadoes to the state. The first of which damaged several small buildings in <a href="/wiki/Bladen_County,_North_Carolina" title="Bladen County, North Carolina">Bladen County</a>. The second tornado was spawned in <a href="/wiki/Sampson_County,_North_Carolina" title="Sampson County, North Carolina">Sampson County</a>, where it destroyed a dwelling with eight occupants, all of whom were hospitalized. Along the coast, wind gusts as high as 100\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h) damaged or destroyed several buildings. Additionally crops were damaged as far as 50 miles (80\xc2\xa0km) inland. Storm tides ranging from 4 to 8 feet (1.2 to 2.4\xc2\xa0m) above normal caused significant beach erosion and structural damage at <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">Wilmington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nags_Head,_North_Carolina" title="Nags Head, North Carolina">Nags Head</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-7"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Topsail_Beach,_North_Carolina" title="Topsail Beach, North Carolina">Topsail Beach</a> was reported to have been 50% destroyed. In <a href="/wiki/Southport,_North_Carolina" title="Southport, North Carolina">Southport</a>, the town docks were almost completely demolished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sno_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-sno-35">[35]</a></sup> There were eight deaths, including three from drowning, two from falling trees, two from weather-related traffic accidents, and one from electrocution. At least 100\xc2\xa0people were injured enough to require hospitalization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-8"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> Damage in North Carolina exceeded $5\xc2\xa0million, with the worst impact occurring in <a href="/wiki/New_Hanover_County,_North_Carolina" title="New Hanover County, North Carolina">New Hanover County</a>.<sup up to 89\xc2\xa0mph (143\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-valate_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-valate-18">[18]</a></sup> Strong winds toppled trees and electrical wires, which blocked streets. Additionally, buildings suffered roof damage and broken windows; some structures were completely destroyed. Offshore, rough seas sunk or destroyed numerous small crafts, while a 12,000 tonnes (26,000,000\xc2\xa0lb) vessel was driven aground. The storm killed three people in Virginia; two of the deaths occurred when a barge collided with a freighter and later sank, and another after a man attempted to safeguard his boat. Strong winds and heavy rains were observed in eastern <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ocean_City,_Maryland" title="Ocean City, Maryland">Ocean City</a> suffered the worst impact, with over $300,000 in property damage. The storm also damaged crops in the area, especially corn and apples. Effects from the storm in Delaware were similar, with property damage and considerable losses to corn and apple crops. In <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, wind gusts up to 59\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h) in the southeastern portions of the state toppled many trees and utility wires. Heavy rains and poor drainage in some areas flooded basements, lawns, and streets. Low-lying areas in <a href="/wiki/Bucks_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Bucks County, Pennsylvania">Bucks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Montgomery County, Pennsylvania">Montgomery counties</a> were inundated with up to 3 feet (0.91\xc2\xa0m) of water after many streams and creeks nearby overflowed. One death in the state was reported after a boy was swept into a swollen creek behind his home in <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Hill,_Pennsylvania" title="Sharon Hill, Pennsylvania">Sharon Hill</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-9"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Winds as strong as 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h) were observed along the coast of New Jersey. Rainfall in the state was generally between 5 and 6 inches (130 and 150\xc2\xa0mm),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-10"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> with a peak of 8.99 inches (228\xc2\xa0mm) near <a href="/wiki/Hammonton,_New_Jersey" title="Hammonton, New Jersey">Hammonton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mid_Atl_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mid_Atl-39">[39]</a></sup> Damage from the storm was most severe in <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_County,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic County, New Jersey">Atlantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_May_County,_New_Jersey" title="Cape May County, New Jersey">Cape May</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monmouth_County,_New_Jersey" title="Monmouth County, New Jersey">Monmouth</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ocean_County,_New_Jersey" title="Ocean County, New Jersey">Ocean</a> counties, where numerous boats, docks, boardwalks, and cottages were damaged or destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-11"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> A resort area in <a href="/wiki/Cliffwood_Beach,_New_Jersey" title="Cliffwood Beach, New Jersey">Cliffwood Beach, New Jersey</a> saw its boardwalk and tourist attractions destroyed by the hurricane, and the area has never recovered. Losses to agriculture were significant, with damage to apple and peach trees "considerable", the former of which lost about one-third of its crops. Wind damage to corn, Sudan grass, and sorgham resulted in a delay in their harvest. Nine deaths were reported in the state of New Jersey. In southeastern <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, heavy rains, hurricane-force winds, and "unprecedented" high tides were observed. Severe small stream flooding caused significant damage, especially on Long Island, the waterfront of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and further north in <a href="/wiki/Greene_County,_New_York" title="Greene County, New York">Greene County</a>. The storm caused three fatalities in the state, two from drowning and another from a person crushed by a falling tree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-12"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere_in_North_America">Elsewhere in North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Elsewhere in North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, strong winds left 15,000\xc2\xa0people without telephone service, while 88,000\xc2\xa0homes lost electricity. Along the coast, tides caused beach erosion, inundated streets, and weakened foundations. Four seaside cottages were destroyed. Crop damage was isolated and mainly limited to apples and corn. In <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, the storm brought a wind gust as strong as 130\xc2\xa0mph (210\xc2\xa0km/h) to <a href="/wiki/Block_Island" title="Block Island">Block Island</a>. Telephone and electrical services were severely disrupted. Along the coast, high tides significantly damaged or destroyed about 200\xc2\xa0homes at <a href="/wiki/Narragansett_Bay" title="Narragansett Bay">Narragansett Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warwick,_Rhode_Island" title="Warwick, Rhode Island">Warwick</a> cove. Damage to these vessels collectively totaled to over $2\xc2\xa0million. Agriculture also suffered impact, particularly to fruit, timber, and poultry, especially in <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_Rhode_Island" title="Portsmouth, Rhode Island">Portsmouth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-13"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Strong winds were also observed in <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, with a wind gust of 145\xc2\xa0mph (233\xc2\xa0km/h) at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Hill_Observatory" title="Blue Hill Observatory">Blue Hill Observatory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-16"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Extensive losses to apple orchards occurred, as the fruit was blown out of trees. Widespread telephone and power outages were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-14"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup> The strong southwest winds associated with Donna, in combination with very little rainfall, led to a significant deposit of salt spray, which whitewashed southwest-facing windows. Many trees and shrubs saw their leaves brown due to the salt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> However, in other areas, 4 to 6 inches (100 to 150\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation fell, causing some washouts and local flooding. Waves along the coast ripped small boats and pleasure craft from their moorings and subsequently smashed them against rocks or seawalls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-15"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>, winds damaged trees, tree branches, and power lines, causing telephone and electrical service outages in a few communities. Rainfall totals ranged 2\xe2\x80\x935 inches (51\xe2\x80\x93127\xc2\xa0mm), resulting in washouts in some areas. Damage to apple orchards totaled $50,000. Along the coast of <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, many boats were smashed or damaged in some way. Strong winds felled trees and power lines, causing residents in the southern portions of the state to lose telephone service and electricity. Additionally, apple orchards suffered $200,000 in damage. Rainfall in the state peaked at 7.25 inches (184\xc2\xa0mm) near <a href="/wiki/Peterborough,_New_Hampshire" title="Peterborough, New Hampshire">Peterborough</a>, resulting in local flooding and washouts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_27-16"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Along the coast, large waves damaged 15 to 20\xc2\xa0boats in <a href="/wiki/Falmouth,_Maine" title="Falmouth, Maine">Falmouth, Maine</a> harbor. Total boat damage was estimated at $250,000. Coastal residents in low-lying beach areas of <a href="/wiki/Cumberland_County,_Maine" title="Cumberland County, Maine">Cumberland</a> and <a href="/wiki/York_County,_Maine" title="York County, Maine">York</a> counties were evacuated in Maine. Several counties lost power during the storm. In <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Harbor,_Maine" title="Southwest Harbor, Maine">Southwest Harbor</a>, lightning struck the Dirigo Hotel, causing a fire that resulted in $100,000 in damages. Winds caused a lost of telephone and electrical services in the <a href="/wiki/Auburn,_Maine" title="Auburn, Maine">Auburn</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lewiston,_Maine" title="Lewiston, Maine">Lewiston</a> area due to falling trees or tree branches. Television antennas were damaged, as were several signs, including a <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a> sign. In addition, 25% to 40% of the apple crop was destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After becoming extratropical, the remnants of Donna continued northeastward into <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, and then <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Labrador</a>. Wind gusts of 53\xc2\xa0mph (85\xc2\xa0km/h) in Quebec snapped electrical poles and trees. One death occurred when a man suffered a heart attack when his home was threatened by a fire. Additionally, weather-related traffic accidents in the province resulted in two injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Depictions_in_popular_culture">Depictions in popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Depictions in popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_for_Literature" title="Nobel Prize for Literature">Nobel Prize</a>-winner <a href="/wiki/John_Steinbeck" title="John Steinbeck">John Steinbeck</a> wrote about Hurricane Donna in his 1962 non-fiction memoir <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Travels_with_Charley:_In_Search_of_America" title="Travels with Charley: In Search of America">Travels with Charley: In Search of America</a></i>. Steinbeck had had a truck fitted with a custom camper-shell for a journey he intended to take across the United States, accompanied by his poodle Charley. He planned on leaving after Labor Day from his home in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sag_Harbor" title="Sag Harbor">Sag Harbor</a>, Long Island, New York. Steinbeck delayed his trip slightly due to Donna, which made a direct hit on Long Island. Steinbeck wrote of saving his boat during the middle of the hurricane, during which he jumped into the water and was blown to shore clinging to a fallen branch driven by the high winds. It was an exploit which foreshadowed his fearless, or even reckless, state of mind to dive into the unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The winds of Donna can be seen in the feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Blast_of_Silence" title="Blast of Silence">Blast of Silence</a></i> (1961); a fist fight scene on Long Island had been previously scheduled, and the filmmakers decided to go ahead and shoot the exterior scene despite the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span id="Aftermath,_records_and_retirement"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath.2C_records_and_retirement">Aftermath, records and retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Donna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aftermath, records and retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p>Following the storm, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a> issued a disaster declaration for Florida and North Carolina, allowing residents of those states to be eligible for public assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The United States military sent a plane carrying doctors and food from <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Air_Force_Base" title="Patrick Air Force Base">Patrick Air Force Base</a> to Mayaguana in the Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap99_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap99-22">[22]</a></sup> Crews of doctors and workers with food and supplies left from Key West and Miami to traverse the Florida Keys, bringing aid to affected residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap911_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-ap911-13">[13]</a></sup> In Marathon, a large reconstruction program rehabilitated the key by <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Coral reefs were damaged in the <a href="/wiki/Key_Largo" title="Key Largo">Key Largo</a> National Marine Sanctuary by the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Donna caused a significant negative impact on aquatic life in north <a href="/wiki/Florida_Bay" title="Florida Bay">Florida Bay</a>. Marine life was either stranded by retreating salt water which had been driven inland or killed by muddied waters in its wake. Oxygen depletion due to animals perishing in the hurricane caused additional mortality. Although salinity levels returned to normal within six weeks, dissolved oxygen concentrations remained quite low for a longer time frame. Marine life was scarce for several months in areas of greatest oxygen depletion. Sports fishing in the area took a few months to recover. Juvenile pink shrimp moved from their estuarine nursery grounds into deeper water about 60 miles (97\xc2\xa0km) offshore, where they were subsequently captured by fishermen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Caspian_tern" title="Caspian tern">Caspian tern</a> was swept up the North American coast well to the north of its traditional breeding grounds, to <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, which was witnessed four hours after the storm went by Digby Neck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Because of its devastating impacts and the high mortality associated with the hurricane, the name "Donna" was retired, and will never again be used for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a>; the name was replaced by "<a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Dora" title="Hurricane Dora">Dora</a>" in 1964.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
