lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Sugar Grove</b> is a small community located in <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_County,_West_Virginia" title="Pendleton County, West Virginia">Pendleton County</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Its population was 758 as of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_U.S._Census" title="2010 U.S. Census">2010 U.S. Census</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/ZIP_Code" title="ZIP Code">ZIP Code</a> is 26815. It is located within the <a href="/wiki/United_States_National_Radio_Quiet_Zone" title="United States National Radio Quiet Zone">United States National Radio Quiet Zone</a>.</p>\n<p>The community was named for a sugar orchard at the original town site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Bowers_House_(Sugar_Grove,_West_Virginia)" title="Bowers House (Sugar Grove, West Virginia)">Bowers House</a> was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nris-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The nearby <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Grove_Station" title="Sugar Grove Station">Sugar Grove Station</a> is operated by the <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Agency" title="National Security Agency">National Security Agency</a>.</p>\n<p>In 2017, the Navy Operations Information Command base in Sugar Grove was auctioned off by the <a href="/wiki/General_Services_Administration" title="General Services Administration">General Services Administration</a>.
