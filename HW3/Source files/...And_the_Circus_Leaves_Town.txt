album">studio album</a> by American <a href="/wiki/Stoner_rock" title="Stoner rock">stoner rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Kyuss" title="Kyuss">Kyuss</a>, released on July 11, 1995, nearly a year before their breakup. Drummer <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Hern%C3%A1ndez" title="Alfredo Hern\xc3\xa1ndez">Alfredo Hern\xc3\xa1ndez</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yawning_Man" title="Yawning Man">Yawning Man</a>) replaces <a href="/wiki/Brant_Bjork" title="Brant Bjork">Brant Bjork</a>, who left Kyuss in 1993. The album features a tighter and more straightforward sound, both in songwriting and production, than the band\'s preceding efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Upon its release, <i>\xe2\x80\xa6And the Circus Leaves Town</i> was not as commercially or critically successful as the previous <i><a href="/wiki/Blues_for_the_Red_Sun" title="Blues for the Red Sun">Blues for the Red Sun</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Sky_Valley" title="Welcome to Sky Valley">Welcome to Sky Valley</a></i>. Critic Dean Brown attributes this partly to a lack of promotion and the band\'s breakup, but also notes that the album "deserves to be cherished as much as the two molten hot records that came right before it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> A video was released for "One Inch Man", the album\'s only official single.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The song "Catamaran" is a cover of a song originally recorded by drummer Alfredo Hernandez\' previous band <a href="/wiki/Yawning_Man" title="Yawning Man">Yawning Man</a>.</p>\n<p>Hidden track "Day One" was originally released in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> as part of the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demon_Cleaner_(song)" title="Demon Cleaner (song)">Demon Cleaner</a>" extended CD single under the title "Day One (To Dave and Krist)". It was dedicated to the remaining <a href="/wiki/Nirvana_(band)" title="Nirvana (band)">Nirvana</a> members <a href="/wiki/Dave_Grohl" title="Dave Grohl">Dave Grohl</a> and <a href="/wiki/Krist_Novoselic" title="Krist Novoselic">Krist Novoselic</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Cobain" title="Kurt Cobain">Kurt Cobain</a>\'s suicide.</p>\n<p>The song "Hurricane" is featured in the 2006 video game, <i><a href="/wiki/Need_for_Speed:_Carbon" title="Need for Speed: Carbon">Need for Speed: Carbon</a></i>. An early recording of the song, as well as "El Rodeo," was released on the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demon_Cleaner_(song)" title="Demon Cleaner (song)">Demon Cleaner</a>" single in 1994.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personnel">Personnel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
