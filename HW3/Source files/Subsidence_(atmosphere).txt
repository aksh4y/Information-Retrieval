As the air cools, it becomes denser and moves towards the ground, as warm air becomes less dense and moves upwards. Subsiding air, when cool, is subject to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adiabatic" title="Adiabatic">adiabatic</a> warming, which tends to cause the evaporation of any clouds that might be present.</p>\n<p>Subsidence generally causes high <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> as more air moves into the same space: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polar_high" title="Polar high">polar highs</a> are areas of almost constant subsidence, as are the <a href="/wiki/Horse_latitudes" title="Horse latitudes">horse latitudes</a>, and the areas of subsidence are the sources of much of the world\'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prevailing_wind" title="Prevailing wind">prevailing wind</a>.</p>\n<p>Subsidence also causes many smaller-scale <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a> phenomena, such as morning <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fog</a>. An extreme form of subsidence is a <a href="/wiki/Downburst" title="Downburst">downburst</a>, which can result in damage similar to that produced by a <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a>. A milder form of subsidence is referred to as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Downdraft" title="Downdraft">downdraft</a>.</p>\n<table class="metadata plainlinks stub" role="presentation" style="background:transparent">\n<tr>\n<td><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cyclone_Catarina_from_the_ISS_on_March_26_2004.JPG"><img alt="Stub icon" data-file-height="2004" data-file-width="3032" height="26" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Cyclone_Catarina_from_the_ISS_on_March_26_2004.JPG/40px-Cyclone_Catarina_from_the_ISS_on_March_26_2004.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Cyclone_Catarina_from_the_ISS_on_March_26_2004.JPG/60px-Cyclone_Catarina_from_the_ISS_on_March_26_2004.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Cyclone_Catarina_from_the_ISS_on_March_26_2004.JPG/80px-Cyclone_Catarina_from_the_ISS_on_March_26_2004.JPG 2x" width="40"/></a></td>\n<td><i>This <a href="/wiki/Climatology" title="Climatology">climatology</a>/<a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorology</a>\xe2\x80\x93related article is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Stub" title="Wikipedia:Stub">stub</a>.
