class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Grace_Jones_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Grace Jones\' previous album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bulletproof_Heart" title="Bulletproof Heart">Bulletproof Heart</a></i>, was released in 1989, and despite several comeback attempts throughout the 1990s, her next full-length record would be released almost two decades later. The singer had decided "never to do an album again",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> changing her mind only after meeting the music producer <a href="/wiki/Ivor_Guest,_4th_Viscount_Wimborne" title="Ivor Guest, 4th Viscount Wimborne">Ivor Guest</a> via mutual friend <a href="/wiki/Philip_Treacy" title="Philip Treacy">Philip Treacy</a>. After becoming acquainted, Guest played Jones a track he had been working on and she set her lyrics "Devil in My Life" to it. They ended up recording as many as 23 tracks, some of which may be part of a future project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailymail_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2007 Guest announced that he and Jones had completed recording the album.</p>\n<p>The album includes a number of autobiographical songs, these include "This Is", "<a href="/wiki/Williams%27_Blood" title="Williams\' Blood">Williams\' Blood</a>" and "I\'m Crying (Mother\'s Tears)". "<a href="/wiki/Love_You_to_Life" title="Love You to Life">Love You to Life</a>" is another track based on real events and "<a href="/wiki/Corporate_Cannibal" title="Corporate Cannibal">Corporate Cannibal</a>" refers to the subject of <a href="/wiki/Corporate_capitalism" title="Corporate capitalism">corporate capitalism</a>. The title track was first recorded as a 1997 collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Tricky_(musician)" title="Tricky (musician)">Tricky</a> under the title "<a href="/wiki/Hurricane_(Cradle_to_the_Grave)" title="Hurricane (Cradle to the Grave)">Cradle to the Grave</a>". "Well Well Well" is dedicated to the memory of <a href="/wiki/Alex_Sadkin" title="Alex Sadkin">Alex Sadkin</a>, who had died in 1987, having co-produced three of Jones\' 1980s albums. "Sunset Sunrise" ponders mankind\'s relationship with nature, and the final song, "Devil in My Life", was written after a party in <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a> while Jones was standing in the corner observing partygoers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailymail_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail-14">[14]</a></sup> Four songs were ultimately removed from the track listing: "The Key to Funky" (co-written by Jones and <a href="/wiki/Diane_Pernet" title="Diane Pernet">Diane Pernet</a> in the late \'80s), "Body Phenomenon", "Sister Sister" and "Misery". Another track recorded by Jones, "Volunteer", was leaked in 2007 by <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Winer" title="Leslie Winer">Leslie Winer</a>, together with "This", an early version of "This Is".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Winer also asserted that she had written both songs with Joe Galdo in the early 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Mainly with <a href="/wiki/Sly_and_Robbie" title="Sly and Robbie">Sly and Robbie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wally_Badarou" title="Wally Badarou">Wally Badarou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Reynolds" title="Barry Reynolds">Barry Reynolds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mikey_Chung" title="Mikey Chung">Mikey Chung</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uziah_%22Sticky%22_Thompson" title=\'Uziah "Sticky" Thompson\'>Uziah "Sticky" Thompson</a>, aka the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compass_Point_Allstars" title="Compass Point Allstars">Compass Point Allstars</a> as a backbone, the album retained the reggae-influenced sound of her three <a href="/wiki/Compass_Point_Studios" title="Compass Point Studios">Compass Point</a> albums even though it was not recorded at the legendary studios in the Bahamas.</p>\n<p>Originally rumoured to be titled <i>Corporate Cannibal</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> the album\'s final title <i>Hurricane</i> and the release date of 27 October were announced in June 2008, at her signing with <a href="/wiki/Wall_of_Sound_(record_label)" title="Wall of Sound (record label)">Wall of Sound</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The album was released on Wall of Sound on November 3, 2008 in the United Kingdom. <a href="/wiki/PIAS_Recordings" title="PIAS Recordings">PIAS</a>, the parent company of Wall of Sound, distributed <i>Hurricane</i> worldwide, excluding North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> It met with positive reception, obtaining a score of 72 out of 100 on review aggregator <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Prior to the release, Jones performed a two-hour concert at <a href="/wiki/Massive_Attack" title="Massive Attack">Massive Attack</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Meltdown_(festival)" title="Meltdown (festival)">Meltdown</a> festival in London on 19 June 2008, during which she performed four new songs from the album and premiered the music video for the first single, "Corporate Cannibal".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> For further album promotion, Jones appeared on British television talk show <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_Night_with_Jonathan_Ross" title="Friday Night with Jonathan Ross">Friday Night with Jonathan Ross</a></i>, several awards galas, and embarked on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Hurricane_Tour" title="The Hurricane Tour">The Hurricane Tour</a> in January 2009, which garnered positive reviews.</p>\n<p>Three years after the original <i>Hurricane</i> release, Jones released a <a href="/wiki/Dub_music" title="Dub music">dub</a> version of the album. <i>Hurricane \xe2\x80\x93 Dub</i> came out on September 5, 2011. The dub versions were made by Ivor Guest, with contributions from <a href="/wiki/Adam_Green_(musician)" title="Adam Green (musician)">Adam Green</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frank_Byng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frank Byng (page does not exist)">Frank Byng</a>, Robert Logan and Ben Cowan.</p>\n<p>Jones dedicated the album to the memory of her father, <a href="/wiki/Grace_Jones#Personal_life" title="Grace Jones">Bishop Robert W. Jones</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artwork">Artwork</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Grace_Jones_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Artwork">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The front and back covers of the album features pictures of chocolate heads of Jones, which she revealed on <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_Night_with_Jonathan_Ross" title="Friday Night with Jonathan Ross">Friday Night with Jonathan Ross</a></i> shortly before <i>Hurricane\'</i>s release. Photographs included in the booklet picture the singer as a chocolate factory worker, complete with uniform and <a href="/wiki/Name_tag" title="Name tag">name tag</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Chocolate heads, as well as arms and legs were <a href="/wiki/Molding_(process)" title="Molding (process)">molded</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Thorntons" title="Thorntons">Thorntons</a> chocolate factory in <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a>, England<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> by <a href="/wiki/Lifecasting" title="Lifecasting">lifecasting</a> expert <a href="/wiki/John_Schoonraad" title="John Schoonraad">John Schoonraad</a>, his son Tristan and artist Nick Reynolds.</p>\n<p>The dub re-release of <i>Hurricane</i> features new artwork by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Paul_Goude" title="Jean-Paul Goude">Jean-Paul Goude</a> of Jones smoking a cigarette whilst wearing a sparkling hat.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Grace_Jones_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>"<a href="/wiki/Corporate_Cannibal" title="Corporate Cannibal">Corporate Cannibal</a>" became the album\'s first single, released in August 2008 and promoted at the <a href="/wiki/Meltdown_(festival)" title="Meltdown (festival)">Meltdown</a> festival. The song did not chart. The second single, "<a href="/wiki/Williams%27_Blood" title="Williams\' Blood">Williams\' Blood</a>", was released in December, and subsequently became a charting success in Belgium. A promotional only single, "Well Well Well", was released in 2009. "<a href="/wiki/Love_You_to_Life" title="Love You to Life">Love You to Life</a>" was chosen as the third commercial single in 2009, but its release would be postponed for over a year.</p>\n<h2><span
