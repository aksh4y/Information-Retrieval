href="/wiki/List_of_International_Space_Station_expeditions" title="List of International Space Station expeditions">expeditions</a></div>\n<div style="width:100%">\n<div class="noprint" style="float: 29th long-duration expedition to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS). The expedition formally began on 16 September 2011, with the departure from the ISS of the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-21" title="Soyuz TMA-21">Soyuz TMA-21</a> spacecraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Astronauts <a href="/wiki/Satoshi_Furukawa" title="Satoshi Furukawa">Satoshi Furukawa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Fossum" title="Michael Fossum">Michael Fossum</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergey_Alexandrovich_Volkov" title="Sergey Alexandrovich Volkov">Sergey Volkov</a>, who had arrived at the ISS aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-02M" title="Soyuz TMA-02M">Soyuz TMA-02M</a> in June 2011, began their Expedition 29 service at this time.</p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-22" title="Soyuz TMA-22">Soyuz TMA-22</a>, which brought the remaining three Expedition 29 crew members to the ISS, was originally scheduled to launch in September 2011, but due to the <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-12M#Launch_failure" title="Progress M-12M">launch failure of the Progress M-12M resupply vehicle</a> on 24 August, its launch was delayed to 14 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It docked successfully with the ISS on 16 November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TMA22docking_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TMA22docking-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Expedition 29 officially ended with the undocking of Soyuz TMA-02M on 21 November 2011. Furukawa, Fossum, and Volkov returned to Earth aboard the spacecraft, while astronauts <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dan_Burbank" title="Dan Burbank">Dan Burbank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anton_Shkaplerov" title="Anton Shkaplerov">Anton Shkaplerov</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anatoli_Ivanishin" title="Anatoli Ivanishin">Anatoli Ivanishin</a> remained on the ISS as part of <a href="/wiki/Expedition_30" title="Expedition 30">Expedition 30</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_29&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Soyuz TMA-02M launch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The expedition\'s first three crew members launched aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-02M" title="Soyuz TMA-02M">Soyuz TMA-02M</a> from <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, at 21:18\xc2\xa0<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a> on 7 June 2011. The crew consisted of Sergey Volkov (<a href="/wiki/Roscosmos" title="Roscosmos">Roscosmos</a>), Satoshi Furukawa (<a href="/wiki/JAXA" title="JAXA">JAXA</a>), and Michael Fossum (<a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>). Their backups were <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Kononenko" title="Oleg Kononenko">Oleg Kononenko</a> (Roscosmos), <a href="/wiki/Donald_Pettit" title="Donald Pettit">Donald Pettit</a> (NASA), and <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Kuipers" title="Andr\xc3\xa9 Kuipers">Andr\xc3\xa9 Kuipers</a> (ESA). Soyuz TMA-02M docked successfully to the ISS on 9 June 2011, at 5:19 pm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Daylight_Time" title="Eastern Daylight Time">EDT</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exp28_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-exp28-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soyuz_TMA-22_launch">Soyuz TMA-22 launch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_29&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Soyuz TMA-22 launch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-22" title="Soyuz TMA-22">Soyuz TMA-22</a> launched from Baikonur Cosmodrome at 04:14\xc2\xa0UTC on 14 November 2011, carrying Anton Shkaplerov and Anatoli Ivanishin (Roscosmos), and Daniel Burbank (NASA). The spacecraft was placed into a 250-kilometre (160\xc2\xa0mi) parking orbit,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and docked successfully with the ISS at 5:24 am GMT on 16 November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TMA22docking_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-TMA22docking-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Departure_of_Soyuz_TMA-02M">Departure of Soyuz TMA-02M</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_29&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Departure of Soyuz TMA-02M">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Expedition 29 concluded with the departure of Soyuz TMA-02M from the ISS at 11:00 pm <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> on 21 November 2011, carrying astronauts Fossum, Volkov and Furukawa.
