href="/wiki/Christian_alternative_rock" title="Christian alternative rock">Christian alternative rock</a> band originating from <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Kansas" title="Kansas City, Kansas">Kansas City, Kansas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CROSS_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CROSS-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HMONE_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-HMONE-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JESUS_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-JESUS-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCN_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-NCN-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BREATHE_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-BREATHE-5">[5]</a></sup> Band members included Dalton Diehl on vocals, piano and guitar, Darren Harms on guitar and Ryan Lytle on the <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HMONE_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-HMONE-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JESUS_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-JESUS-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BREATHE_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-BREATHE-5">[5]</a></sup> Their first studio album, <i>The Alignment</i>, was released on February 28, 2012, by <a href="/wiki/Sparrow_Records" title="Sparrow Records">Sparrow Records</a>, produced by Pete Kipley and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Moak" title="Paul Moak">Paul Moak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CREDITS_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CREDITS-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JESUSALIGN_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-JESUSALIGN-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RHYTHMS_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-RHYTHMS-8">[8]</a></sup> They are best known for their first single, "Hurricane". It reached No.\xc2\xa04 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_1" title="Air 1">Air 1</a>\'s Top Songs Chart on November 12, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AIRONE_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-AIRONE-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In March 2013 the band launched a <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a> campaign to raise funds for an independent EP, <i>The Boxer &amp; The Thief</i>. It was successfully funded and the EP was available in July to backers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KICKSTARTER_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-KICKSTARTER-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On the September 18, 2013, the band announced on their Facebook page that they were parting ways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPLIT_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-SPLIT-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samestate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Samestate was formed in 2008 in <a href="/wiki/Olathe,_Kansas" title="Olathe, Kansas">Olathe, Kansas</a>. Diehl was the impetus for the creation of the band because he was the worship leader at <a href="/wiki/MidAmerica_Nazarene_University" title="MidAmerica Nazarene University">MidAmerica Nazarene University</a> during his sophomore year, which was when he added Harms and Leoni to the band. Diehl said that he began composing songs to perform during meetings at camps and retreats and at places such as Sheridan\'s Frozen Custard and <a href="/wiki/Chick-fil-A" title="Chick-fil-A">Chick-fil-A</a>. This caused him to seek out and add the last two members to the band, Lytle and Wyatt, soon after they had formed the trio. The band signed with <a href="/wiki/Sparrow_Records" title="Sparrow Records">Sparrow Records</a> in 2011, who released a taster EP called <i>Samestate</i>, a precursor to 2012\'s <i>The Alignment</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CROSS_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-CROSS-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HMONE_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-HMONE-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCN_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-NCN-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BREATHE_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-BREATHE-5">[5]</a></sup> The band decided to sign with Sparrow Records instead of remaining independent, citing the talent at Sparrow could help foster their career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCN_4-9"><a href="#cite_note-NCN-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BREATHE_5-8"><a href="#cite_note-BREATHE-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tours">Tours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samestate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The band\'s first tour began in August 2011. Called "So Long Summer", it was with a band named Caleb (composed of Caleb and Will Chapman, who are sons of <a href="/wiki/Steven_Curtis_Chapman" title="Steven Curtis Chapman">Steven Curtis Chapman</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HMTWO_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-HMTWO-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Samestate\'s second outing was the "Rise Up" tour with <a href="/wiki/Family_Force_5" title="Family Force 5">Family Force 5</a> during March and April 2012. The "Radio Listener Appreciation" tour, with <a href="/wiki/Building_429" title="Building 429">Building 429</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andy_Cherry" title="Andy Cherry">Andy Cherry</a>, immediately followed in April and May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HMTHREE_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-HMTHREE-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discography">Discography</span><span
