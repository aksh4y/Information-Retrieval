blues</a> group originally from Edinburgh, now based in London.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Members"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Members</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#About"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">About</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Progression"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Progression</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Timeline"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Timeline</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li>Ed Cox - Lead vocals, piano</li>\n<li>Luke Bullard - Guitar</li>\n<li>Triss Duncan - Bass</li>\n<li>James Adam - Drums</li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About">About</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: About">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The four-piece band consists of a drummer, bassist, guitarist and keyboardist, with three members of the group singing. The band play 1950s <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a> and 1960s rhythm and blues with a modern twist covering various artists including <a href="/wiki/Ray_Charles" title="Ray Charles">Ray Charles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Feelgood_(band)" title="Dr. Feelgood (band)">Dr. Feelgood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Lee_Lewis" title="Jerry Lee Lewis">Jerry Lee Lewis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Coasters" title="The Coasters">The Coasters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muddy_Waters" title="Muddy Waters">Muddy Waters</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Progression">Progression</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Progression">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>After all meeting at school in 2004, the group began performing around Scotland, building up a following. What followed was national radio plays on XFM Scotland (now rebranded as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galaxy_Scotland" title="Galaxy Scotland">Galaxy Scotland</a>) and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Scotland" title="BBC Radio Scotland">BBC Radio Scotland</a> and articles have appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_List_(magazine)" title="The List (magazine)">The List</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fest_Magazine" title="Fest Magazine">Edinburgh Festival Magazine</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FestHurricanes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FestHurricanes-1">[1]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Evening_News" title="Edinburgh Evening News">Edinburgh Evening News</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scotsman" title="The Scotsman">The Scotsman</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheListHurricanes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheListHurricanes-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Skinny_(magazine)" title="The Skinny (magazine)">The Skinny</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WinterDanceParty_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WinterDanceParty-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2007 and 2008, The Fabulous Hurricanes played a string of shows most notably supporting <a href="/wiki/Neils_Children" title="Neils Children">Neils Children</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bombay_Bicycle_Club" title="Bombay Bicycle Club">Bombay Bicycle Club</a>, and they were voted number 4 on the list of <a class="external text" href="http://living.scotsman.com/features/Best-unsigned-band.3632119.jp" rel="nofollow">\'Scotland\'s Best Unsigned Bands\'</a> by <i>The Scotsman</i>. In 2008, the band were sent a letter by <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a> inviting them to his Liverpool concert after he heard their music, and in 2010 the band performed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitty_Daisy_%26_Lewis" title="Kitty Daisy &amp; Lewis">Kitty Daisy &amp; Lewis</a> to a sold-out crowd in Edinburgh. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheHurricanesWebsite_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheHurricanesWebsite-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="timeline-wrapper"><map id="timeline_0fde7b86a96851abfb221ea9d60d5c36" name="timeline_0fde7b86a96851abfb221ea9d60d5c36"></map><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/timeline/0fde7b86a96851abfb221ea9d60d5c36.png" usemap="#timeline_0fde7b86a96851abfb221ea9d60d5c36"/></div>\n<p><br/></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-FestHurricanes-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-FestHurricanes_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.festmag.co.uk/reviews/238-kitty_daisy_lewis" rel="nofollow">"The Hurricanes in Fest Magazine"</a>. <i>Fest</i><span class="reference-accessdate">.
