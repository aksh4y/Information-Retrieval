title="Soyuz spacecraft">Soyuz</a> (Russian \xd0\xa1\xd0\xbe\xd1\x8e\xd0\xb7 \xd0\xa2\xd0\x9c\xd0\x90-2, <i>Union TMA-2</i>) mission to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" The spacecraft docked with the ISS 2003-04-28 and undocked 2003-10-27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><b>Soyuz TMA-2</b> was the second flight for the <i>TMA</i> modification of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyuz_spacecraft" title="Soyuz spacecraft">Soyuz spacecraft</a>, and the 6th Soyuz to fly to the ISS.</p>\n<p>The commander is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuri_Ivanovich_Malenchenko" title="Yuri Ivanovich Malenchenko">Yuri Ivanovich Malenchenko</a> (Russia), and flight engineer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_Tsang_Lu" title="Edward Tsang Lu">Edward Tsang Lu</a> (USA), and after docking with the ISS they exchanged with the resident crew on ISS and became the seventh station crew, called "ISS Expedition Seven". <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_Kaleri" title="Alexander Kaleri">Alexander Kaleri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Foale" title="Michael Foale">Michael Foale</a> were assigned as the backup crew.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Crew"><span from ISS:</b> October 27, 2003, 23:17 UTC (from nadir port of <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">Zarya</a>)</li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_highlights">Mission highlights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soyuz_TMA-2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mission highlights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Originally the Soyuz missions to the ISS were all planned to be only taxi mission to deliver a new Soyuz spacecraft as the station\'s lifeboat every six month with a visiting crew, but not for crew exchange. Until the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Columbia_disaster" title="Space Shuttle Columbia disaster">Space Shuttle Columbia disaster</a> the same was planned for Soyuz TMA-2, a visiting crew consisting of commander <a href="/wiki/Gennady_Padalka" title="Gennady Padalka">Gennady Padalka</a> and ESA-astronaut <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Duque" title="Pedro Duque">Pedro Duque</a> were to spend about one week at the station and then return with the previous <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-1" title="Soyuz TMA-1">Soyuz TMA-1</a> spacecraft. The third seat might have gone to the <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/Klaus_von_Storch" title="Klaus von Storch">Klaus von Storch</a> as a Chilean space agency (Agencia Chilena del Espacio) cosmonaut, but even before the Columbia disaster it looked like his flight would not happen, and the seat would go to the Russian <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">cosmonaut</a> <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Kotov" title="Oleg Kotov">Oleg Kotov</a> or to deliver freight to the station.</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Soyuz_TMA-2_after_landing.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1853" data-file-width="2870" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Soyuz_TMA-2_after_landing.jpg/220px-Soyuz_TMA-2_after_landing.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Soyuz_TMA-2_after_landing.jpg/330px-Soyuz_TMA-2_after_landing.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Soyuz_TMA-2_after_landing.jpg/440px-Soyuz_TMA-2_after_landing.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Soyuz_TMA-2_after_landing.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSoyuz TMA-2 landing</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>During his stay on the station, Malenchenko became the first person to get married in space. His bride was in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> where long distance marriages are legal.</p>\n<p>The spacecraft returned to Earth on October 28, with both the "Expedition 7" crew as well as <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Duque" title="Pedro Duque">Pedro Duque</a> on board. Duque was launched with Soyuz TMA-3 and spent only one week on board of the ISS.</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soyuz_TMA-2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://spaceflight.nasa.gov/station/crew/exp7/index.html" rel="nofollow">"ISS Expedition 7 Crew"</a>.
