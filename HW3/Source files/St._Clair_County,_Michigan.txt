the United States">county</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a> bordering the west bank of the <a href="/wiki/St._Clair_River" title="St. Clair River">St. Clair River</a>. As of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2010" title="United States Census, 2010">2010 census</a>, the population was 163,040.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QF_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-QF-3">[3]</a></sup> It is the 13th-most populous county in the state. The <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> is <a href="/wiki/Port_Huron,_Michigan" title="Port Huron, Michigan">Port Huron</a>, located at the north end of the St. Clair River at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Huron" title="Lake Huron">Lake Huron</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clarke_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Clarke-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-4">[4]</a></sup> The county was created September 10, 1820, and its government was organized in 1821.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clarke_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Clarke-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Located northeast of Detroit, St. Clair County is part of the <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>-<a href="/wiki/Warren,_Michigan" title="Warren, Michigan">Warren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Dearborn,_Michigan" title="Dearborn, Michigan">Dearborn</a>, MI <a href="/wiki/Metro_Detroit" title="Metro Detroit">Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>. Geographically, it lies in <a href="/wiki/The_Thumb" title="The Thumb">the Thumb</a> area of eastern Michigan.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toclevel-1 tocsection-18"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">10</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Clair_County,_Michigan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>An expedition led by the French explorer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Robert_Cavelier,_Sieur_de_La_Salle" title="Ren\xc3\xa9 Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle">Ren\xc3\xa9 Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle</a> named the namesake lake as <i>Lac Sainte-Claire</i>, because the Roman Catholic men entered the lake on August 12, 1679, the <a href="/wiki/Calendar_of_saints" title="Calendar of saints">feast day</a> of <a href="/wiki/Clare_of_Assisi" title="Clare of Assisi">Sainte Clare of Assisi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clarke_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Clarke-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> English maps identified the lake feature on maps as early as 1710 as Saint Clare. By the <a href="/wiki/Mitchell_Map" title="Mitchell Map">Mitchell Map</a> of 1755, the spelling was given as St. Clair, which is the current version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Located along the western shores of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_Saint_Clair_(North_America)" title="Lake Saint Clair (North America)">Lake Saint Clair</a> and the St. Clair River, the county was named for them.</p>\n<p>The name is sometimes attributed to honoring <a href="/wiki/Arthur_St._Clair" title="Arthur St. Clair">Arthur St. Clair</a>, an <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> General and Governor of the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Territory" title="Northwest Territory">Northwest Territory</a>, but it was established with the current spelling long before St. Clair was considered a notable figure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The earlier spelling of the lake\'s name may have been conflated with that of the general, as several political jurisdictions near the lake and the river, such as St. Clair County, <a href="/wiki/St._Clair_Township,_Michigan" title="St. Clair Township, Michigan">St. Clair Township</a>, and the cities of <a href="/wiki/St._Clair,_Michigan" title="St. Clair, Michigan">St. Clair</a> and <a href="/wiki/St._Clair_Shores,_Michigan" title="St. Clair Shores, Michigan">St. Clair Shores</a>, share this spelling. <i>See also</i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Michigan_county_name_etymologies" title="List of Michigan county name etymologies">List of Michigan county name etymologies</a>.</p>\n<p>The name has sometimes been attributed to honoring <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Sinclair" title="Patrick Sinclair">Patrick Sinclair</a>, a British officer who purchased land on the <a href="/wiki/St._Clair_River" title="St. Clair River">St. Clair River</a> at the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Pine_River_(Michigan)" title="Pine River (Michigan)">Pine River</a>. In 1764, he built <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Sinclair&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fort Sinclair (page does not exist)">Fort Sinclair</a> there, which was in use for nearly 20 years before being abandoned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Clair_County,_Michigan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:121px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Blue_Water_Michigan_Map.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="164" data-file-width="119" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/b7/Blue_Water_Michigan_Map.jpg" width="119"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Blue_Water_Michigan_Map.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSt. Clair County is also called the Blue Water Area.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the county has a total area of 837 square miles (2,170\xc2\xa0km<sup>2</sup>), of which 721 square miles (1,870\xc2\xa0km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 115 square miles (300\xc2\xa0km<sup>2</sup>) (14%) is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-9">[9]</a></sup> St. Clair County is one of five counties that form the peninsula, known as <a href="/wiki/The_Thumb" title="The Thumb">the Thumb</a>, that projects into <a href="/wiki/Lake_Huron" title="Lake Huron">Lake Huron</a>. St. Clair County is closely connected in terms of economy with its neighbors, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Detroit" title="Metropolitan Detroit">Metropolitan Detroit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sanilac_County,_Michigan" title="Sanilac County, Michigan">Sanilac County</a> in Michigan, and <a href="/wiki/Lambton_County" title="Lambton County">Lambton County</a> across the river in <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. Saint Clair County is the principal county in <a href="/wiki/The_Thumb#The_Blue_Water_Area" title="The Thumb">The Blue Water Area</a>, a sub-region of the Thumb.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adjacent_counties">Adjacent counties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Clair_County,_Michigan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Adjacent counties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Sanilac_County,_Michigan" title="Sanilac County, Michigan">Sanilac County</a> (north)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Lapeer_County,_Michigan" title="Lapeer County, Michigan">Lapeer County</a> (west)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Macomb_County,_Michigan" title="Macomb County, Michigan">Macomb County</a> (south)</li>\n<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lambton_County,_Ontario" title="Lambton County, Ontario">Lambton County, Ontario</a> (east)</li>\n</ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_highways">Major highways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Clair_County,_Michigan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Major highways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul>\n<li><img alt="" data-file-height="601" data-file-width="601" height="20" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/I-69.svg/20px-I-69.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/I-69.svg/30px-I-69.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/I-69.svg/40px-I-69.svg.png 2x" width="20"/> <span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Interstate_69_in_Michigan" title="Interstate 69 in Michigan">I-69</a></span> enters the county from the west, coming from Lansing and Flint, terminating at the approach to the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Water_Bridge" title="Blue Water Bridge">Blue Water Bridge</a> in Port Huron. a 2010 population of 163,040. This is a decrease of -1,195 people from the <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" title="2000 United States Census">2000 United States Census</a>. Overall, the county had a -0.7% growth rate during this ten-year period. In 2010 there were 63,841 households and 44,238 families in the county. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 226.1 per square mile (87.3 square kilometers). There were 71,822 housing units at an average density of 99.6 per square mile (38.5 square kilometers). 93.9% were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_American" title="White American">White</a>, 2.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">Black or African American</a>, 0.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_American" title="Asian American">Asian</a>, 0.4% <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a>, 0.7% of some other race and 2.0% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiracial_American" title="Multiracial American">of two or more races</a>. 2.9% were <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="Hispanic and Latino Americans">Hispanic or Latino</a> (of any race). 25.9% identified as of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_American" title="German American">German</a>, 10.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish_American" title="Polish American">Polish</a>, 9.3% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_American" title="Irish American">Irish</a>, 8.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_American" title="English American">English</a>, 6.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_American" title="French American">French</a>, 6.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_ethnicity" title="American ethnicity">American</a>, and 5.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_American" title="Italian American">Italian</a> ancestry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmFactFinder_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-AmFactFinder-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<p>There were 63,841 households out of which 31.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 52.9% were <a href="/wiki/Family" title="Family">husband and wife families</a>, 11.4% had a female householder with no husband present, 30.7% were non-families, and 25.5% were made up of individuals. The average household size was 2.52 and the average family size was 3.01.</p>\n<p>In the county, the population was spread out with 23.7% under age of 18, 8.0% from 18 to 24, 23.8% from 25 to 44, 30.1% from 45 to 64, and 14.5% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 41 years. For every 100 females there were 98.1 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 95.7 males.</p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/American_Community_Survey" title="American Community Survey">2010 American Community Survey 1-year estimate</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmFactFinder_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-AmFactFinder-15">[15]</a></sup> indicates the median income for a household in the county was $44,369 and the median income for a family was $53,207. Males had a median income of $30,056 versus $16,771 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the county was $22,390.
