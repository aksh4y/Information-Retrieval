Moore Jr.</td>\n<td>Interim President &amp; CEO</td>\n<td>2013\xe2\x80\x932016</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td></td>\n<td>President</td>\n<td>2016 -</td>\n<td></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The institution was founded on September 18, 1935 as the <b>Norfolk Unit of <a href="/wiki/Virginia_State_University" title="Virginia State University">Virginia State University</a></b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AboutNSU_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-AboutNSU-5">[5]</a></sup> Eighty-five students attended the first classes held in 1935. Mr. Samuel Fischer Scott, an alumnus of Virginia Union and <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_Virginia" title="Portsmouth, Virginia">Portsmouth</a> native, served as the first director with the primary focus of maintaining the solvency of the school. Dr. Lyman Beecher Brooks, a Virginia Union alumnus, succeeded Mr. Scott as director in 1938, and served as provost, 1963\xe2\x80\x931969, and the first president 1969\xe2\x80\x931975.</p>\n<p>In 1942, the school became independent of VSU and was named <b>Norfolk Polytechnic College</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AboutNSU_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-AboutNSU-5">[5]</a></sup> Within two years, by an act of the Virginia Legislature, it became a part of Virginia State College (now <a href="/wiki/Virginia_State_University" title="Virginia State University">Virginia State University</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AboutNSU_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-AboutNSU-5">[5]</a></sup> By 1950, the 15th anniversary of the college founding, the faculty had grown to fifty and the student enrollment to 1,018. In 1952, the college\'s athletic teams adopted the "Spartan" name and identity.</p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">City of Norfolk</a> provided a permanent site for the college on Corprew Avenue, and in 1955 Brown Hall, formerly Tidewater Hall, opened as the first permanent building on the new campus. In 1956 the future Norfolk State College granted its first bachelor\'s degrees.</p>\n<p>In 1969, the college divided from Virginia State College and was named <b>Norfolk State College</b>. The college was issued accreditation from the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Association_of_Colleges_and_Schools" title="Southern Association of Colleges and Schools">Southern Association of Colleges and Schools</a> the same year with an enrollment of 5,400 students. In 1975 and the year following, the first master\'s degrees were awarded in Communications and Social Work, respectively. Dr. Harrison Benjamin Wilson, Jr., in 1975, succeeded Dr. Lyman Beecher Brooks as President after 37 years.</p>\n<p>When the college was granted university status in 1979 by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Assembly_of_Virginia" title="General Assembly of Virginia">General Assembly of Virginia</a>, it changed its name to Norfolk State University.</p>\n<p>Norfolk State University celebrated its 50th anniversary in 1985 with a year of observances and with an enrollment of 7,200. In 1995 Norfolk State University\'s enrollment reached 9,112.</p>\n<p>Upon the retirement of Dr. Harrison Benjamin Wilson in 1997, Dr. Marie Valentine McDemmond, became NSU\'s third President in 1997 and served until her retirement. Dr. Alvin J. Schexnider became interim president in July 2005. <a href="/wiki/Carolyn_Meyers" title="Carolyn Meyers">Dr. Carolyn Winstead Meyers</a> was selected as the fourth President and began service on July 1, 2006. <a href="/wiki/Tony_Atwater" title="Tony Atwater">Dr. Tony Atwater</a> was announced as the new president in 2011 becoming the fifth president and served until he was removed by the board of visitors of Norfolk State University on August 23, 2013. Dr. Sandra DeLoatch the Provost and President of Academic Affairs was named acting president effective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norfolk_State_President_Atwater_Fired_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Norfolk_State_President_Atwater_Fired-6">[6]</a></sup> On September 13, 2013, Eddie N. Moore Jr. was appointed interim president of Norfolk State University and started serving in that capacity on September 23, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interim_President_Moore_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Interim_President_Moore-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In December 2013, the university was placed on probation by its <a href="/wiki/Regional_accreditation" title="Regional accreditation">regional accreditor</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Association_of_Colleges_and_Schools" title="Southern Association of Colleges and Schools">Southern Association of Colleges and Schools</a>, for "financial and governance issues."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The probation was lifted two years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Eddie Moore, Jr. became the 6th president of Norfolk State University on January 10, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a
