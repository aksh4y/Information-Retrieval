colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Agnes</b> was, at the time, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_costliest_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of costliest Atlantic hurricanes">costliest hurricane</a> to hit the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in <a href="/wiki/Recorded_history" title="Recorded history">recorded history</a>. The second <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> and first <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> of the <a href="/wiki/1972_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1972 Atlantic hurricane season">1972 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Agnes developed on June\xc2\xa014 from the interaction of a <a href="/wiki/Polar_front" title="Polar front">polar front</a> and an upper <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</a>. Initially forming as a tropical depression, the storm headed slowly eastward and emerged into the western <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> on June\xc2\xa015. Once in the Caribbean, the depression began to strengthen, and by the following day, it became Tropical Storm Agnes. Thereafter, Agnes slowly curved northward and passed just west of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> on June\xc2\xa017. Early on June\xc2\xa018, the storm intensified enough to be upgraded to Hurricane Agnes. Heading northward, the hurricane eventually made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Panama_City,_Florida" title="Panama City, Florida">Panama City, Florida</a> late on June\xc2\xa019. After moving inland, Agnes rapidly weakened and was only a tropical depression when it entered <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>. The weakening trend halted as the storm crossed over Georgia and into <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>. While over eastern <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, Agnes re-strengthened into a tropical storm on June\xc2\xa021, as a result of <a href="/wiki/Baroclinity" title="Baroclinity">baroclinic activity</a>. Early the following day, the storm emerged into the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> before re-curving northwestward and making landfall near <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> as a strong tropical storm. Agnes quickly became an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> on June\xc2\xa023, and tracked to the northwest of Great Britain before becoming absorbed by another cyclone on July\xc2\xa06.</p>\n<p>Though it moved slowly across the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula, damage in Mexico is unknown. Although the storm bypassed the tip of Cuba, heavy rainfall occurred, killing seven people. In Florida, Agnes caused a significant tornado outbreak, with at least 26\xc2\xa0confirmed twisters, two of which were spawned in Georgia. The tornadoes and two initially unconfirmed tornadoes in Florida alone resulted in over $4.5\xc2\xa0million (1972\xc2\xa0USD) in damage and six fatalities. At least 2,082\xc2\xa0structures in Florida suffered either major damage or were destroyed. About\xc2\xa01,355 other dwellings experienced minor losses. Though Agnes made landfall as a hurricane, no hurricane-force winds were reported. Along the coast abnormally high tides resulted in extensive damage, especially between <a href="/wiki/Apalachicola,_Florida" title="Apalachicola, Florida">Apalachicola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Key,_Florida" title="Cedar Key, Florida">Cedar Key</a>. Light to moderate rainfall was reported in Florida, though no significant flooding occurred. In Georgia, damage was limited to two tornadoes, which caused approximately $275,000 in losses. Minimal effects were also recorded in <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>; though one fatality was reported in Delaware. The most significant effects, by far, occurred in <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, mostly due to intense flooding. The hurricane severely flooded the <a href="/wiki/Susquehanna_River" title="Susquehanna River">Susquehanna River</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lackawanna_River" title="Lackawanna River">Lackawanna River</a> causing major damage to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wilkes-Barre/Scranton_metropolitan_area" title="Wilkes-Barre/Scranton metropolitan area">Wilkes-Barre/Scranton metropolitan area</a>. In both Pennsylvania and <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> combined, about 43,594 structures were either destroyed or significantly damaged. In Canada, a mobile home was toppled, killing two people. Overall, Agnes caused 128\xc2\xa0fatalities and nearly $3\xc2\xa0billion in damage,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> though more recently, it is estimated that there were $2.1\xc2\xa0billion in losses associated with the storm. Due to the significant effects, the name <i>Agnes</i> was retired in the spring of 1973.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">tropical cyclogenesis</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. Banded convection developed in the northwestern Caribbean Sea by June\xc2\xa011, though the system did not significantly organize. After an upper trough moved east, wind shear decreased, causing lower <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressures</a> observations in <a href="/wiki/Cozumel" title="Cozumel">Cozumel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>, Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> It is estimated that a tropical depression developed by 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa014, while centered over the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula, about 78 miles (126\xc2\xa0km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida,_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="M\xc3\xa9rida, Yucat\xc3\xa1n">M\xc3\xa9rida, Yucat\xc3\xa1n</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-besttrack_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-besttrack-3">[3]</a></sup> The depression tracked eastward and entered the western Caribbean Sea on June\xc2\xa015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Operationally, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> did not initiate advisories on the depression until 1500\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Early on June\xc2\xa016 at 0000\xc2\xa0UTC, the depression strengthened into Tropical Storm Agnes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-besttrack_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-besttrack-3">[3]</a></sup> However, the depression was not operationally upgraded until sixteen hours later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After becoming a tropical storm on June\xc2\xa016, Agnes slowly curved northward and approached the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Channel" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Channel">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Channel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-besttrack_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-besttrack-3">[3]</a></sup> Late on June\xc2\xa017, it was noted that projected path indicated the possibly of landfall in western Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, the storm remained offshore, though it closely brushed the western tip of Cuba. At 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa018, Agnes intensified into a hurricane while in the southeastern Gulf of Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-besttrack_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-besttrack-3">[3]</a></sup> Prematurely, the National Hurricane Center operationally upgraded Agnes to a hurricane at 0200\xc2\xa0UTC on that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Upon becoming a hurricane, Agnes attained its maximum sustained winds of 85\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h), though it had not reached its minimum atmospheric pressure. Due to unfavorable conditions, Agnes leveled-off slightly in intensity and weakened to a minimal hurricane while approaching the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="Gulf Coast of the United States">Gulf Coast of the United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Shortly before 2200\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa019, Agnes made landfall near Cape San Blas, Florida with winds of 75\xc2\xa0mph (120\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-advisory14_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-advisory14-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Agnes2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="512" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Agnes2.jpg/250px-Agnes2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Agnes2.jpg/375px-Agnes2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Agnes2.jpg/500px-Agnes2.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Agnes2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRemnants of Agnes over northeastern United States</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa020, only a few hours after moving inland, Agnes weakened to a tropical storm. After crossing the Florida/<a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>/Georgia stateline, Agnes rapidly weakened to a tropical depression. While over Georgia, the depression curved northeastward and eventually to the east-northeast after entering South Carolina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Though the storm had not dissipated, the National Hurricane Center issued its final bulletin on Agnes at 1600\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> By early on June\xc2\xa021, a large extratropical trough spawned a low pressure area, which resulted in baroclinic activity. As a result, Agnes restrengthened into a tropical storm at 1800\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa021, while centered over eastern North Carolina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Three hours later, the National Hurricane Center noted decreasing atmospheric pressures, and indicated that winds had reached gale-force winds and once again upgraded Agnes to a tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> By early on June\xc2\xa022, Agnes emerged into the northwestern Atlantic Ocean, where it continued to re-intensify. At 1200\xc2\xa0UTC, Agnes reached its minimum atmospheric pressure of 977\xc2\xa0mbar (28.9\xc2\xa0inHg), as reported by a reconnaissance aircraft. However, maximum sustained winds were at only 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-besttrack_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-besttrack-3">[3]</a></sup> Late on June\xc2\xa022, Agnes made its final landfall on <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach,_New_York" title="Long Beach, New York">Long Beach, New York</a>, on <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, with winds of 65\xc2\xa0mph (100\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Agnes began its long life as an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> once cold air invaded its circulation late on June\xc2\xa022. The system looped across south-central Pennsylvania on June\xc2\xa023 and then looped across southern Ontario on June\xc2\xa025. It spawned a tornado in Maniwaki, Ontario early that day, which killed two and injured 11\xc2\xa0people. The cyclone sped east-northeast, reaching <a href="/wiki/Cape_Breton_(Nova_Scotia)" title="Cape Breton (Nova Scotia)">Cape Breton</a> on June\xc2\xa027 before intensifying as it re-entered the North Atlantic. After moving as far north as the 61st\xc2\xa0parallel while crossing the 25th\xc2\xa0meridian west on the morning of June\xc2\xa029, Agnes turned southeastward. Its central pressure bottomed out near 980 millibars (29\xc2\xa0inHg) towards 0000 UTC on June\xc2\xa030. Between July\xc2\xa01 and July\xc2\xa03, the long-lived system looped between Ireland and Iceland while weakening once more. The system turned northeast, strengthening one more time while accelerating across the <a href="/wiki/Hebrides" title="Hebrides">Hebrides</a> on July\xc2\xa04 through 5. A stronger system moving in from the west finally absorbed Agnes on July\xc2\xa06 while finally located to the southeast of Iceland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>By 2200\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa017, the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> issued gale warnings and a hurricane watch for the <a href="/wiki/Straits_of_Florida" title="Straits of Florida">Straits of Florida</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Florida Keys</a> from <a href="/wiki/Key_West" title="Key West">Key West</a> to <a href="/wiki/Dry_Tortugas" title="Dry Tortugas">Dry Tortugas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On the following day at 1600\xc2\xa0UTC, another hurricane watch was put into effective from <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Key,_Florida" title="Cedar Key, Florida">Cedar Key</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">Pensacola</a>. In addition, the gale warnings in the Florida Keys were extended to include areas from <a href="/wiki/Fort_Myers_Beach,_Florida" title="Fort Myers Beach, Florida">Fort Myers Beach</a> to <a href="/wiki/Clearwater,_Florida" title="Clearwater, Florida">Clearwater</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> At 2200\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa018, a hurricane warning became effective from <a href="/wiki/St._Marks,_Florida" title="St. Marks, Florida">St. Marks</a> to <a href="/wiki/Panama_City,_Florida" title="Panama City, Florida">Panama City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The gale warnings which were in effect for the Florida Keys and Fort Myers Beach to Clearwater was discontinued at 1000\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It is likely that the hurricane warning was discontinued after the National Hurricane Center downgraded Agnes to a tropical storm at 2200\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-advisory14_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-advisory14-8">[8]</a></sup> Two hours later, all gale warnings along the West Coast of Florida were discontinued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1972_Hurricane_Agnes_tornado_outbreak" title="1972 Hurricane Agnes tornado outbreak">1972 Hurricane Agnes tornado outbreak</a></div>\n<p>In the United States, Agnes affected 15\xc2\xa0states and the capital area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." title="Washington D.C.">Washington D.C.</a>. Almost 110,000\xc2\xa0houses were impacted by Agnes, 3,351\xc2\xa0of which were destroyed. In addition, 5,211\xc2\xa0mobile homes were either damaged or completely destroyed. Farm buildings and small businesses also suffered extensively, with 2,226 and 5,842 structures experiencing major losses or were destroyed. Of the 128\xc2\xa0fatalities caused by Agnes, 119\xc2\xa0occurred in the United States. All of the damage estimates associated with Agnes were within the country.</p>\n<p>Agnes was, at the time, the costliest hurricane in the history of the United States, though it has since been surpassed by numerous other storms.</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Upon landfall, Agnes produced abnormally high tides along much of the Florida coastline. The highest tides reported were at <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Key,_Florida" title="Cedar Key, Florida">Cedar Key</a>, reaching 7 feet (2.1\xc2\xa0m) above normal sea levels. The second largest waves were 6.4 feet (2.0\xc2\xa0m), recorded at <a href="/wiki/Apalachicola,_Florida" title="Apalachicola, Florida">Apalachicola</a>. In contrast, few observations of high tides along the east coast of Florida exist; the highest reported was 3 feet (0.91\xc2\xa0m) above average in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jacksonville" title="Jacksonville">Jacksonville</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Alligator_Point,_Florida" title="Alligator Point, Florida">Alligator Point</a>, at least 16\xc2\xa0homes were swept off their foundations. Six bridges connecting Cedar Key to the mainland areas of Florida were submerged. In addition, a bridge from Coquina Key to <a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" title="St. Petersburg, Florida">St. Petersburg</a> was also underwater due to high tides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pp_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-pp-18">[18]</a></sup> Nearly the entire state of Florida reported rainfall, though it was usually in the light to moderate range. Among the highest amounts of precipitation recorded were 8.97 inches (228\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Naples,_Florida" title="Naples, Florida">Naples</a>, 8.43 inches (214\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Big_Pine_Key,_Florida" title="Big Pine Key, Florida">Big Pine Key</a>, 7.3 inches (190\xc2\xa0mm) in the <a href="/wiki/Everglades" title="Everglades">Everglades</a>, and 7.17 inches (182\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Tallahassee,_Florida" title="Tallahassee, Florida">Tallahassee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Near <a href="/wiki/Okeechobee,_Florida" title="Okeechobee, Florida">Okeechobee</a>, larger amounts of rainfall may have occurred, though there were no specific observations in that vicinity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-19">[19]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Agnes_effects_in_Key_West_MM00001844x_(15293230179).jpg" landfall as a hurricane, no reports of hurricane-force winds exist. However, several locations observed tropical storm force winds. The highest sustained winds and gusts were 52 and 69\xc2\xa0mph (84 and 111\xc2\xa0km/h), both recorded at the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">Kennedy Space Center</a>. Other reports of at least tropical storm force sustained winds were at 39\xc2\xa0mph (63\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Crestview,_Florida" title="Crestview, Florida">Crestview</a> and Jacksonville, 42\xc2\xa0mph (68\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flamingo,_Florida" title="Flamingo, Florida">Flamingo</a>, 43\xc2\xa0mph (69\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">Key West</a>, and two observations of 40\xc2\xa0mph (64\xc2\xa0km/h) in Panama City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Throughout the state, Agnes spawned at least 15\xc2\xa0tornadoes, while several tornadoes were operationally unconfirmed. Initially referred to as a "windstorm", one of those twisters destroyed 50\xc2\xa0mobile homes and a fishing camp in <a href="/wiki/Okeechobee,_Florida" title="Okeechobee, Florida">Okeechobee</a>, as well as cause six fatalities. Another significant tornado occurred in <a href="/wiki/Cape_Canaveral,_Florida" title="Cape Canaveral, Florida">Cape Canaveral</a>, which destroyed two homes and 30\xc2\xa0trailers; it also damaged 20\xc2\xa0houses and the <a href="/wiki/Port_Canaveral" title="Port Canaveral">Port Canaveral</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">Coast Guard</a> station. Overall, more than 100\xc2\xa0people were left homeless and caused 23 injuries and over $500,000 in damage. The costliest tornado was also spawned in <a href="/wiki/Brevard_County,_Florida" title="Brevard County, Florida">Brevard County</a> and it destroyed 44\xc2\xa0planes at the <a href="/wiki/Merritt_Island_Airport" title="Merritt Island Airport">Merritt Island Airport</a> and an apartment building. In addition, several houses in a nearby subdivision were also damaged. Losses from this tornado are estimated at $3\xc2\xa0million.</p>\n<p>Due to a combination of high tides, rainfall, winds, and tornadoes, 96\xc2\xa0dwellings were destroyed, while about 1,802\xc2\xa0suffered damage to some degree. The destruction of 177\xc2\xa0mobile homes was reported and 374\xc2\xa0others were significantly damaged. Furthermore, 988\xc2\xa0small businesses in the state were either destroyed or had major damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table2_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-table2-20">[20]</a></sup> Eight <a href="/wiki/Pinellas_County,_Florida" title="Pinellas County, Florida">Pinellas</a>, $7.1\xc2\xa0million in <a href="/wiki/Sarasota_County,_Florida" title="Sarasota County, Florida">Sarasota</a>, $4.1\xc2\xa0million in Brevard, $3.1\xc2\xa0million in <a href="/wiki/Pasco_County,_Florida" title="Pasco County, Florida">Pasco</a>, $2\xc2\xa0million in <a href="/wiki/Manatee_County,_Florida" title="Manatee County, Florida">Manatee</a>, $1.4\xc2\xa0million in <a href="/wiki/Wakulla_County,_Florida" title="Wakulla County, Florida">Wakulla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Franklin_County,_Florida" title="Franklin County, Florida">Franklin</a>, $1.3\xc2\xa0million in <a href="/wiki/Monroe_County,_Florida" title="Monroe County, Florida">Monroe</a>, and $1\xc2\xa0million in losses in <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_County,_Florida" title="Hillsborough County, Florida">Hillsborough Counties</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MCD_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-MCD-21">[21]</a></sup> Florida, impact was relatively minor. Rainfall was generally light in Georgia, though peaking at 8.55 inches (217\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Brunswick,_Georgia" title="Brunswick, Georgia">Brunswick</a>. Other locations that reported precipitations include 4.54 inches (115\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Albany,_Georgia" title="Albany, Georgia">Albany</a>, 3.95 inches (100\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah</a>, and 3.18 inches (81\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Macon,_Georgia" title="Macon, Georgia">Macon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> During the tornado outbreak, two of the tornadoes were spawned in Georgia. The tornado in <a href="/wiki/Pierce_County,_Georgia" title="Pierce County, Georgia">Pierce County</a> caused $25,000 in damage and one injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PCtornado_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-PCtornado-23">[23]</a></sup> Losses were more significant from the twister in <a href="/wiki/Coffee_County,_Georgia" title="Coffee County, Georgia">Coffee County</a>, reaching $250,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCtornado_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCtornado-24">[24]</a></sup> One mobile home in Georgia suffered significant damage, though it is unknown if this was related to the tornadoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table2_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-table2-20">[20]</a></sup> Though the damage from both tornadoes combined was about $275,000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PCtornado_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-PCtornado-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCtornado_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-CCtornado-24">[24]</a></sup> the National Hurricane Center notes only $205,000 in losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table1_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-table1-22">[22]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Alabama, tides up to 1.3 feet (0.40\xc2\xa0m) above normal were reported in the <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Bay" title="Mobile Bay">Mobile Bay area</a>. Though rainfall only reached 3.93 inches (100\xc2\xa0mm) and 1.08 inches (27\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Dothan,_Alabama" title="Dothan, Alabama">Dothan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-19">[19]</a></sup> precipitation from the storm in the state of Alabama peaked at 7.67 inches (195\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Headland,_Alabama" title="Headland, Alabama">Headland</a>. Damage in the state was minor, though low-lying streets in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Shores,_Alabama" title="Gulf Shores, Alabama">Gulf Shores</a> were flooded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In some areas of South Carolina, the storm dropped as much as 9.5 inches (240\xc2\xa0mm) of rain in less than 36\xc2\xa0hours. As a result, several rivers flooded. Along the <a href="/wiki/Pee_Dee_River" title="Pee Dee River">Pee Dee River</a>, pastures, small grains, and soy bean crops near <a href="/wiki/Cheraw,_South_Carolina" title="Cheraw, South Carolina">Cheraw</a> suffered major damage; similar effects occurred along the <a href="/wiki/Congaree_River" title="Congaree River">Congaree River</a> near <a href="/wiki/Columbia,_South_Carolina" title="Columbia, South Carolina">Columbia</a>, though damage was lesser. In <a href="/wiki/Greenville,_South_Carolina" title="Greenville, South Carolina">Greenville</a>, damage to basements, streets, and water systems occurred after the <a href="/wiki/Reedy_River" title="Reedy River">Reedy River</a> overflowed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-26">[26]</a></sup> Damage in South Carolina was minor, totaling to only $50,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table1_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-table1-22">[22]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Effects were very minimal in Tennessee and limited to light rainfall in the eastern portions of the state, generally reaching no more than 3 inches (76\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-19">[19]</a></sup> The highest amount of precipitation recorded was 4.59 inches (117\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Unicoi,_Tennessee" title="Unicoi, Tennessee">Unicoi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCSE_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPCSE-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Virginia">Virginia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Virginia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Richmond_Rooftops_(7790613150).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4350" class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Richmond_Rooftops_(7790613150).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nBuildings almost fully submerged in Richmond</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond</a>, four people drowned after their car plunged into the swollen <a href="/wiki/James_River" title="James River">James River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tws_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-tws-28">[28]</a></sup> A train bound for Washington D.C. stopped due to flooding in Richmond, which temporarily stranded 537 passengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mh0623p20-a_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-mh0623p20-a-29">[29]</a></sup> The Peak Creek in western Virginia overflowed its banks, flooding a low-income housing area of <a href="/wiki/Pulaski,_Virginia" title="Pulaski, Virginia">Pulaski</a> with water up to rooftops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> At the height of the flooding, over 600 miles (970\xc2\xa0km) of highways were submerged, resulting in $14.8 million in damage to roads in the state. Severe damage also occurred to sewer and water facilities, totaling to $34.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> 95 houses were destroyed and 4,393 others were damaged, while 125 mobile homes were destroyed and another were significantly affected. Additionally, 205 small businesses were either damaged or destroyed. The <a href="/wiki/Interstate_95_in_Virginia" title="Interstate 95 in Virginia">Interstate\xc2\xa095</a> (I-95) Purple Heart Bridge over northern Virginia\'s <a href="/wiki/Occoquan_River" title="Occoquan River">Occoquan River</a> was severely damaged and closed when rammed by a large barge carried by floodwaters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Capital_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Capital-32">[32]</a></sup> In the Washington DC Virginia suburbs the Lake Barcroft dam was undermined and breached, draining the entire lake, previously the reservoir for Alexandria, VA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lbwid_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-lbwid-33">[33]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Overall, flooding was described as "the worst in 50 years".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tws_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-tws-28">[28]</a></sup> In Virginia alone, 13 fatalities and $125.9 million in losses were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table1_22-4"><a href="#cite_note-table1-22">[22]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Maryland">Maryland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Maryland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore</a>, three children drowned when their mother\'s car was swept off a highway. However, the mother survived, and was rescued by firemen using rowboats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In the state of Maryland, damage totaled to $110\xc2\xa0million and 19\xc2\xa0fatalities were reported.</p>\n<p>Hardest hit was the <a href="/wiki/Patapsco_River" title="Patapsco River">Patapsco River</a> valley, with widespread destruction of buildings, roads, and railroads in the state park, at <a href="/wiki/Daniels,_Maryland" title="Daniels, Maryland">Daniels</a> including its mill,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> at <a href="/wiki/Ellicott_City,_Maryland" title="Ellicott City, Maryland">Ellicott City</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oella,_Maryland" title="Oella, Maryland">Oella</a>. The flooding was first reported in the early morning hours of June 22 and water was reported as high as 40 feet above normal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> River Road along the Patapsco was almost completely washed out. More than 900 people were evacuated from their homes. National guard helicopters were used to rescue workmen from the roof of the Daniels\xe2\x80\x99 plant. In Elkridge, a family of six was forced from their home and they attempted to reach high ground in a small boat. It capsized and all six held onto the boat for several hours until it reached more shallow water.</p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Howard_County,_Maryland" title="Howard County, Maryland">Howard County</a>, a total of 704 county residents were left homeless. More than 80 homes in the Ellicott City area were damaged and 72 homes in Elkridge were affected. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laurel,_MD" title="Laurel, MD">Laurel</a>, the 9th Street Bridge crossing the <a href="/wiki/Patuxent_River" title="Patuxent River">Patuxent River</a> was washed away by flood waters. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anne_Arundel_County" title="Anne Arundel County">Anne Arundel County</a>, all roads linking the county with Baltimore city or county were closed (including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltimore_Beltway" title="Baltimore Beltway">Baltimore Beltway</a>) as were all roads "near the Patuxent River" including <a href="/wiki/Waysons_Corner,_Maryland" title="Waysons Corner, Maryland">Waysons Corner</a> where over 300 homes were evacuated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Capital_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-Capital-32">[32]</a></sup> The Patapsco flooded residential homes in parts of the county\'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linthicum_Heights" title="Linthicum Heights">North Linthicum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pumphrey,_Maryland" title="Pumphrey, Maryland">Pumphrey</a>, and Belle Grove Road, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Park,_Maryland" title="Brooklyn Park, Maryland">Brooklyn Park</a> neighborhoods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Capital_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-Capital-32">[32]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Gwynn_Oak_Park" title="Gwynn Oak Park">Gwynn Oak Amusement Park</a> closed after suffering severe damage from flooding when Hurricane Agnes caused <a href="/wiki/Gwynns_Falls" title="Gwynns Falls">Gwynns Falls</a> to overflow. In 1974, the park\'s rides were auctioned off. The carousel was moved and is still in operation on the <a href="/wiki/National_Mall" title="National Mall">National Mall</a> in Washington, D.C.</p>\n<p>A popular footbridge in the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_and_Ohio_Canal_National_Historical_Park" title="Chesapeake and Ohio Canal National Historical Park">Chesapeake and Ohio Canal National Historical Park</a> which led across a branch of the Potomac River to an overlook of <a href="/wiki/Great_Falls_(Potomac_River)" title="Great Falls (Potomac River)">Great Falls</a> was washed away and not rebuilt until 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As a result of Agnes\' rains, <a href="/wiki/Conowingo_Dam" title="Conowingo Dam">Conowingo Dam</a>, astride the <a href="/wiki/Susquehanna_River" title="Susquehanna River">Susquehanna River</a> just north of the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">Chesapeake Bay</a>, recorded its all-time highest flow rate and stream heights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crest_history_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-crest_history-38">[38]</a></sup> Before the river crested, the water came within feet of overtopping the dam. As the dam\'s normal flood control devices seemed unable to cope, engineers had placed charges to blow out a section of the dam to prevent a catastrophic failure.</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Pennsylvania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>In Pennsylvania, heavy rainfall was reported, with much of the state experiencing more than 7 inches (180\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation. Furthermore, a large swath of rainfall exceeding 10 inches (250\xc2\xa0mm) was reported in the central part of the state. Overall, the rains peaked at 19 inches (480\xc2\xa0mm) in the western portions of <a href="/wiki/Schuylkill_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Schuylkill County, Pennsylvania">Schuylkill County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-19">[19]</a></sup> As a result, Agnes is listed as the wettest tropical cyclone on record for the state of Pennsylvania. Overall, more than 100,000\xc2\xa0people were forced to leave their homes due to flooding. Some buildings were under 13 feet (4.0\xc2\xa0m) of water in <a href="/wiki/Harrisburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Harrisburg, Pennsylvania">Harrisburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mh0624p20-a_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-mh0624p20-a-39">[39]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Governor%27s_Residence" title="Pennsylvania Governor\'s Residence">Governor\'s Mansion</a>, the first floor was submerged by flood waters. Hundreds were trapped in their homes in <a href="/wiki/Wilkes-Barre,_Pennsylvania" title="Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania">Wilkes-Barre</a> due to the overflowing Susquehanna River. At the historic cemetery in <a href="/wiki/Forty_Fort,_Pennsylvania" title="Forty Fort, Pennsylvania">Forty Fort</a>, 2,000\xc2\xa0caskets were washed away, leaving body parts on porches, roofs, and in basements. In <a href="/wiki/Luzerne_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Luzerne County, Pennsylvania">Luzerne County</a> alone, 25,000\xc2\xa0homes and businesses were either damaged or destroyed. Losses in that county totaled to $1\xc2\xa0billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TTL_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-TTL-40">[40]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Chadds_Ford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Chadds Ford Township, Delaware County, Pennsylvania">Chadds Ford Township</a> in <a href="/wiki/Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Delaware County, Pennsylvania">Delaware County</a>, the Brandywine Creek crested at 16.5 feet (5.0\xc2\xa0m), sending flood waters into the city. Water poured into the first floor of an art museum in Chadds Ford Township, which threatened at least $2.5\xc2\xa0million in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N.C._Wyeth" title="N.C. Wyeth">N.C. Wyeth</a> paintings, though they were quickly moved to the upper floors. Along the <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_River" title="Allegheny River">Allegheny River</a>, it was above flood stage in some low-lying areas. During the height of the storm, the river was rising at about 7 inches (180\xc2\xa0mm) per hour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mh0624p20-a_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-mh0624p20-a-39">[39]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Pennsylvania" title="Reading, Pennsylvania">Reading</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Schuylkill_River" title="Schuylkill River">Schuylkill River</a> reached a record flood of 31.5 feet. Hundreds of people were evacuated and over a hundred homes destroyed. Floods reached as far inland as 3rd street in the heart of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BerksTimeTrain_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-BerksTimeTrain-41">[41]</a></sup></p>\n<p>More than one hundred Harrisburg YMCA campers and staff were evacuated using two CH-47 Chinook helicopters flown by the <a href="/wiki/National_Guard_of_the_United_States" title="National Guard of the United States">National Guard</a> at Camp Shikellimy located downstream of DeHart Dam in Middle Paxton Township. Additionally, 36\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Girl_Scouts_of_the_USA" title="Girl Scouts of the USA">Girl Scouts</a> were rescued by state police while at a camp in <a href="/wiki/York,_Pennsylvania" title="York, Pennsylvania">York</a>. A bridge collapsed in <a href="/wiki/Danville,_Pennsylvania" title="Danville, Pennsylvania">Danville</a>, which caused two diesel locomotives and several freight cars to fall into a swollen creek.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mh0623p20-a_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-mh0623p20-a-29">[29]</a></sup> In the state of Pennsylvania, more than 68,000\xc2\xa0homes and 3,000\xc2\xa0businesses were destroyed. Due to the destroyed houses, at least 220,000\xc2\xa0people were left homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TTL_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-TTL-40">[40]</a></sup> The damage and death toll was the highest in Pennsylvania, with 50\xc2\xa0fatalities and $2.3\xc2\xa0billion in losses in that state alone.</p>\n<p>On June 26, 1972, three news correspondents were killed in a helicopter crash in Harrisburg, where they had been covering the flooding. The victims were <a href="/wiki/Del_Vaughn" title="Del Vaughn">Del Vaughn</a> of <a href="/wiki/CBS_News" title="CBS News">CBS News</a> and Sid Brenner and Louis Clark of <a href="/wiki/WCAU" title="WCAU">WCAU</a> in Philadelphia, and the pilot, Mike Sedio. The helicopter lost its rotor some three hundred feet above the Capital City Airport, crashed, and exploded on the <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York">New York</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: New York">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Olean,_New_York" title="Olean, New York">Olean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elmira,_New_York" title="Elmira, New York">Elmira</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Corning_(city),_New_York" title="Corning (city), New York">Corning</a>, as well as many other <a href="/wiki/Southern_Tier" title="Southern Tier">Southern Tier</a> towns, were severely flooded. Sections of the former <a href="/wiki/Erie_Railroad" title="Erie Railroad">Erie Railroad</a> main line between Hornell and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Binghamton" title="Binghamton">Binghamton</a> were damaged. Flooding in the upper Allegheny River basin was particularly exacerbated by the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Kinzua_Dam" title="Kinzua Dam">Kinzua Dam</a> less than a decade prior; several towns in <a href="/wiki/Cattaraugus_County,_New_York" title="Cattaraugus County, New York">Cattaraugus County</a> suffered extensive road and bridge damage that, four decades later, has yet to be repaired, with the state and local governments opting to abandon creek and river crossings instead of reconstruct them. However, said dam construction allegedly succeeded in preventing further damage downstream in Pennsylvania, and much of the flood-prone areas in the <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_Reservoir" title="Allegheny Reservoir">Allegheny Reservoir</a> region had already been cleared of residents in preparation for the dam\'s construction.</p>\n<p>In Elmira, porches and garages were ripped loose, the Walnut St. bridge was carried away, Maple Ave. to <a href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_High_School_(Elmira,_New_York)" title="Notre Dame High School (Elmira, New York)">Notre Dame High School</a> was "underwater", and on S. Main St., Gerould\'s Pharmacy at W. Hudson was engulfed by 10\xc2\xa0feet 3\xc2\xa0inches (3.12\xc2\xa0m) of water.</p>\n<p>Hornell\'s damage was minimal largely due to the protection of two dam systems installed after the devastating 1935 flood. Both the Arkport and Almond Dams saved Hornell from the level of damage found in neighboring communities. Three US Army Corp of Engineers were killed in Hornell when their helicopter came in contact with power lines over Crosby Creek.</p>\n<p>The height of the floodwaters has been marked in the entrance to the <a href="/wiki/Corning_Museum_of_Glass" title="Corning Museum of Glass">Corning Museum of Glass</a>, showing that much of the museum was <a class="external text" href="http://www.cmog.org/collection/exhibitions/museum-under-water" rel="nofollow">under water</a> to a height of about 1.6 m above the floor, causing substantial damage to the collection and archives. Parts of Corning were under more than 3 m of flood waters.</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Virginia_and_Ohio">West Virginia and Ohio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: West Virginia and Ohio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Rainfall in <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> was generally light, though heavier in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Panhandle_of_West_Virginia" title="Eastern Panhandle of West Virginia">Eastern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Panhandle_of_West_Virginia" title="Northern Panhandle of West Virginia">Northern Panhandles</a> of the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_19-4"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-19">[19]</a></sup> Precipitation peaked at 7.94 inches (202\xc2\xa0mm) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bath_(Berkeley_Springs),_West_Virginia" title="Bath (Berkeley Springs), West Virginia">Berkeley Springs</a>. 108\xc2\xa0houses were destroyed and 1,558\xc2\xa0others suffered damage. Furthermore, 118\xc2\xa0mobile homes were destroyed and 91\xc2\xa0were significantly affected. 10\xc2\xa0farm buildings reported major losses, while 18\xc2\xa0small businesses were either damaged or destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table2_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-table2-20">[20]</a></sup> In the state of West Virginia, no fatalities were reported and damage was slightly more than $7.7\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table1_22-5"><a href="#cite_note-table1-22">[22]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Although no reports of abnormally high winds or rainfall exists in Ohio,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> the storm caused minor damage to about 302\xc2\xa0dwellings and severely affected at least three houses. In addition, at least 100\xc2\xa0mobile homes suffered major damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table2_20-3"><a href="#cite_note-table2-20">[20]</a></sup> Northeasterly winds from the storm waves reaching a height of 15 feet (4.6\xc2\xa0m) along the shore of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie" title="Lake Erie">Lake Erie</a> and also caused the water level to rise by 3.5 feet (1.1\xc2\xa0m). Houses, cars, boats, buildings, ships, and docks in the vicinity of Lake Erie were damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-26">[26]</a></sup> Overall, losses in Ohio totaled to at least $4\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table1_22-6"><a href="#cite_note-table1-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-26">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere_in_the_United_States">Elsewhere in the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Elsewhere in the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>While approaching Delaware, Agnes produced tides about 1.5 feet (0.46\xc2\xa0m) above normal at the <a href="/wiki/Indian_River_(Delaware)" title="Indian River (Delaware)">Indian River</a> Inlet. High winds were also reported in the state, and at the <a href="/wiki/Dover_Air_Force_Base" title="Dover Air Force Base">Dover Air Force Base</a>, a wind gust as high as 67\xc2\xa0mph (108\xc2\xa0km/h) was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Rainfall was generally light, peaking at 7.6 inches (190\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Middletown,_Delaware" title="Middletown, Delaware">Middletown</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mid_Atl_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mid_Atl-43">[43]</a></sup> Street flooding was reported and basements in low-lying area were flooded, though damage in Delaware was minimal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> One fatality occurred in that state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> area, moderate rainfall was reported, with precipitation amounts peaking at 13.65 inches (347\xc2\xa0mm) at the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Dulles_International_Airport" title="Washington Dulles International Airport">Washington Dulles International Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Significant flooding occurred and it was described as the worst in at least 44\xc2\xa0years. In the <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_(Washington,_D.C.)" title="Georgetown (Washington, D.C.)">Georgetown</a> section, waterfront streets along the Potomac River were submerged in as much as 7 feet (2.1\xc2\xa0m) of water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tbdf_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-tbdf-45">[45]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Rock_Creek_(Potomac_River)" title="Rock Creek (Potomac River)">Rock Creek</a> also overflowed its banks, which sent about 10 feet (3.0\xc2\xa0m) of water onto the <a href="/wiki/Rock_Creek_and_Potomac_Parkway" title="Rock Creek and Potomac Parkway">Rock Creek Parkway</a>. As a result, at least 100\xc2\xa0cars were abandoned and several motorists required rescuing by police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mh0624p20-a_39-2"><a href="#cite_note-mh0624p20-a-39">[39]</a></sup> No <a href="/wiki/National_Monument_(United_States)" title="National Monument (United States)">national monuments</a> were affected by flood waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tbdf_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-tbdf-45">[45]</a></sup> However, about 350\xc2\xa0dwellings in Washington, D.C. suffered minor damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-table2_20-4"><a href="#cite_note-table2-20">[20]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a>, reporters noted wet carpets in the press room of the basement floor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mh0624p20-a_39-3"><a href="#cite_note-mh0624p20-a-39">[39]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Though the storm passed just west of some areas in New England, minimal impact in that region of the United States. In <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, winds were well below tropical storm force, although a wind gust of 46\xc2\xa0mph (74\xc2\xa0km/h) was reported in <a href="/wiki/Hartford,_Connecticut" title="Hartford, Connecticut">Hartford</a>. Rainfall in the state was light, peaking at 1.62 inches (41\xc2\xa0mm), also recorded in Hartford.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-14"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Along the coast of Connecticut, high tides and heavy rainfall flooded coastal roads and basements. High tides occurred along the coastline of <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, reaching 3.2 feet (0.98\xc2\xa0m) above normal in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-15"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> In combination with rainfall and high tides, some coastal roads were flooded. Strong winds felled trees and numerous limbs in <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, causing power outages and property damage in the western and central portions of the state. One home in <a href="/wiki/Great_Barrington,_Massachusetts" title="Great Barrington, Massachusetts">Great Barrington</a> was struck by a tree; a similar incident occurred in <a href="/wiki/Milford,_Massachusetts" title="Milford, Massachusetts">Milford</a>. High winds in southern and eastern <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> knocked over trees and snapped off limbs, which resulted in scattered power outages, roads being blocked, and minor property damage. In <a href="/wiki/Mendon,_Vermont" title="Mendon, Vermont">Mendon</a>, a tree fell on a house, while two cars were damaged after being struck by limbs. Similar conditions occurred in <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, albeit effects were lesser.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_26-3"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-26">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>As a precaution, more than 8,000\xc2\xa0people in western Cuba, including on Isla de la Juventud (Isle of Pines), were evacuated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RE_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-RE-46">[46]</a></sup> In western Cuba, Agnes dropped heavy rainfall, peaking at 16.76 inches (426\xc2\xa0mm) on Isla de la Juventud. At <a href="/wiki/Cape_San_Antonio,_Cuba" title="Cape San Antonio, Cuba">Cape San Antonio</a>, the westernmost point in Cuba, precipitation reached 15.32 inches (389\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_2-16"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-2">[2]</a></sup> Due to high winds and flooding, at least 97\xc2\xa0houses were destroyed and another 270\xc2\xa0were damaged. In the <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province</a>, the cities of <a href="/wiki/Guane" title="Guane">Guane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mantua,_Cuba" title="Mantua, Cuba">Mantua</a> were isolated by swollen rivers. It was also reported that extensive crop damage occurred in low-lying areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RE_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-RE-46">[46]</a></sup> Overall, seven fatalities occurred in Cuba, though the damage toll is unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> However, some sources claim Agnes caused 16\xc2\xa0fatalities and reported 24\xc2\xa0people missing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, Hurricane Agnes gave heavy rains and winds over southern <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, causing numerous floodings around <a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie" title="Lake Erie">Lake Erie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">Lake Ontario</a>. In the town of <a href="/wiki/Maniwaki" title="Maniwaki">Maniwaki</a>, Quebec, the storm toppled a mobile home, killing two people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Following the storm, then-<a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a> declared 14\xc2\xa0counties in Florida as a disaster area, including: Brevard County, <a href="/wiki/Dixie_County,_Florida" title="Dixie County, Florida">Dixie County</a>, Franklin County, <a href="/wiki/Hendry_County,_Florida" title="Hendry County, Florida">Hendry County</a>, Hillsborough County, <a href="/wiki/Lee_County,_Florida" title="Lee County, Florida">Lee County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Levy_County,_Florida" title="Levy County, Florida">Levy County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manatee_County,_Florida" title="Manatee County, Florida">Manatee County</a>, Monroe County, <a href="/wiki/Okeechobee_County,_Florida" title="Okeechobee County, Florida">Okeechobee County</a>, Pasco County, Pinellas County, Sarasota County, and Wakulla County.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Panama_City,_Florida" title="Panama City, Florida">Panama City, Florida</a>, where over 25,000\xc2\xa0tourists evacuated, effects were not significant. As a result, the city lost millions of dollars from tourism, which led the officials of Panama City to file a $100\xc2\xa0million lawsuit against the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a>. Officials in that city believed that the National Weather Service and other media outlets made "exaggerated and erroneous" forecasts and reports on the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> 20\xc2\xa0of Maryland\'s 24\xc2\xa0counties were declared a disaster area.</p>\n<p>Following the storm, then-<a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a> declared the states of Florida, Virginia, Maryland, Pennsylvania, and New York as disaster areas. Agnes had a devastating impact on the already-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bankrupt" title="Bankrupt">bankrupt</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railroad" title="Railroad">railroads</a> in the northeastern United States, as lines were washed out and shipments were delayed:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/Erie_Lackawanna_Railway" title="Erie Lackawanna Railway">Erie Lackawanna Railway</a> (EL) estimated that damage between Binghamton and Salamanca, New York, amounted to $2 million. EL filed for Chapter 77 bankruptcy.</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/Penn_Central_Transportation_Company" title="Penn Central Transportation Company">Penn Central Railroad</a> sustained nearly $20 million in damages</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The resulting cost of repairing the damage was one of the factors leading to the creation of the federally financed <a href="/wiki/Conrail" title="Conrail">Conrail</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<p>The severe floods near Lawrenceville, Pennsylvania were the catalyst for the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Tioga_River_(Chemung_River)" title="Tioga River (Chemung River)">Tioga Reservoir</a> in 1973. The flooding in <a href="/wiki/Wilkes-Barre,_Pennsylvania" title="Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania">Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania</a> and the adjacent town of <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Pennsylvania" title="Kingston, Pennsylvania">Kingston</a> led to the construction of a <a href="/wiki/Levee" title="Levee">levee</a> system that in 2006 successfully prevented massive flooding and, in the same year, was deemed very safe and protective by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">Army Corps of Engineers</a>. The levee also protected the area from the remnants of Tropical Storm Lee and Hurricane Irene in 2011, with the water cresting just barely below the height of the structure. Conversely, the existing <a href="/wiki/Kinzua_Dam" title="Kinzua Dam">Kinzua Dam</a>, built against the wishes of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seneca_Nation_of_New_York" title="Seneca Nation of New York">Seneca Nation of New York</a>, spared much of <a href="/wiki/Western_Pennsylvania" title="Western Pennsylvania">Western Pennsylvania</a> from the worst flooding, by filling the <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_Reservoir" title="Allegheny Reservoir">Allegheny Reservoir</a> to capacity.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Agnes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p>Because of extensive damage and severe death tolls, the name "Agnes" was retired following this storm, and will never again be used for another <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Because the tropical cyclone naming lists were changed in 1979, there was no replacement name selected.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
