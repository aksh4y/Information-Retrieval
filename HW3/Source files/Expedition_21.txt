The expedition began on 30 October 2009, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frank_de_Winne" title="Frank de Winne">Frank de Winne</a> becoming the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ESA" title="ESA">ESA</a> astronaut to command a space mission.</p>\n<p>The handover between <a href="/wiki/Expedition_20" title="Expedition 20">Expedition 20</a> and Expedition 21 required three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyuz_spacecraft" title="Soyuz spacecraft">Soyuz</a> vehicles being docked to the station at the same time, the first time this has occurred.</p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-16" title="Soyuz TMA-16">Soyuz TMA-16</a> brought the final members of Expedition 21 to the ISS, along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_tourist" title="Space tourist">space tourist</a> <a href="/wiki/Guy_Lalibert%C3%A9" title="Guy Lalibert\xc3\xa9">Guy Lalibert\xc3\xa9</a>. Lalibert\xc3\xa9 returned to Earth on <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-14" title="Soyuz TMA-14">Soyuz TMA-14</a> with two members of Expedition 20 on 11 October 2009.</p>\n<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicole_P._Stott" title="Nicole P. Stott">Nicole P. Stott</a> was the last ISS expedition crew member to fly on the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a>. She returned to Earth aboard <a href="/wiki/STS-129" title="STS-129">STS-129</a> in November 2009.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div
