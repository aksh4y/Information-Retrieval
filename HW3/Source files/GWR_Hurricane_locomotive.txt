W. Hawthorn &amp; Co.">R. &amp; W. Hawthorn &amp; Co.</a> whose design was very different from other locomotives. In order to meet <a href="/wiki/Isambard_Kingdom_Brunel" title="Isambard Kingdom Brunel">Isambard Kingdom Brunel</a>\'s strict specifications, a <a href="/wiki/2-2-2" title="2-2-2">2-2-2</a> frame carried the \'engine\', while the boiler was on a separate six-wheeled frame.</p>\n<p>The locomotive was delivered to the GWR on 6 October 1838 and ceased work in December 1839<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> after running for just 10,527 miles.
