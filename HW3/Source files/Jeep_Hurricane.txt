the 2005 <a href="/wiki/North_American_International_Auto_Show" title="North American International Auto Show">North American International Auto Show</a> in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a> by American off-road brand <a href="/wiki/Jeep" title="Jeep">Jeep</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">Hurricane</a>\'s one-piece body is composed largely of light-weight structural <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber" title="Carbon fiber">carbon fiber</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeep_hurricane_info_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jeep_hurricane_info-1">[1]</a></sup> Its <a href="/wiki/Skid_plate" title="Skid plate">skid plate</a> is an aluminum spine that connects the chassis to the underside of the vehicle. The Hurricane doesn\xe2\x80\x99t have side doors or a roof, and there is only seating for two people. The driver and passenger enter the vehicle over bulkheads on each side. The Hurricane features a Chrysler designed and patented four-wheel steering system, which was outsourced to <a href="/wiki/MillenWorks" title="MillenWorks">MillenWorks</a> that allows the Hurricane to move sideways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeep_4-wheel_steering_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jeep_4-wheel_steering-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Its principal exterior designer was Aaron Pizzuti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aaron_Pizzuti_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aaron_Pizzuti-4">[4]</a></sup> The concept went on to win IDEA Silver Award,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IDEA_Silver_Award_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-IDEA_Silver_Award-5">[5]</a></sup> a Popular Science "Best of what\'s new" award,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popular_Science_Award_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Popular_Science_Award-6">[6]</a></sup> and an AutoWeek Editor\'s Choice award in the "Most Fun" category.<sup
