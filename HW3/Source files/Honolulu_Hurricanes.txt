According to the team\'s media guide, the ownership partners \xe2\x80\x93 registered as Pro Sports Limited Liability Company \xe2\x80\x93 were Rev. John Frederick, the team\'s founder and co head coach\xc2\xa0; Sig Schuster, the CEO; Dennis Enomoto; Neil Wiedemann; Louis "Sonny" Souza, the team\'s on-field coach; and James K. Wong. The team office was based in Honolulu, and played their home games at the <a href="/wiki/Neal_S._Blaisdell_Center" title="Neal S. Blaisdell Center">Neal S. Blaisdell Center</a>, also in Honolulu. The team colors were red and gold. The Hurricanes were coached by the Hawaii Police Department\'s Louis "Sonny" Souza.</p>\n<p>The Hurricanes played two preseason PIFL games in 1998, both victories at home:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Las Vegas Outlaws\xe2\x80\xa0 14 at Honolulu Hurricanes 53</li>\n<li>March 27 \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/Tucson_Mirage" title="Tucson Mirage">Tucson Mirage</a>\xe2\x80\xa0 19 at Honolulu Hurricanes 25</li>\n</ul>\n<p>\xe2\x80\xa0scheduled 1999 PIFL expansion team</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:181px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HonHurricanes-homehelmet.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="137" data-file-width="179" height="137" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/e/ec/HonHurricanes-homehelmet.png" width="179"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">1998 PIFL Honolulu Hurricanes away games helmet design</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>All players on the Honolulu opening day roster played high school or college football in Hawaii. The list included: receivers Micah Matsuzaki and Dan Ahuna, running backs Tupu Alualu and Jerry Papalii, quarterback John Hao, offensive lineman Damon Kakalia, defensive back Niko Vitale, linebackers Manly Williams and George Noga and defensive linemen Kalei Cockett and Junior Tagoai.</p>\n<p>The Hurricanes franchise drew about 5,500 for its first game (preseason), and 3,800 for the second, but averaged between 500 and 1,000 per game after that. Honolulu ended the 1998 PIFL season with a 6-8 record. However, the team nearly made the playoffs when commissioner Richard "Dick" Suess nearly expelled the <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay_Bombers" title="Green Bay Bombers">Green Bay Bombers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madison_Mad_Dogs" title="Madison Mad Dogs">Madison Mad Dogs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Wildcats" title="Colorado Wildcats">Colorado Wildcats</a> after the announcement that they were leaving to start a rival league following the season.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:181px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HonHurricanes-awayhelmet.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="137" data-file-width="179" height="137" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/b5/HonHurricanes-awayhelmet.png" width="179"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">1998 PIFL Honolulu Hurricanes home games helmet design</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>After the season, the Hurricanes were sold. One reason was the mounting financial burden of having to travel to the mainland for games, a problem common to nearly all Hawaii sports teams who schedule competitions on the mainland. The new owners were Straub chief executive officer Dr. Blake Waterhouse, former Hawaii Winter Baseball League owner <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Duane_Kurisu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Duane Kurisu (page does not exist)">Duane Kurisu</a>, insurance company executive Carl Hennrich, Borthwick Group owners John and Diane Farias Jr., Borthwick general manager Scott Sells, Robertson and Co. Jewelers owner Robert Wu and Regal Travel owner Ray Miyashiro. Along with ownership change, the Hurricanes were renamed the "<a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Hammerheads" title="Hawaii Hammerheads">Hawaii Hammerheads</a>" for the 1999 season, for which the league was likewise renamed as the "Indoor Professional Football League" rather than the Professional Indoor Football League.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Season-by-season">Season-by-season</span><span
