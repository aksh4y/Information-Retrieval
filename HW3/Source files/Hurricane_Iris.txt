Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Iris</b> of 2001 was the most destructive hurricane in <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a> since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hattie" title="Hurricane Hattie">Hurricane Hattie</a> in 1961. Iris was the second-strongest storm of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">2001 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, behind <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Michelle" title="Hurricane Michelle">Hurricane Michelle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> It was the ninth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a>, fifth hurricane, and third major hurricane<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[nb 1]</a></sup> of the year, forming from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on October\xc2\xa04 just southeast of <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>. It moved westward through the Caribbean, intensifying into a tropical storm on October\xc2\xa05 south of Puerto Rico and into a hurricane the following day. While passing south of the Dominican Republic, Iris dropped heavy rainfall that caused landslides, killing eight people. Later, the hurricane passed south of Jamaica, where it destroyed two houses. On reaching the western Caribbean Sea, Iris <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensified</a> into a Category\xc2\xa04 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a>. A small hurricane with an eye of only 7\xc2\xa0mi (11\xc2\xa0km) in diameter, Iris reached peak winds of 145\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h) before making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> in southern Belize near <a href="/wiki/Monkey_River_Town" title="Monkey River Town">Monkey River Town</a> on October\xc2\xa09. The hurricane quickly dissipated over Central America, although its remnants contributed to the formation of <a href="/wiki/2001_Pacific_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Manuel" title="2001 Pacific hurricane season">Tropical Storm Manuel</a> in the eastern Pacific Ocean.</p>\n<p>Destruction was heaviest in Belize and totaled $250\xc2\xa0million (2001\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[nb 2]</a></sup> Because Iris was compact, the damage was largely confined to 72% of the houses in the <a href="/wiki/Toledo_District" title="Toledo District">Toledo district</a> and 50% of the houses in the <a href="/wiki/Stann_Creek_District" title="Stann Creek District">Stann Creek</a> <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Belize" title="Districts of Belize">district</a>. The hurricane damaged or destroyed 3,718\xc2\xa0homes nationwide, and wrecked more than 95% of the homes in 35\xc2\xa0villages in the poorest parts of the country. Iris left about 15,000\xc2\xa0people homeless, many receiving assistance from the government and the local <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Societies" title="International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies">Red Cross</a> chapter. High winds also damaged large swaths of forest and crops, mostly affecting the banana industry. Iris killed 24\xc2\xa0people in Belize, including 20 who died when a scuba diving boat capsized near <a href="/wiki/Big_Creek,_Belize" title="Big Creek, Belize">Big Creek</a>. The storm also killed eight people and damaged about 2,500\xc2\xa0homes in neighboring Guatemala, and later dropped heavy rainfall in southern Mexico, where two people died.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Iris_2001_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Iris_2001_track.png/220px-Iris_2001_track.png" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Toward the end of September 2001, a poorly defined <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved westward across the tropical Atlantic Ocean through an area of hostile <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, which was caused by a large <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">upper-level low</a> within a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> to the northeast of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> A few days later, the upper-level low detached from the trough and moved southwestward over the Caribbean Sea, allowing for an upper-level <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a>, or high-pressure area, to form over the tropical wave. The change provided a favorable environment for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">tropical development</a>, and an area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a> soon blossomed along the wave\'s axis. As the tropical wave approached the Lesser Antilles, a mid-level <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">wind circulation</a> formed within the deepest part of the convection, and a low-level circulation became gradually more pronounced on satellite imagery. Although its low-level circulation was small and poorly defined, the system increased in organization enough to be classified as Tropical Depression Eleven at 12:00\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on October\xc2\xa04, located about 100\xc2\xa0mi (160\xc2\xa0km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> Operationally, however, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> did not confirm the depression\'s formation until nine hours later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-6">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In its early stages, the depression moved west-northwestward between the islands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Vincent_(island)" title="St. Vincent (island)">St. Vincent</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lucia" title="St. Lucia">St. Lucia</a> under the influence of a strong ridge to its north. Compared to its appearance 24\xc2\xa0hours before forming, the depression exhibited improved <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> and more distinct convection, although its lower circulation remained very poorly organized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-6">[4]</a></sup> This was confirmed by a Hurricane Hunters flight into the system, which failed to report a closed circulation despite the depression\'s well-organized appearance on satellite imagery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc2-7">[5]</a></sup> At 21:00\xc2\xa0UTC on October\xc2\xa05, they reported a strengthening circulation with flight-level winds of 74\xc2\xa0mph (119\xc2\xa0km/h), corresponding to a surface wind intensity of 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h). Based on these data, the depression was upgraded to Tropical Storm Iris, situated about 155\xc2\xa0mi (250\xc2\xa0km) south of the southern coast of Puerto Rico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc5_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-8">[6]</a></sup> In post-season analysis, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) estimated that Iris had attained tropical storm status about nine hours earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<div href="/wiki/File:2001_Hurricane_Iris.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Iris in the western <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> on October 8</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Despite the storm\'s intensification and well-organized satellite appearance, the circulation failed to become better defined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> In their first discussion on Iris, the NHC mentioned the potential for the system to degenerate into a tropical wave if it maintained its fast forward speed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-6">[4]</a></sup> One forecaster noted that the center was fragile and that the cyclone could dissipate quickly if it encountered stronger wind shear to its south.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc5_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-8">[6]</a></sup> Although its overall appearance did not change significantly, the Hurricane Hunters reported a closed <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> with a diameter 23\xc2\xa0mi (37\xc2\xa0km) and a <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">stadium effect</a> (eyewall curvature) on October\xc2\xa06.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc8_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc8-9">[7]</a></sup> Later that day, Iris reached hurricane strength just southwest of the southern tip of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> and the NHC remarked that land interaction with the Greater Antilles was the only factor impeding further development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc9_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc9-10">[8]</a></sup> After Iris reached winds of 85\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h) early on October\xc2\xa07, its intensity remained steady for about 24\xc2\xa0hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> During that time, the satellite appearance became slightly ragged as its outflow became restricted, possibly due to an <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">upper-level low</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc11_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc11-11">[9]</a></sup> By late on October\xc2\xa07, the area of hurricane\xc2\xa0force winds associated with Iris extended only 25\xc2\xa0mi (35\xc2\xa0km) from its 16\xc2\xa0mi (22\xc2\xa0km) wide eye.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Early on October\xc2\xa08, after turning west-southwestward away from the Greater Antilles, Iris began strengthening again, with warm waters and an absence of significant wind shear. The NHC predicted peak winds of 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h) before the storm would hit Belize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[12]</a></sup> It <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensified</a> with the favorable conditions, intensifying from 95\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h) to 140\xc2\xa0mph (225\xc2\xa0km/h) in a 12-hour period on October\xc2\xa08, making Iris a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 4 hurricane</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson scale</a>; in the same duration, the <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">minimum central pressure</a> dropped 38\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (1.12\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> While intensifying, the hurricane developed <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)#Eyewall_replacement_cycles" title="Eye (cyclone)">concentric eyewalls</a>, with an innermost eye having a diameter of 7\xc2\xa0mi (11\xc2\xa0km).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc16_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc16-15">[13]</a></sup> For comparison, the smallest known eye diameter on record for an Atlantic hurricane was about 3\xc2\xa0mi (5\xc2\xa0km), during <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Wilma" title="Hurricane Wilma">Hurricane Wilma</a> in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wilma_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-wilma-16">[14]</a></sup> With such a small eye, a Hurricane Hunters flight could not deploy a <a href="/wiki/Dropsonde" title="Dropsonde">dropsonde</a> into the center of Iris,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc16_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc16-15">[13]</a></sup> and shortly after the flight, the innermost eye collapsed as the core paralleled the Honduras coastline just offshore. This resulted in a temporary and slight weakening during an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)#Eyewall_replacement_cycles" title="Eye (cyclone)">eyewall replacement cycle</a>, but within a few hours Iris re-intensified to attain peak winds of 145\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h) just off Belize. At 02:00\xc2\xa0UTC on October\xc2\xa09, it made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> at peak intensity in <a href="/wiki/Monkey_River_Town" title="Monkey River Town">Monkey River Town</a> in the southern portion of Belize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Initially, Hurricane Iris was forecast to remain a tropical cyclone while crossing <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> and to re-intensify in the eastern Pacific Ocean; had it done so, it would have retained the name Iris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc16_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-disc16-15">[13]</a></sup> Instead, the hurricane rapidly weakened after moving into the mountainous terrain of Guatemala, and within six hours of landfall the hurricane weakened to a tropical storm. Late on October\xc2\xa09, within sixteen hours of landfall, the circulation dissipated over extreme southeastern Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> As the remnants approached the Pacific Ocean, a new area of convection developed south of the original circulation of Iris. It gradually organized while continuing westward, developing into <a href="/wiki/2001_Pacific_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Manuel" title="2001 Pacific hurricane season">Tropical Storm Manuel</a>; the new storm ultimately lasted until October\xc2\xa018 before succumbing to cooler waters and wind shear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-manuel_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-manuel-17">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Over a stretch of four days, sixteen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_watches_and_warnings" title="Tropical cyclone watches and warnings">tropical cyclone watches and warnings</a> were issued in association with Iris, affecting the Dominican Republic, the <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuban</a> provinces of <a href="/wiki/Granma_Province" title="Granma Province">Granma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_Cuba_Province" title="Santiago de Cuba Province">Santiago de Cuba</a>, Jamaica, <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-9"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> The threat from Iris prompted the Jamaica National Emergency Operations Center to be activated. Shelters were opened in the country but were ultimately unused.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jam_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-jam-18">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Belize, a hurricane warning was issued about 23\xc2\xa0hours before Iris moved ashore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-10"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> A state of national emergency was declared on October\xc2\xa08 as Hurricane Iris neared landfall. All emergency response committees were activated to quickly begin recovery efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[17]</a></sup> A mandatory evacuation was issued for Stann Creek and Toledo coastal villages and all offshore islands. The main hospital in Belize City was evacuated as a precaution and the city itself was placed under a voluntary evacuation order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-paho-20">[18]</a></sup> Overall, 11,380\xc2\xa0people evacuated their homes in Belize,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wfp1017_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-wfp1017-21">[19]</a></sup> including many in <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">Belize City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1009_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp1009-22">[20]</a></sup> These evacuations were later credited for limiting the death toll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dref1010_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-dref1010-23">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Keith" title="Hurricane Keith">Hurricane Keith</a> had struck the nation a year prior, preparing some citizens for what to expect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1009_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp1009-22">[20]</a></sup> Disaster response teams arrived the day after Iris was projected to make landfall. Pan American Health Organization staff were on standby in Belize, Guatemala and Honduras and were ready to respond to any post-storm disease outbreaks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-paho-20">[18]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On October\xc2\xa08, the Government of Honduras declared a red alert for all northern regions, advising residents to expect "extreme weather conditions".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[22]</a></sup> About 5,000\xc2\xa0people in the country evacuated from their homes. To the north of Belize, officials in Mexico evacuated people from fishing villages and closed ports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="wikitable" style="float:right;margin:0 0 1em 1em;">\n<caption><b>Death toll by area</b></caption>\n<tr>\n<th>State/country</th>\n<th>Deaths</th>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a></td>\n<td>23</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a></td>\n<td>3</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a></td>\n<td>8</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a></td>\n<td>2</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Total</th>\n<th>36</th>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lesser_and_Greater_Antilles">Lesser and Greater Antilles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lesser and Greater Antilles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>While Iris was in its development stages, residents as far north as <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">Saint Thomas</a> reported rain and thunderstorms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[24]</a></sup> In the Dominican Republic, Iris dropped around 3\xc2\xa0in (76\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall along the coast, forcing 35\xc2\xa0families to evacuate their homes after rivers exceeded their banks. The rains triggered a landslide outside of <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a> that destroyed a home, killing a family of three. There was another landslide in the region that injured two people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[25]</a></sup> Iris\'s passage near Jamaica destroyed two houses and damaged the roofs of two others, causing one injury. Otherwise, damage in the country was minimal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jam_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-jam-18">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Offshore">Offshore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Offshore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A 120\xc2\xa0ft (37\xc2\xa0m) <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">scuba diving</a> boat overturned during the hurricane near <a href="/wiki/Big_Creek,_Belize" title="Big Creek, Belize">Big Creek, Belize</a>, possibly hit by a tornado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1016_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap1016-28">[26]</a></sup> The boat, named the <i>Wave Dancer</i>, had 28\xc2\xa0people on board,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-11"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> including 20 from the Richmond Dive Club out of <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>; most of them were upstairs in the boat, and none were diving. The captain had delayed returning to shore, and the passengers waited for the storm to pass along a dock, not anticipating the ferocity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surv_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-surv-29">[27]</a></sup> Iris cut the ropes connecting the boat to the dock, causing it to overturn in 12\xc2\xa0ft (3.7\xc2\xa0m) waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1016_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap1016-28">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surv_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-surv-29">[27]</a></sup> Eight people survived, and 11\xc2\xa0bodies were recovered; it was presumed that 20\xc2\xa0people died during the wreck,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-12"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> including 15 from the Richmond area and three crew members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1016_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap1016-28">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Another boat, the <i>Vendera</i>, also reportedly capsized with people on board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-13"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Belize">Belize</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Belize">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="wikitable" style="float:right; id="cite_ref-GEO_33-3"><a href="#cite_note-GEO-33">[31]</a></sup></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>Hurricane Iris moved ashore in Belize with winds of 145\xc2\xa0mph (233\xc2\xa0km/h), although the highest measured winds were 106\xc2\xa0mph (171\xc2\xa0km/h) at a station in Big Creek. Because of its small diameter, Iris produced heavy damage only in a 70\xc2\xa0mi (110\xc2\xa0km) area of southern Belize. In that region, the hurricane produced a <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> of up to 15\xc2\xa0ft (4.6\xc2\xa0m),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-14"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> with waves of over 13\xc2\xa0ft (4.0\xc2\xa0m) in height,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dref1010_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-dref1010-23">[21]</a></sup> causing street flooding and some damage to the offshore cayes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tour_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-tour-37">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As it moved ashore, Iris damaged houses and schools in dozens of villages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sitrep5_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-sitrep5-38">[36]</a></sup> In 35\xc2\xa0villages, the storm destroyed more than 95% of the buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1012_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc1012-39">[37]</a></sup> Its small size confined the worst damage largely to <a href="/wiki/Toledo_District" title="Toledo District">Toledo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stann_Creek_District" title="Stann Creek District">Stann Creek</a> <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Belize" title="Districts of Belize">districts</a>, which are the two southernmost and poorest districts of the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dref1010_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-dref1010-23">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-un1017_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-un1017-40">[38]</a></sup> The percentage of damaged houses was 72% in Toledo district and about 50% in Stann Creek,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1019-1_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1019-1-41">[39]</a></sup> leaving about 15,000\xc2\xa0people homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1018_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc1018-42">[40]</a></sup> In both districts, the storm caused power outages and contaminated water supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1012_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc1012-39">[37]</a></sup> In the worst-affected areas, poor Mayan people living on farms lost much of what they owned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1012_39-2"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc1012-39">[37]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Placencia" title="Placencia">Placencia</a> near the coast, about 80% of the homes were destroyed and many of the remaining buildings had roof damage, with downed power poles in the streets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dref1010_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-dref1010-23">[21]</a></sup> About 90% of the houses in nearby Seine Bight were destroyed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha1010_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocha1010-43">[41]</a></sup> and where Iris made landfall, over 90% of the homes were destroyed throughout Monkey River Town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dref1010_23-4"><a href="#cite_note-dref1010-23">[21]</a></sup> The storm damaged several roads and fishing piers in southern Belize. Iris also damaged tourism facilities, including minor impact to the <a href="/wiki/Maya_ruins_of_Belize" title="Maya ruins of Belize">Maya ruins of Belize</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1011_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1011-44">[42]</a></sup> and damaged 20% of the hotel rooms in the country, accounting for $37\xc2\xa0million in losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oas1017_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-oas1017-45">[43]</a></sup> The remainder of the country remained generally unaffected during the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rony_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-rony-46">[44]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In southern Belize, the storm\'s strong winds left crop damage, in some cases where the harvest had just begun. About 5,000 acres (2,000\xc2\xa0ha) of bananas were destroyed, along with over 3,500 acres (1,400\xc2\xa0ha) of rice, 3,000 acres (1,200\xc2\xa0ha) of corn, and other crops to a lesser degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1011_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1011-44">[42]</a></sup> The storm also flooded fields and killed several livestock. The shrimp industry lost 25% of its catch,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1011_44-2"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1011-44">[42]</a></sup> partly due to contaminated waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1011b_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1011b-47">[45]</a></sup> Crop damage in Belize was estimated at $103\xc2\xa0million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oas1017_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-oas1017-45">[43]</a></sup> mostly from banana losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1011_44-3"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1011-44">[42]</a></sup> Iris\'s strong winds also damaged large swaths of forest, with upwards of 40% of trees affected in some areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1011_44-4"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1011-44">[42]</a></sup> This disrupted the habitats of several animals, and it is likely that many of the <a href="/wiki/Howler_monkey" title="Howler monkey">howler monkeys</a> near Monkey River were killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eprints_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-eprints-48">[46]</a></sup> The storm\'s strong waves eroded the beach, although marine effects were much less than those of Hurricane Keith in the previous year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1011_44-5"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1011-44">[42]</a></sup> Nevertheless, there were reports of <a href="/wiki/Fish_kill" title="Fish kill">fish die-offs</a> after the storm, possibly from low oxygen due to too much decaying matter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eprints_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-eprints-48">[46]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Nationwide, Iris damaged or destroyed 3,718\xc2\xa0homes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc123_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc123-49">[47]</a></sup> directly affecting a total of 21,568\xc2\xa0people,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-un1017_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-un1017-40">[38]</a></sup> or 8.5% of the total population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unicef_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-unicef-50">[48]</a></sup> The storm damaged or destroyed 31\xc2\xa0schools and 17\xc2\xa0health facilities,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-un1017_40-2"><a href="#cite_note-un1017-40">[38]</a></sup> along with 21\xc2\xa0government buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha1018_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocha1018-51">[49]</a></sup> There was about $25\xc2\xa0million in damage to the transportation sector, including highways and bridges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oas1017_45-2"><a href="#cite_note-oas1017-45">[43]</a></sup> Iris killed 24\xc2\xa0people in and around the country,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1114_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc1114-52">[50]</a></sup> including the victims of the <i>Wave Dancer</i> shipwreck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arc1012_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-arc1012-53">[51]</a></sup> Overall damage was estimated at $250\xc2\xa0million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[52]</a></sup> making it the most damaging storm in the country since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hattie" title="Hurricane Hattie">Hurricane Hattie</a> in 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rony_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-rony-46">[44]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere_in_Central_America">Elsewhere in Central America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Elsewhere in Central America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>High tides and heavy rainfall caused power outages across both Guatemala and Honduras.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1009_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-afp1009-22">[20]</a></sup> In the former, the hurricane\'s rainfall generally amounted to 3 to 4\xc2\xa0in (76 to 102\xc2\xa0mm), triggering flash flooding and landslides that injured nearly 100\xc2\xa0people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guatemala1_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guatemala1-55">[53]</a></sup> The damage was heaviest in <a href="/wiki/Pet%C3%A9n_Department" title="Pet\xc3\xa9n Department">Pet\xc3\xa9n Department</a> in the northern portion of the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[54]</a></sup> The storm damaged 26\xc2\xa0schools and 2,500\xc2\xa0homes in the country\'s interior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[56]</a></sup> An estimated 27,500\xc2\xa0people were affected by the storm throughout Guatemala.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guatemala1_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guatemala1-55">[53]</a></sup> There were eight deaths in the country,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_5-15"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-5">[3]</a></sup> two of them the result of falling trees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1010_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp1010-59">[57]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The remnants of Iris dropped heavy rainfall over southern Mexico, accumulating 4.80\xc2\xa0in (122\xc2\xa0mm) in the southern state of <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[58]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a>, the storm produced heavy rains and damaged a total of 120\xc2\xa0houses. A mudslide in one village demolished 20\xc2\xa0homes and killed a child, while elsewhere in the state a man drowned after being swept away in a flooded river.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-un_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-un-61">[59]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On October\xc2\xa09, the government of Belize issued the "all clear" signal, indicating that the storm had fully passed, and began reconstruction efforts and damage assessment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[60]</a></sup> The government declared Stann Creek and Toledo districts as disaster areas,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1011b_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1011b-47">[45]</a></sup> and officials declared a nighttime curfew. By the day after the storm struck, the airport in Belize City had been reopened, and transportation in all but the southern portion of the country returned to normal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha1010_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-ocha1010-43">[41]</a></sup> Residents in the southern part of the country lost access to fresh water, forcing them to drink unclean water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1012_39-3"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc1012-39">[37]</a></sup> Officials sent medical teams to southern Belize in the most affected areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dref1010_23-5"><a href="#cite_note-dref1010-23">[21]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Belmopan" title="Belmopan">Belmopan</a> Red Cross issued an appeal for residents to donate money, clothing, and food for storm victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha1010_43-2"><a href="#cite_note-ocha1010-43">[41]</a></sup> The Red Cross also set up shelters and gave food to more than 7,000\xc2\xa0people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arc1012_53-1"><a href="#cite_note-arc1012-53">[51]</a></sup> By October\xc2\xa019, most roads in southern Belize were reopened. The Belize government printed a new postage stamp to help pay for reconstruction costs, and officials authorized spending $1.2\xc2\xa0million to rebuild damaged homes. To assist the farmers who lost crops, the Belize government provided 18,000\xc2\xa0lb (8,200\xc2\xa0kg) of maize seeds, as well as fertilizer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1019-1_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1019-1-41">[39]</a></sup> After the storm, the World Food Programme and the Belize Red Cross collectively provided food for the 9,000\xc2\xa0families in need of subsidence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[61]</a></sup> By October\xc2\xa031, the Red Cross had provided blankets, tarps, and hygienic supplies to 4,800\xc2\xa0people severely affected by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[62]</a></sup> Homes were gradually repaired, and crop production returned to normal by early 2002. Around Christmas of 2001, the Belize Red Cross provided presents to school children in 14\xc2\xa0villages affected by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc123_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc123-49">[47]</a></sup> The lost banana crop caused sales to decrease by 22% in 2002, although sales gradually recovered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[63]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The government of Belize issued an appeal to the international community for assistance in the days following Iris\'s landfall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wfp1011_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-wfp1011-66">[64]</a></sup> and various countries provided aid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha1010_43-3"><a href="#cite_note-ocha1010-43">[41]</a></sup> The United Kingdom sent a helicopter to assist in damage assessment and a crew to clean the water. The United States also sent a crew for damage assessment and donated plastic sheeting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1019-2_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1019-2-67">[65]</a></sup> Although sustaining significant damage, the Government of Guatemala deployed a working team with members from throughout the country to assist in recovery in Belize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[66]</a></sup> Mexico sent blankets, mattresses, food, and water, as well as a medical team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1019-2_67-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1019-2-67">[65]</a></sup> The Japanese government sent tents and blankets,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[67]</a></sup> and the Chinese government donated 500\xc2\xa0lb (230\xc2\xa0kg) of rice and dried fruits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera1019-2_67-2"><a href="#cite_note-cdera1019-2-67">[65]</a></sup> Various United Nations departments donated about $225,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha1010_43-4"><a href="#cite_note-ocha1010-43">[41]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The American victims of the <i>Wave Dancer</i> boat wreck were flown back to the Richmond, Virginia area following the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1016_28-3"><a href="#cite_note-ap1016-28">[26]</a></sup> The insurance company covering the boat reached a $4\xc2\xa0million settlement, which was disbursed among the survivors and the victims\' families. The boat operator remained in business following the accident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surv_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-surv-29">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Following the major damage in Belize, the name <i>Iris</i> was retired in the spring of 2002 by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> and will never again be used for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a>. The name was subsequently replaced by the name <i>Ingrid</i> and was first used during the <a href="/wiki/2007_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2007 Atlantic hurricane season">2007 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[68]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
