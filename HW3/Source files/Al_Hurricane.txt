October 22, 2017), known professionally as <b>Al Hurricane</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a>, dubbed "The Godfather" of <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico_music" title="New Mexico music">New Mexico music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AlibiTributeGodfather_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AlibiTributeGodfather-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He released more than thirty albums, and is best known for his contributions to New Mexico\xe2\x80\x99s unique style of Spanish music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HISPANONEWMEXICO_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-HISPANONEWMEXICO-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>He received his nickname, Hurricane, from his mother. As a child, he would accidentally knock things over;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the nickname became synonymous with his band, studio, and a recording label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> His signature look, which included an eye-patch, was due to an automobile accident that occurred during the 1960s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> These things, as well as his performance style, led to Hurricane being known for his ability to improvise and adapt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AaronMartinezABQtrib_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-AaronMartinezABQtrib-8">[8]</a></sup> They have also led to his music and image being ultimately entangled with the history of New Mexico music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walker2008_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Walker2008-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Al Hurricane was often accompanied on stage by his children, sons <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Hurricane,_Jr." title="Al Hurricane, Jr.">Al Hurricane, Jr.</a> and Jerry Dean, and daughter Erika. He also performed frequently with his brothers <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Baby_Gaby&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Baby Gaby (page does not exist)">Baby Gaby</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tiny_Morrie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tiny Morrie (page does not exist)">Tiny Morrie</a>. He was the subject of a tribute concert which was recorded as a set of two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tribute_album" title="Tribute album">tribute albums</a>, as well a DVD. He has also performed alongside <a href="/wiki/Fats_Domino" title="Fats Domino">Fats Domino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Gaye" title="Marvin Gaye">Marvin Gaye</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Berry" title="Chuck Berry">Chuck Berry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Clanton" title="Jimmy Clanton">Jimmy Clanton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chubby_Checker" title="Chubby Checker">Chubby Checker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Biography"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Biography</span></a>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-2"><a href="#Hard-working_father_and_tenacious_mother_.281930s.E2.80.931950s.29"><span class="tocnumber">1.1</span> <span class="toctext">Hard-working father and tenacious mother (1930s\xe2\x80\x931950s)</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-3"><a href="#Al_Hurricane_.26_the_Night_Rockers_.281950s.E2.80.931960s.29"><span class="tocnumber">1.2</span> <span class="toctext">Al Hurricane &amp; id="Hard-working_father_and_tenacious_mother_.281930s.E2.80.931950s.29">Hard-working father and tenacious mother (1930s\xe2\x80\x931950s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hard-working father and tenacious mother (1930s\xe2\x80\x931950s)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Al Hurricane was born on July 10, 1936<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HISPANONEWMEXICO_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-HISPANONEWMEXICO-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> as Alberto Nelson Sanchez the first of four children to Jose Margarito Sanchez (April 28, 1910, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_Road_to_Taos,_New_Mexico#Ojo_Sarco" title="High Road to Taos, New Mexico">Ojo Sarco</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> \xe2\x80\x93 September 10, 1979, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albuquerque" title="Albuquerque">Albuquerque</a>, New Mexico<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>) and Bennie L. Sanchez (November 26, 1918, Albuquerque, New Mexico \xe2\x80\x93 January 27, 2011, Albuquerque, New Mexico<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>) in Dixon, New Mexico.</p>\n<p>The Sanchez family moved to <a href="/wiki/Silver_City,_New_Mexico" title="Silver City, New Mexico">Silver City</a> and the father worked in the mines, until he became injured. At which point Bennie returned to work as a clerk at a department store in a Silver City. Jose had a band, called Los Sanchez, Bennie would often join in on vocals and guitar. She eventually became a licensed practical nurse while traveling with physicians throughout Northern New Mexico. His father and mother were extremely supportive of their children. Jose, Al\'s father, was a miner during most of his childhood; but he still found the time to teach the five-year-old Alberto how to play the guitar. Al\'s mother, Bennie, made quite a name for herself, she became the president of Hurricane Enterprises. She not only promoted her musically-inclined family, but also promoted concerts for <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Cash" title="Johnny Cash">Johnny Cash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ray_Charles" title="Ray Charles">Ray Charles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chubby_Checker" title="Chubby Checker">Chubby Checker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fats_Domino" title="Fats Domino">Fats Domino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Little_Richard" title="Little Richard">Little Richard</a> and, the one she was particularly proud of, a 1972 <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> concert in Albuquerque.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walker_2008_p._120_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Walker_2008_p._120-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Her own musically talented family didn\'t just consist of her sons Al Hurricane, Baby Gaby and Tiny Morrie; it also consisted of Al Hurricane\'s sons Al Hurricane, Jr. and Jerry Dean; as well as Tiny Morrie\'s children <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Antonio" title="Lorenzo Antonio">Lorenzo Antonio</a> and the members of <a href="/wiki/Sparx_(US_band)" title="Sparx (US band)">Sparx</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walker2008_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Walker2008-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>During his childhood he moved to Albuquerque, where he began to play and perform in <a href="/wiki/Old_Town_Albuquerque" title="Old Town Albuquerque">Old Town</a> at the age of 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inc.1974_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inc.1974-17">[17]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque_High_School" title="Albuquerque High School">Old Albuquerque High</a>, which he graduated from in 1954.</p>\n<h3><span id="Al_Hurricane_&amp;_the_Night_Rockers_(1950s\xe2\x80\x931960s)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Al_Hurricane_.26_the_Night_Rockers_.281950s.E2.80.931960s.29">Al Hurricane &amp; the Night Rockers (1950s\xe2\x80\x931960s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Al Hurricane &amp; The two lead singles off that album were "Sentimiento" and its eponymous song "Mi Saxophone". "Sentimiento" had already been released in 1965 as a single. These early recordings were recorded on equipment purchased from <a href="/wiki/Norman_Petty" title="Norman Petty">Norman Petty</a>, that <a href="/wiki/Buddy_Holly" title="Buddy Holly">Buddy Holly</a> recorded on. This recording equipment gave the recordings their signature <a href="/wiki/1950s_in_music" title="1950s in music">50s</a> sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-concerts.eventful.com_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-concerts.eventful.com-21">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<p>He began to perform outside the New Mexico music scene, performing in throughout the <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">American Southwest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_United_States" title="Western United States">Western United States</a> in <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_(U.S._state)" title="Washington (U.S. state)">Washington</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>; he even toured Northern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baja_California" title="Baja California">Baja California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_(state)" title="Chihuahua (state)">Chihuahua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coahuila" title="Coahuila">Coahuila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Durango" title="Durango">Durango</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n">Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">Sonora</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walker2008_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Walker2008-9">[9]</a></sup> In fact, it was on his way to a concert in <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, Colorado. on November 1, 1969, where he got into a car accident in which he lost his right-eye.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> This did not deter him from pursuing his musical career, instead it gave him his unique performing look, an eye-patch across his right eye. He even went on to play other American cities in other regions such as <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>; Al Hurricane has even played internationally in the cities of <a href="/wiki/Asunci%C3%B3n" title="Asunci\xc3\xb3n">Asunci\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga" title="M\xc3\xa1laga">M\xc3\xa1laga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-concerts.eventful.com_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-concerts.eventful.com-21">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Al recorded several albums from 1967-to-1974, these included three albums recorded around 1973; <a href="/wiki/Canciones_del_Alma" title="Canciones del Alma">Canciones del Alma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sigue_Cantando" title="Sigue Cantando">Sigue Cantando</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Corridos_Canta" title="Corridos Canta">Corridos Canta</a>. These were each Spanish language releases, which continued to blend the sounds of New Mexico, the Southwestern United States\'. The <a href="/wiki/Latin_music_(genre)" title="Latin music (genre)">Latin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country</a>/<a href="/wiki/Western_music_(North_America)" title="Western music (North America)">western</a> sound was a hit each of the three communities of fans. Both Chicano and Country venues would be popular spots for Al Hurricane to play his music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SandbergWeissman1989_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-SandbergWeissman1989-23">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Another release at the time, <a href="/wiki/Instrumentales_con_Al_Hurricane" title="Instrumentales con Al Hurricane">Instrumentales con Al Hurricane</a>, saw Al return to his old <a href="/wiki/Instrumental" title="Instrumental">instrumental</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> style of music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SandbergWeissman1989_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-SandbergWeissman1989-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tatum2013_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tatum2013-25">[25]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In 1974, Al Hurricane saw two major milestones. his first full-length collaborative album with his brother Tiny Morrie; <a href="/wiki/Para_Las_Madrecitas" title="Para Las Madrecitas">Para Las Madrecitas</a>, the album is a tribute album to their mom, and a tribute to mothers in general. It also saw "Sentimiento", his first hit single, selling its two-hundred-and-fifty-thousandth copy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span id=\'"Val_de_la_O_Show"_&amp;_"The_Far_West_Club"_(1970s\xe2\x80\x931980s)\'></span><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Val_de_la_O_Show.22_.26_.22The_Far_West_Club.22_.281970s.E2.80.931980s.29">"Val de la O Show" &amp; "The Far West Club" (1970s\xe2\x80\x931980s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title=\'Edit section: "Val de la O Show" &amp; "The Far West Club" (1970s\xe2\x80\x931980s)\'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Al Hurricane performed on a popular nationally syndicated television show called the <a href="/wiki/Val_De_La_O_Show" title="Val De La O Show">Val De La O Show</a>. It was a <a href="/wiki/Talk_show" title="Talk show">talk show</a> and a music <a href="/wiki/Variety_show" title="Variety show">variety show</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In his appearances he chatted with the host, and performed new hits from his recently released albums, which included material from his albums up to <a href="/wiki/Vestido_Mojado" title="Vestido Mojado">Vestido Mojado</a>. The songs he played on the show also included a live performance of a <a href="/wiki/Mariachi" title="Mariachi">Mariachi</a> version of his hit "Sentimiento", to <a href="/wiki/Lip_sync" title="Lip sync">lip sync</a>-style <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a> of recent hits like "Vestido Mojado". The music video clips also contained some of his first video appearances performing with his son, Al Hurricane, Jr., as well as his brothers Tiny Morrie and Baby Gaby.</p>\n<p>During the late 1960s, Al Hurricane, Tiny Morrie, and Bennie Sanchez purchased The Sky Line Club and renamed it "The Far West" in the early 1970s. From here Al performed with a band billed as <b>Al Hurricane Band</b>. The nightclub also became a hotspot for Country/Western and Spanish language music and dance. Artists like <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Purple_Haze_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Purple Haze (band) (page does not exist)">Purple Haze</a> made the club their regular place to perform. It was also a spot where traveling Chicano and Tejano artists would perform, including <a href="/wiki/Selena" title="Selena">Selena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HISPANONEWMEXICO_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-HISPANONEWMEXICO-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LazellPayne2007_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-LazellPayne2007-28">[28]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In 1979, father and son, Al Hurricane and Al Hurricane, Jr. recorded their first album together called <a href="/wiki/Cantan_Corridos" title="Cantan Corridos">Cantan Corridos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span id=\'"La_Prision_de_Santa_Fe"_and_"Bandido"_(1980s\xe2\x80\x931990s)\'></span><span class="mw-headline" id=".22La_Prision_de_Santa_Fe.22_and_.22Bandido.22_.281980s.E2.80.931990s.29">"La Prision de Santa Fe" and "Bandido" (1980s\xe2\x80\x931990s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title=\'Edit section: "La Prision de Santa Fe" and "Bandido" (1980s\xe2\x80\x931990s)\'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>During Selena\'s concert in the early 1980s, which Bennie Sanchez promoted, Selena met Al Hurricane and heard him perform his song "Sentimiento". She went on to perform a cover, called "Sentimientos", on her <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpha_(%C3%A1lbum_de_Selena)" title="es:Alpha (\xc3\xa1lbum de Selena)">Alpha LP</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After the <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico_State_Penitentiary_riot" title="New Mexico State Penitentiary riot">New Mexico State Penitentiary riot</a>, in 1980, Al Hurricane wrote a song that was "strictly narrative, not a subjective account or soapbox. The song does not attempt to assign blame."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GarciaPeter2001_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-GarciaPeter2001-31">[31]</a></sup> The song was called "(El <a href="/wiki/Corrido" title="Corrido">Corrido</a> De) La Prison De Santa Fe" it is the lead song on an album titled, <a href="/wiki/La_Prision_de_Santa_Fe" title="La Prision de Santa Fe">La Prision de Santa Fe</a>.</p>\n<p>A few of Bennie Sanchez\'s kids, Al Hurricane, Tiny Morrie, and Baby Gaby, got together to do a tribute album for her. It was similar in concept as Al and Morrie\'s previous mother related album, but it was a bit more ambitious than their previous effort. <a href="/wiki/Madrecita,_Te_Debo_Tanto" title="Madrecita, Te Debo Tanto">Madrecita, Te Debo Tanto</a> also had performances by Lorenzo Antonio and Gloria Pohl, the wife of Tiny Morrie, the mother of Lorenzo Antonio.</p>\n<p>After these two projects, Al Hurricane became the lead singer in the <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> <a href="/wiki/Bandido_(supergroup)" title="Bandido (supergroup)">Bandido</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Madrid2011_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Madrid2011-32">[32]</a></sup> The band released four albums during the 1980s,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walker2008_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Walker2008-9">[9]</a></sup> it saw success not just on New Mexico radio; but also in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">along the west coast</a>, and internationally in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.</p>\n<p>The 80s also saw the release of another collaborative effort, this time it was a collaborative compilation with Morrie and Jr., entitled <a href="/wiki/15_Exitos_Rancheros" title="15 Exitos Rancheros">15 Exitos Rancheros</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OfficialDiscography_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-OfficialDiscography-33">[33]</a></sup> The album was a hit within Albuquerque, and got plenty of airtime on <a href="/wiki/KLVO_(FM)" title="KLVO (FM)">Radio Lobo</a>.</p>\n<p>After three Bandido releases, two collaborative family LPs, and a compilation album called <a href="/wiki/Exitos_De_Al_Hurricane" title="Exitos De Al Hurricane">Exitos De Al Hurricane</a>. Al Hurricane released his next solo album <a href="/wiki/The_Return_of_Al_Hurricane_%22EL%22_Godfather" title=\'The Return of Al Hurricane "EL" Godfather\'>The Return of Al Hurricane "EL" Godfather</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OfficialDiscography_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-OfficialDiscography-33">[33]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span id=\'"La_Leyenda"_and_"The_Godfather"_(1990s\xe2\x80\x932000s)\'></span><span class="mw-headline" id=".22La_Leyenda.22_and_.22The_Godfather.22_.281990s.E2.80.932000s.29">"La Leyenda" and "The Godfather" (1990s\xe2\x80\x932000s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title=\'Edit section: "La Leyenda" and "The Godfather" (1990s\xe2\x80\x932000s)\'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>He did another collaborative compilation album with Tiny Morrie and Al Hurricane, Jr. <a href="/wiki/15_Exitos_Rancheros,_Vol._2" title="15 Exitos Rancheros, Vol. 2">15 Exitos Rancheros, Vol. 2</a> in 1994.</p>\n<p>Once he returned to his solo albums Al Hurricane began to focus more on <a href="/wiki/Ranchera" title="Ranchera">Ranchera</a> music<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walker2008_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-Walker2008-9">[9]</a></sup> on his 1995 <a href="/wiki/Sigue..._%22La_Leyenda%22!!!" title=\'Sigue... "La Leyenda"!!!\'>Sigue... "La Leyenda"!!!</a> and mid-90s album <i>The Legend of New Mexico</i>. Not only did he want emphasize the Ranchera sound, he also wanted to focus on his roots in Rock and Country/Western, he re-added <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Berry" title="Chuck Berry">Chuck Berry</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Johnny_B._Goode" title="Johnny B. Goode">Johnny B. Goode</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hank_Williams" title="Hank Williams">Hank Williams</a>\' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jambalaya_(song)" title="Jambalaya (song)">Jambalaya</a> to his live-music repertoire.</p>\n<p>In 2000, Al Hurricane was the subject of a documentary by DJR Productions, <i>Al Hurricane: Native Legend</i>. The documentary focused on the life and career of Al Hurricane. It ran sixty minutes, and contained old footage and photographs of Al Hurricane throughout his life. It also contained live performances of "Sentimiento", "Johnny B. Goode", and his, then unreleased, "Siempre".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The album, <i>Siempre</i>, came out soon after the release of the documentary.; it continued Al Hurricane\'s focus on Ranchera and Western sound, while adding in a <a href="/wiki/Cumbia" title="Cumbia">Cumbia</a> vibe into the beat. <i>\xc2\xa1Que Viva El Godfather!</i>, released in 2003, showed an increased enthusiasm to include an even stronger mix of Ranchera and Cumbia. Both albums contained number one hits on local New Mexico radio stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HISPANONEWMEXICO_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-HISPANONEWMEXICO-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Al Hurricane\'s touring hadn\'t stopped, he had continued to tour nationally and internationally. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saginaw" title="Saginaw">Saginaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, Al even managed to pull in a crowd of 8,000 people. According to a local paper, "the bouncy music was something even those who don\'t habla Espa\xc3\xb1ol still could savor." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span id=\'"Tribute"_and_"Hey_Sugar_Baby!"_(2000s\xe2\x80\x932010s)\'></span><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Tribute.22_and_.22Hey_Sugar_Baby.21.22_.282000s.E2.80.932010s.29">"Tribute" and "Hey Sugar Baby!" (2000s\xe2\x80\x932010s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title=\'Edit section: "Tribute" and "Hey Sugar Baby!" (2000s\xe2\x80\x932010s)\'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Al Hurricane recorded his next album, <i>Albuquerque</i>, in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AaronMartinezABQtrib_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-AaronMartinezABQtrib-8">[8]</a></sup> The following year <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_of_Isleta#Casino" title="Pueblo of Isleta">Isleta Casino &amp; Showroom</a> played host to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Tribute_To_Al_Hurricane" title="A Tribute To Al Hurricane">A Tribute To Al Hurricane</a>, the concert was recorded and released a set of two CDs and a DVD. The DVD contained small documentary-like slideshows that educate the audience in order to inform them on the upcoming artists and songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AlibiTributeGodfather_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-AlibiTributeGodfather-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The last album he released during his lifetime, 2010\'s <i>Hey Sugar Baby!</i> had Al Hurricane playing around his sound in fun and creative ways. The album ventures into <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">Reggae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reggaeton" title="Reggaeton">Reggaeton</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Italy#Folk_music" title="Music of Italy">Italian</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazilian_music#Popular_music" title="Brazilian music">Brazilian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Turkey" title="Music of Turkey">Turkish</a> genres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_death">Personal life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>He was married twice. He had eight children: Al Hurricane Jr., Darlene, Sandra, and Jerry Dean from his first marriage, and Nelson, Erika, and Danielle and Lynnea from his second marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Lynnea, the twin of Danielle, died at the age of 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>\n<p>He
