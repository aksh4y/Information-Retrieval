identified by <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> as <b>Progress 12</b> or <b>12P</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">Progress</a> spacecraft used to resupply the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>. It was a <a href="/wiki/Progress-M" title="Progress-M">Progress-M</a> 11F615A55 spacecraft, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serial_number" title="Serial number">serial number</a> 248.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-LL_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-JSR-LL-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Progress M-48 was launched by a <a href="/wiki/Soyuz-U" title="Soyuz-U">Soyuz-U</a> carrier rocket from <a href="/wiki/Gagarin%27s_Start" title="Gagarin\'s Start">Site 1/5</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a>. Launch occurred at 01:47:59 GMT on 29 August 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-LL_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-JSR-LL-1">[1]</a></sup> The spacecraft docked with the Aft port of the <i><a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">Zvezda</a></i> module at 03:40:45 GMT on 31 August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EA_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EA-3">[3]</a></sup> It remained docked for five months before undocking at 08:35:56 GMT on 28 January 2004<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">[2]</a></sup> to make way for <a href="/wiki/Progress_M1-11" title="Progress M1-11">Progress M1-11</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSW_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-RSW-4">[4]</a></sup> It was deorbited at 13:11 GMT on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">[2]</a></sup> The spacecraft burned up in the atmosphere over the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, with any remaining debris landing in the ocean at around 13:57:12 GMT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-SC_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-JSR-SC-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Progress M-48 carried supplies to the International Space Station, including food, water and oxygen for the crew and equipment for conducting scientific research.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
