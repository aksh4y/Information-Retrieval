the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the population was 33,920.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QF_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-QF-1">[1]</a></sup> Its <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> is <a href="/wiki/Manteo,_North_Carolina" title="Manteo, North Carolina">Manteo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-2">[2]</a></sup> The county is named after <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Dare" title="Virginia Dare">Virginia Dare</a>, the first child born in the Americas to English parents, who was born in what is now Dare County.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Dare County is included in the <a href="/wiki/Kill_Devil_Hills,_North_Carolina" title="Kill Devil Hills, North Carolina">Kill Devil Hills</a>, NC <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Micropolitan_Statistical_Area" title="Micropolitan Statistical Area">Micropolitan Statistical Area</a>, which is also included in the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach</a>-<a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">VA</a>-NC <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Combined Statistical Area</a>.</p>\n<p>At one time, the now-abandoned town of <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_City,_North_Carolina" title="Buffalo City, North Carolina">Buffalo City</a> was the largest community in the county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Buffalo_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Buffalo-4">[4]</a></sup> Because it includes much of <a href="/wiki/Pamlico_Sound" title="Pamlico Sound">Pamlico Sound</a>, Dare County is the largest county in North Carolina by total area, although if one were to consider land area only, it drops down to 68th in size among the state\'s 100 counties. This is because, according to the Census Bureau\'s 2010 statistics, only 24.54% of its area is land, the lowest percentage of all counties in the state. <a href="/wiki/Robeson_County,_North_Carolina" title="Robeson County, North Carolina">Robeson County</a> is the largest county in North Carolina by land area only.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-11">[11]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 33,920 people, 12,690 households, and 8,450 families residing in the county. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 78 people per square mile (30/km\xc2\xb2). There were 26,671 housing units at an average density of 70 per square\xc2\xa0mile (27/km\xc2\xb2). The racial makeup of the county was 92.3% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 2.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.6% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.0% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 2.4% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.8% from two or more races. 6.5% of the population were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.</p>\n<p>There were 12,690 households out of which 27.30% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 55.00% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.10% had a female householder with no husband present, and 33.40% were non-families. 25.00% of all households were made up of individuals and 7.90% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.34 and the average family size was 2.79.</p>\n<p>In the county, the population was spread out with 21.40% under the age of 18, 6.30% from 18 to 24, 30.80% from 25 to 44, 27.70% from 45 to 64, and 13.80% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 40 years. For every 100 females there were 101.50 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 100.20 males.</p>\n<p>The median income for a household in the county was $42,411, and the median income for a family was $49,302. Males had a median income of $31,240 versus $24,318 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the county was $23,614. About 5.50% of families and 8.00% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 9.90% of those under age 18 and 5.30% of those age 65 or over.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Communities">Communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a No Democratic presidential nominee has carried <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dare_County" title="Dare County">Dare County</a> since Jimmy Carter did so in 1976. Before the 1970s it was a typical \xe2\x80\x9c<a href="/wiki/Solid_South" title="Solid South">Solid South</a>\xe2\x80\x9d Democratic county that did not vote Republican between 1900 and 1952 \xe2\x80\x93 a period during which the South\xe2\x80\x99s black population was effectively completely <a href="/wiki/Disenfranchisement_after_the_Reconstruction_Era" title="Disenfranchisement after the Reconstruction Era">disfranchised</a>.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_presidential_primary">2016 presidential primary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dare_County,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2016 presidential primary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_presidential_primaries,_2016" title="Republican Party presidential primaries, 2016">2016 Republican primary</a>, Donald Trump received 2,650 votes in Dare County followed by Ted Cruz who came in second with 1,156 votes. In the 2016 Democratic Primary, Bernie Sanders received 2,307 votes whereas Hillary Clinton only won 2,003 votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In the general election Donald Trump received 11,460 votes (or 58.4% of the total vote) whereas Hillary Clinton received 7,222 votes (36.8% of the vote) and Libertarian Candidate Gary Johnson received 674 votes (3.4% of total votes in the county).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In this regards Dare County has the distinction of being one of many counties in the state of North Carolina which Donald Trump won in both the primary election and the general election, and which Hillary Clinton lost in both the primary election and the general election.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_government">Law and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dare_County,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Law and government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Dare County is governed by the <a href="/wiki/Dare_County_Board_of_Commissioners" title="Dare County Board of Commissioners">Dare County Board of Commissioners</a>. Dare County is a part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Albemarle_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Albemarle Commission (page does not exist)">Albemarle Commission</a> regional council of governments.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lighthouses">Lighthouses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dare_County,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Lighthouses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Dare County is home to two popular lighthouses: The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras_Lighthouse" title="Cape Hatteras Lighthouse">Cape Hatteras Lighthouse</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bodie_Island_Lighthouse" title="Bodie Island Lighthouse">Bodie Island Lighthouse</a>. There is also a beacon atop the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wright_Brothers_Memorial" title="Wright Brothers Memorial">Wright Brothers Memorial</a>. A third lighthouse was built by the Town of Manteo and dedicated on September 25, 2004. The Roanoke Marshes Lighthouse is an exterior recreation of the 1877 screwpile lighthouse of the same name and is located on the Manteo waterfront. It serves as exhibit space for the N.C. Maritime Museum on Roanoke Island.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
