colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The <b>1933 Cuba\xe2\x80\x93Brownsville hurricane</b> was one of two storms in the <a href="/wiki/1933_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1933 Atlantic hurricane season">1933 Atlantic hurricane season</a> to reach the intensity of a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5</a> strength on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>. It formed on August\xc2\xa022 off the west coast of Africa, and for much of its duration it maintained a west-northwest track. The system intensified into a tropical storm on August\xc2\xa026 and into a hurricane on August\xc2\xa028. Passing north of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>, the hurricane rapidly intensified as it approached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos" title="Turks and Caicos">Turks and Caicos</a> islands. It reached Category\xc2\xa05 status and its peak winds of 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h) on August\xc2\xa031. Subsequently, it weakened before striking northern Cuba on September\xc2\xa01 with winds of 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h). In the country, the hurricane left about 100,000\xc2\xa0people homeless and killed over 70\xc2\xa0people. Damage was heaviest near the storm\'s path, and the strong winds destroyed houses and left areas without power. Damage was estimated at $11\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[nb 1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After exiting from Cuba, the hurricane entered the Gulf of Mexico and restrengthened. On September\xc2\xa02, it re-attained winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h). Initially the hurricane posed a threat to the area around <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi, Texas</a>, and the local <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">United States Weather Bureau</a> forecaster advised people to stay away from the Texas coastline during the busy <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day" title="Labor Day">Labor Day Weekend</a>. Officials declared martial law in the city and mandated evacuations. However, the hurricane turned more to the west and struck near <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">Brownsville</a> early on September\xc2\xa05 with winds estimated at 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h). It quickly dissipated after causing heavy damage in the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_Valley" title="Rio Grande Valley">Rio Grande Valley</a>. High winds caused heavy damage to the citrus crop. The hurricane left $16.9\xc2\xa0million in damage and 40\xc2\xa0deaths in southern Texas.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1933_Cuba%E2%80%93Brownsville_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1933_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/1933_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png/220px-1933_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/1933_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png/330px-1933_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/1933_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png/440px-1933_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1933_Atlantic_hurricane_10_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The origins of the hurricane were from a tropical disturbance near the west coast of Africa on August\xc2\xa020. Two days later, the system organized enough to be classified a tropical depression just southwest of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>. For the next few days, the system moved west-northwestward with little change in intensity. It is estimated the depression strengthened into a tropical storm on August\xc2\xa026, about halfway between the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> and Cape Verde. Initially, the storm was elongated, although beginning on August\xc2\xa028 it began to intensify more quickly. That night, the storm attained hurricane status, and many nearby ships reported gale force winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-meta-2">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On August\xc2\xa029, the hurricane passed north of the Lesser Antilles as it approached the southeastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>. It underwent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid deepening</a>: in a 24\xe2\x80\x91hour period beginning late on August\xc2\xa029, the winds increased from 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h) to 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h). It also became a small storm, as <a href="/wiki/Grand_Turk_Island" title="Grand Turk Island">Grand Turk Island</a> reported winds of 56\xc2\xa0mph (90\xc2\xa0km/h) while the hurricane passed slightly to the north on August\xc2\xa030. At 0130\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> the next day, a ship near <a href="/wiki/Mayaguana" title="Mayaguana">Mayaguana</a> reported a <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 930\xc2\xa0mbar (27\xc2\xa0inHg) and hurricane-force winds. The pressure would ordinarily suggest winds of 152\xc2\xa0mph (245\xc2\xa0km/h), but because it was not reported in the <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> and the storm was smaller than normal, the winds were estimated at 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h). The hurricane\'s winds rank as a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>, one of two such storms in the 1933 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-meta-2">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After maintaining peak winds for about 12\xc2\xa0hours, the hurricane began weakening as it passed through the southern Bahamas. At around 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa01, the hurricane made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on northern Cuba near <a href="/wiki/Sagua_La_Grande" title="Sagua La Grande">Sagua La Grande</a>, with winds of about 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h). The eye moved along the northern coast of Cuba, crossing over <a href="/wiki/Matanzas" title="Matanzas">Matanzas</a>. Shortly thereafter the storm exited into the <a href="/wiki/Straits_of_Florida" title="Straits of Florida">Straits of Florida</a>, and late on September\xc2\xa01 the hurricane passed about 16\xc2\xa0mi (26\xc2\xa0km) north of <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>. After entering the Gulf of Mexico, the hurricane restrengthened, and a ship reported a pressure of 948\xc2\xa0mbar (28.0\xc2\xa0inHg) late on September\xc2\xa02; this suggested winds of about 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h). The hurricane turned more to the west on September\xc2\xa03, and as it approached southern Texas it weakened slightly as it decelerated. At 0400\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa05, the hurricane made its final landfall on <a href="/wiki/South_Padre_Island" title="South Padre Island">South Padre Island</a> in southern Texas, with winds estimated at 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h). It quickly weakened over land as it crossed into northeastern Mexico, and the storm dissipated late on September\xc2\xa05.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-meta-2">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1933_Cuba%E2%80%93Brownsville_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:September_1933_Texas_rainfall.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="414" data-file-width="423" height="215" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/September_1933_Texas_rainfall.png/220px-September_1933_Texas_rainfall.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/September_1933_Texas_rainfall.png/330px-September_1933_Texas_rainfall.png 1.5x, href="/wiki/File:September_1933_Texas_rainfall.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRainfall totals in south Texas</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Throughout its path, the hurricane killed at least 179\xc2\xa0people collectively in the Turks and Caicos Islands, Cuba, and south Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup> It first affected the Turks and Caicos, producing winds of 54\xc2\xa0mph (87\xc2\xa0km/h) on Grand Turk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Before the hurricane struck Cuba, officials warned of the impending storm, and military workers warned people to remain indoors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pb_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pb-5">[4]</a></sup> About 4,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated the coastal town of Isabela on three trains specifically run for the residents. In Havana, business owners secured their properties in anticipation of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> Most of the deaths from the hurricane occurred in northern Cuba. Winds of over 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) affected the coastline, and Havana reported maximum winds of 94\xc2\xa0mph (151\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-meta-2">[1]</a></sup> There, the winds downed power lines and uprooted trees. High waves flooded six blocks of the city with up to 4\xc2\xa0ft (1.2\xc2\xa0m) of water, entering the cellars of some homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pb_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-pb-5">[4]</a></sup> East of Havana in <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1rdenas,_Cuba" title="C\xc3\xa1rdenas, Cuba">C\xc3\xa1rdenas</a>, high waves destroyed the aqueduct, while high winds severely damaged the local sugar industry. A nearby coastal town 20\xc2\xa0mi (32\xc2\xa0km) east of C\xc3\xa1rdenas was described as being "virtually leveled by the storm" in news reports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Cienfuegos" title="Cienfuegos">Cienfuegos</a> along the southern coast, the hurricane destroyed many ships and piers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pb_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-pb-5">[4]</a></sup> High waves washed four ships ashore, one of which damaged another ship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup> High winds destroyed hundreds of houses and damaged many others along the storm\'s path.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94c_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-94c-9">[8]</a></sup> About 100,000\xc2\xa0people were left homeless, many of whom lacking food or medical supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> In the interior of the country, the hurricane dropped rainfall that flooded rivers and towns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> Damage was estimated at $11\xc2\xa0million, and a report published in 2003 indicated that there were about 70\xc2\xa0deaths in the country;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> however, newspaper accounts from shortly after the hurricane indicated there were around 100\xc2\xa0deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> After the storm, police officers were deployed to maintain order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pb_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-pb-5">[4]</a></sup> Officers shot and killed five people caught looting in Havana, although the deaths were not included in the overall death toll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup> Officials deployed trucks to pick up wounded citizens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<p>While the hurricane was over the Bahamas late on August\xc2\xa030, the <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">United States Weather Bureau</a> issued <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">storm warnings</a> for southern Florida. Two days later, the storm struck Cuba and passed south of the state, producing peak winds of 42\xc2\xa0mph (68\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">Key West</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[3]</a></sup> The winds caused little damage; however, high waves destroyed a sea wall and washed onto a coastal roadway. High waves also sank a boat, killing three people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loc_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-loc-16">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Three days before the hurricane made its final landfall, officials at the Weather Bureau in Brownsville issued a warning to all stations along the Texas coast, declaring that it was "uncertain where tropical storm in Gulf will reach coast line, but all persons should be warned to remain away from inaccessible places on Texas coast over week end." The early warning was to prevent travelers from being on the Texas beaches on <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day" title="Labor Day">Labor Day Weekend</a>. As a result, an official at the <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi</a> Weather Bureau office estimated that the warning "probably saved thousands of lives." Early on September\xc2\xa04 about a day before landfall, the Weather Bureau issued a hurricane warning from Corpus Christi to <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Texas" title="Freeport, Texas">Freeport</a> and storm warnings for other locations along the Texas coast. When the storm\'s westward motion became more apparent, the hurricane warning was dropped north of Corpus Christi and extended southward to Brownsville.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[3]</a></sup> Officials in Corpus Christi declared <a href="/wiki/Martial_law" title="Martial law">martial law</a> before the storm struck and ordered mandatory evacuations of low-lying areas. Shelters opened in the city, and many businesses closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corp_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-corp-17">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<p>When the storm moved across the area, winds were estimated at 90\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h) in Brownsville,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[3]</a></sup> with gusts to 125\xc2\xa0mph (201\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-18">[17]</a></sup> High tides were reported along the coast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loc_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-loc-16">[15]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> reached 13\xc2\xa0ft (4.0\xc2\xa0m) near Brownsville. The tides flooded portions of Corpus Christi about 3\xc2\xa0ft (0.91\xc2\xa0m) deep, sinking boats and damaging piers. Storm-generated waves destroyed 20\xc2\xa0buildings in one settlement. The storm destroyed the causeway from <a href="/wiki/Padre_Island" title="Padre Island">Padre Island</a> to <a href="/wiki/Flour_Bluff,_Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Flour Bluff, Corpus Christi, Texas">Flour Bluff</a>, and there were over 40\xc2\xa0breaches in the island,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-18">[17]</a></sup> some up to a mile wide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corp_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-corp-17">[16]</a></sup> The storm dropped heavy rainfall from southern Texas through northeastern Mexico,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-butterfree_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-butterfree-19">[18]</a></sup> peaking at over 15.0\xc2\xa0in (380\xc2\xa0mm) at a station near <a href="/wiki/Mercedes,_Texas" title="Mercedes, Texas">Mercedes</a>; the total was one of four 24\xe2\x80\x91hour precipitation records set by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-18">[17]</a></sup> The rains from the storm and two previous hurricanes led to a large increase in tropical butterfly species across the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-butterfree_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-butterfree-19">[18]</a></sup> Throughout the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_Valley" title="Rio Grande Valley">Rio Grande Valley</a>, the strong winds destroyed about 90% of the citrus crop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-18">[17]</a></sup> Overall, the hurricane left $16.9\xc2\xa0million in damage and 40\xc2\xa0deaths, mostly in <a href="/wiki/Cameron_County,_Texas" title="Cameron County, Texas">Cameron County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loc_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-loc-16">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_18-4"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-18">[17]</a></sup> However, none of the deaths were in Brownsville or Corpus Christi, which was credited to the advance warning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[3]</a></sup> Damage was less than expected in Corpus Christi, and many business owners who lost revenue sent letters of outcry to the Weather Bureau Headquarters; however, the Bureau determined that the evacuations and warnings were warranted given the storm\'s threat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corp_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-corp-17">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
