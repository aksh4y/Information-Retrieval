It was the tenth named <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">storm</a>, the seventh <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">hurricane</a>, and the fifth major hurricane of the season, as well as the third hurricane and fourth named storm of the season to make landfall in Florida. After wreaking havoc on Hispaniola, Jeanne struggled to reorganize, eventually strengthening and performing a complete loop over the open Atlantic. It headed westwards, strengthening into a Category 3 hurricane and passing over the islands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Abaco" title="Great Abaco">Great Abaco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Bahama" title="Grand Bahama">Grand Bahama</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> on September 25. Jeanne made landfall later in the day in Florida just 2\xc2\xa0miles (3\xc2\xa0kilometers) from where Frances had struck a mere 3\xc2\xa0weeks earlier. Building on the rainfall of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Frances" title="Hurricane Frances">Frances</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">Ivan</a>, Jeanne brought near-record flood levels as far north as <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> before its remnants turned east into the open Atlantic. Jeanne is blamed for at least 3,006 deaths in <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> with about 2,800 in <a href="/wiki/Gona%C3%AFves" title="Gona\xc3\xafves">Gona\xc3\xafves</a> alone, which was nearly washed away by floods and mudslides. The storm also caused 7 deaths in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, 18 in the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> and at least 4 in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, bringing the total number of deaths to at least 3,025; Jeanne is the 12th deadliest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a> ever. Final property damage in the United States was $6.8\xc2\xa0billion, making this the 13th costliest hurricane in U.S. history.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div September 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> By that time, Jeanne had weakened to tropical depression strength.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>, a new center formed well to the northeast and the previous circulation dissipated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The system restrengthened, becoming a hurricane on September 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Jeanne continued to meander for several days (making a complete loop in the process) before beginning a steady westward motion toward the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Jeanne continued strengthening as it headed west, passing over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Abaco" title="Great Abaco">Great Abaco</a> in the Bahamas on the morning of September 25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Shortly thereafter, the hurricane reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 3</a> strength.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Jeanne maintained this intensity as it passed <a href="/wiki/Grand_Bahama" title="Grand Bahama">Grand Bahama</a> Island. At 11:50\xc2\xa0p.m. EDT on September 25 (0350 UTC September 26), Jeanne made landfall on <a href="/wiki/Hutchinson_Island_(Florida)" title="Hutchinson Island (Florida)">Hutchinson Island</a>, just east of <a href="/wiki/Sewall%27s_Point,_Florida" title="Sewall\'s Point, Florida">Sewall\'s Point, Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stuart,_Florida" title="Stuart, Florida">Stuart, Florida</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_Saint_Lucie,_Florida" title="Port Saint Lucie, Florida">Port Saint Lucie, Florida</a>, at Category\xc2\xa03 strength.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This is the same place <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Frances" title="Hurricane Frances">Hurricane Frances</a> struck Florida three weeks earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Jeanne\'s track continued to follow within 20 miles (32\xc2\xa0km) of that of Frances until it reached <a href="/wiki/Pasco_County,_Florida" title="Pasco County, Florida">Pasco County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-16">[16]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclone</a> then swung more rapidly to the north, and the center remained over land all the way to the <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> state line, unlike Frances which exited into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. Jeanne became an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> over <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> on September 28 and the system moved back into the Atlantic offshore the <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> coast the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-16">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Puerto_Rico_and_the_Leeward_Islands">Puerto Rico and the Leeward Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Puerto Rico and the Leeward Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On the afternoon of September 13, tropical storm watches were issued for the British Virgin Islands, Saba, St. Eustatius, and St. Maarten while tropical storm warnings were raised for <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and the U.S. Virgin Islands. The watches were upgraded to tropical storm warnings early on the morning of September 14. Later in the morning, tropical storm warnings were issued for St. Kitts and Nevis, while tropical storm watches were issued for Anguilla. During the afternoon, tropical storm warnings were lowered for Saba, St. Eustatius, and St. Maarten, while hurricane warnings were issued for Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands. Late on the morning of September 15, a hurricane watch was issued for the British Virgin Islands. That afternoon, tropical storm warnings were dropped for St. Kitts and Nevis, while hurricane warnings were lowered to tropical storm warnings for the U.S. Virgin Islands. On the evening of September 15, tropical storm warnings were dropped Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands, while hurricane warnings were downgraded to tropical storm warnings for Puerto Rico, and all watches and warnings were dropped for the British Virgin Islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup> The entire power grid of Puerto Rico was shut down by the government of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sila_Maria_Calder%C3%B3n" title="Sila Maria Calder\xc3\xb3n">Sila Maria Calder\xc3\xb3n</a> as the storm approached to prevent electrocutions and infrastructure damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:272px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jeanne_2004_Hispaniola.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4000" 16.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Tropical storm watches were issued from Cabrera to Isla Saona early in the afternoon on September 14. Later that afternoon, hurricane watches and tropical storm warnings were raised from Cabrera to Santo Domingo. Late in the morning of September 15, hurricane warnings were issued from Cabrera to Isla Saona, while hurricane watches and tropical storm warnings were raised from Cabrera to Puerto Plata. That evening, hurricane warnings were extended westward from Cabrera to Puerto Plata while hurricane watches and tropical storm warnings were issued from Puerto Plata to Monte Cristi. Late in the morning of September 16, tropical storm warnings were issued from <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B4le-Saint-Nicolas" title="M\xc3\xb4le-Saint-Nicolas">M\xc3\xb4le-Saint-Nicolas</a> to Puerto Plata. That afternoon, hurricane warnings were downgraded to tropical storm warnings from Puerto Plata to Isla Saona while all hurricane watches were dropped. Late on the afternoon of September 17, tropical storm warnings were dropped for the remainder of Hispaniola.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bahamas">Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the Bahamas, the first hurricane watch was issued at 2100\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa015 and included <a href="/wiki/Acklins_and_Crooked_Islands" title="Acklins and Crooked Islands">Acklins and Crooked Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inagua" title="Inagua">Inagua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayaguana" title="Mayaguana">Mayaguana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ragged_Island,_Bahamas" title="Ragged Island, Bahamas">Ragged Island</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a>. By 1500\xc2\xa0UTC the next day, that hurricane watch was upgraded to a hurricane warning. Simultaneously, another hurricane watch became in effect for <a href="/wiki/Cat_Island,_Bahamas" title="Cat Island, Bahamas">Cat Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exuma" title="Exuma">Exuma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Long_Island,_Bahamas" title="Long Island, Bahamas">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rum_Cay" title="Rum Cay">Rum Cay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">San Salvador</a>. The hurricane warning was downgraded to a tropical storm warning at 2100\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa017, while the hurricane watch was lowered to a tropical storm watch. Both the tropical storm warning and tropical storm watch were discontinued at 1000\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Although Jeanne then tracked away from the Bahamas, the storm was threatening the archipelago again by September\xc2\xa023. As a result, the tropical storm watch for Cat Island, Exumas, Long Island, Rum Cay and San Salvador was resulted at 0900\xc2\xa0UTC. A hurricane watch was then issued for the northwestern Bahamas by 1730\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa023. Around 0900\xc2\xa0UTC the next day, a hurricane warning was posted for Abaco Islands, <a href="/wiki/Andros,_Bahamas" title="Andros, Bahamas">Andros Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berry_Islands" title="Berry Islands">Berry Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bimini" title="Bimini">Bimini</a>, Eleuthera, Grand Bahama Island, and <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a>, while a tropical storm watch was simultaneously issued for Cat Island, Exumas, Long Island, Rum Cay, and San Salvador. Late on September\xc2\xa025, the tropical storm warning was canceled. Early on the following day, the hurricane warning was downgraded to a tropical storm warning for Abaco Islands, Berry Islands, Bimini and Grand Bahama Island, while the remaining portion \xe2\x80\x93 Andros Islands, Eleuthera and New Providence \xe2\x80\x93 was discontinued. All tropical cyclone warnings and watches were canceled by 0900\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa026.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Because Hurricane Frances struck only about two week prior, numerous houses were still patched with plastic sheeting on their roofs, while other residents were still living with neighbors or relatives. Officials urged residents in low-lying homes to evacuate. Shelters were set up at the churches and schools on Abaco Islands, Eleuthera, Grand Bahama. At least 700\xc2\xa0people went to a shelter in Abaco Islands alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Preparations in <a href="/wiki/Central_Florida" title="Central Florida">Central Florida</a> were rushed and sudden, as it did not become apparent that the storm would make a direct hit until the morning of the 23rd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Indeed, it had appeared the storm would pass safely offshore just the night before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Voluntary evacuations were advised on Thursday and Friday, plans for opening shelters on Saturday were distributed to the public, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Power_and_Light" title="Florida Power and Light">Florida Power and Light</a> warned that power could be out "for an extended period of time".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-21">[21]</a></sup> Canals were also drained on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-21">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On Friday, the <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_Zoo" title="Palm Beach Zoo">Palm Beach Zoo</a> prepared for the storm by moving small animals and birds into buildings such as restrooms and restaurants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Evacuations began in earnest, with many residents leaving for the Keys, noting that the islands were the only location definitely out of harm\'s way. For once, evacuation to the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">Keys</a> made sense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The center of Jeanne\'s eye achieved landfall near Stuart, at virtually the identical spot that Frances had come ashore three weeks earlier, the first time in record keeping that a hurricane made landfall in the same place as a previous storm of the same season. Maximum winds at the time of landfall were estimated to be near 120 m.p.h.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hurricane_History_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hurricane_History-24">[24]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a colspan="3"></td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="6"><small>Because of differing sources, totals may not match.</small></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Guadeloupe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In its early states, Jeanne dropped heavy rainfall in Guadeloupe, peaking at 11.81 inches (300\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The communes of <a href="/wiki/Bouillante" title="Bouillante">Bouillante</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deshaies" title="Deshaies">Deshaies</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pointe-Noire,_Guadeloupe" title="Pointe-Noire, Guadeloupe">Pointe-Noire</a> were the hardest hit. In Bouillante, 60\xc2\xa0homes were damaged. The storm ruptured water pipes in the city of Bois Malher, isolating about 1,000\xc2\xa0people. Damage to businesses resulted in 30\xc2\xa0employees being laid-off. Crops also suffered impact, especially bananas. At the Malendure resort, which is located along the coast, the pier, restaurants, and dive base were rendered unusable. In Deshaies, 110\xc2\xa0homes were severely damaged, including 60\xc2\xa0in the city of Ferry. About a dozen boats were beached or capsized. Many roads and bridges were inflicted with damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maxi_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-maxi-26">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Point-Noire, nearly 300\xc2\xa0single-family homes were damaged or demolished. Three bridges were destroyed, while numerous roads were also affected. Further south in <a href="/wiki/Vieux-Habitants" title="Vieux-Habitants">Vieux-Habitants</a>, roads also suffered damage, particularly in the Beaugendre area, leaving a dozen households isolated. A primary school was impacted beyond repairs. In <a href="/wiki/Saint-Fran%C3%A7ois,_Guadeloupe" title="Saint-Fran\xc3\xa7ois, Guadeloupe">Saint-Fran\xc3\xa7ois</a>, a trench was dug along a major highway to prevent a residential subdivision from flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maxi_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-maxi-26">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Puerto_Rico">Puerto Rico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Puerto Rico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Hurricane_Jeanne_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Effects of Hurricane Jeanne in Puerto Rico">Effects of Hurricane Jeanne in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>Puerto Rico was impacted by tropical storm force winds and heavy rain, with flooding on a historic scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hurricane_History_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hurricane_History-24">[24]</a></sup> The storm made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Maunabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Maunabo, Puerto Rico">Maunabo</a> midday on September 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-27">[27]</a></sup> The storm generally moved northwest through the island, exiting on the northwest coast near the town of Mayag\xc3\xbcez around 11 p.m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-27">[27]</a></sup> Jeanne passed directly over the towns of <a href="/wiki/Arroyo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arroyo, Puerto Rico">Arroyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patillas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Patillas, Puerto Rico">Patillas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Salinas, Puerto Rico">Salinas</a> on its trip over the Commonwealth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-27">[27]</a></sup> San Juan reported a wind gust of 73\xc2\xa0mph (117\xc2\xa0km/h),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-7"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup> Carolina reported gusts to 71\xc2\xa0mph (114\xc2\xa0km/h),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_27-3"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-27">[27]</a></sup> and rainfall ranged from 5.98 inches (152\xc2\xa0mm) in the city to over 24 inches (610\xc2\xa0mm) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vieques" title="Vieques">Vieques</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-28">[28]</a></sup> This excessive rainfall resulted in damage to roads, landslides, and collapsed bridges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-28">[28]</a></sup> This resulted in one death and the evacuation of 400 people near the R\xc3\xado Grande de A\xc3\xb1asco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-28">[28]</a></sup> A total of eight people were reported dead in Puerto Rico as a result of Jeanne. Damages from the storm were estimated at $169.5\xc2\xa0million (2004\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Haiti">Haiti</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Haiti">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Haiti_flood_1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="330" data-file-width="248" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Haiti_flood_1.jpg/220px-Haiti_flood_1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/31/Haiti_flood_1.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Haiti_flood_1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nFlooding in Haiti</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>By September 17, heavy rains totaling about 13\xc2\xa0inches (330\xc2\xa0mm) in the northern mountains of <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> caused severe flooding and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mudslide" title="Mudslide">mudslides</a> in the department of <a href="/wiki/Artibonite_(department)" title="Artibonite (department)">Artibonite</a>, causing particular damage in the coastal city of <a href="/wiki/Gona%C3%AFves" title="Gona\xc3\xafves">Gona\xc3\xafves</a>, where it affected about 80,000 of the city\'s 100,000 residents. As of October 6, 2004 the official report counted 3,006 people dead, with 2,826 of those in Gona\xc3\xafves alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Another 2,601 people were injured, and 7 people died.</p>\n<p>Decades of deforestation left surrounding valleys unable to hold the 30 hours of rain from Hurricane Jeanne, causing massive landslides. Aid trucks were forced to ford floodwaters and mudslides on National Route 1. In addition, the flooding destroyed all of the rice and fruit harvest in the Artibonite which has been regarded as "Haiti\'s breadbasket". Some residents had buried unclaimed corpses in their backyards. There were also mass burials of bodies tipped into a massive pit from dump trucks, despite objections to their ceremonious nature and discouragement from the World Health Organization, due to the popular but incorrect belief that dead bodies would lead to catastrophic outbreaks of exotic diseases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dominican_Republic">Dominican Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Dominican Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, the storm dumped torrential flooding rains and killing over two dozen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Damage totaled $270\xc2\xa0million (2004\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Hurricane_Jeanne_in_the_Mid-Atlantic_region" title="Effects of Hurricane Jeanne in the Mid-Atlantic region">Effects of Hurricane Jeanne in the Mid-Atlantic region</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jeanne_2004_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="909" data-file-width="1045" height="191" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Jeanne_2004_rainfall.gif/220px-Jeanne_2004_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Jeanne_2004_rainfall.gif/330px-Jeanne_2004_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Jeanne_2004_rainfall.gif/440px-Jeanne_2004_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jeanne_2004_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nStorm total Rainfall from Jeanne</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Millions in Florida were left without electricity, some for the third time in a month. There were only five direct deaths in the mainland United States, three in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, one in <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> and one in <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>. The final US damage was determined to be around $6.9 billion, making it the 15th costliest hurricane in United States history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> It was difficult to isolate this from damage caused by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Frances" title="Hurricane Frances">Hurricane Frances</a> (and, around <a href="/wiki/Polk_County,_Florida" title="Polk County, Florida">Polk County</a> and <a href="/wiki/Highlands_County,_Florida" title="Highlands County, Florida">Highlands County</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Charley" title="Hurricane Charley">Hurricane Charley</a> as well).</p>\n<p>As the storm moved northward east of the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">Appalachian Mountains</a>, it continued producing heavy rains and flash flooding. Rainfall exceeded 6.00\xc2\xa0inches (150\xc2\xa0mm) as far north as <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mid_Atl_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mid_Atl-35">[35]</a></sup> resulting in severe flash flooding in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a> and its <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> suburbs on September 28. Tornadoes also touched down in <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_Delaware" title="Wilmington, Delaware">Wilmington, Delaware</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cherry_Hill,_New_Jersey" title="Cherry Hill, New Jersey">Cherry Hill, New Jersey</a>.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jeanne_Orlando_damage.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Jeanne_Orlando_damage.jpg/220px-Jeanne_Orlando_damage.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Jeanne_Orlando_damage.jpg/330px-Jeanne_Orlando_damage.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Jeanne_Orlando_damage.jpg/440px-Jeanne_Orlando_damage.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jeanne_Orlando_damage.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDamaged signs in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a> caused by Jeanne.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Delaware_and_Maryland">Delaware and Maryland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Delaware and Maryland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Throughout Delaware, the remnants of Jeanne produced between 4 and 8\xc2\xa0in (100 and 200\xc2\xa0mm) of rain,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDCDE1_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-NCDCDE1-36">[36]</a></sup> peaking at 7.1\xc2\xa0in (180\xc2\xa0mm) at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Delaware" title="University of Delaware">University of Delaware</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mid_Atl_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mid_Atl-35">[35]</a></sup> This led to widespread street flooding and several rivers overflowed their banks. Forty people had to be rescued from a bus along the White Clay Creek after the creek crested at .59\xc2\xa0ft (0.18\xc2\xa0m) above flood-stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDCDE1_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-NCDCDE1-36">[36]</a></sup> A strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujita_Scale" title="Fujita Scale">F2</a> tornado touched down in the state, injuring five people and leaving $1\xc2\xa0million in damages. The tornado touched down in northern <a href="/wiki/New_Castle_County,_Delaware" title="New Castle County, Delaware">New Castle County</a> and tracked for 5\xc2\xa0mi (8.0\xc2\xa0km) and generated winds up to 130\xc2\xa0mph (210\xc2\xa0km/h). The county airport sustained significant damage, five C-130 cargo planes were damaged, thousands of pounds of jet fuel spilled, and damaged hangars. At a nearby industrial park, metal siding was torn off buildings, windows were shattered and power lines were downed. A self-storage facility sustained substantial damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Maryland, Jeanne produced up to 4\xc2\xa0in (100\xc2\xa0mm) of rain,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mid_Atl_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-Mid_Atl-35">[35]</a></sup> triggering flash flooding throughout the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Numerous roads were flooded,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> including parts of <a href="/wiki/Maryland_Route_17" title="Maryland Route 17">Maryland Route 17</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Several rivers rose above their flood-stage, with the Big Elk Creek cresting at 9.3\xc2\xa0ft (2.8\xc2\xa0m), 0.3\xc2\xa0ft (0.091\xc2\xa0m) above food-stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> A total of 50 roads were closed due to high water throughout the state. Numerous reports of stranded vehicles were sent to the Emergency Operations Center. In <a href="/wiki/Carroll_County,_Maryland" title="Carroll County, Maryland">Carroll County</a>, a group of inmates required rescue after the jail they were in flooded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> One brief F0 tornado touched down in the state near <a href="/wiki/Solomons,_Maryland" title="Solomons, Maryland">Solomons</a>, causing minor damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Jeanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p>As a result of its impact the name Jeanne was retired in the spring of 2005, by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>\'s hurricane committee, and will never again be used for an Atlantic hurricane. It was replaced with the name Julia ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2010 Atlantic hurricane season">2010 Atlantic hurricane season</a>.</p>\n<h2><span
