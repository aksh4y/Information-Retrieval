rel="nofollow">abc-clio.com</a></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>ABC-CLIO, LLC</b> is a publishing company for academic reference works and periodicals primarily on topics such as history and social sciences for educational and public library settings. ABC-CLIO provides service to fifteen different online databases which contain over one million online textbooks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> The company consults academic leaders in the fields they cover in order to provide authority for their reference titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The headquarters are located in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara,_California" title="Santa Barbara, California">Santa Barbara, California</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ABC-CLIO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>ABC-CLIO was founded in 1953 as a privately held corporation by Eric Boehm with its first publication, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Historical_Abstracts" title="Historical Abstracts">Historical Abstracts</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">[3]</a></sup> The name represents the company\'s two original divisions when it incorporated in 1969: ABC stands for American Bibliographical Center and CLIO Press is named after <a href="/wiki/Clio" title="Clio">Clio</a>, the muse of history from ancient Greek mythology. According to Boehm, he had always been interested in history so when he noticed that there were not many good historical abstracting services available, he started publishing the <i>Historical Abstracts</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-:4-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>During the 1960s, a sister bibliographic and abstract publication on American history was added, <i>America: History and Life</i>, which was considered an award-winning title. The company entered into digital publishing with electronic data in the 1960s and in 1975, it published its first online database<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">[3]</a></sup> called <i>DIALOG</i>. During the 1980s, ABC-CLIO expanded into providing primary reference books such as encyclopedias and dictionaries and stopped publishing bibliographic books.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-:4-4">[4]</a></sup> In the 1990s, ABC-CLIO began to provide access to its humanities database on CD-ROM. The <i>Exegy Current Events</i> CD-ROM was named "Best Disc of the Year" by Library Journal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abc-clio_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-abc-clio-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1998, ABC-CLIO provided electronic access to <i>America: History and Life</i>. By the 2000s, one of company\'s most popular products had become online databases for researching many topics in the field of the humanities. In 2001, ABC-CLIO began to publish eBooks, initially providing 150 different titles to schools and libraries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">[3]</a></sup> The company\'s reference books had won numerous awards, and the company started a series of subject-related online databases for secondary school use. In 2004, the company acquired the quarterly historical journal, <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_of_the_West" title="Journal of the West">Journal of the West</a></i>, which has been published since 1962.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abc-clio_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-abc-clio-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In 1996, ABC-CLIO merged with an electronic publishing company, Intellimation, which also produced educational software. The merger brought Becky Snyder, ABC-CLIO\'s current president, from Intellimation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">[6]</a></sup> It sold <i>Historical Abstracts</i> and <i>America: History and Life</i> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EBSCO_Publishing" title="EBSCO Publishing">EBSCO Publishing</a> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidiaries">Subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span id="Imprints_and_series">Imprints and series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ABC-CLIO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Imprints and series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span id="ABC-CLIO/Greenwood"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="ABC-CLIO.2FGreenwood">ABC-CLIO/Greenwood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ABC-CLIO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: ABC-CLIO/Greenwood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenwood_Press" title="Greenwood Press">Greenwood Press</a></div>\n<p>In 2008, ABC-CLIO acquired the Greenwood Publishing Group from <a href="/wiki/Houghton_Mifflin_Harcourt" title="Houghton Mifflin Harcourt">Houghton Mifflin Harcourt</a>. The deal gave ABC-CLIO a "perpetual license" to use the imprints of Greenwood Press and publish all of its titles. Acquiring the publishing group gave ABC-CLIO access to Greenwood Press, Praeger Publishers, Praeger Security International and Libraries Unlimited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">[9]</a></sup> Greenwood focuses on publishing full-text reference works which are authoritative on various topics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:5_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-:5-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Libraries_Unlimited">Libraries Unlimited</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ABC-CLIO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Libraries Unlimited">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Libraries Unlimited came to ABC-CLIO as part of a deal with Greenwood Press. In 2012, Kathyrn Su\xc3\xa1rez was named Publisher for this division which focuses on publishing for librarians and information professionals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:5_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-:5-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="ABC-CLIO_Solutions">ABC-CLIO Solutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ABC-CLIO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: ABC-CLIO Solutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>ABC-CLIO databases are provided under the umbrella of ABC-CLIO Solutions. There are fifteen different databases providing access to different subject areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> ABC-CLIO Solutions provides digital curriculum with multimedia content, text-to-speech features, translation tools which covers various topics relating to history and the humanities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> After ABC-CLIO acquired Greenwood Press\'s databases, they revamped the look and feel of their interface in order to provide common access through the ABC-CLIO interface.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> ABC-CLIO also provided the ability to search across multiple databases through one search, also providing options for narrowing the search after revamping their interface.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The new interface is considered user-friendly and provides access to personalized features.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Much of the praise for the direction and expansion of digital services has gone to President of ABC-CLIO, Becky Snyder,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> who has been with the company since 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Praeger">Praeger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ABC-CLIO&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Praeger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Praeger is known for publishing "scholarly and professional books" in the subject areas of social science and the humanities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:5_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-:5-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span
