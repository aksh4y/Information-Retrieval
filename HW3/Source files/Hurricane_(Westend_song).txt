\xe2\x96\xba</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>"Hurricane"</b> was the <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrian</a> entry in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_1983" title="Eurovision Song Contest 1983">Eurovision Song Contest 1983</a>, performed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Language" title="German Language">German</a> by <a href="/wiki/Westend_(band)" title="Westend (band)">Westend</a>.</p>\n<p>The song was performed eighteenth on the night, following <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Armando_Gama" title="Armando Gama">Armando Gama</a> with "<a href="/wiki/Esta_balada_que_te_dou" title="Esta balada que te dou">Esta balada que te dou</a>" and preceding <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Pas_de_Deux_(band)" title="Pas de Deux (band)">Pas de Deux</a> with "<a href="/wiki/Rendez-vous_(song)" title="Rendez-vous (song)">Rendez-vous</a>". At the close of voting, it had received 53 points, placing 9th in a field of 20 entries.</p>\n<p>The song is an up-tempo number, in which the band sings about being without the object of their affections and likens this condition to being in a hurricane.</p>\n<p>It was succeeded as <a href="/wiki/Austria_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Austria in the Eurovision Song Contest">Austrian representative</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_1984" title="Eurovision Song Contest 1984">1984 contest</a> by <a href="/wiki/Anita_(singer)" title="Anita (singer)">Anita</a> singing "<a href="/wiki/Einfach_weg" title="Einfach weg">Einfach weg</a>".</p>\n<div aria-labelledby="23x15px.7Cborder_.7Calt.3D.7Clink.3D.C2.A0Austria_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest" class="navbox"
