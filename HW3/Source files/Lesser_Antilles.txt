href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. Most form a long, partly <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_arc" title="Volcanic arc">volcanic</a> <a href="/wiki/Island_arc" title="Island arc">island arc</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a> to the north-west and the continent of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MW_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MW-1">[1]</a></sup> The islands form the eastern boundary of the Caribbean Sea with the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. Together, the Lesser Antilles and the Greater Antilles compose the <a href="/wiki/Antilles" title="Antilles">Antilles</a> (or the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> in its narrowest definition). When combined with the <a href="/wiki/Lucayan_Archipelago" title="Lucayan Archipelago">Lucayan Archipelago</a>, all three are known as the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Geography"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Antilles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The islands of the Lesser Antilles are divided into three groups: the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a> in the south, the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a> in the north, and the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Antilles" title="Leeward Antilles">Leeward Antilles</a> in the west.</p>\n<p>The Windward Islands are so called because they were more <a href="/wiki/Windward_and_leeward" title="Windward and leeward">windward</a> to sailing ships arriving in the New World than the Leeward Islands, given that the prevailing trade winds blow east to west. The trans-Atlantic currents and winds that provided the fastest route across the ocean brought these ships to the rough dividing line between the Windward and Leeward Islands.</p>\n<p>The Leeward Antilles consist of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/ABC_islands_(Lesser_Antilles)" title="ABC islands (Lesser Antilles)">ABC islands</a> just off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, plus a group of Venezuelan islands.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geological_formation">Geological formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Antilles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geological formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Lesser Antilles more or less coincide with the outer edge of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Plate" title="Caribbean Plate">Caribbean Plate</a>. Many of the islands were formed as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subduction</a> of oceanic crust of the South American Plate under the Caribbean Plate in the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles_subduction_zone" title="Lesser Antilles subduction zone">Lesser Antilles subduction zone</a>. This process is ongoing and is responsible not only for many of the islands, but also for volcanic and earthquake activity in the region. The islands along the South American coast are largely the result of the interaction of the <a href="/wiki/South_American_Plate" title="South American Plate">South American Plate</a> and the Caribbean Plate which is mainly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strike-slip" title="Strike-slip">strike-slip</a>, but includes a component of compression.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_divisions">Political divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Antilles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Lesser Antilles are divided into eight independent nations and numerous dependent and non-sovereign states (which are politically associated with the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>). Over one third of the total area and population of the Lesser Antilles lies within <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>, a sovereign nation comprising the two southernmost islands of the Windward Island chain.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sovereign_states">Sovereign states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
