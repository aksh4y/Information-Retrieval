It is strongest during the northern hemisphere summer and shifts towards the equator during the winter, when the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Low" title="Aleutian Low">Aleutian Low</a> becomes more active. It is responsible for California\'s typically dry <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Autumn" title="Autumn">fall</a> and typically wet <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spring_(season)" title="Spring (season)">spring</a>, as well as Hawaii\'s year-round <a href="/wiki/Trade_winds" title="Trade winds">trade
