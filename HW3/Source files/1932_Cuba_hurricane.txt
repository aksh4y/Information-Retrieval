style="text-align:center;padding:0px 2px"></td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="2" style="text-align:center; border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/1932_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1932 Atlantic hurricane season">1932 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>The <b>1932 Cuba hurricane</b>, known also as the <b>Hurricane of Santa Cruz del Sur</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-1">[1]</a></sup> or the <b>1932 Camag\xc3\xbcey hurricane</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Report1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Report1-2">[2]</a></sup> was the deadliest and one of the most intense <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a> in <a href="/wiki/History_of_Cuba" title="History of Cuba">Cuban history</a>. The only Category 5 <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">Atlantic hurricane</a> ever recorded in November, the cyclone had a path through the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> atypical to most hurricanes developing late in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="Atlantic hurricane season">Atlantic hurricane season</a>. The storm\'s strong winds, <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a>, and rain devastated an extensive portion of central and eastern Cuba, where the storm was considered the worst natural disaster of the 20th century. Though the effects from the hurricane were concentrated primarily on Cuba, significant effects were also felt in the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, with lesser effects felt elsewhere.</p>\n<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a> that would later develop into the destructive hurricane was first located east of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> on October\xc2\xa030, and tracked westward into the Caribbean Sea, reaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">tropical storm</a> strength the next day. Moving southwestward towards the southern portion of the Caribbean, the storm reached hurricane strength on November\xc2\xa02 before a period of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid intensification</a> ensued. On November\xc2\xa06, the tropical cyclone reached its peak intensity as a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane</a> with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> of 175\xc2\xa0mph (280\xc2\xa0km/h). The storm weakened to Category\xc2\xa04 intensity while recurving northeast, moving ashore Cuba\'s <a href="/wiki/Camag%C3%BCey_Province" title="Camag\xc3\xbcey Province">Camag\xc3\xbcey Province</a> on November\xc2\xa09 with winds of 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h). After traversing the island, the storm gradually weakened as it crossed the central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> Islands and near <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>. On November\xc2\xa013, the system transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> and dissipated the next day.</p>\n<p>As an intensifying hurricane in the southern Caribbean Sea, the storm moved near the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Netherlands Antilles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, causing widespread effects. A prolonged passage of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Curacao" title="Curacao">Curacao</a> resulted in the damaging of the harbor fortification. The storm lashed the coast of Colombia with strong winds and torrential rainfall, severely hampering the <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">banana</a> crop in the region and disrupting telecommunications. Several towns, particularly those near the coast, sustained significant infrastructural damage. Marked, albeit localized, damage to banana crops was also reported in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, where strong winds toppled numerous trees. In open waters, the storm\'s track brought it across numerous shipping lanes, largely disrupting shipping primarily in the central Caribbean and damaging several ships.</p>\n<p><br/></p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Meteorological_history"><span <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a>, first identified at 06:00\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on October\xc2\xa030 by ship observations roughly 200\xc2\xa0mi (320\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4">[4]</a></sup> Tracking generally westward, href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lucia" title="St. Lucia">St. Lucia</a> confirmed the presence of a developing <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-1">[1]</a></sup> Shortly after traversing the islands, the disturbance strengthened to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">tropical storm</a> intensity at 18:00\xc2\xa0UTC on October\xc2\xa031.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> Concurrently as the storm was gradually intensifying, the tropical cyclone began to take an unusual course towards the southwest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4">[4]</a></sup> On November\xc2\xa02, the storm intensified to hurricane status while north of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Netherlands Antilles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Steadily strengthening, the hurricane reached Category\xc2\xa02 status on November\xc2\xa03.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> During the day, the storm passed approximately 50\xc2\xa0mi (80\xc2\xa0km) north of <a href="/wiki/Punta_Gallinas" title="Punta Gallinas">Punta Gallinas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, the northernmost extent of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4">[4]</a></sup> However, at the same time the hurricane curved towards the west, causing the storm to move away from Colombia and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> During the next few days, the hurricane <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensified</a>, reaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_hurricane" title="Major hurricane">major hurricane</a> status late on November\xc2\xa04. The next day, the hurricane\'s intensity was analyzed to have been the equivalent of a modern-day <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane</a> while curving northward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> Although the storm was analyzed operationally to have maximized as a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project" title="Atlantic hurricane reanalysis project">reanalysis</a> of the hurricane indicated that the tropical cyclone was much more intense than suggested initially based on observations by the <i>S.S. Phemius</i>, the crew of which visually estimated winds of around 200\xc2\xa0mph (320\xc2\xa0km/h) at the maximum of the storm and measured unusually low pressures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-1">[1]</a></sup> Early on November\xc2\xa06, the hurricane peaked with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> estimated at 175\xc2\xa0mph (280\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> During that time, the <i>S.S. Phemius</i> recorded a minimum barometric pressure of 915\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.02\xc2\xa0inHg); this measurement was the lowest documented throughout the storm\'s existence. As this report did not occur within the hurricane\'s <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>, the storm\'s true minimum pressure may have been much less.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Pacing northward, the hurricane gradually weakened after maximum intensity on November\xc2\xa06 but held its Category\xc2\xa05 strength for 72\xc2\xa0consecutive hours before finally dropping to Category\xc2\xa04 status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> Due to a gradual curve towards the northeast, the hurricane tracked near <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Brac" title="Cayman Brac">Cayman Brac</a> on November\xc2\xa09;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-1">[1]</a></sup> a minimum barometric pressure of 939\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.73\xc2\xa0inHg) was documented on the island. Only slight weakening occurred before the hurricane made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean coast</a> of Cuba\'s <a href="/wiki/Camag%C3%BCey_Province" title="Camag\xc3\xbcey Province">Camag\xc3\xbcey Province</a> at 14:00\xc2\xa0UTC that day. At the time of landfall, the tropical cyclone was estimated to have had a minimum pressure of 918\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.11\xc2\xa0inHg) and maximum sustained winds of 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h), with these winds extending as far as 40\xc2\xa0mi (65\xc2\xa0km) from the center of the hurricane. The storm traversed Cuba in six hours before emerging into the Atlantic Ocean late on November\xc2\xa09, after which it began to cross the central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamian</a> archipelago. At 07:00\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa010, the hurricane made a second landfall on <a href="/wiki/Long_Island,_Bahamas" title="Long Island, Bahamas">Long Island, Bahamas</a> with an intensity equivalent to that of a modern-day Category\xc2\xa03. Continuing to track northeast, the storm moved east of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> two days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-1">[1]</a></sup> On November\xc2\xa013, the hurricane began to interact with an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> centered over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Maritimes" title="Canadian Maritimes">Canadian Maritimes</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4">[4]</a></sup> and later weakened to tropical storm strength.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> The tropical cyclone later became extratropical itself before it was last noted at 18:00\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">Preparations and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Cuba_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span id="Caribbean_Sea,_Jamaica,_and_northern_South_America"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean_Sea.2C_Jamaica.2C_and_northern_South_America">Caribbean Sea, Jamaica, and northern South America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Cuba_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Caribbean Sea, Jamaica, and northern South America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:S.S._Phemius.jpg"><img alt="Sepia-toned image of a ship in open waters with a smokestack at center and two masts on either side of the funnel." class="thumbimage" data-file-height="263" data-file-width="550" height="105" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/06/S.S._Phemius.jpg/220px-S.S._Phemius.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/06/S.S._Phemius.jpg/330px-S.S._Phemius.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/06/S.S._Phemius.jpg/440px-S.S._Phemius.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:S.S._Phemius.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nThe British vessel S.S. Phemius, which followed the progress of the hurricane from November\xc2\xa05-7 and documented the storm\'s lowest measured pressure</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>As the hurricane moved slowly through the eastern and southern Caribbean, the rough seas and strong winds disrupted shipping routes. On November\xc2\xa06, the American <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">schooner</a> <i>Abundance</i> encountered the storm east of <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, resulting in a destroyed <a href="/wiki/Rudder" title="Rudder">rudder</a> and a beaching on Jamaica\'s <a href="/wiki/Morant_Point" title="Morant Point">Morant Point</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComingNorth_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ComingNorth-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShipsSuffer_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ShipsSuffer-6">[6]</a></sup> However, the ship\'s six crew were extracted from the disabled vessel and were transported to the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComingNorth_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-ComingNorth-5">[5]</a></sup> Although the <i>S.S. Phemius</i> would later return safely to port in Cuba,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-Metadata-1">[1]</a></sup> reports that the ship had lost its smokestack and the relaying of an <a href="/wiki/SOS" title="SOS">SOS</a> message prompted the <a href="/wiki/United_Fruit_Company" title="United Fruit Company">United Fruit Company</a> liner <i>Tela</i> to search for the initially missing ship, which failed due to poor visibility conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComingNorth_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-ComingNorth-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3Afloat_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-3Afloat-7">[7]</a></sup> This was followed by a search operation undertaken by the <a href="/wiki/USS_Overton_(DD-239)" title="USS Overton (DD-239)">USS Overton (DD-239)</a> and <a href="/wiki/USS_Swan_(AM-34)" title="USS Swan (AM-34)">USS Swan (AM-34)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USWarships_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-USWarships-8">[8]</a></sup> On November\xc2\xa08, the S.S. Phemius was found damaged and later towed to shore by a salvage tug. However, during the same period of time both the steamer <i>Tacira</i> and the freighter <i>San Simeon</i> were disabled after encountering the storm, requiring towing to safety.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HelpReaches_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-HelpReaches-9">[9]</a></sup> Several other ships in the immediate area were also hampered by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormRavages_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-StormRavages-10">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Some damage was reported in northern South America as the hurricane swept near the coast. In Colombia, rail telecommunications were interrupted near <a href="/wiki/Santa_Marta" title="Santa Marta">Santa Marta</a>, and banana plantations in the area were believed to have suffered to some extent due to the storm\'s winds and rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComingNorth_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-ComingNorth-5">[5]</a></sup> In Santa Marta itself, several homes were inundated and automotive traffic was hampered by the storm\'s effects. Docks on Santa Marta\'s port were also damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DamageHeavy_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-DamageHeavy-11">[11]</a></sup> Heavy rains also washed out some railways and bridges, further disrupting the distribution of banana shipments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmericanSchooner_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmericanSchooner-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SecondShipDistress_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-SecondShipDistress-13">[13]</a></sup> Elsewhere along the coast, many of Colombia\'s seaports were damaged by the storm\'s effects, while inland farms suffered extensive flood damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CoastlineColombia_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CoastlineColombia-14">[14]</a></sup> Due to sparse reports from the remainder of Colombia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CentralAmericaDevastated_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-CentralAmericaDevastated-15">[15]</a></sup> the extent of damage in <a href="/wiki/Barranquilla" title="Barranquilla">Barranquilla</a> remains unspecified,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ComingNorth_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-ComingNorth-5">[5]</a></sup> with other nearby towns reporting "heavy" damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CentralAmericaDevastated_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-CentralAmericaDevastated-15">[15]</a></sup> The village of <a href="/wiki/Sevilla,_Valle_del_Cauca" title="Sevilla, Valle del Cauca">Sevilla, Valle del Cauca</a> was almost entirely destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InPeril_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-InPeril-16">[16]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Providencia_Island" title="Providencia Island">Providencia Island</a> just off of Colombia sustained significant agricultural damage and the loss of 36\xc2\xa0homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4">[4]</a></sup> To the northeast in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Curacao" title="Curacao">Curacao</a>, the passing hurricane destroyed the island harbor\'s fortifications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CoastlineColombia_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-CoastlineColombia-14">[14]</a></sup> A seawall near the entrance to <a href="/wiki/Willemstad" title="Willemstad">Sint Anna harbor</a> suffered partial collapse, while a <a href="/wiki/Pontoon_bridge" title="Pontoon bridge">pontoon bridge</a> linking both sides of the harbor was completely destroyed. In nearby <a href="/wiki/Bonaire" title="Bonaire">Bonaire</a>, a pier succumbed to the driving rain and rough seas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SeawallCollapses_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-SeawallCollapses-17">[17]</a></sup></p>\n<p>With the hurricane threatening Jamaica, <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_World_Airways" title="Pan American World Airways">Pan American World Airways</a> cancelled its flights servicing <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Jamaica" title="Kingston, Jamaica">Kingston, Jamaica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FlightsCanceled_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FlightsCanceled-18">[18]</a></sup> In Jamaica, the storm\'s passage to the west caused intense winds as strong as 71\xc2\xa0mph (114\xc2\xa0km/h) to sweep across the island,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JamaicaHurricane_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-JamaicaHurricane-19">[19]</a></sup> destroying over 2\xc2\xa0million trees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HitsFreighter_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-HitsFreighter-20">[20]</a></sup> Although effects overall were generally minimal, some localized areas on the island experienced as much as a 50%\xc2\xa0loss of banana trees due to the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-4">[4]</a></sup> The cost of damage on Jamaica was <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>4\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4MillionLoss_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-4MillionLoss-21">[21]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cayman_Islands">Cayman Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Cuba_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cayman Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The storm devastated the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Brac" title="Cayman Brac">Cayman Brac</a> which was inundated by the <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a>, which was reported to be as high as 10\xc2\xa0m (33\xc2\xa0ft). Many homes and buildings were washed out to sea as a result of the storm and many people had to climb trees to escape the floodwaters. 110\xc2\xa0people died on the islands; one of them was on <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">Grand Cayman</a> and the rest were on Cayman Brac.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The ship <i>Balboa</i> also sank as a result of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Cuba_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SCS1932.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="539" data-file-width="725" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/SCS1932.jpg/220px-SCS1932.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/SCS1932.jpg/330px-SCS1932.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/92/SCS1932.jpg/440px-SCS1932.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SCS1932.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSanta Cruz del Sur, Camag\xc3\xbcey Province, Cuba after the hurricane</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Although no warnings were issued initially, the National Observatory of Cuba voiced concerns that the intense tropical cyclone presented a danger to Cuba, particularly Camag\xc3\xbcey Province, as early as November\xc2\xa05. However, the observatory indicated that forecasting the future motion of the hurricane was difficult as the storm\'s intensity and previous motion was not consistent with climatology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CubaDanger_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-CubaDanger-24">[24]</a></sup> On November\xc2\xa08, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" title="Hurricane warning">hurricane warning</a> was issued for the southeastern extent of Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WarningIssued_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-WarningIssued-25">[25]</a></sup> As a precautionary measure, shipping routes servicing ports in eastern Cuba were suspended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HitsECuba_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-HitsECuba-26">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The town of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_del_Sur" title="Santa Cruz del Sur">Santa Cruz del Sur</a> in <a href="/wiki/Camag%C3%BCey_Province" title="Camag\xc3\xbcey Province">Camag\xc3\xbcey Province</a> was virtually obliterated by a massive <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> which measured 6.5\xc2\xa0m (21.3\xc2\xa0ft) in height.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhr_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhr-27">[27]</a></sup> Few buildings remained standing in the area. In that coastal town alone, a total of 2,870\xc2\xa0people lost their lives. In total, 3,033\xc2\xa0people died in Cuba and damage there was estimated at $40\xc2\xa0million (1932 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>; $702.1 million today).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhr_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-nhr-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p><br/></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
