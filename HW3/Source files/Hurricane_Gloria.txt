border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/1985_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1985 Atlantic hurricane season">1985 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Gloria</b> was the first significant system to strike the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">northeastern United States</a> since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Agnes" title="Hurricane Agnes">Hurricane Agnes</a> in <a href="/wiki/1972_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1972 Atlantic hurricane season">1972</a> and the first major storm to affect New York and Long Island directly since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Donna" title="Hurricane Donna">Hurricane Donna</a> in <a href="/wiki/1960_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1960 Atlantic hurricane season">1960</a>. It was also the most intense <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_4_hurricane" title="Category 4 hurricane">Category 4 hurricane</a> that did not attain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_5_hurricane" title="Category 5 hurricane">Category 5 status</a> until surpassed by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Opal" title="Hurricane Opal">Hurricane Opal</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1995_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1995 Atlantic hurricane season">1995 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. It was a powerful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde-type_hurricane" title="Cape Verde-type hurricane">Cape Verde-type</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a> that formed during the <a href="/wiki/1985_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1985 Atlantic hurricane season">1985 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, originating from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on September\xc2\xa016 in the eastern Atlantic Ocean. After remaining a weak tropical cyclone for several days, Gloria intensified into a hurricane on September\xc2\xa022 north of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>. During that time, the storm had moved generally westward, although it turned to the northwest due to a weakening of the <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a>. Gloria quickly intensified on September\xc2\xa024, and the next day reached peak winds of 145\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h). The hurricane weakened before striking the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> of North Carolina on September\xc2\xa027. Later that day, Gloria made two subsequent landfalls on <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a> and later western Connecticut, before becoming <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> on September\xc2\xa028 over New England. The remnants moved through Atlantic Canada, eventually dissipating on October\xc2\xa02.</p>\n<p>Before Gloria made landfall, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> issued <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane warnings</a> at some point for the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a> from South Carolina to Maine. Hundreds of thousands of people evacuated, and the hurricane was described as the "storm of the century". In general, Gloria\'s strongest winds remained east of the center, which largely spared locations from North Carolina to New Jersey, and the passage at low tide reduced <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a>. Hurricane-force winds and gusts affected much of the path, which knocked down trees and power lines. This left over 4\xc2\xa0million people without power, including the worst power outage in Connecticut history related to a natural disaster. The extended power outage on Long Island, affecting 1.5\xc2\xa0million people at some point, caused the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Lighting_Company" title="Long Island Lighting Company">Long Island Lighting Company</a> to be shut down and be replaced with a public company. Fallen trees caused six of the storm\'s fourteen deaths.</p>\n<p>In North Carolina, high waves damaged many homes along the Outer Banks and caused heavy beach erosion. High waves also damaged piers, boats, and docks throughout the Mid-Atlantic states. Flooding forced several highways to close, and in Pennsylvania, thousands were forced to evacuate their homes. The storm surge destroyed 48\xc2\xa0homes on Long Island, while the winds damaged the roofs of many more. Widespread crop damage occurred, amounting to about $20\xc2\xa0million (1985\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.22damage_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-.22damage-1">[nb 1]</a></sup>. Overall damage in the United States was estimated at $900\xc2\xa0million, which was less than expected. In neighboring Canada, the remnants of Gloria caused minor power outages in New Brunswick, although confusion related to the storm\'s arrival led to the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[nb 2]</a></sup> moved off the west coast of Africa on September\xc2\xa015. Based on data from <a href="/wiki/Satellite_imagery" title="Satellite imagery">satellite imagery</a>, it is estimated that a tropical depression formed on September\xc2\xa016 near <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> with an associated <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">low-level circulation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr1-5">[3]</a></sup> The next day, the depression intensified into Tropical Storm Gloria, but there was no further intensification. On September\xc2\xa018, Gloria weakened back to tropical depression status, but re-intensified into a tropical storm on September\xc2\xa020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-6">[4]</a></sup> During this time, it moved generally westward due to a strong <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> to the north. On September\xc2\xa021, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> began flying into Gloria to measure the storm\'s intensity, and the next day, a flight observed winds of 78\xc2\xa0mph (126\xc2\xa0km/h) at a height of 1,500\xc2\xa0ft (460\xc2\xa0m). As a result, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) upgraded Gloria to a hurricane about 465\xc2\xa0mi (750\xc2\xa0km) east-northeast of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After having moved generally to the west, Gloria turned more to the west-northwest on September\xc2\xa022. This occurred after tropical storms <a href="/wiki/1985_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Fabian" title="1985 Atlantic hurricane season">Fabian</a> and <a href="/wiki/1985_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Henri" title="1985 Atlantic hurricane season">Henri</a> weakened the ridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> On September\xc2\xa023, Gloria passed about 155\xc2\xa0miles (250\xc2\xa0km) to the north of <a href="/wiki/Anegada" title="Anegada">Anegada</a>, the northernmost island in the Lesser Antilles. It initially remained a minimal hurricane until September\xc2\xa024 when it began quickly intensifying. That day, it became a major hurricane<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[nb 3]</a></sup> while passing northeast of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-6">[4]</a></sup> Gloria developed a 10\xc2\xa0mi (16\xc2\xa0km) wide <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-8">[5]</a></sup> surrounded by an eyewall, and was producing the <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)#stadium_effect" title="Eye (cyclone)">stadium effect</a>. At 0120\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on September\xc2\xa025, Hurricane Hunters extrapolated a <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 919\xc2\xa0mbar (27.1\xc2\xa0inHg), and reported flight-level winds of 145\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-9">[6]</a></sup> At the time, this was the lowest pressure measured by reconnaissance aircraft over the northern Atlantic Ocean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> Because there was little difference in the flight-level winds and the surface winds, this was estimated to have been Gloria\'s peak intensity, making it a Category\xc2\xa04 on the Saffir-Simpson scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-9">[6]</a></sup> However, a preliminary reanalysis released in 2008 suggested peak winds of 155\xc2\xa0mph (249\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-8">[5]</a></sup> At peak intensity, the hurricane was located about 930\xc2\xa0mi (1500\xc2\xa0km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">Cape Hatteras</a> in North Carolina,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> and had an eye diameter of only 8\xc2\xa0mi (13\xc2\xa0km).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-8">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Gloria_25_Sep_1985_1241z.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="598" data-file-width="600" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Hurricane_Gloria_25_Sep_1985_1241z.png/220px-Hurricane_Gloria_25_Sep_1985_1241z.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Hurricane_Gloria_25_Sep_1985_1241z.png/330px-Hurricane_Gloria_25_Sep_1985_1241z.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Hurricane_Gloria_25_Sep_1985_1241z.png/440px-Hurricane_Gloria_25_Sep_1985_1241z.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Gloria_25_Sep_1985_1241z.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Gloria to the east of Florida</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>After peak intensity, Gloria weakened while turning to the north, moving around the western end of the ridge,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> toward a <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">cold front</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-8">[5]</a></sup> By September\xc2\xa026, the winds had decreased to 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h), just 30\xc2\xa0hours after its peak intensity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-6">[4]</a></sup> Later that day, a buoy about 60\xc2\xa0mi (100\xc2\xa0km) east of the center recorded a <a href="/wiki/Wave_height" title="Wave height">wave height</a> of 46.9\xc2\xa0ft (14.3\xc2\xa0m), which at the time was the highest buoy wave recording in an Atlantic hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> While accelerating toward North Carolina, Gloria re-intensified slightly to winds of 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h), making it a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane. At 0530\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa027, the hurricane struck southern <a href="/wiki/Hatteras_Island" title="Hatteras Island">Hatteras Island</a> in the Outer Banks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr5_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr5-10">[7]</a></sup> After weakening further, Gloria passed just east of the <a href="/wiki/Delmarva_Peninsula" title="Delmarva Peninsula">Delmarva Peninsula</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> and interacted with a cold front. Its strongest winds remained on the eastern edge of the circulation, and the storm was gradually losing tropical characteristics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-8">[5]</a></sup> At 1600\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa027, the hurricane made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> with a broad and poorly defined center between <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_International_Airport" title="John F. Kennedy International Airport">John F. Kennedy International Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Islip,_New_York" title="Islip, New York">Islip, New York</a>, with winds of 85\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h) on western Long Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr5_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr5-10">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr3-11">[8]</a></sup> This was only about three days after Henri struck the same general area as a much weaker tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>About an hour after striking Long Island, Gloria made its final landfall in western Connecticut near <a href="/wiki/Westport,_Connecticut" title="Westport, Connecticut">Westport</a>, and proceeded to move through New England while weakening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-8">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup> The NHC estimated that Gloria became <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> over Maine early by 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa028.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-9">[6]</a></sup> The storm continued to the northeast through <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a> passing south of Greenland on September\xc2\xa030. The extratropical circulation of Gloria was last noted on October\xc2\xa02,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-6">[4]</a></sup> although the remnants later affected Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-9">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p><br/></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Gloria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:H52_hurricane_gloria_1985.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="755" data-file-width="527" height="315" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/H52_hurricane_gloria_1985.jpg/220px-H52_hurricane_gloria_1985.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/H52_hurricane_gloria_1985.jpg/330px-H52_hurricane_gloria_1985.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/H52_hurricane_gloria_1985.jpg/440px-H52_hurricane_gloria_1985.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:H52_hurricane_gloria_1985.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nFlooding from Hurricane Gloria in <a href="/wiki/Cape_May_Point,_New_Jersey" title="Cape May Point, New Jersey">Cape May Point, New Jersey</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Early in Gloria\'s duration, it threatened the northern Lesser Antilles, prompting a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane watch</a> and later warning for the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr1-5">[3]</a></sup> Gloria also threatened the Bahamas, prompting that government to issue hurricane watches and warnings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr7_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr7-13">[10]</a></sup></p>\n<p>While Gloria was off the east coast of Florida, the NHC issued a hurricane watch from <a href="/wiki/Edisto_Beach,_South_Carolina" title="Edisto Beach, South Carolina">Edisto Beach, South Carolina</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cape_Henry" title="Cape Henry">Cape Henry</a>, Virginia, which was upgraded to a hurricane warning at 1000\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa026, or 19.5\xc2\xa0hours before landfall. By later that day, a hurricane watch was issued for the rest of the eastern United States to <a href="/wiki/Eastport,_Maine" title="Eastport, Maine">Eastport, Maine</a>. By two hours before Gloria\'s landfall on Long Island, a hurricane warning was in effect for the same area, after warnings were gradually extended further north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-9">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr7_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr7-13">[10]</a></sup> Officials anticipated higher winds and <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[nb 4]</a></sup> than what occurred, due to the hurricane weakening as it approached landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr3-11">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Officials in Delaware and Maryland declared a state of emergency before the storm struck, and thousands of people from coastal communities evacuated to shelters. Schools closed in Delaware, and non-emergency workers were sent home. Along Long Island in New York, hundreds of thousands of people evacuated their homes, and a state of emergency was declared for several counties. In Connecticut, about 20,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated along the coast, and 7,300\xc2\xa0evacuated from neighboring Rhode Island. Residents also evacuated portions of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As Gloria approached the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>, National Hurricane Center director <a href="/wiki/Neil_Frank" title="Neil Frank">Neil Frank</a> called it the "Storm of the Century", due to its intensity and potential track over the densely populated region of <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-chc-15">[11]</a></sup> Such a track gathered the attention of many people, and led to the evacuation of 380,000\xc2\xa0people along the coast from North Carolina to Connecticut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-evac_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-evac-16">[12]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, officials implemented <a href="/wiki/Contraflow_lane_reversal" title="Contraflow lane reversal">lane reversing</a> to expedite the evacuation process, a policy many other coastal states use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-evac2_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-evac2-17">[13]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Officials advised 95,000 citizens along the <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> coastline, an area that rarely experiences hurricanes, to evacuate. <a href="/wiki/Cape_May_County,_New_Jersey" title="Cape May County, New Jersey">Cape May County</a>\xe2\x80\x94the most vulnerable part of the state and among the most susceptible in the entire country\xe2\x80\x94would require 36\xc2\xa0hours in 2005 to evacuate the 100,000\xc2\xa0citizens and 900,000\xc2\xa0tourists that were commonly present during busy summer weekends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cpo_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-cpo-18">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Offices and classes of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> closed only for the third time in the 20th century, the previous cases being the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_England_Hurricane_of_1938" title="New England Hurricane of 1938">New England Hurricane of 1938</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States_Blizzard_of_1978" title="Northeastern United States Blizzard of 1978">Blizzard of \'78</a>. Although Gloria\'s winds downed numerous trees and caused tens of thousands in damage in the area, overall effects were much less than expected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harvard_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-harvard-19">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<p>At the same time Gloria was making landfall on Long Island, a storm warning was issued for western New Brunswick and Nova Scotia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-9">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr7_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr7-13">[10]</a></sup> Across Atlantic Canada, the threat of Hurricane Gloria caused many citizens to rely on American media for storm coverage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ec_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-ec-20">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Gloria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gloria_OC.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="512" data-file-width="771" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Gloria_OC.jpg/220px-Gloria_OC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Gloria_OC.jpg/330px-Gloria_OC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Gloria_OC.jpg/440px-Gloria_OC.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gloria_OC.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nBoardwalk damage in Ocean City, New Jersey</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Gloria was a large hurricane that affected much of the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">northeastern United States</a>. Gloria brought strong wind gusts, which downed trees and left hundreds of thousands without power. Overall damage was estimated at $900\xc2\xa0million, and there were fourteen deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HRD_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-HRD-21">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> Gloria was the first significant hurricane to affect New England since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Donna" title="Hurricane Donna">Hurricane Donna</a> in 1960.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Carolinas_and_Mid-Atlantic">Carolinas and Mid-Atlantic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Gloria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Carolinas and Mid-Atlantic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>While offshore the eastern United States, the hurricane\'s strongest winds were on the eastern periphery, which reduced the winds over land. In North Carolina, where Gloria made its first landfall, the strongest winds were 74\xc2\xa0mph (119\xc2\xa0km/h) at a station near <a href="/wiki/Buxton,_North_Carolina" title="Buxton, North Carolina">Buxton</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">Cape Hatteras</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> office; a gust of 87\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h) was also observed there. At <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Shoal_Light" title="Diamond Shoal Light">Diamond Shoal Light</a>, located offshore, sustained winds reached 98\xc2\xa0mph (158\xc2\xa0km/h), with gusts to 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h). A peak storm surge of 5.9\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m) was estimated at the <a href="/wiki/Neuse_River" title="Neuse River">Neuse River</a>. A minimal surge of around 1.3\xc2\xa0ft (0.4\xc2\xa0ft) occurred in neighboring South Carolina, where winds were below tropical storm force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-9"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> The highest rainfall related to Gloria was 9.7\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm), recorded in <a href="/wiki/Edenton,_North_Carolina" title="Edenton, North Carolina">Edenton, North Carolina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCMAX_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPCMAX-22">[18]</a></sup> High waves and storm surge resulted in heavy coastal flooding and beach erosion along the Outer Banks,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-10"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> and several new inlets were created. Coastal flooding damaged several homes in the Outer Banks. Damage in the state was estimated at $8.1\xc2\xa0million, including $2.4\xc2\xa0million in crop damage. There was one death in North Carolina when a man was struck by a fallen tree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Throughout much of the Mid-Atlantic, the winds remained generally below hurricane force; however, winds reached 92\xc2\xa0mph (147\xc2\xa0km/h) along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay_Bridge-Tunnel" title="Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel">Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel</a> in Virginia and gusts to 89\xc2\xa0mph (144\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Ocean_City,_Maryland" title="Ocean City, Maryland">Ocean City, Maryland</a>. The strongest winds in the region occurred after the center passed the area. Due to Gloria\'s fast motion, there was minimal coastal flooding because the highest storm surge occurred during low tide, generally less than 4.9\xc2\xa0ft (1.5\xc2\xa0m). Beach erosion was reported in both Maryland and Delaware.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-11"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> The heaviest rainfall was west of the center. Statewide peaks included 9.17\xc2\xa0in (233\xc2\xa0mm) at <a href="/wiki/Valley_Forge_National_Historical_Park" title="Valley Forge National Historical Park">Valley Forge National Historical Park</a> in Pennsylvania, 8.6\xc2\xa0in (220\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Holland,_Virginia" title="Holland, Virginia">Holland, Virginia</a>, and 7.19\xc2\xa0in (183\xc2\xa0mm) in <a href="/wiki/Annapolis,_Maryland" title="Annapolis, Maryland">Annapolis, Maryland</a>. Rainfall spread as far inland as West Virginia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mid_Atl_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mid_Atl-23">[19]</a></sup></p>\n<p>High waves washed away 30\xc2\xa0ft (9.1\xc2\xa0m) of a fishing pier in <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach, Virginia</a>. The rainfall caused minor flooding, and high tides flooded coastal portions of the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a> region. There was minor damage to trees, roofs, and signs in southeastern Virginia. The center of Gloria passed about 30\xc2\xa0mi (48\xc2\xa0km) offshore eastern Maryland. In Ocean City, waves of 15\xc2\xa0ft (4.6\xc2\xa0m) severely damaged the boardwalk, washing sand and debris a block inland. Downed trees left about 150,000\xc2\xa0people in Maryland. High winds forced the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay_Bridge" title="Chesapeake Bay Bridge">Chesapeake Bay Bridge</a> to close, and there was little damage outside of eastern Maryland. High waves in Delaware eroded beaches, wrecked dunes, and damaged oceanfront buildings and boardwalks. Storm flooding closed several highways, including <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Route_1" title="Delaware Route 1">Delaware Route 1</a>. Damage was heaviest in <a href="/wiki/Sussex_County,_Delaware" title="Sussex County, Delaware">Sussex County</a>, although the storm damaged corn and soybean crops in northern Delaware. Monetary damage totaled over $500,000 in the state. In neighboring Pennsylvania, wind gusts reached 56\xc2\xa0mph (90\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown</a>, which knocked down many trees and left thousands without power. Heavy rainfall flooded many streams, which closed or damaged several highways, bridges, and rail lines. About 3,000\xc2\xa0houses were flooded, forcing thousands to evacuate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-5"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>While just off the New Jersey coast, Gloria produced strong winds, peaking at 81\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Ocean_City,_New_Jersey" title="Ocean City, New Jersey">Ocean City</a>, where a gust of 101\xc2\xa0mph (162\xc2\xa0km/h) was also reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-12"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> In the town, an <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F0</a> tornado was reported damaging a house.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-6"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup> Rainfall in the state reached 6.00\xc2\xa0in (152\xc2\xa0mm) at the Charlotteburg Reservoir near <a href="/wiki/West_Milford,_New_Jersey" title="West Milford, New Jersey">West Milford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mid_Atl_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mid_Atl-23">[19]</a></sup> Heavy beach erosion occurred along the coast, and several coastal towns sustained damage to boardwalks. Strong winds downed trees and power lines, which damaged homes and cars. One person was killed in <a href="/wiki/Long_Branch,_New_Jersey" title="Long Branch, New Jersey">Long Branch</a> after touching a downed power line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-7"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Though Gloria moved quickly through the region, it dropped moderate rainfall in locations, including 6.04\xc2\xa0inches (153\xc2\xa0mm) at <a href="/wiki/Baltimore%E2%80%93Washington_International_Airport" title="Baltimore\xe2\x80\x93Washington International Airport">Baltimore\xe2\x80\x93Washington International Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhc_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhc-24">[20]</a></sup> In addition, some unofficial reports in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> indicated amounts of up to 8\xc2\xa0inches (200\xc2\xa0mm) of rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vahistory_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-vahistory-25">[21]</a></sup> Because much of the Mid-Atlantic experienced the western, weaker side of this hurricane, damage was relatively light. High winds downed numerous trees throughout the area, leaving hundreds of thousands without power, including 237,000 in New Jersey, 124,000 in Maryland, and 56,000 in Virginia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-geo-26">[22]</a></sup> Extreme rainfall in <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> resulted in $5.5\xc2\xa0million (1985\xc2\xa0USD) in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vahistory_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-vahistory-25">[21]</a></sup> Intense flood waters split <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach_Island" title="Long Beach Island">Long Beach Island</a> in half for a period of time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hville_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-hville-27">[23]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Long_Island_and_New_York">Long Island and New York</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Gloria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Long Island and New York">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gloria_1985_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="924" data-file-width="774" height="263" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Gloria_1985_rainfall.gif/220px-Gloria_1985_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Gloria_1985_rainfall.gif/330px-Gloria_1985_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Gloria_1985_rainfall.gif/440px-Gloria_1985_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gloria_1985_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRainfall totals related to Hurricane Gloria</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>While making its second landfall, Gloria was accompanied by a storm surge of 6.9\xc2\xa0ft (2.1\xc2\xa0m) at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battery_Park" title="Battery Park">Battery Park</a>, the highest along its path. The highest wind report was a gust of 85\xc2\xa0mph (137\xc2\xa0km/h) at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islip_Airport" title="Islip Airport">Islip Airport</a>. <a href="/wiki/Central_Park" title="Central Park">Central Park</a> reported a gust of 51\xc2\xa0mph (83\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-13"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> Rainfall in the state reached 8.04\xc2\xa0in (204\xc2\xa0mm) at <a href="/wiki/Unadilla,_New_York" title="Unadilla, New York">Unadilla</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mid_Atl_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-Mid_Atl-23">[19]</a></sup> although was much less near the coast, reaching 3.48\xc2\xa0in (88.4\xc2\xa0mm) in Central Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-14"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Along Long Island, the high storm surge flooded hundreds of streets and caused heavy beach erosion. High winds downed thousands of trees and damaged hundreds of homes, causing widespread power outages. About 1.5\xc2\xa0million people in the state lost power, including two-thirds of the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Lighting_Company" title="Long Island Lighting Company">Long Island Lighting Company</a> customers, making it one of the worst power outages in the state. There were four deaths on Long Island, two of whom related to heart attacks and the other two related to fallen trees. There were also 14\xc2\xa0injuries in the region, many of them due to downed tree branches. In the New York mainland, heavy rainfall flooded rivers and alleviated drought conditions. Rough waves damaged boats and docks along the <a href="/wiki/Hudson_River" title="Hudson River">Hudson River</a>. Unsettled weather contributed to a traffic fatality and an airplane crash that killed one in the New York mainland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-8"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>It is believed peak gusts reached 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) in eastern <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-geo-26">[22]</a></sup> Weather forecasters believe that damage across parts of Long Island indicated winds in the Category Three range, as evidence of the damage received at MacArthur Airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-15"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> Gloria\'s high winds caused significant damage across Long Island and southeastern New York. The area hit the worst was eastern Long Island, where high wind gusts blew thousands of trees into buildings and across roads. The broadcast tower of <a href="/wiki/WBLI" title="WBLI">WBLI</a>-FM toppled on Bald Hill in Farmingville. In addition, the winds ripped roofs off of many buildings, including hangars at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MacArthur_Airport" title="MacArthur Airport">MacArthur Airport</a>, a hangar at the Bayport Aerodrome and the roof of the Islip Police Station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-geo-26">[22]</a></sup> Prolonged exposure to high winds and waves led to moderate beach erosion, washing away several piers and docks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_26-3"><a href="#cite_note-geo-26">[22]</a></sup> The storm surge, though relatively weak, destroyed 48\xc2\xa0houses on the ocean side of the island. Gloria\'s high winds left 683,000\xc2\xa0people in New York without power, with some lacking electricity for over eleven days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyafter_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyafter-28">[24]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_England_and_Canada">New England and Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Gloria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: New England and Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gloria_sept_27_1985_8570Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3000" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Gloria_sept_27_1985_8570Z.jpg/220px-Gloria_sept_27_1985_8570Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Gloria_sept_27_1985_8570Z.jpg/330px-Gloria_sept_27_1985_8570Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Gloria_sept_27_1985_8570Z.jpg/440px-Gloria_sept_27_1985_8570Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gloria_sept_27_1985_8570Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSatellite image of Gloria nearing New England</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>While moving across New England, Gloria was a weakened hurricane that passed quickly through the area. Though still a large hurricane, Gloria hit at low tide, resulting in low to moderate storm surges of 5\xc2\xa0feet (1.5\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton, Connecticut</a>, 6\xc2\xa0feet (1.8\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford,_Massachusetts" title="New Bedford, Massachusetts">New Bedford, Massachusetts</a>, and 3\xc2\xa0feet (1\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland, Maine</a>. The high waves caused heavy beach erosion in Connecticut and Rhode Island. The highest sustained winds were reports of 83\xc2\xa0mph (135\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Waterbury,_Connecticut" title="Waterbury, Connecticut">Waterbury, Connecticut</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Blue_Hill_Meteorological_Observatory" title="Blue Hill Meteorological Observatory">Blue Hill Meteorological Observatory</a> in Massachusetts, although neither location is at sea level. Gusts peaked at 110\xc2\xa0mph (176\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Chatham,_Massachusetts" title="Chatham, Massachusetts">Chatham, Massachusetts</a>, and widespread areas across New England reported hurricane-force gusts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-16"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> It is estimated that hurricane-force winds occurred in Connecticut, Massachusetts, and New Hampshire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_8-6"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-8">[5]</a></sup> Rainfall in the region peaked at 6.90\xc2\xa0in (175\xc2\xa0mm) at Borden Brook Reservoir in <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="Springfield, Massachusetts">Springfield, Massachusetts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Eng_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Eng-29">[25]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Connecticut, Gloria crossed from Westport along the Long Island Sound, passed near <a href="/wiki/Hartford,_Connecticut" title="Hartford, Connecticut">Hartford</a>, and exited into Massachusetts. Wind gusts peaked at 92\xc2\xa0mph (148\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Connecticut" title="Bridgeport, Connecticut">Bridgeport</a>, and also reached 82\xc2\xa0mph (132\xc2\xa0km/h) in Hartford. The winds downed thousands of trees, many of which knocked down power lines. This left about 727,000\xc2\xa0residents without power, setting a record for the state related to a weather event. High waves damaged or sunk hundreds of boats along the coast, and several beachfront homes and docks were damaged. Light rainfall caused some minor flooding, mostly in northwestern Connecticut. Statewide there was about $6\xc2\xa0million in crop damage. Damage was heaviest near Hartford, and overall storm damage was estimated at $91\xc2\xa0million. Fallen trees contributed to two of the three deaths in the state, as well as several other injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-9"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Hurricane-force gusts affected Rhode Island, peaking at 92\xc2\xa0mph (148\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Westerly,_Rhode_Island" title="Westerly, Rhode Island">Westerly</a>. The winds uprooted thousands of trees and caused widespread roof damage. About 300,000\xc2\xa0people statewide lost power, the greatest since the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_blackout_of_1965" title="Northeast blackout of 1965">Northeast blackout of 1965</a>. Coastal flooding was minimized due to the storm striking at low tide, although were high enough in <a href="/wiki/Narragansett_Bay" title="Narragansett Bay">Narragansett Bay</a> to damage docks and hundreds of boats. Damage in the state was estimated at $20\xc2\xa0million, including the cost for restoring the power outages. There were two deaths in Rhode Island; one was related to a fallen tree, and one occurred when a man was trying to secure his boat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-10"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Massachusetts, the hurricane moved from Connecticut through the <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="Springfield, Massachusetts">Springfield</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-11"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup> There was a small F1 tornado in <a href="/wiki/Middlesex_County,_Massachusetts" title="Middlesex County, Massachusetts">Middlesex County</a> that damaged trees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd2_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-sd2-30">[26]</a></sup> High winds knocked over thousands of trees, and about 500,000\xc2\xa0people statewide lost power. Heavy rainfall caused stream flooding, although the heaviest damage was from the winds. The hurricane destroyed a radio tower in <a href="/wiki/Framingham,_Massachusetts" title="Framingham, Massachusetts">Framingham</a> and damaged hundreds of homes in the eastern portion of the state. Along the coast, the storm tide caused minor coastal flooding and damaged hundreds of boats. Across Massachusetts, damage was estimated at $61\xc2\xa0million, including $6\xc2\xa0million from crop damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-12"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>By the time Gloria reached New Hampshire, it had weakened and was becoming extratropical, although hurricane-force wind gusts were reported across the state. On the top of Mount Washington, wind gusts reached 127\xc2\xa0mph (204\xc2\xa0km/h). The winds were strong enough to knock down large trees, many over 50\xc2\xa0years old and 50\xc2\xa0ft (15\xc2\xa0m). Downed trees cut power lines, which left 97,116\xc2\xa0people without power, mostly along the coast. Fallen trees also damaged 16\xc2\xa0homes and several vehicles. High rainfall damaged portions of <a href="/wiki/North_Woodstock,_New_Hampshire" title="North Woodstock, New Hampshire">North Woodstock</a>, although water damage was generally minor. There was one death in the state, when an elderly woman was knocked over by the strong winds and died two weeks later. There was about $2.5\xc2\xa0million in crop damage, mostly to the apple crop. In neighboring Maine, about 600,000\xc2\xa0people lost power due to the storm, the most since the passage of hurricanes <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Carol" title="Hurricane Carol">Carol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Edna" title="Hurricane Edna">Edna</a> in 1954. Winds in Maine reached 86\xc2\xa0mph (138\xc2\xa0km/h), and the storm knocked down about 100\xc2\xa0power poles in addition to the downed lines. Downed trees blocked roads and damaged houses and cars. The winds damaged roofs, including the 127\xe2\x80\x91year\xe2\x80\x91old spire of a church in <a href="/wiki/Buxton,_Maine" title="Buxton, Maine">Groveville</a>. Crop damage to the apple crop was estimated at $3\xc2\xa0million. High waves along the coast damaged lobster traps and dozens of boats, many of which were driven ashore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd2_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-sd2-30">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Gloria\'s high winds downed numerous trees across New England, causing minor to moderate damage. In the region, Connecticut received the worst of the hurricane, where tree and structural damage was greatest. Along the coastline, storm surge and strong waves washed away several fishing piers, and some roadways were underwater during the storm\'s passage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_26-4"><a href="#cite_note-geo-26">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a> was affected only slightly from the hurricane, and was limited to minor wind damage and localized flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newh_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-newh-31">[27]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, damage was more severe, where strong wind gusts ripped off roofs and uprooted hundreds of trees. High winds across New England resulted in significant power outages, leaving 250,000 in Maine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maine_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-maine-32">[28]</a></sup> In all, seven deaths occurred in New England, many of which occurred from falling tree limbs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_26-5"><a href="#cite_note-geo-26">[22]</a></sup></p>\n<p>While the remnants of Gloria were moving through Atlantic Canada, they produced rainfall to the north of the track, reaching as high as 4.53\xc2\xa0in (115\xc2\xa0mm) in Quebec. The highest winds in the country were 58\xc2\xa0mph (93\xc2\xa0km/h), peaking in the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Fundy" title="Bay of Fundy">Bay of Fundy</a> and to the northeast of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>. The remnants of Gloria caused wind and wave damage in Nova Scotia. Wind gusts of 55\xc2\xa0mph (89\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Fredericton" title="Fredericton">Fredericton</a>, New Brunswick knocked down power lines, affecting more than 1,800\xc2\xa0people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[29]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Gloria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p>On Long Island, thousands were left without power for over a week,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-13"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup> until the last outages were restored two weeks after the storm on October\xc2\xa08. The long duration without electricity forced hundreds of schools to close, extended train times by six hours due to lack of powered crossings, and <a href="/wiki/Price_gouging" title="Price gouging">price gouging</a> on food, ice, and generators. The lack of power led to a general disdain for the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Lighting_Company" title="Long Island Lighting Company">Long Island Lighting Company</a> (LILCO), which increased further when the company left the $40\xc2\xa0million (1985\xc2\xa0USD) repair bill to the ratepayers, citing the company\'s lack of hurricane insurance. Citizens quickly protested LILCO\'s decision, and within years the publicly owned <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Power_Authority" title="Long Island Power Authority">Long Island Power Authority</a> was formed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyafter_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyafter-28">[24]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Residents in both Massachusetts and Connecticut were also without power for up to one week, which caused many food products to spoil due to lack of refrigeration. The states brought in workers from other states, although the delay in restoring the widespread outages caused many schools to remain closed. In Connecticut, one man was electrocuted while repairing downed power lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd_12-14"><a href="#cite_note-sd-12">[9]</a></sup> About 477,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northeast_Utilities" title="Northeast Utilities">Connecticut Light &amp; Power</a> customers in the state lost power, a record that was surpassed by the 754,000 who lost power during <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irene" title="Hurricane Irene">Hurricane Irene</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[30]</a></sup> Later in 2011, a <a href="/wiki/2011_Halloween_nor%27easter" title="2011 Halloween nor\'easter">nor\'easter in October</a> left over 830,000\xc2\xa0people statewide without power, also breaking the record set by Gloria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[31]</a></sup> In New Hampshire, most power outages were quickly restored, and all were restored within six days. In Maine, all power outages were restored in 11\xc2\xa0days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sd2_30-2"><a href="#cite_note-sd2-30">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Maine, most citizens remained indoors during the passage of the hurricane. One police officer noted that even the criminals stayed home. In the <a href="/wiki/Lewiston,_Maine" title="Lewiston, Maine">Lewiston</a> area, restaurants experienced a great surge in business. During the power outage after the storm, several businesses stored frozen goods for houses without a generator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maine_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-maine-32">[28]</a></sup></p>\n<p>After the storm, President <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a> declared several counties in New Jersey, Pennsylvania, New York, Rhode Island, Connecticut, and Massachusetts as federal disaster areas. This declaration allowed those counties to apply for disaster assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disaster_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-disaster-36">[32]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Canada, the damage was less than expected, but confusion related to the storm threat prompted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environment_Canada" title="Environment Canada">Environment Canada</a> to research the need for a dedicated hurricane centre in 1986. The following year, the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a> was established with offices on both the Atlantic and Pacific coasts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ec_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-ec-20">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to its impact, the name Gloria was retired from the Atlantic tropical storm naming list. It will never again be used for an <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">Atlantic hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[33]</a></sup> It was replaced with Grace in the <a href="/wiki/1991_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1991 Atlantic hurricane season">1991 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-6">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
