id="bodyContent">\n<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\n<div id="contentSub"></div>\n<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">\n\t\t\t\t\tJump to:\t\t\t\t\t<a navigation-not-searchable" role="note">This article is about the geographical feature on the coast of North Carolina. For other uses, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Cape_Fear_(disambiguation)" title="Cape Fear (disambiguation)">Cape Fear (disambiguation)</a>.</div>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cape_Fear_Topo.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="210" data-file-width="300" height="210" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6b/Cape_Fear_Topo.jpg" width="300"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cape_Fear_Topo.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nCape Fear, on the coast of North Carolina.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nasa_photo_cape_fear.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="550" data-file-width="600" height="275" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Nasa_photo_cape_fear.jpg/300px-Nasa_photo_cape_fear.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Nasa_photo_cape_fear.jpg/450px-Nasa_photo_cape_fear.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/73/Nasa_photo_cape_fear.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nasa_photo_cape_fear.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nCape Fear in a NASA satellite photo, showing the estuary of the Cape Fear River.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p><b>Cape Fear</b> is a prominent <a href="/wiki/Headland" title="Headland">headland</a> jutting into the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bald_Head_Island,_North_Carolina" title="Bald Head Island, North Carolina">Bald Head Island</a> on the coast of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> in the southeastern <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It is largely formed of barrier beaches and the silty outwash of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Fear_River" title="Cape Fear River">Cape Fear River</a> as it drains the southeast coast of North Carolina through an <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuary</a> south of <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">Wilmington</a>. Cape Fear is formed by the intersection of two sweeping arcs of shifting, low-lying beach, the result of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Longshore_currents" title="Longshore currents">longshore currents</a> which also form the treacherous, shifting <a href="/wiki/Frying_Pan_Shoals" title="Frying Pan Shoals">Frying Pan Shoals</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Graveyard_of_the_Atlantic" title="Graveyard of the Atlantic">Graveyard of the Atlantic</a>.</p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Dune" title="Dune">Dunes</a> dominated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_oat" title="Sea oat">sea oats</a> occur from the upper beach driftline back to the stable secondary dunes, where they mix with other grasses such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saltmeadow_Cordgrass" title="Saltmeadow Cordgrass">Saltmeadow Cordgrass</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panic_grass" title="Panic grass">panic grass</a>, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seaside_goldenrod" title="Seaside goldenrod">seaside goldenrod</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spurge" title="Spurge">spurge</a> and other herbs to form a stable salt-tolerant <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a>.</p>\n<p>The Cape Fear estuary drains the largest watershed in North Carolina, containing 27% of the state\'s population.</p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Giovanni_da_Verrazzano" title="Giovanni da Verrazzano">Giovanni da Verrazzano</a>, the Italian explorer sailing for <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, made landfall after crossing the Atlantic at or near Cape Fear on March 1, 1524.</p>\n<p>The name comes from the 1585 expedition of Sir <a href="/wiki/Richard_Grenville" title="Richard Grenville">Richard Grenville</a>. Sailing to <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Island" title="Roanoke Island">Roanoke Island</a>, his ship became embayed behind the cape. Some of the crew were afraid they would wreck, giving rise to the name Cape Fear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is the fifth-oldest surviving English place name in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Cape Fear was the landing place of General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_Clinton_(1730%E2%80%931795)" title="Henry Clinton (1730\xe2\x80\x931795)">Sir Henry Clinton</a> during the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> on May 3, 1775. The 1962 film <i><a href="/wiki/Cape_Fear_(1962_film)" title="Cape Fear (1962 film)">Cape Fear</a></i> and its <a href="/wiki/Cape_Fear_(1991_film)" title="Cape Fear (1991 film)">1991 remake</a> were set at Cape Fear.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Fear_(headland)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Geography_of_North_Carolina" title="Geography of North Carolina">Geography of North Carolina</a></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Fear_(headland)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">Gannett, Henry (1905). <a class="external text" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=9V1IAAAAMAAJ&amp;pg=PA124#v=onepage&amp;q&amp;f=false" rel="nofollow"><i>The Origin of Certain Place Names in the United States</i></a>.
