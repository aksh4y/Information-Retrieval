American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Shreveport,_Louisiana" title="Shreveport, Louisiana">Shreveport, Louisiana</a>. He was well known for his commercial debut <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, "<a href="/wiki/A_Bay_Bay" title="A Bay Bay">A Bay Bay</a>"; as well as his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/51/50_Ratchet" title="51/50 Ratchet">51/50 Ratchet</a></i> (2007).</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li Dooley, Jr. was born on February 27, 1989, in <a href="/wiki/Shreveport,_Louisiana" title="Shreveport, Louisiana">Shreveport, Louisiana</a>. He attended <a href="/wiki/Huntington_High_School_(Shreveport,_Louisiana)" title="Huntington High School (Shreveport, Louisiana)">Huntington High School</a>, before he dropped out to pursue his rap career.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Chris_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span id="2007\xe2\x80\x9309:_51/50_Ratchet_and_Unleashed"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9309:_51.2F50_Ratchet_and_Unleashed">2007\xe2\x80\x9309: <i>51/50 Ratchet</i> and <i>Unleashed</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Chris_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007\xe2\x80\x9309: 51/50 Ratchet and Unleashed">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On April 10, 2007, Hurricane Chris released his debut <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> for his then-upcoming major-label debut album, called "<a href="/wiki/A_Bay_Bay" title="A Bay Bay">A Bay Bay</a>". The song was <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">produced</a> by Phunk Dawg. On August 8, 2007, he released the second single from the album, called "<a href="/wiki/The_Hand_Clap" title="The Hand Clap">The Hand Clap</a>". Phunk Dawg produced the track as well.</p>\n<p>On August 17, 2007, Hurricane Chris released his debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, called <i>Louisi-Animal</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remixed</a> version of "A Bay Bay" was included on the mixtape; "A Bay Bay" was officially remixed, featuring <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rappers</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" title="Game (rapper)">The Game</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lil_Boosie" title="Lil Boosie">Lil Boosie</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Baby</a>, Angie Locc and <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>.</p>\n<p>On October 23, 2007, Hurricane Chris released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/51/50_Ratchet" title="51/50 Ratchet">51/50 Ratchet</a></i>. The record debuted at number 24 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling 26,000 copies in the first week.</p>\n<p>On March 3, 2009, Hurricane Chris released the first single from his upcoming second album, called "<a href="/wiki/Halle_Berry_(She%27s_Fine)" title="Halle Berry (She\'s Fine)">Halle Berry (She\'s Fine)</a>". The song features guest vocals from fellow local rapper Superstarr, who also produced the track, alongside production duo <a href="/wiki/Play-N-Skillz" title="Play-N-Skillz">Play-N-Skillz</a>, and Q Smith.</p>\n<p>On September 8, 2009, Hurricane Chris released the second single for <i>Unleashed</i>, called "<a href="/wiki/Headboard_(song)" title="Headboard (song)">Headboard</a>". The song features guest appearances from American <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mario_(entertainer)" title="Mario (entertainer)">Mario</a> and fellow American rapper <a href="/wiki/Plies_(rapper)" title="Plies (rapper)">Plies</a>, while the production was provided by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Major" title="Lee Major">The Inkredibles</a>.</p>\n<p>On December 21, 2009, Hurricane Chris released his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Unleashed_(Hurricane_Chris_album)" title="Unleashed (Hurricane Chris album)">Unleashed</a></i>. <i>Unleashed</i> did not chart internationally, however, the record peaked at number 46 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">\'s</span> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>, and number 20 on the US <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">\'s</span> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Rap_Albums" title="Top Rap Albums">Top Rap Albums</a> charts, respectively.</p>\n<h3><span id="2010\xe2\x80\x9316:_Departure_from_Polo_Grounds_and_J"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9316:_Departure_from_Polo_Grounds_and_J">2010\xe2\x80\x9316: Departure from Polo Grounds and J</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Chris_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Records.</p>\n<p>Since being dropped from (or leaving) both Polo Grounds and J Records, Hurricane Chris released several mixtapes including <i>Caniac</i> on January 2, 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i>Hurricane Season</i> on September 4, 2015, and the singles "Bend It Over" on March 12, 2012, "Ratchet" featuring Lil Boosie on May 11, 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and "Sections" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla $ign</a> on August 21, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017:_King_Cane">2017: <i>King Cane</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
