border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/1970_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1970 Atlantic hurricane season">1970 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Celia</b> was the costliest <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> history until <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Alicia" title="Hurricane Alicia">Hurricane Alicia</a> in <a href="/wiki/1983_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1983 Atlantic hurricane season">1983</a>. The third <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a>, second hurricane, and first major hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1970_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1970 Atlantic hurricane season">1970 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Celia developed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> in the western <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> on July\xc2\xa031. Initially, the depression tracked north-northwestward without significantly strengthening, and crossed over western <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> on August\xc2\xa01. Heavy rains on the island caused severe flooding, leading to five fatalities. The depression entered the Gulf of Mexico and was upgraded to Tropical Storm Celia later on August\xc2\xa01. Due to warm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperatures" title="Sea surface temperatures">sea surface temperatures</a>, Celia <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensified</a> into a major hurricane on August 1 and after the creation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> in 1971 she was estimated to have been a Category 3 storm. Storm surge and swells lashed the west coast of Florida, especially the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">Panhandle</a>, causing eight people to drown. Early on August\xc2\xa02, Celia began to weaken. However, the storm underwent rapid deepening again and peaked as a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane with winds of 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) on August\xc2\xa03.</p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, tides caused minor coastal flooding. Minor erosion damage was reported along <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Highway_82" title="Louisiana Highway 82">Highway 82</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Cameron Parish, Louisiana">Cameron Parish</a>. Late on August\xc2\xa03, Celia made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi, Texas</a>. Throughout the state, 8,950\xc2\xa0homes were destroyed and damaged about 55,650\xc2\xa0others. About 252\xc2\xa0small businesses, 331\xc2\xa0boats, and 310\xc2\xa0farm buildings were either damaged or destroyed. Impact was the worst in <a href="/wiki/Nueces_County,_Texas" title="Nueces County, Texas">Nueces County</a>, which observed gusts as high as 180\xc2\xa0mph (290\xc2\xa0km/h). In Corpus Christi, about one-third of houses suffered severe damage or were destroyed. Additionally, about 90% of the buildings in downtown were damaged to some degree. Celia weakened as it continued further inland and dissipated over <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> on August\xc2\xa06. The remnants of Celia brought up to 2 inches (51\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall to the state. Overall, this storm caused 28\xc2\xa0deaths and $930\xc2\xa0million (1970\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>) in damage.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Celia_1970_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Celia_1970_track.png/220px-Celia_1970_track.png" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Celia_1970_track.png/440px-Celia_1970_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Celia_1970_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A tropical wave emerged into the Atlantic Ocean from the west coast of Africa in late July; a weather station in <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">Dakar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> observed a wind shift on July\xc2\xa023. The system moved quickly across the Atlantic at 20 and 25\xc2\xa0mph (32 and 40\xc2\xa0km/h) and reached the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> by July\xc2\xa028.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr1-1">[1]</a></sup> The wave then continued moving rapidly westward across the Caribbean Sea. However, falling <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressures</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">Southern United States</a> eroded a subtropical ridge, causing the system to decelerate by July\xc2\xa030. Around that time, a <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">closed circulation</a> began to organize in the northwestern Caribbean between the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swan_Islands,_Honduras" title="Swan Islands, Honduras">Swan Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-2">[2]</a></sup> At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on July\xc2\xa031, a tropical depression developed about 90\xc2\xa0mi (145\xc2\xa0km) west-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">Grand Cayman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> The depression strengthened while heading north-northwestward, though a <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance_aircraft" title="Reconnaissance aircraft">reconnaissance aircraft</a> could not complete its mission on July\xc2\xa031 due to the depression\'s proximity to Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Early on August\xc2\xa01, the depression made landfall in western <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province</a>, Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> A weather station in <a href="/wiki/Cape_San_Antonio,_Cuba" title="Cape San Antonio, Cuba">Cape San Antonio</a> recorded a minimum barometric pressure of 1,007\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (29.7\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>). After reaching the southeastern Gulf of Mexico on August\xc2\xa01, warm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperatures" title="Sea surface temperatures">sea surface temperatures</a> caused the depression to undergo <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid deepening</a>. Later that day, the depression reached tropical storm intensity and was named "Celia", based on a reconnaissance flight observing tropical storm force winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-2">[2]</a></sup> Satellite imagery indicated that Celia was becoming significantly more organized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr3-4">[4]</a></sup> At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa02, reconnaissance aircraft data indicated that Celia suddenly strengthened into a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane with sustained winds of 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr3-4">[4]</a></sup> However, the storm weakened to a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane about six hours later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> Due to Celia\'s steady west-northwestward motion across the Gulf of Mexico, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> marked that the storm "aimed at the Corpus Christi area like a wild beast stalking its prey."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr3-4">[4]</a></sup> Early on August\xc2\xa03, it weakened further to a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Unexpectedly, Celia underwent rapid deepening once again while nearing the coast of Texas later on August\xc2\xa03, including a 39\xc2\xa0mbar (1.2\xc2\xa0inHg) decrease in pressure in only 15\xc2\xa0hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr3-4">[4]</a></sup> At 1800\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa03, Celia attained its peak intensity with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> of 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) and a minimum barometric pressure of 945\xc2\xa0mbar (27.9\xc2\xa0inHg). Shortly thereafter, the storm made landfall near Corpus Christi, Texas at the same intensity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> Celia "maintained strength for an unusually long time" after moving inland,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr3-4">[4]</a></sup> weakening to a tropical storm at 0600\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa04. Early on the following day, the storm further weakened to a tropical depression. Celia dissipated over western Texas at 1800\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa05,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> though the remnants persisted until reaching New Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CeliaNM_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CeliaNM-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the Gulf of Mexico, oil rig crew workers began leaving and headed for land. Residents of Texas coastal towns of <a href="/wiki/High_Island,_Texas" title="High Island, Texas">High Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Port_Bolivar,_Texas" title="Port Bolivar, Texas">Port Bolivar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gilchrist,_Texas" title="Gilchrist, Texas">Gilchrist</a> were evacuated further inland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnunknown_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-mnunknown-6">[6]</a></sup> The National Hurricane Center also issued <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical cyclone warnings and watches</a> along the coast of Texas in anticipation of the storm. At 1600\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa02, a hurricane warning was posted from <a href="/wiki/Palacios,_Texas" title="Palacios, Texas">Palacios</a> to <a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur,_Texas" title="Port Arthur, Texas">Port Arthur</a>. About six hours later, the warning was extended southward to <a href="/wiki/Rockport,_Texas" title="Rockport, Texas">Rockport</a>. The hurricane warning was expanded further southward to Corpus Christi at 1000\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa03.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr4_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr4-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Celia caused 27 fatalities, four in Cuba, eight in Florida and 15 in Texas and left $930\xc2\xa0million in damages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dead_2011_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dead_2011-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi, Texas</a> suffered the worst impact, with at least 85% of all the structures in the city damaged, with 90% of downtown buildings were damaged or destroyed. Additionally, about one-third of houses were severely damaged or flattened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba_and_Florida">Cuba and Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cuba and Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>As a tropical depression, Celia dropped heavy rains over western <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, resulting in major flooding. Four people drowned and another man was electrocuted in the water when he tried to retrieve a downed power line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-2">[2]</a></sup> While over the central Gulf of Mexico, strong <a href="/wiki/Rip_current" title="Rip current">rip currents</a> and large swells affected Florida, measuring up to 10 feet (3.0\xc2\xa0m) in the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">Panhandle</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Escambia_County,_Florida" title="Escambia County, Florida">Escambia County</a> alone, at least 12\xc2\xa0lifeguard rescues occurred. <a href="/wiki/Pensacola_Beach,_Florida" title="Pensacola Beach, Florida">Pensacola Beach</a> was closed to the public on September\xc2\xa03, after several rescues on the previous day. Eight people drowned due to adverse water conditions in the Florida Panhandle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Texas">Texas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Texas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_celia_grounded_boats.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="639" data-file-width="700" height="183" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Hurricane_celia_grounded_boats.jpg/200px-Hurricane_celia_grounded_boats.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Hurricane_celia_grounded_boats.jpg/300px-Hurricane_celia_grounded_boats.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Hurricane_celia_grounded_boats.jpg/400px-Hurricane_celia_grounded_boats.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_celia_grounded_boats.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nBoats blown ashore by Celia at <a href="/wiki/Aransas_Pass,_Texas" title="Aransas Pass, Texas">Aransas Pass</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The most severe damage was in <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aransas_Pass,_Texas" title="Aransas Pass, Texas">Aransas Pass</a>. Throughout the state, 8,950\xc2\xa0homes were destroyed and it damaged about 55,650\xc2\xa0others. About 252\xc2\xa0small businesses, 331\xc2\xa0boats, and 310\xc2\xa0farm buildings were either damaged or destroyed. Initially, losses were estimated to have reached $434\xc2\xa0million. However, due to lower insurance rate of coverage, the original insured losses, $310\xc2\xa0million, were tripled, rather than doubled. Thus, the damage toll was revised upward to $930\xc2\xa0million in 2011. Damage to crops was slightly more than $8.8\xc2\xa0million. In addition, Celia caused 15\xc2\xa0fatalities and injured 466\xc2\xa0others. The most severe damage occurred in the streaks of damage, not by tornadoes, but a series <a href="/wiki/Microburst" title="Microburst">microbursts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Downburst" title="Downburst">downbursts</a>, most of which occurred in a 15\xc2\xa0minute span. Survivors of the storm described the downbursts as "rocket shells" exploding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PR5_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-PR5-10">[10]</a></sup> However, areas between the streaks suffered surprisingly little damage, mainly ornamental due to debris from the homes nearby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PR8_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-PR8-11">[11]</a></sup> Celia dropped heavy rains as it made landfall. However, due to the small size and fast movement of the storm, precipitation was not widespread. At the time of landfall, much of Texas was suffering from a drought. Most areas received 3 to 4\xc2\xa0in (76 to 102\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PR9_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-PR9-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Alvin,_Texas" title="Alvin, Texas">Alvin</a>, a small tornado caused the destruction of a few fences and moved a pickup truck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup> Downed trees caused slight property damage in Amistad Dam, a municipality in <a href="/wiki/Val_Verde_County,_Texas" title="Val Verde County, Texas">Val Verde County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Gusts between 170 and 180\xc2\xa0mph (270 and 290\xc2\xa0km/h) were reported within the <a href="/wiki/Aransas_County,_Texas" title="Aransas County, Texas">Aransas County</a> portion of <a href="/wiki/Aransas_Pass,_Texas" title="Aransas Pass, Texas">Aransas Pass</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Artesia_Wells,_Texas" title="Artesia Wells, Texas">Artesia Wells</a>, minimal damage occurred to property, reaching only $5,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The storm damage some homes and 15% of crops in <a href="/wiki/Austwell,_Texas" title="Austwell, Texas">Austwell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Losses in that section of the city reached an estimated $20\xc2\xa0million. Minor damage was inflicted on weak structures and shingle or metal roofs in <a href="/wiki/Crystal_City,_Texas" title="Crystal City, Texas">Crystal City</a>. Impact on trees and crops were minimal. Thus, the reported wind speeds of 85\xe2\x80\x9390\xc2\xa0mph (137\xe2\x80\x93145\xc2\xa0km/h) were considered "doubtful".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Property damage in <a href="/wiki/Del_Rio,_Texas" title="Del Rio, Texas">Del Rio</a> was about $1\xc2\xa0million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> with wind impacts mostly to roofs, mobile homes, signs, boats, windows, trees, and homes under construction, while some businesses and homes were flooded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Frio_County,_Texas" title="Frio County, Texas">Frio County</a>, a tornado spawned in the <a href="/wiki/Dilley,_Texas" title="Dilley, Texas">Dilley</a> area toppled utility poles, destroyed several chicken houses, and blew the roof of a house 600 to 900 feet (180 to 270\xc2\xa0m) away. In addition, two farm houses were deroofed and several outhouses were damaged. Another tornado spawned nearby caused "considerable" damage to the Dilley Civic Center, destroyed machine sheds, unroofed outbuildings, and felled many electrical poles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup> Throughout Dilley, there was $250,000 in property damage and $350,000 in crop losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> An estimated $50,000\xc2\xa0 was inflected to property in <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Pass,_Texas" title="Eagle Pass, Texas">Eagle Pass</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Strong winds in <a href="/wiki/George_West,_Texas" title="George West, Texas">George West</a> caused damage to 90% of trees, some houses, and cotton crops. Damage estimates in the city range from $250,000-$500,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Gregory,_Texas" title="Gregory, Texas">Gregory</a>, property losses was about $1\xc2\xa0million, while there was about $25,000\xc2\xa0in damage to crops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Near <a href="/wiki/Lake_Corpus_Christi" title="Lake Corpus Christi">Lake Corpus Christi</a>, a man died after he was struck by debris from his house, which was hit by a tornado. Two other people were injured by that tornado and several homes were destroyed. The storm spawned at least 2\xc2\xa0other tornadoes, those neither caused any known damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Damage in <a href="/wiki/Langtry,_Texas" title="Langtry, Texas">Langtry</a> was very minor, reaching only $600.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> A tornado in <a href="/wiki/Port_O%27Connor,_Texas" title="Port O\'Connor, Texas">Port O\'Connor</a>, destroyed a 30 by 230 feet (9.1 by 70.1\xc2\xa0m) boat storage shed, which was owned by Cooperative Weather Observed Bill H. Young.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Refugio,_Texas" title="Refugio, Texas">Refugio</a>, a rancher observed wind gusts up to 142\xc2\xa0mph (229\xc2\xa0km/h). About $707,500 in damage occurred to property, while crop losses reached $425,000. One injury was reported after a person was struck by flying glass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Wind gusts up to 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Sandia,_Texas" title="Sandia, Texas">Sandia</a> damaged every house, 90% of cotton crops, and caused 1\xc2\xa0fatality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Although wind gusts of 168\xc2\xa0mph (270\xc2\xa0km/h) were observed in <a href="/wiki/Taft,_Texas" title="Taft, Texas">Taft</a>, only $5\xc2\xa0million in property and $500,000 in crop damages were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Tilden,_Texas" title="Tilden, Texas">Tilden</a>, the storm brought maximum sustained winds up to 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h). As a result, the entire city lost telephone and electrical services and there was "lots of damage" to trees, 80% of houses, and roofs. Property damage reached about $3.4\xc2\xa0million, while there was also $25,000\xc2\xa0in crop losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Property losses in <a href="/wiki/Uvalde,_Texas" title="Uvalde, Texas">Uvalde</a> reached $100,000, while damage to crops was estimated at $250,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Additionally, a tornado was spawned in <a href="/wiki/Yoakum,_Texas" title="Yoakum, Texas">Yoakum</a>, though it apparently caused negligible impact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nueces_County">Nueces County</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nueces County">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alamo_Loan_after_Hurricane_Celia.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="884" data-file-width="1521" height="128" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Alamo_Loan_after_Hurricane_Celia.jpg/220px-Alamo_Loan_after_Hurricane_Celia.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Alamo_Loan_after_Hurricane_Celia.jpg/330px-Alamo_Loan_after_Hurricane_Celia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Alamo_Loan_after_Hurricane_Celia.jpg/440px-Alamo_Loan_after_Hurricane_Celia.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alamo_Loan_after_Hurricane_Celia.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDamaged building in Corpus Christi</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p><br/>\nThe highest tides produced by the storm lashed <a href="/wiki/Nueces_County,_Texas" title="Nueces County, Texas">Nueces County</a>. Tides were 9.2 feet (2.8\xc2\xa0m) and 9 feet (2.7\xc2\xa0m) mean sea level at Port Aransas Beach and the Port Aransas Jetty, respectively. The heaviest rainfall totals observed from the storm also fell in Nueces County, with 7.26 inches (184\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation reported in <a href="/wiki/Robstown,_Texas" title="Robstown, Texas">Robstown</a>. Because rainfall was relatively light, minor, if any flood damage occurred. Strong winds were reported, with winds gusts measuring as high as 161\xc2\xa0mph (259\xc2\xa0km/h) and 180\xc2\xa0mph (290\xc2\xa0km/h) at the <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi</a> Weather Bureau Office and <a href="/wiki/Aransas_Pass,_Texas" title="Aransas Pass, Texas">Aransas Pass</a>, respectively. Further inland, wind gusts were estimated to have reached 180\xc2\xa0mph (290\xc2\xa0km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Robstown_High_School" title="Robstown High School">Robstown High School</a>, as the oil derricks on the property, built to withstand winds of 175\xc2\xa0mph (282\xc2\xa0km/h), were knocked down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to high winds, 85% of the total property damage caused by the storm occurred in Corpus Christi, with 90% of the buildings in downtown either damaged or destroyed. About a third of the houses in the city had serious damage or were destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-7"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Texas_A%26M_University%E2%80%93Corpus_Christi" title="Texas A&amp;M University\xe2\x80\x93Corpus Christi">University of Corpus Christi</a>, a private institution located on <a href="/wiki/Ward_Island_(Texas)" title="Ward Island (Texas)">Ward Island</a>, suffered so much damage that it could not afford to rebuild, and it was sold to the State in 1973. Just northeast of the <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_International_Airport" title="Corpus Christi International Airport">Corpus Christi International Airport</a>, several hundred mobile homes were ripped into small fragments, and the remains were scattered for hundreds of yards. Facilities owned by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southwestern_Bell_Corporation" title="Southwestern Bell Corporation">Southwestern Bell Corporation</a> (which later became AT&amp;T) suffered $10\xc2\xa0million in losses. Another telephone company in the area, <a href="/wiki/GTE" title="GTE">General Telephone Company</a>, estimated $700,000 in damage occurred to its business. At the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> helicopter repair center in Corpus Christi, there were about $5\xc2\xa0million in damage. Around 800\xe2\x80\x93900\xc2\xa0family housing units at the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Corpus_Christi" title="Naval Air Station Corpus Christi">Naval Air Station Corpus Christi</a> were considered uninhabitable, indicating $35\xc2\xa0million in losses at that location.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_9-8"><a href="#cite_note-stormdata-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Two large <a href="/wiki/Sunoco" title="Sunoco">Sunoco</a> oil tanks and another owned by <a href="/wiki/Humble_Oil" title="Humble Oil">Humble Oil</a> burned after possibly being struck by lightning. Approximately $17\xc2\xa0million in losses occurred to both companies. Heavy property damage was also reported in rural areas, totaling slightly more than $20\xc2\xa0million. At least 75% of the buildings in <a href="/wiki/Port_Aransas,_Texas" title="Port Aransas, Texas">Port Aransas</a> were damaged. Overall, 74% of property losses associated with Celia were in Nueces County.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PR6_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-PR6-31">[31]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>While crossing the Gulf of Mexico, Celia produced tides 1 to 2 feet (0.30 to 0.61\xc2\xa0m) above normal on the southwestern coast of Louisiana. As a result, slight erosion of <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Highway_82" title="Louisiana Highway 82">Louisiana Highway 82</a> occurred in <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Cameron Parish, Louisiana">Cameron Parish</a> between <a href="/wiki/Johnson_Bayou,_Louisiana" title="Johnson Bayou, Louisiana">Johnson Bayou</a> and <a href="/wiki/Holly_Beach,_Louisiana" title="Holly Beach, Louisiana">Holly Beach</a>. No damage or fatalities were reported in that state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> After dissipating, the remnants of Celia produced up to 2 inches (51\xc2\xa0mm) of rain in <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, though no other impacts in the state are known.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CeliaNM_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-CeliaNM-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Following the storm, then-<a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a> declared <a href="/wiki/List_of_counties_in_Texas" title="List of counties in Texas">seven counties in Texas</a> as disaster areas \xe2\x80\x93 Aransas, Bee, Jim Wells, Live Oak, Nueces, Refugio, and San Patricio \xe2\x80\x93 allowing affected residents to be eligible for federal relief.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Immediately, then-<a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Texas" title="Governor of Texas">Governor of Texas</a> <a href="/wiki/Preston_Smith_(governor)" title="Preston Smith (governor)">Preston Smith</a> sent <a href="/wiki/National_Guard_of_the_United_States" title="National Guard of the United States">National Guardsmen</a> to the area to patrol and assist with recovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shj_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-shj-34">[34]</a></sup> About a week after the storm, Governor Smith stated that the most urgent need was financial assistance, and designated the <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> as the official relief agency. The American Red Cross chapters in <a href="/wiki/Colbert_County,_Alabama" title="Colbert County, Alabama">Colbert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lauderdale_County,_Alabama" title="Lauderdale County, Alabama">Lauderdale</a> counties in <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a> announced that they were accepting donations to send to southern Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shj_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-shj-34">[34]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In Corpus Christi, officials applied to pollution control officers for permission to burn 1,300,000 cubic yards (990,000\xc2\xa0m<sup>3</sup>) of debris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shj_34-2"><a href="#cite_note-shj-34">[34]</a></sup> The University of Corpus Christi (UCC) suffered so much damage that repairs were not affordable. In May 1971, the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Legislature" title="Texas Legislature">Texas Legislature</a> passed a bill to establish a Texas A&amp;I campus in Corpus Christi. Two years later, after local residents raised approximately $1.8\xc2\xa0million to support the school, the UCC transferred its properties to Texas A&amp;I University (now known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Texas_A%26M_University-Corpus_Christi" title="Texas A&amp;M University-Corpus Christi">Texas A&amp;M University-Corpus Christi</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In addition, most homeowners insurance policies refused to write insurance for areas considered to be "high risk" along the Texas coast, prompting the state government to form the Texas Catastrophe Property Insurance Association, now known as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Texas_Windstorm_Insurance_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Texas Windstorm Insurance Association (page does not exist)">Texas Windstorm Insurance Association</a>. Today, TWIA offers coverage to the 14 coastal counties of Texas and a small portion of <a href="/wiki/Harris_County,_Texas" title="Harris County, Texas">Harris County</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWIA_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-TWIA-36">[36]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Celia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p>Due to the severity of damage caused by the hurricane, the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> retired the name <i>Celia</i> the following year. The name was then replaced by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Carmen" title="Hurricane Carmen">Carmen</a> which was retired upon its first use in <a href="/wiki/1974_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1974 Atlantic hurricane season">1974</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> However, the name Celia is still used for Eastern Pacific name lists and was most recently used in <a href="/wiki/2016_Pacific_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Celia" title="2016 Pacific hurricane season">2016</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
