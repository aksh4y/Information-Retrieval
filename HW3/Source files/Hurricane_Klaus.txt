border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/1990_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1990 Atlantic hurricane season">1990 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Klaus</b> was a minimal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a> that dropped heavy rainfall across the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> in October 1990. The eleventh <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> and sixth hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1990 Atlantic hurricane season">1990 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Klaus developed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on October 3 a short distance east of <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a>. It drifted northwestward, and quickly intensified to attain hurricane status on October 5. Though its closest approach to the Lesser Antilles was within 12\xc2\xa0miles (19\xc2\xa0km), the strongest winds remained to its northeast due to strong <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, which caused Klaus to steadily weaken. After deteriorating into a tropical depression, Klaus briefly restrengthened over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> before dissipating on October 9 under the influence of developing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">tropical storm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Marco_(1990)" title="Tropical Storm Marco (1990)">Marco</a>.</p>\n<p>Heavy rainfall on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a> destroyed about 15% of its yearly banana crop. Damage was heaviest on <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, where seven casualties occurred and 1,500\xc2\xa0people were left homeless as the heavy rainfall caused severe flooding on the island, resulting in some landslides. The remnant moisture of Klaus entered the southeastern <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, dropping heavy rainfall and causing four casualties; the same area was affected with more precipitation a few days later by Tropical Storm Marco. Due to the damage caused by the storm, the name Klaus was retired from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_tropical_cyclone_names" title="List of tropical cyclone names">list of tropical cyclone names</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Meteorological_history"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Klaus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Klaus_1990_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Klaus_1990_track.png/220px-Klaus_1990_track.png" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Klaus_1990_track.png/440px-Klaus_1990_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Klaus_1990_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> on September 27. It tracked westward to the south of a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a>, becoming convectively active, and a low-level circulation was observed as it passed south of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> islands on September 28. The organization of the convection oscillated over the subsequent days, and a few times the system showed signs of developing into a tropical depression. As it approached the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> it organized further, and despite unfavorable upper-level <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> the system developed into Tropical Depression Thirteen on October 3 while located about 115\xc2\xa0miles (185\xc2\xa0km/h) east of <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a>. Located in an area of weak <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Steering_winds" title="Tropical cyclone">steering currents</a>, the depression drifted to the northwest, and about six hours after first developing the cyclone intensified into a tropical storm; the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> designated it with the name <i><a href="/wiki/Klaus" title="Klaus">Klaus</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Upon becoming a tropical storm, Klaus was located in an area of 29\xc2\xa0mph (47\xc2\xa0km/h) of wind shear, although concurrently it was located over warm water temperatures of 83.1\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (28.4\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Tracking through a highly <a href="/wiki/Baroclinity" title="Baroclinity">baroclinic</a> environment,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baro_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-baro-3">[3]</a></sup> the storm became better organized, and at 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on October 5 Klaus attained hurricane status about 30\xc2\xa0miles (50\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>; shortly thereafter, it passed 12\xc2\xa0miles (19\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbuda</a>, its closest point of approach to the Lesser Antilles. Klaus reached peak winds of 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) and a minimum central pressure of 985.0\xc2\xa0mbar (29.09\xc2\xa0inHg), though most of its deep convection and strong winds remained to its northeast due to wind shear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> At the time the hurricane was forecast to continue tracking to the north-northwest. However, after weakening to a tropical storm on October 6, Klaus turned westward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baro_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-baro-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Klaus continued tracking just north of the Lesser Antilles, and after continuing to deteriorate from the wind shear, the cyclone weakened to a tropical depression on October 8 to the north of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. Later that day, convection redeveloped over the center, and Klaus re-attained tropical storm status as it accelerated toward the northeast <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>; it briefly reached winds of 50\xc2\xa0mph (85\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> A low pressure area to its west over <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> had been steadily intensifying and building toward the surface, and on October 9 it developed into a tropical depression; the cyclone became the dominant system, eventually becoming <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Marco_(1990)" title="Tropical Storm Marco (1990)">Marco</a>, and Klaus dissipated under the influence of the system late on October 9. The remnant moisture continued to the northwest, reaching the coast of <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> by October 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Klaus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Shortly after Klaus attained tropical storm status early on October 4, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm warning</a> was issued for the northern <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a> from <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">Saint Martin</a> to <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>, which was upgraded to a hurricane warning as its quick strengthening became apparent; additionally, the government of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> issued a tropical storm warning for <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>. A hurricane watch was issued for the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">Virgin Islands</a>, though it was dropped as Klaus began to weaken.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr5_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr5-5">[5]</a></sup> In Guadeloupe, officials advised citizens to transport livestock to safer areas, and also to avoid potentially flooded areas. Prior to its arrival, schools were closed in <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap104_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap104-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VC_Bird_International_Airport" title="VC Bird International Airport">VC Bird International Airport</a> was closed during the passage of the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap105_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap105-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Later in its duration, the government of <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a> issued a tropical storm warning for the central and later northern Bahamas, though it was discontinued as the cyclone dissipated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr5_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr5-5">[5]</a></sup> Due to Hurricane Klaus, Space Shuttle Columbia\'s launch was delayed.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Klaus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Hurricane Klaus dropped moderate to heavy rainfall across the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>, potentially as high as 15\xc2\xa0inches (380\xc2\xa0mm); the hurricane affected many areas struck by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">Hurricane Hugo</a> in the previous year. On <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>, flooding from the rainfall blocked a few roads and forced a few families to move to safer areas; lightning from the outskirts of the storm left a portion of the island without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap104_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap104-6">[6]</a></sup> High winds and rainfall affected the island of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a>, which destroyed about 15% of the nation\'s banana crop for the year; damage totaled about $1\xc2\xa0million (1990\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The rainfall resulted in severe flooding on <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, which accrued to almost 10\xc2\xa0feet (3\xc2\xa0m) in some locations; two sisters drowned near <a href="/wiki/Saint-Joseph,_Martinique" title="Saint-Joseph, Martinique">Saint-Joseph</a> after a bridge was washed away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap104_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap104-6">[6]</a></sup> Several <a href="/wiki/Landslide" title="Landslide">landslides</a> were reported on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The passage of the cyclone left damaged and power and telephone systems. 750\xc2\xa0people evacuated their homes in <a href="/wiki/Le_Lamentin" title="Le Lamentin">Le Lamentin</a> due to flooding,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap104_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-ap104-6">[6]</a></sup> and a total of 1,500\xc2\xa0residents were left homeless on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap105_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap105-7">[7]</a></sup> Offshore, rough conditions damaged a fishing vessel, leaving its two passengers drifting on the boat for 25\xc2\xa0days before being rescued about 640\xc2\xa0miles (1035\xc2\xa0km) to the north-northwest of Martinique.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In all, seven people were killed on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a>, winds from the hurricane damaged power lines and downed trees in the northern portion of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap104_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-ap104-6">[6]</a></sup> High winds on <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a> damaged a few roofs, and also downed communications from two radio networks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap105_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap105-7">[7]</a></sup> The outer rainbands of the storm dropped light rainfall on the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">United States Virgin Islands</a>, reaching about 1.25\xc2\xa0inches (32\xc2\xa0mm) on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">Saint Thomas</a>; wind gusts peaked at 33\xc2\xa0mph (53\xc2\xa0km/h) on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Croix,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Croix, U.S. Virgin Islands">Saint Croix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Moderate rainfall continued into the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Grand_Turk_Island" title="Grand Turk Island">Grand Turk</a> reporting a total of 4\xc2\xa0inches (100\xc2\xa0mm) in 36\xc2\xa0hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On the east coast of <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, Klaus produced 15\xc2\xa0foot (4.5\xc2\xa0m) waves and tides of 3\xc2\xa0feet (1\xc2\xa0m) above normal. Beach erosion was reported along the east coast, due to persistent easterly winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> As the remnant moisture of Klaus entered the southeastern <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, it produced heavy rainfall between 10\xe2\x80\x9315\xc2\xa0inches (250\xe2\x80\x93380\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall in <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, with slightly lower totals in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>. In South Carolina, the rainfall caused a dam to burst, killing four people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr2-4">[4]</a></sup> About two days after the remnants of Klaus entered the southeastern United States, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Marco_(1990)" title="Tropical Storm Marco (1990)">Tropical Storm Marco</a> made landfall on northwestern <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, dropping more heavy rainfall and causing heavy damage across the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rain-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Klaus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote names</a></div>\n<p>Due to its impact on <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, the government of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> requested the retirement of the name <b>Klaus</b>; the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> retired the name from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_tropical_cyclone_names" title="List of tropical cyclone names">list of tropical cyclone names</a> in the following year and replaced it with the name <a
