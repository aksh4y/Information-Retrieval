activity">spacewalks</a> outside the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">ISS</a> during Expedition 12. MacArthur and Tokarev participated in both of them.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EVA_1">EVA 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: EVA 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The first EVA was on 7 November 2005 for 5 hours and 22 minutes. There were two main objectives, both of which were completed. The first was to install and set up a new camera on the <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">P1 Truss</a> which was later used in the installation of more <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">truss segments</a>. The second was to jettison the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Floating_Potential_Probe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Floating Potential Probe (page does not exist)">Floating Potential Probe</a> which was a failed instrument, designed to measure the station\'s electrical potential and compare it to the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Plasma_(physics)" title="Plasma (physics)">plasma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EVA_2">EVA 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: EVA 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The second spacewalk took place on 3 February 2006 and lasted 5 hours and 43 minutes. The astronauts jettisoned an old Russian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orlan_space_suits" title="Orlan space suits">Orlan spacesuit</a>, named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SuitSat-1" title="SuitSat-1">SuitSat-1</a>, that was equipped with a radio for broadcasts to students around the world. The suit reached the end of its operation life in 2004. They also retrieved the Biorisk experiment, photographed a sensor for a micrometeoroid experiment, and tied off the surviving <a href="/wiki/Umbilical_cable" title="Umbilical cable">umbilical</a> of the mobile transporter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_eclipse">Solar eclipse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Solar eclipse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_2006_March_29" title="Solar eclipse of 2006 March 29">Solar eclipse of 2006 March 29</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Solar_eclipse_from_space_29_Mar_2006.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2008" data-file-width="3032" height="199" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Solar_eclipse_from_space_29_Mar_2006.jpg/300px-Solar_eclipse_from_space_29_Mar_2006.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Solar_eclipse_from_space_29_Mar_2006.jpg/450px-Solar_eclipse_from_space_29_Mar_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Solar_eclipse_from_space_29_Mar_2006.jpg/600px-Solar_eclipse_from_space_29_Mar_2006.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Solar_eclipse_from_space_29_Mar_2006.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSolar eclipse from space 29 Mar 2006</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On 29 March 2006 a total <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">solar eclipse</a> took place, and the adjacent picture was taken by the Expedition 12 crew. It clearly shows the shadow of the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> being cast on the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concert">Concert</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Concert">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>While wake-up music is a tradition aboard space shuttle missions, the ISS crew generally use an alarm clock to wake up. Expedition 12 astronauts received a special treat on 3 November 2005 when <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a> performed <i>Good Day Sunshine</i> and <i>English Tea</i> in a first ever concert linkup from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arrowhead_Pond" title="Arrowhead Pond">Arrowhead Pond</a> in Anaheim, California on his <i>US</i> tour.
