title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Juan</b> was a significant <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> that heavily damaged parts of Atlantic Canada in late September 2003. Juan is also the first hurricane name and one of two to be requested to be retired by the <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_Canada" title="Meteorological Service of Canada">Meteorological Service of Canada</a> (the other being <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Igor" title="Hurricane Igor">Igor</a> of <a href="/wiki/2010_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2010 Atlantic hurricane season">2010</a>). It was the tenth named storm and the sixth hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. Juan formed southeast of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on September\xc2\xa024 from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> that had tracked across the subtropical <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. It tracked northward and strengthened over the warm waters of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>, reaching Category\xc2\xa02 strength on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane scale">Saffir-Simpson hurricane scale</a> on September\xc2\xa027. The hurricane peaked in intensity with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">sustained winds</a> of 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h) that same day, losing some strength as it raced over cooler waters toward the coast of <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>. Juan made landfall between <a href="/wiki/Shad_Bay,_Nova_Scotia" title="Shad Bay, Nova Scotia">Shad Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prospect,_Nova_Scotia" title="Prospect, Nova Scotia">Prospect</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Municipality" title="Halifax Regional Municipality">Halifax Regional Municipality</a> early on September\xc2\xa029 as a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane with winds of 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Juan retained hurricane strength while crossing Nova Scotia from south to north, though it weakened to a Category 1 storm over <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>. It was absorbed by another <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical low</a> later on September\xc2\xa029 near <a href="/wiki/Anticosti_Island" title="Anticosti Island">Anticosti Island</a> in the northern <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Saint_Lawrence" title="Gulf of Saint Lawrence">Gulf of Saint Lawrence</a>.</p>\n<p>The hurricane inflicted extensive damage across central Nova Scotia and into Prince Edward Island, with lesser damage east and west of the storm centre. Most of the damage occurred as a result of the intense winds that buffeted the region. Juan\'s passage resulted in eight fatalities and over CA $300 million (US $200 million) in damage. It was described as the worst storm to hit Halifax since <a href="/wiki/1893_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Three" title="1893 Atlantic hurricane season">1893</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juan2003summary_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-juan2003summary-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Juan_2003_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="719" data-file-width="1024" height="154" href="/wiki/File:Juan_2003_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A large <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> accompanied by a broad <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">area of low pressure</a> moved off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> on September\xc2\xa014, 2003. It initially tracked westward and remained disorganized due to unfavourable upper-level <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sept17two_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sept17two-4">[4]</a></sup> On September\xc2\xa020, the <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a> around the system greatly increased during interaction with the circulation of a large upper-level low, though unfavorable conditions caused the activity to remain disorganized. The system as a whole moved to the northwest around the upper-level low and developed a mid-level circulation. It interacted with a <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">frontal zone</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> and became better organized on September\xc2\xa023 while located 450\xc2\xa0mi (725\xc2\xa0km) south of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sept23two_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-sept23two-5">[5]</a></sup> Later that day, a low-level circulation developed within the system, though its involvement with the proximate frontal zone prevented it from being classified a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_low" title="Tropical low">tropical depression</a>. Deep convection increased near the centre on September\xc2\xa024, and the system quickly developed <a href="/wiki/Squall" title="Squall">banding features</a> and distinct <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>. Based on the increase in organization, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center (NHC)</a> classified it as Tropical Depression Fifteen later that day while it was located about 345\xc2\xa0miles (555\xc2\xa0km) southeast of Bermuda. Operationally, the agency did not initiate storm advisories until 27\xc2\xa0hours after the time of its formation determined by post-hurricane season analysis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Initially, the depression possessed a combination of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical characteristics</a>; it remained attached to a nearby frontal zone, though the organization of the convection and a warm core within the system resulted in classification as a tropical cyclone. Forecasters had predicted the depression would only slowly strengthen and reach a peak intensity of 65\xc2\xa0mph (105\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-td15disc1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-td15disc1-6">[6]</a></sup> However, the depression steadily organized and had strengthened into Tropical Storm Juan by early September\xc2\xa025.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Juan moved northwestward at around 10\xc2\xa0mph (16\xc2\xa0km/h) in response to a developing <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> to its east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juan2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-juan2-7">[7]</a></sup> On September\xc2\xa026, an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> feature developed, and very deep convection increased around the circulation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juan4_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-juan4-8">[8]</a></sup> The cloud pattern continued to consolidate, and Juan attained hurricane status later on September\xc2\xa026 while located 165\xc2\xa0mi (270\xc2\xa0km) southeast of Bermuda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The hurricane moved into an area of warm waters and light wind shear, allowing for additional strengthening; by September\xc2\xa027, Juan had attained a peak intensity of 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h) while located 635\xc2\xa0mi (1,020\xc2\xa0km) south of <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_Nova_Scotia" title="Halifax, Nova Scotia">Halifax, Nova Scotia</a>. At this peak strength, the eye of the hurricane was distinct and embedded within a well-defined and round central dense overcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avila10_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-avila10-9">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Juan_at_landfall03.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="527" data-file-width="720" height="161" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Juan_at_landfall03.JPG/220px-Juan_at_landfall03.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Juan_at_landfall03.JPG/330px-Juan_at_landfall03.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Juan_at_landfall03.JPG/440px-Juan_at_landfall03.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Juan_at_landfall03.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Juan making landfall in Nova Scotia on September 29</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Juan remained at peak intensity for over 24\xc2\xa0hours. After moving northwestward for an extended period of time, it turned and accelerated to the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The eye became less distinct on September\xc2\xa028, and the hurricane weakened slightly. Due to its fast forward motion, Juan had little time to weaken over significantly colder waters before making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> between <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shad_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shad Bay (page does not exist)">Shad Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prospect,_Nova_Scotia" title="Prospect, Nova Scotia">Prospect</a> (near Halifax) on September\xc2\xa029 with winds of 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pasch14_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-pasch14-10">[10]</a></sup> It weakened quickly while rapidly moving across the southern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Maritimes" title="Canadian Maritimes">Canadian Maritimes</a> and crossing the <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia_peninsula" title="Nova Scotia peninsula">Nova Scotia peninsula</a> as a hurricane. Juan had diminished to a tropical storm as it moved over <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a> by early September\xc2\xa029. Later that afternoon, the storm was absorbed by a large <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical low</a> over the northwestern <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Saint_Lawrence" title="Gulf of Saint Lawrence">Gulf of Saint Lawrence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The first advisories were issued by the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a> on September\xc2\xa026, although at that time they broadly suggested the possibility existed for wind and rain across <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC2612_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-CHC2612-11">[11]</a></sup> As Juan approached on September\xc2\xa027, warning broadcasts on local media in Atlantic Canada were changed accordingly. The public and <a href="/wiki/Emergency_management" title="Emergency management">emergency officials</a> in the expected landfall area were told to make preparations for a potential disaster, after the CHC bulletins indicated the possibility existed for significant wind damage and flooding from both heavy rain and <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surges</a>, as well as power outages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC2718_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-CHC2718-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On the morning of September\xc2\xa028, reports indicated that Juan would make landfall either as a tropical storm or marginal Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC2806_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-CHC2806-13">[13]</a></sup> Weather broadcasts up to that time gave every indication that the storm would weaken prior to landfall. By 6 p.m. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_Standard_Time_Zone" title="Atlantic Standard Time Zone">ADT</a> (2100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a>), additional warnings had been issued since Juan was expected to make landfall as a strong Category\xc2\xa01 or weak Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC2818_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CHC2818-14">[14]</a></sup> Most businesses in the areas affected were <a href="/wiki/Sunday_shopping#Canada" title="Sunday shopping">closed on Sundays</a>, which meant that preparations could not be made at the last minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-collapses_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-collapses-15">[15]</a></sup> Although no large-scale <a href="/wiki/Emergency_evacuation" title="Emergency evacuation">evacuations</a> were made, local evacuations for low-lying areas were issued on the evening of September 28. In all, several hundred people were affected by these evacuations. <a href="/wiki/Public_utility" title="Public utility">Utility</a> workers also stood on standby before the storm hit, preparing for large-scale power outages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bearsdown_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-bearsdown-16">[16]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nova_Scotia">Nova Scotia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Nova Scotia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Barringtonhurricane.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="349" height="303" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Barringtonhurricane.jpg/220px-Barringtonhurricane.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Barringtonhurricane.jpg/330px-Barringtonhurricane.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/7e/Barringtonhurricane.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Barringtonhurricane.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDebris on the streets of Halifax following the hurricane</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Juan\'s maximum sustained wind speed at landfall in Nova Scotia was measured at 100\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The urban concentration surrounding <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Harbour" title="Halifax Harbour">Halifax Harbour</a> bore the brunt of the highest sustained winds and strongest wind gusts during the storm; some unofficial estimates have placed gusts as high as 145\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-classify_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-classify-17">[17]</a></sup> Wave-rider <a href="/wiki/Weather_buoy" title="Weather buoy">weather buoys</a> off the entrance of Halifax Harbour snapped their moorings after reportedly recording waves in excess of 65\xc2\xa0ft (20\xc2\xa0m). Significant erosion occurred on the populated shores of the harbour, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Bedford_Basin" title="Bedford Basin">Bedford Basin</a> where residential properties and railway tracks received most of the wave action. Storm surges of 5 to 7\xc2\xa0feet (1.5 to 2\xc2\xa0m) were reported in the harbour; it was the highest surge ever recorded in Halifax Harbour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surge_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-surge-18">[18]</a></sup> Rainfall was fairly light due to the fast movement and dry air on the southern side of the storm. There were no rainfall reports greater than 2\xc2\xa0inches (52\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-19">[19]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Juan caused widespread structural and vegetation damage across the region, particularly in and around the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Municipality" title="Halifax Regional Municipality">Halifax Regional Municipality</a>. Extensive damage to trees was reported, which blocked many streets and knocked down power lines. Many homes and businesses suffered property damage, particularly roof damage on structures, and some weaker structures were destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup> HRM estimated that 31% of residential homes suffered some degree of damage and 27% of homes had enough damage to warrant an <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a> claim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-climatesmart_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-climatesmart-20">[20]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Halifax" title="Downtown Halifax">Downtown Halifax</a>, erosion-control boulders the size of garbage cans were hurled from Halifax Harbour onto boardwalks and parking lots and piers. The Victoria General Hospital experienced roof and water damage and was evacuated during the storm, as were numerous tall apartment buildings and other multi-family residences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-21">[21]</a></sup> Billboards and signs were also destroyed, and dozens of vehicles were crushed by trees and other debris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup> The city\'s cherished <a href="/wiki/Point_Pleasant_Park" title="Point Pleasant Park">Point Pleasant Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Public_Gardens" title="Halifax Public Gardens">Public Gardens</a> suffered massive loss of trees and remained closed for months.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Brokengarden.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1206" data-file-width="1759" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Brokengarden.jpg/220px-Brokengarden.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Brokengarden.jpg/330px-Brokengarden.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Brokengarden.jpg/440px-Brokengarden.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Brokengarden.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nDamage to the <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Public_Gardens" title="Halifax Public Gardens">Halifax Public Gardens</a> after Hurricane Juan</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Juan set an all-time maximum wind gust speed record at the <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Stanfield_International_Airport" title="Halifax Stanfield International Airport">Halifax Stanfield International Airport</a>; during the peak of the storm, a gust reached 143\xc2\xa0km/h, a record which still stands to this day. Another high windspeed occurred at the <a href="/wiki/McNabs_Island" title="McNabs Island">McNabs Island</a> autostation, which recorded a 2-minute sustained wind of 151\xc2\xa0km/h, with gusts to 176\xc2\xa0km/h. Hurricane-force gusts extended as far east as <a href="/wiki/Beaver_Island,_Nova_Scotia" title="Beaver Island, Nova Scotia">Beaver Island</a>, where gusts reached 132\xc2\xa0km/h. The hurricane was strong enough to destroy the <a href="/wiki/Anemometer" title="Anemometer">anemometers</a> at Shannon Hill, <a href="/wiki/Chebucto_Head,_Nova_Scotia" title="Chebucto Head, Nova Scotia">Chebucto Head</a> and Sambro Island, even though the Sambro Island instrument had survived a 193\xc2\xa0km/h storm some years earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The hurricane caused severe damage to shipping in Halifax Harbour. A visiting recreational schooner named <i><a href="/wiki/Larinda" title="Larinda">Larinda</a></i> was sunk at the wharf beside the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Museum_of_the_Atlantic" title="Maritime Museum of the Atlantic">Maritime Museum of the Atlantic</a> and a harbour tour ketch <a href="/wiki/Mar_(boat)" title="Mar (boat)"><i>Mar</i></a> was driven ashore in Eastern Passage on the opposite side of Halifax Harbour. Another harbour tour vessel, the tern schooner <i>Silva,</i> broke from her moorings and caused extensive damage to the Cable Wharf on the Halifax Waterfront. Dozens of smaller yachts were also driven ashore; extensive damage occurred to yacht clubs in the Bedford Basin and Northwest Arm. Dozens of containers were knocked off two container ships at the South End Container Terminal. Wharves on the Halifax and Dartmouth waterfront suffered large amounts of damage and several railcars were washed into the harbour at the Dartmouth railway yard; one of the tracks for the double-track main line was washed out in several places along the Bedford Basin near Millview. Coastal flooding was also reported around Halifax Harbour as a result of the storm surge,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surge_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-surge-18">[18]</a></sup> although inland flooding was minor as rainfall was not heavy due to Juan\'s fast forward movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Less severe property damage was recorded west of the storm\'s track into <a href="/wiki/St._Margarets_Bay,_Nova_Scotia" title="St. Margarets Bay, Nova Scotia">St. Margarets Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mahone_Bay" title="Mahone Bay">Mahone Bay</a>. In addition to Halifax Regional Municipality\'s urban core, the town of <a href="/wiki/Truro,_Nova_Scotia" title="Truro, Nova Scotia">Truro</a> and all of rural <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colchester_County,_Nova_Scotia" title="Colchester County, Nova Scotia">Colchester County</a> as well as the western part of <a href="/wiki/Pictou_County,_Nova_Scotia" title="Pictou County, Nova Scotia">Pictou County</a> experienced property damage and power outages from falling trees; numerous barns and other agricultural buildings were damaged east of the storm\'s path, including a replica of the <i><a href="/wiki/Hector_(ship)" title="Hector (ship)">Hector</a></i> in Pictou Harbour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup> The severity of property damage in the metropolitan areas of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halifax_Urban_Area" title="Halifax Urban Area">Halifax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dartmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Dartmouth, Nova Scotia">Dartmouth</a> of Halifax Regional Municipality initially led some forecasters to believe that Juan was likely a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane; however, the sustained wind reports did not justify that suggestion. Many of the deciduous trees in central Nova Scotia still had leaves, which magnified the effects of wind damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hithard_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-hithard-23">[23]</a></sup> Overall, the number of damaged trees was estimated to be in the millions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Juan claimed six lives (two directly) in Nova Scotia. Both of the direct deaths were due to fallen trees; one was a Halifax <a href="/wiki/Paramedic" title="Paramedic">paramedic</a> and the other was a motorist in <a href="/wiki/Enfield,_Nova_Scotia" title="Enfield, Nova Scotia">Enfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cleanup_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-cleanup-24">[24]</a></sup> Three of the four indirect deaths were as a result of a house fire started by candles when electricity was cut, and the fourth was in relief work after the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rest_of_Atlantic_Canada">Rest of Atlantic Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rest of Atlantic Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Juan_2003_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="802" data-file-width="968" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Juan_2003_rainfall.gif/220px-Juan_2003_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Juan_2003_rainfall.gif/330px-Juan_2003_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Juan_2003_rainfall.gif/440px-Juan_2003_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Juan_2003_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRainfall from Hurricane Juan</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>When the storm tracked across Nova Scotia and into the <a href="/wiki/Northumberland_Strait" title="Northumberland Strait">Northumberland Strait</a>, it was still a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane, weakening to a tropical storm as it emerged into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Saint_Lawrence" title="Gulf of Saint Lawrence">Gulf of Saint Lawrence</a>. Wind gusts of 86\xc2\xa0mph (139\xc2\xa0km/h) were reported in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlottetown,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island">Charlottetown</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juan2003summary_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-juan2003summary-3">[3]</a></sup> and 67\xc2\xa0mph (107\xc2\xa0km/h) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iles_de_la_Madeleine" title="Iles de la Madeleine">Iles de la Madeleine</a> in the Gulf of Saint Lawrence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Damage was also reported in <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a> as a result of the storm, particularly around Charlottetown, where its waterfront sustained heavy wave damage to pleasure craft and sea walls, as well as significant damage to the older <a href="/wiki/Urban_forest" title="Urban forest">urban forest</a> in that city\'s downtown core. Extensive tree damage was also reported across the island, as well as structural damage to weaker buildings, such as barns and silos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup> The hurricane left portions of the island without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The narrow path meant that damage was quite localized; little damage was reported in <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> or western Prince Edward Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juanedna_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-juanedna-26">[26]</a></sup> Voting in the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island_general_election,_2003" title="Prince Edward Island general election, 2003">PEI general election</a> on September\xc2\xa029 was also disrupted, though more than 80% of voters made it to polling stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-election_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-election-27">[27]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Two deaths were reported in the Gulf of Saint Lawrence off the remote <a href="/wiki/Anticosti_Island" title="Anticosti Island">Anticosti Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. They were fishermen from New Brunswick operating near Anticosti Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:JuanHFXwaterfront.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/JuanHFXwaterfront.jpg/220px-JuanHFXwaterfront.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/JuanHFXwaterfront.jpg/330px-JuanHFXwaterfront.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/JuanHFXwaterfront.jpg/440px-JuanHFXwaterfront.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:JuanHFXwaterfront.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHalifax Waterfront after Hurricane Juan</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In the hours following the storm a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> was declared in Halifax Regional Municipality and parts of neighbouring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Hants,_Nova_Scotia" title="East Hants, Nova Scotia">East Hants Municipal District</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colchester_County,_Nova_Scotia" title="Colchester County, Nova Scotia">Colchester County</a>. The Government of Nova Scotia requested that the federal government deploy 600 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces" title="Canadian Forces">Canadian Forces</a> personnel to assist local authorities in HRM with cleanup in the urban area and to assist utility crews with electrical power restoration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fallen_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-fallen-28">[28]</a></sup> Many utility workers, especially those employed by <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia_Power" title="Nova Scotia Power">Nova Scotia Power</a> and the dozens of crews from <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Electric" title="Maritime Electric">Maritime Electric</a> and <a href="/wiki/NB_Power" title="NB Power">NB Power</a> who responded under mutual assistance agreements, worked for almost three\xc2\xa0weeks without any considerable amount of rest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-summary_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-summary-2">[2]</a></sup> Many residents praised utility crews for their hard work in restoring the extensive damage.</p>\n<p>The devastated <a href="/wiki/Point_Pleasant_Park" title="Point Pleasant Park">Point Pleasant Park</a> remained closed for cleanup after the storm before re-opening in June 2004, nine months after the storm hit, with a damaged shoreline and almost 85% of its trees removed. A revitalization and <a href="/wiki/Reforestation" title="Reforestation">reforestation</a> program began in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ptpleasant_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-ptpleasant-29">[29]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The Government of Nova Scotia pledged $10\xc2\xa0million (2003 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_dollars" title="Canadian dollars">CAD</a>) in relief money after the hurricane hit, and private contributions were also made quickly after the storm hit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsrelief_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-nsrelief-30">[30]</a></sup> Prince Edward Island also pledged $200,000 (2003 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_dollars" title="Canadian dollars">CAD</a>) immediately after the hurricane hit,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peirelief_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-peirelief-31">[31]</a></sup> and the federal government also announced their own package.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juanhome_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-juanhome-32">[32]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Toronto" title="Mayor of Toronto">Mayor of Toronto</a> at the time, <a href="/wiki/Mel_Lastman" title="Mel Lastman">Mel Lastman</a>, also contributed $50,000 (2003 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_dollars" title="Canadian dollars">CAD</a>) to replace damaged trees in Prince Edward Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torontofunds_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-torontofunds-33">[33]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Hurricane Juan alerted residents, governments, utilities, and emergency management agencies throughout Atlantic Canada to improve preparations for devastating events such as hurricanes, especially with climatological data pointing to possible increased frequency of major ocean storms and extratropical cyclones. In addition to Juan, three other storms\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x94 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fabian" title="Hurricane Fabian">Fabian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Isabel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Kate_(2003)" title="Hurricane Kate (2003)">Kate</a>\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x94 had a significant effect on land or offshore in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enviro_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-enviro-34">[34]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Preparations and planning have been underway since 2003 and were first tested when <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ophelia_(2005)" title="Hurricane Ophelia (2005)">Hurricane Ophelia</a> was forecast to brush near Nova Scotia in early September 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-preview_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-preview-35">[35]</a></sup> Hurricane Juan has also resulted in several changes to the <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_Canada" title="Meteorological Service of Canada">Meteorological Service of Canada</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a>, which has relocated from a vulnerable and exposed location in an office building in <a href="/wiki/Dartmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Dartmouth, Nova Scotia">Dartmouth, Nova Scotia</a>, to a more secure location that can withstand hurricane damage. CHC\'s hurricane warning system has also been improved; traditionally, CHC did not issue standard hurricane or tropical storm watches or warnings, just high wind and heavy rainfall warnings, which were often not heeded by local residents. Beginning in the <a href="/wiki/2004_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2004 Atlantic hurricane season">2004 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, CHC began using standard hurricane warnings for storms potentially affecting Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-work_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-work-36">[36]</a></sup> Canada issued its first hurricane warning in 2008 in anticipation of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Kyle_(2008)" title="Hurricane Kyle (2008)">Hurricane Kyle</a> and in 2009, issued its second hurricane watch in anticipation of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Bill_(2009)" title="Hurricane Bill (2009)">Hurricane Bill</a>.</p>\n<p>Hurricane Juan lent its name to a severe blizzard that struck Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island a few months later in February 2004 which quickly became known as <a href="/wiki/White_Juan" title="White Juan">White Juan</a> because the paralyzing effects of the massive snowfall evoked the recent hurricane.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Because of its effects in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, the name Juan was retired in April 2004 and will never be used again for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a>. Environment Canada noted that its request for retirement was "in consideration of the lost and damaged lives, the impact to economy, and the widespread destruction of trees throughout two provinces".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> It was the first time that the Meteorological Service of Canada had specifically requested a hurricane name be retired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The name was replaced by <i>Joaquin</i> for the <a href="/wiki/2009_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2009 Atlantic hurricane season">2009 season</a>, but since it was not used during that year, it was used in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2015 Atlantic hurricane season">2015 season</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Joaquin" title="Hurricane Joaquin">although it was later retired</a>.</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
