colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Tropical Storm Larry</b> was the twelfth tropical storm in the <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. It was one of eight storms to impact <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> from either the Pacific or Atlantic Oceans in the season, a near-record. Larry formed in early October from an extratropical storm in the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a>, and reached a peak intensity of 65\xc2\xa0mph (100\xc2\xa0km/h). Due to weak steering currents, the storm moved southward, which resulted in the storm hitting the <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a> coastline. The storm was the first Tabascan landfall since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Brenda_(1973)" title="Hurricane Brenda (1973)">Tropical Storm Brenda</a> in 1973.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mexgob_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mexgob-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Larry drifted across the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Tehuantepec" title="Isthmus of Tehuantepec">Isthmus of Tehuantepec</a>, dropping heavy rainfall of over 9\xc2\xa0in (229\xc2\xa0mm) in places. The rainfall led to flooding and mudslides, causing damage to thousands of houses. The flooding killed five people and resulted in $53.6\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD) in damage. Larry was one of three tropical cyclones to hit <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> in a short period of time, including <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Nora_(2003)" title="Hurricane Nora (2003)">Tropical Depression Nora</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Olaf_(2003)" title="Hurricane Olaf (2003)">Tropical Storm Olaf</a> in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Meteorological_history"><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Larry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Larry_2003_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Larry_2003_track.png/220px-Larry_2003_track.png" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Larry_2003_track.png/440px-Larry_2003_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Larry_2003_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> on September 17. It moved across the shear-ridden <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> without development, and remained disorganized until reaching the western <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> on September 26. There, the wave situated itself beneath an upper-level anticyclone, allowing for favorable upper-level outflow and for deep convection to develop. On the September 27, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> developed while the system was located a few hundred miles to the east of the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</a>. The system continued to organize, and nearly developed into a tropical depression before moving ashore on the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula on September 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Dry air and land weakened the tropical wave, and when it entered the area of the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a>, it merged with a stationary frontal boundary. Cool, dry air around the system caused the system to develop a cold core, and the area organized into an extratropical low on September 30. A large high pressure system over the northern <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> forced the system southward, where it developed significant convection. The system developed a warm core, and on October 1 the storm organized into Tropical Storm Larry while located 300\xc2\xa0mi (483\xc2\xa0km) east-southeast of <a href="/wiki/Tampico" title="Tampico">Tampico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Weak steering currents allowed for Larry to drift westward at about two\xc2\xa0mph\xc2\xa0 (3\xc2\xa0km/h) while marginally favorable conditions allowed the storm to strengthen to a peak of 65\xc2\xa0mph (105\xc2\xa0km/h) on October 3. A mid-level ridge forced the storm more to the south-southeast, where after remaining a 60\xc2\xa0mph (97\xc2\xa0km/h) storm for three days, Larry made landfall on <a href="/wiki/Para%C3%ADso,_Tabasco" title="Para\xc3\xadso, Tabasco">Para\xc3\xadso</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a> on October 5. It steadily weakened over land, and degenerated into a remnant low on October 6 while midway through the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Tehuantepec" title="Isthmus of Tehuantepec">Isthmus of Tehuantepec</a>. The remnant low turned to the southwest, and reached the eastern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> on the October 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> The remnants of Larry re-organized in the eastern Pacific, with the National Hurricane Center indicating for the possibility of redevelopment into a tropical depression on October 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-two_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-two-3">[3]</a></sup> However, the convection diminished, and further development was no longer anticipated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-two2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-two2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span on October 4</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Due to its erratic motion, the <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> government issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">Tropical Storm Warning</a> and a Hurricane Watch early in Larry\'s lifetime from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veracruz,_Veracruz" title="Veracruz, Veracruz">Veracruz</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Campeche,_Campeche" title="Campeche, Campeche">Campeche</a>. The watches and warnings were extended westward to <a href="/wiki/Tuxpan" title="Tuxpan">Tuxpan</a> on the 4th and extended eastward to <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_del_Carmen" title="Ciudad del Carmen">Ciudad del Carmen</a> on October 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> Due to the threat of the storm, officials closed three <a href="/wiki/Pemex" title="Pemex">Pemex</a> oil ports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mexprep_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mexprep-5">[5]</a></sup> The company used its reserves to make sure profits weren\'t disrupted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mexprep2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-mexprep2-6">[6]</a></sup> In addition, the storm closed shipping ports in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dos_Bocas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dos Bocas (page does not exist)">Dos Bocas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coatzacoalcos" title="Coatzacoalcos">Coatzacoalcos</a> in <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cayo_Arcas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cayo Arcas (page does not exist)">Cayo Arcas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Campeche" title="Campeche">Campeche</a>. The Mexican government placed six coastal states on maximum alert,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mexprep_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-mexprep-5">[5]</a></sup> while authorities set up 75 evacuation shelters for around 1,500\xc2\xa0people. Because of the storm, the government declared much of eastern Mexico a state of emergency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mexprep3_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-mexprep3-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Larry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Larry_2003_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="758" data-file-width="1187" height="192" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Larry_2003_rainfall.gif/300px-Larry_2003_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Larry_2003_rainfall.gif/450px-Larry_2003_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Larry_2003_rainfall.gif/600px-Larry_2003_rainfall.gif 2x" width="300"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Larry_2003_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRainfall totals from Larry</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Tropical Storm Larry was one of eight storms to hit Mexico from either the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, the highest since the record of nine in 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmo-8">[8]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> expected the storm to produce a storm surge of 3 to 5\xc2\xa0feet (.9 to 1.5\xc2\xa0m), with high waves on top,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laradv10_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-laradv10-9">[9]</a></sup> though no official surge readings were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> The highest recorded winds on land were 59\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=El_Alacr%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="El Alacr\xc3\xa1n (page does not exist)">El Alacr\xc3\xa1n</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a>. The worst of Larry\'s effects came from its rainfall, peaking at 24.77\xc2\xa0inches (629.2\xc2\xa0mm) in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Upper_Juarez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Upper Juarez (page does not exist)">Upper Juarez</a> in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-rain-10">[10]</a></sup> The highest 24-hour rainfall total was 9.6\xc2\xa0inches (245.5\xc2\xa0mm) in Tortuguero, <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmo_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmo-8">[8]</a></sup> while several other locations reported over 4\xc2\xa0inches (102\xc2\xa0mm) in 24\xc2\xa0hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mexgob_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mexgob-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The flooding damaged more than 21,000\xc2\xa0houses across Mexico, in combination with the damage from Eastern Pacific Hurricanes <a href="/wiki/2003_Pacific_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Nora" title="2003 Pacific hurricane season">Nora</a> and <a href="/wiki/2003_Pacific_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Olaf" title="2003 Pacific hurricane season">Olaf</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laraft_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-laraft-11">[11]</a></sup> Damage was greatest around the Chiapas capital of <a href="/wiki/Tuxtla_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Tuxtla Guti\xc3\xa9rrez">Tuxtla Guti\xc3\xa9rrez</a>, where over 9,000\xc2\xa0houses were affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chipias_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-chipias-12">[12]</a></sup> The rainfall caused mudslides across the country, hospitalizing two individuals in central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hidalgo_(Mexico)" title="Hidalgo (Mexico)">Hidalgo</a>. The flooding also caused severe crop damage along Larry\'s path.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aplar_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-aplar-13">[13]</a></sup> Strong wind gusts caused outages to telephone and power services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmo_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmo-8">[8]</a></sup> In all, Larry caused five deaths<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> and $53.6\xc2\xa0million in damage (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $59\xc2\xa0million 2005\xc2\xa0USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zuniga_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-zuniga-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, rainfall from the remnants of Larry\xe2\x80\x94combined with previous rainfall\xe2\x80\x94caused mudslides and flooding, forcing several thousand people to evacuate in <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador" title="San Salvador">San Salvador</a>. The flooding destroyed or damaged hundreds of houses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eldam_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-eldam-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Larry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Tropical Storm Larry hit Mexico at around the same time as two other tropical storms. The Mexican Red Cross provided aid for 6,587 families throughout the country, while the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies launched an international appeal for aid. The appeal raised $284,472.8 (2003\xc2\xa0USD). The Mexican Red Cross distributed 4,000 food and hygiene packets to various places, and delivered 2,750 family packets and over 4,300 mattresses to citizens in Chiapas, as well as 5,000 school kits. A total of 38,750\xc2\xa0people benefited from the operation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laraft_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-laraft-11">[11]</a></sup></p>\n<div aria-label="Portals"
