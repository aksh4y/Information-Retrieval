(active)</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Site 81</b> at the <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> is a launch site used, along with <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome_Site_200" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome Site 200">Site 200</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Proton_(rocket_family)" title="Proton (rocket family)">Proton rockets</a>. It consists of two launch pads, areas 23 and 24. Area 24 is used for Proton-K and Proton-M launches, while Area 23 is inactive.</p>\n<p>Several planetary probes have been launched from Site 81. Area 23 was used to launch <a href="/wiki/Mars_3" title="Mars 3">Mars 3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mars_4" title="Mars 4">Mars 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mars_6" title="Mars 6">Mars 6</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venera_11" title="Venera 11">Venera 11</a>, whilst Area 24 was used by <a href="/wiki/Mars_2" title="Mars 2">Mars 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mars_5" title="Mars 5">Mars 5</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mars_7" title="Mars 7">Mars 7</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venera_9" title="Venera 9">Venera 9</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venera_10" title="Venera 10">Venera 10</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venera_12" title="Venera 12">Venera 12</a>. Several <a href="/wiki/Luna_programme" title="Luna programme">Luna</a> probes were also launched from both areas.</p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">Zarya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">Zvezda</a> modules of the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Salyut_2" title="Salyut 2">Salyut 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salyut_3" title="Salyut 3">3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salyut_5" title="Salyut 5">5</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Spektr" title="Spektr">Spektr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Priroda" title="Priroda">Priroda</a> modules of <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">Mir</a>, were launched from Area 23. Area 24 was used to launch <a href="/wiki/Salyut_1" title="Salyut 1">Salyut 1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salyut_4" title="Salyut 4">4</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salyut_6" title="Salyut 6">6</a>.</p>\n<p>On 2 July 2013, a <a href="/wiki/Proton-M" title="Proton-M">Proton-M</a>/<a href="/wiki/Blok_DM-03" title="Blok DM-03">DM-03</a> launched from Site 81/24 carrying three <a href="/wiki/GLONASS" title="GLONASS">GLONASS</a> navigation satellites. Immediately after liftoff, the rocket began to pitch over, and rolled out of control. The rocket fell close to the launch pad and exploded, the extent of any damage to Site 81 or the Baikonur Cosmodrome is unclear.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baikonur_Cosmodrome_Site_81&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><cite class="citation web">Wade, Mark. <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20081202182748/http://www.astronautix.com/sites/baic8123.htm" rel="nofollow">"Baikonur LC81/23"</a>.
