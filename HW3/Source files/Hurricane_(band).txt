Ames</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band formed in the 1980s, originally featuring current <a href="/wiki/Foreigner_(band)" title="Foreigner (band)">Foreigner</a> lead vocalist <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Hansen" title="Kelly Hansen">Kelly Hansen</a> (vocals/rhythm guitar), <a href="/wiki/Robert_Sarzo" title="Robert Sarzo">Robert Sarzo</a> (guitar), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Cavazo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tony Cavazo (page does not exist)">Tony Cavazo</a> (bass), and <a href="/wiki/Jay_Schellen" title="Jay Schellen">Jay Schellen</a> (drums). Cavazo and Sarzo are the younger brothers of <a href="/wiki/Quiet_Riot" title="Quiet Riot">Quiet Riot</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Cavazo" title="Carlos Cavazo">Carlos Cavazo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rudy_Sarzo" title="Rudy Sarzo">Rudy Sarzo</a>.</p>\n<p>Hurricane released four albums: <i>Take What You Want</i> (1985), <i>Over the Edge</i> (1988), <i>Slave to the Thrill</i> (1990), and <i>Liquifury</i> (2001). <i>Over the Edge</i> was their most successful album featuring their only top 40 hit (at 33), "I\'m on to You" in 1988 (written by Jeff Jones).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Quiet_Riot" title="Quiet Riot">Quiet Riot</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Kevin_DuBrow" title="Kevin DuBrow">Kevin DuBrow</a> introduced <a href="/wiki/Robert_Sarzo" title="Robert Sarzo">Robert Sarzo</a> and Tony Cavazo in the early 1980s. After deciding to form a band, Sarzo and Cavazo recruited singer <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Hansen" title="Kelly Hansen">Kelly Hansen</a>, drummer <a href="/wiki/Jay_Schellen" title="Jay Schellen">Jay Schellen</a> and guitarist Michael Guy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> With little label interest, the band decided to release an album themselves, <i>Take What You Want</i>. The album and constant touring led to them getting a major label deal.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Increasing_popularity">Increasing popularity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Increasing popularity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1986 &amp; 1987 they opened for <a href="/wiki/Stryper" title="Stryper">Stryper</a> on their <i><a href="/wiki/To_Hell_with_the_Devil" title="To Hell with the Devil">To Hell with the Devil</a></i> album tour. They were soon signed to <a href="/wiki/Enigma_Records" title="Enigma Records">Enigma Records</a>, the same label as Stryper. They also opened for Gary Moore\'s 87 US tour.</p>\n<p>In 1988, the band released their major label debut album, <i>Over the Edge</i>. Propelled by a powerful, yet melodic <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a> sound, the album dipped into the top 100 (at 92) on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">album charts</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and featured a cover of <a href="/wiki/Alice_Cooper" title="Alice Cooper">Alice Cooper</a>\'s "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Eighteen" title="I\'m Eighteen">I\'m Eighteen</a>," as well as the band\'s most successful hit, the <a class="external text" href="http://www.myspace.com/jeffersonjones#!" rel="nofollow">Jeff Jones</a>-penned "I\'m On to You." The title track, "Over the Edge" was also released as a single.</p>\n<p>In 1989, Sarzo left the group and was replaced by ex-Lion guitarist, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Aldrich" title="Doug Aldrich">Doug Aldrich</a>. This lineup recorded the album <i>Slave to the Thrill</i>, which was released in 1990. While <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> hailed the record as the band\'s most "focused" album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> its success paled in comparison to its predecessor. Noteworthy, however, is the fact that the album\'s relative lack of success could also be attributed to changing musical climates (indeed, the topic of which Hurricane album was most classic, <i>Over the Edge</i> or <i>Slave to the Thrill</i> remains heavily debated amongst Hurricane fans<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>). Shortly after the release of this album, Aldrich moved on to <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords_(band)" title="House of Lords (band)">House of Lords</a> and the rest of the group quickly disintegrated with the onslaught of grunge.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1991 Schellen wrote, recorded and toured extensively with both <a href="/wiki/Unruly_Child" title="Unruly Child">Unruly Child</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sircle_of_Silence&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sircle of Silence (page does not exist)">Sircle of Silence</a> while Hansen did a considerable amount of session work. Schellen suggested that Hansen help with the recording of Unruly Child\'s second release, and shortly after they agreed to resurrect Hurricane. <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a> guitarist Sean Manning (former bandmate and Kevin Dubrow /Quiet Riot guitarist) (guitar) and Larry Antonino (bass) rounded out the new version of the band, and they released <i>Liquifury</i>. Guitarists <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Villalobos" title="Carlos Villalobos">Carlos Villalobos</a> and Randall Strom also appear as session musicians on this album, performing on one track apiece. Villalobos also co-wrote the track on which he performs. In the late 1990s Schellen begins a continuing collaboration <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_(band)" title="World Trade (band)">World Trade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_(band)" title="Conspiracy (band)">Conspiracy</a> with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Squire" title="Chris Squire">Chris Squire</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yes_(band)" title="Yes (band)">Yes</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIRCA:" title="CIRCA:">CIRCA:</a> with friend and band mate <a href="/wiki/Billy_Sherwood" title="Billy Sherwood">Billy Sherwood</a>. Schellen joined the popular progressive pop band <a href="/wiki/Asia_(band)" title="Asia (band)">Asia</a> in 2005 and continues now in <a href="/wiki/Asia_Featuring_John_Payne" title="Asia Featuring John Payne">Asia Featuring John Payne</a>.</p>\n<p>Hansen joined <a href="/wiki/Foreigner_(band)" title="Foreigner (band)">Foreigner</a>, and Aldrich is currently in the Las Vegas production Raiding The Rock Vault.</p>\n<p>Manning went on to form The Exiles with 3 other English expatriates, original <a href="/wiki/Bonham_(band)" title="Bonham (band)">Bonham</a> vocalist Paul Rafferty, bassist Paul Stanley, and drummer Terry Muscall. The band split up in the mid 1990s but a collection of demos, in part co-produced by <a href="/wiki/Pat_Benatar" title="Pat Benatar">Pat Benatar</a> guitarist Neil Giraldo, surfaced in 1996 as Sean Manning &amp; Paul Raffery - <i>The Exiles</i> (Indivision/SDM), with additional musical contributions by Giraldo, longtime Benatar drummer Myron Grombacher, <a href="/wiki/Greg_D%27Angelo" title="Greg D\'Angelo">Greg D\'Angelo</a>, and Richard Baker, among others.</p>\n<p>In 2010, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Sarzo" title="Robert Sarzo">Robert Sarzo</a> and Tony Cavazo, founding members, reunited and recruited singer <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Freeman_(musician)" title="Andrew Freeman (musician)">Andrew Freeman</a> and drummer Mike Hansen. They announced a tour and a CD in 2011. The band is currently on a hiatus.</p>\n<p>In 2013, it was announced that Sarzo would be named the new lead guitarist of <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Tate" title="Geoff Tate">Geoff Tate</a>\'s version of <a href="/wiki/Queensr%C3%BFche" title="Queensr\xc3\xbfche">Queensr\xc3\xbfche</a>.</p>\n<p>Hurricane was the opening act on the 2014 tour with Tate\'s Queensr\xc3\xbfche. Sarzo played with both the opening and headlining acts on the entire tour.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
