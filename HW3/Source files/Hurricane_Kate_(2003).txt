colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Kate</b> was the second-longest lived <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. The eleventh tropical storm, fifth hurricane, and third major hurricane of the season, Kate developed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> in the central tropical Atlantic on September 25. Its unusual track included four major changes in direction. The storm moved northwestward until a weakness in the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> forced it eastward. Kate strengthened to a hurricane, turned sharply westward while moving around a mid-level low, and intensified to a 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) major hurricane on October 4. Kate turned sharply northward around the periphery of an <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a>, weakened, and became extratropical after passing to the east of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>. The extratropical storm persisted for three days until losing its identity near <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>.</p>\n<p>The storm had minimal effects on land, limited to moderately strong winds and heavy rainfall over Newfoundland. Kate threatened <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a> just one week after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Juan" title="Hurricane Juan">Hurricane Juan</a> caused severe damage in <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Kate_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kate_2003_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="1024" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Kate_2003_track.png/220px-Kate_2003_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Kate_2003_track.png/330px-Kate_2003_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Kate_2003_track.png/440px-Kate_2003_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kate_2003_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On September 21, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> \xe2\x80\x93 a trough of relatively low atmospheric pressure \xe2\x80\x93 began moving slowly westward from the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, passing near the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> islands on September 23. Though the wave was disorganized, a low-level circulation developed on September 24 a few hundred miles to the southwest of Cape Verde. Steady organization ensued, including the development of convection into curved <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainbands" title="Rainbands">bands</a> around the system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Though it lacked a well-defined center, the system organized enough to be classified Tropical Depression Sixteen on September 25 while located about 920\xc2\xa0mi (1,480\xc2\xa0km) west-southwest of the Cape Verde Islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-td16disc1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-td16disc1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The depression moved northwestward due to a weakness in the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Despite early predictions of steady strengthening to reach 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h) within 48\xc2\xa0hours,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-td16disc1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-td16disc1-2">[2]</a></sup> the depression remained weak due to strong south-southwesterly <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>. The shear remained strong,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> and though the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> forecast the system to remain a depression through September 30,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-td16disc8_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-td16disc8-3">[3]</a></sup> the depression unexpectedly strengthened to Tropical Storm Kate on September 27, coinciding with an increase in deep convection near the center. Kate turned to the north and northeast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> and despite 35\xc2\xa0mph (55\xc2\xa0km/h) of shear over the storm Kate continued to strengthen. A banding <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> developed early on September 29,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kate16_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-kate16-4">[4]</a></sup> and later that day it intensified into a hurricane while located 655\xc2\xa0mi (1,055\xc2\xa0km) southwest of the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Hurricane Kate maintained hurricane status for twelve hours before decelerating to the north and weakening to a tropical storm. The storm then turned sharply west-southwestward while moving around the eastern side of a mid-level circulation. Passing over progressively warmer waters and into an area with decreasing vertical shear, Kate regained hurricane status on October 1. Guided by an <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a> to its north and a cyclonic circulation to its south, the hurricane continued west-southwestward over the subtropical waters of 30\xc2\xb0\xc2\xa0N, and presented a well-defined eye on October 2. On the next day it strengthened to attain major hurricane status, and on October 4 Hurricane Kate reached a peak intensity of 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) while located 650\xc2\xa0mi (1,050\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Kate_(2003)-_Good_pic.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="680" data-file-width="1000" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Hurricane_Kate_%282003%29-_Good_pic.jpg/200px-Hurricane_Kate_%282003%29-_Good_pic.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Hurricane_Kate_%282003%29-_Good_pic.jpg/300px-Hurricane_Kate_%282003%29-_Good_pic.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Hurricane_Kate_%282003%29-_Good_pic.jpg/400px-Hurricane_Kate_%282003%29-_Good_pic.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Kate_(2003)-_Good_pic.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSatellite image of Kate</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Shortly after peaking, the western portion of the central dense overcast, or the area of high, thick clouds around the eye, began to deteriorate, and Hurricane Kate started to weaken. On October 5, the storm dropped below major hurricane status. Kate turned sharply northward due to it moving around the western periphery of a powerful anticyclone, and consequently slowed its forward motion. It slowly weakened as it turned to the north-northeast, with an eye occasionally re-appearing on satellite images. On October 7, however, Kate weakened to a tropical storm while moving over cooler waters. It accelerated to the northeast, and after passing about 260\xc2\xa0mi (420\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>, Kate became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical" title="Extratropical">extratropical</a> as cold air clouds entrained the center. The system remained a powerful system, and later on October 8 the extratropical remnant of Kate retained winds of up to 75\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h) while passing south of <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>. The storm turned to the east, passed a short distance south of <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, and merged with another extratropical storm near <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> on October 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">Preparations and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Kate_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Just a week after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Juan" title="Hurricane Juan">Hurricane Juan</a> hit <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland and Labrador</a> Emergency Measures Organization advised the general public to take appropriate actions for the anticipated effects of Hurricane Kate, including high winds and potentially up to 3\xc2\xa0in (80\xc2\xa0mm) of rain. Such actions include securing loose objects, cleaning debris from storm drains, and owning appropriate hurricane supplies (such as a charged cell phone and batteries in the event of power outages).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nalemo_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nalemo-5">[5]</a></sup> A cruise ship scheduled to stop in <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John\'s, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. John\'s</a> bypassed Newfoundland to avoid the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbc_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbc-6">[6]</a></sup> The Newfoundland and Maritimes Weather Center issued a storm warning for <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a> issued a hurricane-force wind warning. Due to the expected combination of moisture from Kate and a <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">cold front</a>, the Canadian Hurricane Centre also issued a heavy rainfall warning for southeastern Newfoundland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chcbulletin_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-chcbulletin-7">[7]</a></sup> Offshore, waves were expected to reach 33\xc2\xa0feet (10\xc2\xa0m), while marine wind warnings were issued as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chcbulletin2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-chcbulletin2-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p>From September 28 to October 7, 33\xc2\xa0ships reported winds of over 40\xc2\xa0mph (65\xc2\xa0km/h) in relation to Kate, though nearly all occurred as the storm was becoming extratropical. The highest wind reported was 59\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h), while the lowest pressure reported from a ship was 989\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The interaction between Kate and a high pressure area to its north produced 3\xe2\x80\x934\xc2\xa0foot (1\xc2\xa0m) waves along the coast of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surfline_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-surfline-9">[9]</a></sup> The strongest winds of Kate remained away from any landmasses, though the large circulation produced sustained winds of up to 40\xc2\xa0mph (65\xc2\xa0km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Cape_Race" title="Cape Race">Cape Race</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland</a>. The storm also generated strong swells and surf along the southern portion of the <a href="/wiki/Avalon_Peninsula" title="Avalon Peninsula">Avalon Peninsula</a>, reaching heights of 9\xe2\x80\x9313\xc2\xa0feet (3\xe2\x80\x934\xc2\xa0m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-katebulletin3_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-katebulletin3-10">[10]</a></sup> The combination of moisture from Kate and a stalled frontal system produced moderate amounts of rainfall, with some regions in southeastern Newfoundland receiving more than 4\xc2\xa0inches (100\xc2\xa0mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc03_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-chc03-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John\'s, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. John\'s</a> reported 1.8\xc2\xa0inches (45\xc2\xa0mm) on October 6, a record for the date. Due to dry conditions in the preceding weeks, the ground easily absorbed the water, resulting in a lack of flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbcnews_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbcnews-12">[12]</a></sup> The extratropical remnant of Hurricane Kate produced winds of up to 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) in northern <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-garypadgett_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-garypadgett-13">[13]</a></sup> Overall, there were no reports of damage or deaths associated with Kate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
