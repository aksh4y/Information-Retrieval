(NASA)</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<div style="clear:left;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_objectives">Mission objectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_4&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mission objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The International Space Station expanded its science investigations, almost doubling the previous amount of experiments performed during the Expedition Four mission. The fourth resident crew launched on 5 December 2001 on board Space Shuttle Endeavour during mission <a href="/wiki/STS-108" title="STS-108">STS-108</a>. They became official station residents at 20:03 UTC on 7 December 2001, and remained on board until June 2002, when they landed on <a href="/wiki/STS-111" title="STS-111">STS-111</a>.</p>\n<p>An international crew of three were the fourth crew to live aboard the International Space Station. The team was led by Russian Yuri I. Onufrienko and joined by American crewmates Daniel W. Bursch and Carl E. Walz, both flight engineers. As a part of the STS-108 mission, Endeavour delivered the Expedition 4 crew to the station. They returned to Earth 19 June 2002, aboard Space Shuttle Endeavour following the STS-111 mission.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalks">Spacewalks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_4&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Spacewalks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Expedition Four crew conducted three spacewalks during its stay on board the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>. The crew spent a total of 17 hours and 51 minutes outside the station. These spacewalks brought the total up to 34\xe2\x80\x94nine station-based and 25 shuttle-based\xe2\x80\x94that have been conducted at the station for total of 208 hours and 5 minutes.</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<tr bgcolor="#EFEFEF">\n<th style="background:#ccccff;color:black;text-align:left" width="10%"><b>Mission</b></th>\n<th style="background:#ccccff;color:black;text-align:left" width="20%"><b>Spacewalkers</b></th>\n<th style="background:#ccccff;color:black;text-align:left" width="16%"><b>Start (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>)</b></th>\n<th style="background:#ccccff;color:black;text-align:left" width="16%"><b>End (UTC)</b></th>\n<th style="background:#ccccff;color:black;text-align:left" width="12%"><b>Duration</b></th>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td rowspan="2" style="border-bottom:2px solid grey;">Expedition 4<br/>\n<small>EVA 1</small><br/></td>\n<td><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yury_Onufrienko" title="Yury Onufrienko">Yury Onufrienko</a><br/>\n<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carl_Walz" title="Carl Walz">Carl Walz</a></td>\n<td>14 January 2002<br/>\n20:59</td>\n<td>15 January 2002<br/>\n03:02</td>\n<td>6 hours 3 minutes</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="4" style="border-bottom:2px solid grey;">Onufrienko and Walz relocated the cargo boom for the Russian Strela crane. They moved the boom from Pressurized Mating Adapter 1 to the exterior of the Pirs Docking Compartment. The crew also installed an amateur radio antenna onto the end of the <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">Zvezda Service Module</a>. The space walk was based out of the <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)">Pirs Airlock</a> and used Russian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orlan_space_suits" title="Orlan space suits">Orlan space suits</a>.</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td rowspan="2" style="border-bottom:2px solid grey;">Expedition 4<br/>\n<small>EVA 2</small><br/></td>\n<td>Onufrienko<br/>\n<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_Bursch" title="Daniel Bursch">Daniel Bursch</a></td>\n<td>25 January 2002<br/>\n15:19</td>\n<td>25 January 2002<br/>\n21:18</td>\n<td>5 hours 59 minutes</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="4" style="border-bottom:2px solid grey;">During Expedition Four\'s second spacewalk, Onufrienko and Bursch installed six deflector shields for the <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">Zvezda Service Module\'s</a> jet thrusters. Also, they installed an amateur radio antenna, attached four science experiments, and retrieved and replaced a device to measure material from the thrusters. Like the first EVA, this one was based out of <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)">Pirs</a>, and the spacewalkers used Orlan suits.</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td rowspan="2" style="border-bottom:2px solid grey;">Expedition 4<br/>\n<small>EVA 3</small><br/></td>\n<td>Walz<br/>\nBursch</td>\n<td>20 February 2002<br/>\n15:19</td>\n<td>20 February 2002<br/>\n17:25</td>\n<td>5 hours 49 minutes</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="4" style="border-bottom:2px solid grey;">This spacewalk was based out of the <a href="/wiki/Quest_Joint_Airlock" title="Quest Joint Airlock">Quest Airlock</a>, using U.S. spacesuits. Walz and Bursch tested the airlock and prepared for the four spacewalks that were to be performed during <a href="/wiki/STS-110" title="STS-110">STS-110</a> in April. The STS-110 crew were to install the <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">S0 Truss</a> onto the station. This spacewalk was the first spacewalk to be based out of <a href="/wiki/Quest_Joint_Airlock" title="Quest Joint Airlock">Quest</a> without a space shuttle at the station.</td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
