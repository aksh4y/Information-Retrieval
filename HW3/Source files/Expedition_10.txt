class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mission objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Chiao and Sharipov docked at the Space Station on 16 October 2004 aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-5" title="Soyuz TMA-5">Soyuz TMA-5</a>, to relieve <a href="/wiki/Expedition_9" title="Expedition 9">Expedition 9</a> crewmates <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Fincke" title="Mike Fincke">Mike Fincke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gennady_Padalka" title="Gennady Padalka">Gennady Padalka</a>. Chiao was the expedition commander and NASA science officer, and Sharipov was the Soyuz commander and flight engineer. Both Astronauts were researchers in the <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Diagnostic_Ultrasound_in_Microgravity" title="Advanced Diagnostic Ultrasound in Microgravity">Advanced Diagnostic Ultrasound in Microgravity</a> Project.</p>\n<p>Notable accomplishments included replacing critical hardware in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joint_Airlock" title="Joint Airlock">Quest Joint Airlock</a>; repairing U.S. spacesuits; and submitting a scientific research paper on <a href="/wiki/Ultrasound" title="Ultrasound">ultrasound</a> use in space. During preflight testing, an explosive bolt was accidentally activated on the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-5" title="Soyuz TMA-5">Soyuz TMA-5</a> spacecraft. The resulting damage caused was repaired and the mission launched from <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> on 14 October 2004.</p>\n<p>During the Expedition 10 mission, two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progress_spacecraft" title="Progress spacecraft">Progress supply spacecraft</a> docked with the International Space Station. The first (Progress 51) docked on 25 December 2004 followed by the second (Progress 52) on 28 February 2005.</p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">European Space Agency</a> Astronaut <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Vittori" title="Roberto Vittori">Roberto Vittori</a> of Italy launched to the Station with the <a href="/wiki/Expedition_11" title="Expedition 11">Expedition 11</a> crew and spent eight days onboard performing scientific experiments. Vittori returned to Earth with the Expedition 10 crew. He was aboard under a contract between ESA and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federal_Space_Agency" title="Russian Federal Space Agency">Russian Federal Space Agency</a>.</p>\n<p>The re-entry of the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-5" title="Soyuz TMA-5">Soyuz TMA-5</a> spacecraft was perfect, returning the astronauts to <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> 53 miles northeast of the town of Arkalyk after 192 days, 19 hours and 2 minutes in space. The recovery team reached the capsule in minutes.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Crew_2">Crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2165" data-file-width="2000" height="238" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg/220px-Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg/330px-Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg/440px-Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nSalizhan Sharipov (left) and Leroy Chiao (right) work with their Russian Orlan spacesuits in the Pirs Docking Compartment of the International Space Station (ISS). (NASA)</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Chiao is a veteran of three <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> flights, and spent 13 hours on two spacewalks in 2000 as part of a construction mission at the Station. Sharipov has logged over 211 hours in space. He served as a mission specialist on the Shuttle during the eighth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shuttle-Mir" title="Shuttle-Mir">Shuttle-Mir</a> docking mission in 1998.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalks">Spacewalks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Spacewalks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Expedition 10 crew completed two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">spacewalks</a>, including experiment installation and tasks to prepare the Station for the arrival of the new European <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">Automated Transfer Vehicle</a> in 2006. Instead, the Automated Transfer Vehicle came in 2008. The first spacewalk took place on 26 January 2005 and the second on 28 March 2005.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
