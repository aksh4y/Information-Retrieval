NWS.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p><b>James L. Franklin</b> is a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_forecaster" title="Weather forecaster">weather forecaster</a> who served with the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> as its branch chief.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He is a graduate of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and of <a href="/wiki/Ransom_Everglades_School" title="Ransom Everglades School">Ransom Everglades School</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in 1976 in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, where he was a co-<a href="/wiki/Valedictorian" title="Valedictorian">valedictorian</a>.</p>\n<p>Franklin liked to add his wit to many of his forecasts, much like his counterpart <a href="/wiki/Lixion_Avila" title="Lixion Avila">Lixion Avila</a> and in contrast with the often dry commentary of other forecasters. For example:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>In forecasting <a href="/wiki/2002_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2002 Atlantic hurricane season">2002 Atlantic hurricane season</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Kyle_(2002)" title="Hurricane Kyle (2002)">Hurricane Kyle</a>, Franklin noted "for fun, [a] long-range run of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_prediction_model" title="Tropical cyclone prediction model">GFS [model]</a> has Kyle \xe2\x80\x94 its decayed remnants actually \xe2\x80\x94 reaching south <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> just in time for the kickoff of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">Miami</a>/<a href="/wiki/Florida_State_University" title="Florida State University">Florida State</a> <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">game</a>, one week from tomorrow."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Later, as the storm deteriorated, Franklin started his forecast discussion with a line from the <a href="/wiki/Bee_Gees" title="Bee Gees">Bee Gees</a>\' song <a href="/wiki/Stayin%27_Alive" title="Stayin\' Alive">Stayin\' Alive</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>\n<li>When forecasting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season_storms#Tropical_Storm_Franklin" title="List of 2005 Atlantic hurricane season storms">Tropical Storm Franklin</a> in 2005, Franklin said "Franklin \xe2\x80\x94 the storm, not the forecaster \xe2\x80\x94 has become a little better organized overnight. [...] It is quite possible that little or nothing will be left of Franklin \xe2\x80\x94 the storm, not the forecaster \xe2\x80\x94 in 2\xe2\x80\x933 days." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>\n<li>In the Tropical Cyclone Report for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Vince_(2005)" title="Hurricane Vince (2005)">Hurricane Vince</a> in 2005, Franklin alluded to <i><a href="/wiki/My_Fair_Lady" title="My Fair Lady">My Fair Lady</a></i> when he quipped "<a href="/wiki/The_Rain_in_Spain" title="The Rain in Spain">The rain in Spain</a> was mainly less than 2 inches, although 3.30 inches fell in the plain at Cordoba."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>\n<li>In a discussion for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Ana_(2009)" title="Tropical Storm Ana (2009)">Tropical Depression Ana</a> in 2009, Franklin noted that "[t]he intensity forecast is complicated by the uncertain existence of an actual tropical cyclone..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Franklin_(meteorologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.nola.com/hurricane/index.ssf/2017/07/forecaster_says_budget_cuts_co.html" rel="nofollow">"Forecaster says budget cuts could hurt hurricane predictions"</a>. <i>NOLA.com</i>.
