K\xc3\xa4rpp\xc3\xa4</a></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurriganes</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band that was formed in the early 1970s. They were very popular in <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a> in the 1970s and early 1980s, as well as a popular live act in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> during this time. Their classic line-up consisted of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Remu_Aaltonen" title="Remu Aaltonen">Remu Aaltonen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_J%C3%A4rvinen" title="Albert J\xc3\xa4rvinen">Albert J\xc3\xa4rvinen</a>, and Cisse H\xc3\xa4kkinen. The misspelling of the name ("Hurriganes" with a "g") is intentional. Their style of music is very much of a nostalgic, pre-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beatles" title="Beatles">Beatles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roots_rock" title="Roots rock">roots rock\'n\'roll</a> orientation. During their "classic" period, the band\'s output consisted largely of revved-up cover versions of well-known 1950s rock \'n\' roll songs, in addition to their own original material. In this sense, Hurriganes can be viewed as Finland\'s answer to British <a href="/wiki/Pub_rock_(United_Kingdom)" title="Pub rock (United Kingdom)">pub rock</a> performers of the 1970s like <a href="/wiki/Dave_Edmunds" title="Dave Edmunds">Dave Edmunds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Feelgood_(band)" title="Dr. Feelgood (band)">Dr. Feelgood</a>.</p>\n<p>The founding members were drummer and vocalist Henry "Remu" Aaltonen, bassist Hugo Christer "Cisse" H\xc3\xa4kkinen and guitarist Ilkka "Ile" Kallio. Kallio temporarily quit the band in the spring of 1972, being replaced by guitarist Pekka "Albert" J\xc3\xa4rvinen who was part of the band\'s most famous line-up.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=Hurriganes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurriganes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Hurriganes was founded in 1971 in North Haaga, Helsinki, where a similar rock band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jig-Saw" title="Jig-Saw">Jig-Saw</a> played. Influenced by the name of the North American band <a href="/wiki/Johnny_and_the_Hurricanes" title="Johnny and the Hurricanes">Johnny And The Hurricanes</a>, Remu Aaltonen devised the name for his own band. Hurriganes practiced in a garage in Ohjaajantie.</p>\n<h3><span id="Rock_and_Roll_All_Night_Long_(1972\xe2\x80\x9373)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Rock_and_Roll_All_Night_Long_.281972.E2.80.9373.29"><i>Rock and Roll All Night Long</i> (1972\xe2\x80\x9373)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurriganes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rock and Roll All Night Long (1972\xe2\x80\x9373)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Hurriganes\' first album <i>Rock and Roll All Night Long</i> was released in 1973. It included songs such as "Say Mama" and "Keep On Knocking". The album\'s A-side was recorded in concert <a href="/wiki/Klaukkala" title="Klaukkala">Klaukkala</a>, such as the B-side of the first paragraph, but the rest was recorded in the studio. This album became the first gold selling record for the band\'s record label, Love Records.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
