music">country music</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Luke_Combs" title="Luke Combs">Luke Combs</a>. The song was released through <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> in 2016 after initially charting in 2015. It is included on his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/This_One%27s_for_You_(Luke_Combs_album)" title="This One\'s for You (Luke Combs album)">This One\'s for You</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The song is about comparing an ex-girlfriend to a natural disaster.</p>\n<p>"Hurricane" reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart. Its second week atop that position gave Combs two distinctions: the first artist to have a multi-week number one debut single chart since <a href="/wiki/Florida_Georgia_Line" title="Florida Georgia Line">Florida Georgia Line</a>\'s "<a href="/wiki/Cruise_(song)" title="Cruise (song)">Cruise</a>" in 2012 and the first male solo artist to accomplish that since <a href="/wiki/Darius_Rucker" title="Darius Rucker">Darius Rucker</a>\'s "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Think_I_Don%27t_Think_About_It" title="Don\'t Think I Don\'t Think About It">Don\'t Think I Don\'t Think About It</a>" in 2008. It also peaked at numbers 3 and 31 on both the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> charts respectively. The song received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Gold</a> certification from the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA), and has sold 604,000 copies in the United States as of September 2017. It garnered similar chart success in Canada, reaching number 2 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada_Country" title="Canada Country">Canada Country</a> chart and number 62 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> chart.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li
