object at or near the surface. Its <a href="/wiki/METAR" title="METAR">METAR</a> code is <b>FZDZ</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spence2006_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spence2006-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Formation"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Formation</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Effects"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Effects</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freezing_drizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Freezing drizzle is formed in low level <a href="/wiki/Stratus_cloud" title="Stratus cloud">stratus</a> type <a href="/wiki/Cloud" title="Cloud">clouds</a> when vertical motion is weak. It consists of relatively small drops, light in nature. Freezing drizzle generally occurs when drizzle forms in an airmass at below freezing temperatures but warmer than -10\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C (14\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F). At such a temperature, the water droplets stay <a href="/wiki/Supercooling" title="Supercooling">supercooled</a> as there are few <a href="/wiki/Ice_nucleus" title="Ice nucleus">ice nuclei</a> to change them to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice_crystal" title="Ice crystal">ice crystals</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Freezing_rain" title="Freezing rain">freezing rain</a>). In winter arctic conditions it can happen at even lower surface temperatures as the air is even cleaner.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freezing_drizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When freezing drizzle accumulates on land it creates an icy layer of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glaze_ice" title="Glaze ice">glaze</a>. Freezing drizzle alone does not generally result in significant <a href="/wiki/Ice" title="Ice">ice</a> accumulations due to its light, low-intensity nature. However, even thin layers of slick ice deposited on roads as <a href="/wiki/Black_ice" title="Black ice">black ice</a> can cause extremely hazardous conditions resulting in vehicle crashes.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freezing_drizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Black_ice" title="Black ice">Black ice</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Freezing_rain" title="Freezing rain">Freezing rain</a></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freezing_drizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="mw-references-wrap">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-Spence2006-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Spence2006_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">Spence, Charles F.
