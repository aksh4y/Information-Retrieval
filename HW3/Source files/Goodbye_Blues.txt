The album was recorded in the fall of 2007 at North Hollywood\'s Fairfax Recordings with producer/engineer Kevin Augunas (<a href="/wiki/Cold_War_Kids" title="Cold War Kids">Cold War Kids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Brion" title="Jon Brion">Jon Brion</a>) at the helm. With tracks such as "Honey" and "Medicine Man", The Hush Sound shows influence of blues, swing, and folk. The album is different than the band\'s last two albums, as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greta_Salpeter" title="Greta Salpeter">Greta Salpeter</a> takes over lead vocals on nine of the thirteen tracks.</p>\n<p><i>Goodbye Blues</i> was available for pre-order from January 29, through The Hush Sound\'s official website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After preordering it, a free digital download of "Honey" is available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On October 23, 2007, The Hush Sound posted a video on FriendsorEnemies.com featuring a list of songs they were working on.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Viral_marketing">Viral marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goodbye_Blues&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Viral marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Hush Sound has been using <a href="/wiki/Viral_marketing" title="Viral marketing">viral marketing</a> to promote their new album along with a supporting spot on the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Civic_Tour" title="Honda Civic Tour">Honda Civic Tour</a>. One of these schemes used is the use of a person known as "The Medicine Man" who has been giving clues, such as song clips, videos, and images to its friends on <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a>, Friends or Enemies, and Sweet Tangerine.
