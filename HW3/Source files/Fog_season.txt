Visibility in this photo is less than 500 feet.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The <b>fog season</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Season" title="Season">season</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fog</a> that occurs in some places, because of special meteorological and topographical characteristics, after a rainy period. The fog season is usually based in the cooler months (late autumn, winter and early spring).</p>\n<p>An example is found in the <a href="/wiki/San_Joaquin_Valley" title="San Joaquin Valley">San Joaquin Valley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_Valley" title="Sacramento Valley">Sacramento Valley</a> areas of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>\'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/California_Central_Valley" title="California Central Valley">Great Central Valley</a>, where a thick ground fog, known as <a href="/wiki/Tule_fog" title="Tule fog">Tule fog</a>, may form, in particular in the months from November through March. In the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay" title="Tampa Bay">Tampa Bay</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, the fog season is from December to February. <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>\'s fog season is longer, starting from April through to October. Though it\'s more frequent in June due to more <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">rain</a>.</p>\n<p>It is not generally true that fog season in a given area is during Fall or <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">Winter</a> (the cooler months); for example, the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> coast of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> has a dense fog season from May to August. The <a href="/wiki/June_Gloom" title="June Gloom">June Gloom</a>, a cloudy and foggy phenomena, experienced in the southern coast of California occurs in late spring and early summer (May and June).</p>\n<div aria-labelledby="Seasons" class="navbox" role="navigation" style="padding:3px">\n<table class="nowraplinks collapsible autocollapse navbox-inner"
