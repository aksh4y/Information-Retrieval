28th long-duration expedition to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>, and began on 23 May 2011 with the departure of the members of <a href="/wiki/Expedition_27" title="Expedition 27">Expedition 27</a>. The first three members of Expedition 28 arrived on the ISS aboard the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-21" title="Soyuz TMA-21">Soyuz TMA-21</a> spacecraft on 4 April 2011, and were joined on 9 June 2011 by the three other crew members, who arrived aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-02M" title="Soyuz TMA-02M">Soyuz TMA-02M</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exp28nasa_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-exp28nasa-1">[1]</a></sup> The expedition saw a number of significant events, including the final <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> mission, <a href="/wiki/STS-135" title="STS-135">STS-135</a>, which took place in July 2011. Expedition 28 was superseded by <a href="/wiki/Expedition_29" title="Expedition 29">Expedition 29</a> on 16 September 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a Nespoli">Paolo Nespoli</a> aboard at 2:27 UTC on 24 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exp28nasa_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-exp28nasa-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tma20land_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-tma20land-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Before departing the vicinity of the International Space Station, the crew of TMA-20 photographed the exterior of the station during a fly-around, capturing photos of the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">Space Shuttle <i>Endeavour</i></a> docked with the ISS on its final mission, <a href="/wiki/STS-134" title="STS-134">STS-134</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tma20land_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-tma20land-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="STS-134">STS-134</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_28&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: STS-134">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/STS-134" title="STS-134">STS-134</a></div>\n<p>At the time Expedition 28 began, Space Shuttle <i>Endeavour</i> was docked to the ISS on her final mission, <a href="/wiki/STS-134" title="STS-134">STS-134</a>. During this mission, the crew of <i>Endeavour</i> installed on the station\'s exterior the <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Magnetic_Spectrometer" title="Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer">Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer</a> and several spare parts to aid in station operations after the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_retirement" title="Space Shuttle retirement">retirement of the Space Shuttle</a>. STS-134 was the 36th Space Shuttle mission to the International Space Station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-134nasa_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-134nasa-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<p><i>Endeavour</i> and her crew, consisting of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Kelly_(astronaut)" title="Mark Kelly (astronaut)">Mark Kelly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregory_H._Johnson" title="Gregory H. Johnson">Gregory Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Fincke" title="Michael Fincke">Michael Fincke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Vittori" title="Roberto Vittori">Roberto Vittori</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_J._Feustel" title="Andrew J. Feustel">Andrew Feustel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Chamitoff" title="Gregory Chamitoff">Gregory Chamitoff</a>, returned to Earth on 1 June 2011, at 6:34 UTC. STS-134 was the second-to-last mission of the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-134nasa_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-134nasa-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-134land_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-134land-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soyuz_TMA-02M_docking">Soyuz TMA-02M docking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_28&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Soyuz TMA-02M docking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-02M" title="Soyuz TMA-02M">Soyuz TMA-02M</a></div>\n<p>The remainder of the Expedition 28 crew (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergey_Alexandrovich_Volkov" title="Sergey Alexandrovich Volkov">Sergey Volkov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_E._Fossum" title="Michael E. Fossum">Michael E. Fossum</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Satoshi_Furukawa" title="Satoshi Furukawa">Satoshi Furukawa</a>) launched aboard Soyuz TMA-02M from the <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> in Kazakhstan at 20:12 UTC on 7 June 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2mlaunch_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-2mlaunch-9">[9]</a></sup> Originally scheduled to dock to the ISS on 9 June at approximately 21:22 UTC,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> the Soyuz did so several minutes early at 21:18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exp28nasa_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-exp28nasa-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Johannes_Kepler_ATV_undocking"><i>Johannes Kepler</i> ATV undocking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_28&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Johannes Kepler ATV undocking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Kepler_ATV" title="Johannes Kepler ATV">Johannes Kepler ATV</a></div>\n<p>On 20 June 2011, the European Space Agency\'s robotic cargo ship <i><a href="/wiki/Johannes_Kepler_ATV" title="Johannes Kepler ATV">Johannes Kepler</a></i> disengaged from the ISS, having been docked since February 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 21 June 2011, the ATV was deorbited, burning up in the atmosphere over the southern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> at around 22:44 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Time" title="Central European Time">CET</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Progress_M-11M_docking">Progress M-11M docking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_28&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Progress M-11M docking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-11M" title="Progress M-11M">Progress M-11M</a></div>\n<p>A Russian <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">Progress</a> cargo ship, designated M-11M (Progress 42 or 42P by NASA) was launched on 21 June 2011 to resupply and deliver equipment to the International Space Station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-manifest_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-manifest-13">[13]</a></sup> Progress M-11M transferred more than 2.5 tons of cargo to the Space Station, including food, water, scientific hardware, propellant, and cargo. The cargo ship docked with the Zvezda service module of the Space Station at 16:37 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a> on 23 June 2011. The docking occurred 245 miles above eastern Kazakhstan.</p>\n<h3><span id="STS-135\xe2\x80\x94final_space_shuttle_mission"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="STS-135.E2.80.94final_space_shuttle_mission">STS-135\xe2\x80\x94final space shuttle mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_28&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: STS-135\xe2\x80\x94final space shuttle mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/STS-135" title="STS-135">STS-135</a></div>\n<p>On 8 July 2011, <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Atlantis" title="Space Shuttle Atlantis">Space Shuttle <i>Atlantis</i></a> launched on the STS-135 mission, the final mission in NASA\'s <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_program" title="Space Shuttle program">Space Shuttle program</a>. <i>Atlantis</i> docked to the ISS on 10 July 2011 at 15:07 UTC. The mission was crewed by NASA astronauts <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Ferguson" title="Christopher Ferguson">Christopher Ferguson</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Douglas_Hurley" title="Douglas Hurley">Douglas Hurley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Magnus" title="Sandra Magnus">Sandra Magnus</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rex_Walheim" title="Rex Walheim">Rex Walheim</a>, and departed from the ISS on 19 July 2011. The purpose of the mission was to deliver the <a href="/wiki/Raffaello_MPLM" title="Raffaello MPLM"><i>Raffaello</i> MPLM</a>, stocked with supplies, to the space station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mission135_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-mission135-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soyuz_TMA-21_undocking">Soyuz TMA-21 undocking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_28&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Soyuz TMA-21 undocking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-21" title="Soyuz TMA-21">Soyuz TMA-21</a></div>\n<p>The Soyuz TMA-21 spacecraft departed from the International Space Station on 16 September 2011 at 00:38 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASATwitter_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-NASATwitter-15">[15]</a></sup> Soyuz Commander Aleksandr Samokutyayev and Flight Engineers Andrei Borisenko and Ronald Garan returned to Earth on 16 September at 03:59 UTC, landing safely in central <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exp28LandingNASA_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Exp28LandingNASA-16">[16]</a></sup> Their landing marked the beginning of Expedition 29.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalks">Spacewalks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
