border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Fabian</b> was a powerful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde-type_hurricane" title="Cape Verde-type hurricane">Cape Verde-type hurricane</a> that hit <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> in early September during the <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. Fabian was the most recent major hurricane to directly impact <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> until <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Nicole_(2016)" title="Hurricane Nicole (2016)">Hurricane Nicole</a> of <a href="/wiki/2016_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2016 Atlantic hurricane season">2016</a>. It was the sixth named storm, fourth hurricane, and first <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Hurricane_or_typhoon" title="Tropical cyclone">major hurricane</a> of the season, developed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> in the tropical Atlantic Ocean on August 25. It moved west-northwestward under the influence of the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> to its north, and steadily strengthened in an area of warm <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">water temperatures</a> and light <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>. The hurricane attained a peak intensity of 145\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h) on September 1, and it slowly weakened as it turned northward. On September 5, Fabian made a direct hit on <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> with wind speeds of over 120\xc2\xa0mph (195\xc2\xa0km/h). After passing the island, the hurricane turned to the northeast, and became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical" title="Extratropical">extratropical</a> on September 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Fabian was the strongest hurricane to hit Bermuda since <a href="/wiki/1963_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Arlene" title="1963 Atlantic hurricane season">Hurricane Arlene</a> in 1963.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc2003_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc2003-2">[2]</a></sup> It was both the most damaging and the first hurricane to cause a death on the island since 1926.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mostdaming_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mostdaming-3">[3]</a></sup> The hurricane\'s powerful winds resulted in moderate damage and destroyed roofs throughout the island. A strong storm surge associated with the hurricane killed four people crossing <a href="/wiki/The_Causeway,_Bermuda" title="The Causeway, Bermuda">a causeway</a> on Bermuda, temporarily closing the only link between two islands. The endangered <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_petrel" title="Bermuda petrel">Bermuda petrel</a>, better known as the cahow, was threatened by the hurricane, which destroyed ten nests, although volunteer work transported the species to a safer location. Strong swells resulted in damage in northern <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, and also caused four people to drown along the United States\' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_coast" title="Atlantic coast">Atlantic coast</a>. class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fabian_2003_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" href="/wiki/File:Fabian_2003_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On August 25, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> emerged off the coast of Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Tracking westward, the wave developed <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a> over its center, and conducive conditions allowed it to <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">develop</a> further.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aug26two_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-aug26two-4">[4]</a></sup> The system passed through the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> islands later that day as convection steadily weakened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aug26frank_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-aug26frank-5">[5]</a></sup> Early on August 27, convection again increased and consolidated near the center, and later that day the wave developed into Tropical Depression Ten while located 420\xc2\xa0miles (675\xc2\xa0km) west of the Cape Verde islands. Moving westward into an area of warm waters and low vertical shear, the depression steadily intensified, and was named Tropical Storm Fabian on August 28 as convection increased and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainbands" title="Rainbands">banding features</a> became more prominent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabiandisc5_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabiandisc5-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Banding features and outflow continued to develop as a ring of convection formed around the center of Fabian,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian8_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian8-7">[7]</a></sup> and on August 30, the storm intensified into a hurricane while gradually slowing its motion to the west-northwest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> As banding features and outflow became better defined, an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> developed in the center of the deepest convection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian12_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian12-8">[8]</a></sup> Fabian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">quickly strengthened</a> and attained major hurricane status late that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Deep convection became very <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concentric" title="Concentric">concentric</a> around the 11.5\xc2\xa0mile (18.5\xc2\xa0km) wide eye, and the hurricane reached winds of 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) early on August 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian14_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian14-9">[9]</a></sup> At this time, the deepest convection near the eye degraded in appearance, and Fabian temporarily stopped its strengthening trend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian15_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian15-10">[10]</a></sup> Later that day, the eye became distinct again within a perfectly round area of deep convection. Outflow continued to expand in all directions, and Fabian intensified into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 4 hurricane</a> late on August 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian17_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian17-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">Thunderstorm</a> activity near the eyewall became more intense, and the cloud tops in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyewall" title="Eyewall">eyewall</a> became much cooler; simultaneously, outflow away from the eye became much more symmetrical, both signs of an intensifying tropical cyclone. Fabian reached its peak intensity of 145\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h) on September 1 while located 345\xc2\xa0miles (555\xc2\xa0km) east of the northern <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian21_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian21-12">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:2003_Hurricane_Fabian.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="7200" data-file-width="5600" height="283" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/2003_Hurricane_Fabian.jpg/220px-2003_Hurricane_Fabian.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/2003_Hurricane_Fabian.jpg/330px-2003_Hurricane_Fabian.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/2003_Hurricane_Fabian.jpg/440px-2003_Hurricane_Fabian.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:2003_Hurricane_Fabian.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Fabian south of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on September 5</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>After maintaining its peak intensity for 12\xc2\xa0hours Fabian degraded due to internal fluctuations, and began to weaken.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fabian23_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fabian23-13">[13]</a></sup> The hurricane turned to the northwest on September 2 in response to a weakness in the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a>, a break caused by a mid-level circulation over the southwest Atlantic Ocean. After fading back to a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane, Fabian re-intensified on September 4, and regained Category\xc2\xa04 status for a short time. The hurricane weakened again as it accelerated northward towards <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, a motion due to an approaching mid-level <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> As small pockets of dry air became entrained in the eyewall, Fabian weakened slightly,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian34_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian34-14">[14]</a></sup> passing just 14\xc2\xa0miles (23\xc2\xa0km) west of Bermuda on September 5 as a 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 3 hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The eastern portion of the eyewall moved over the island, resulting in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Direct_hit_(meteorology)" title="Direct hit (meteorology)">direct hit</a>; however, as the center did not move over the island, Fabian did not make <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a>. After passing the island, the hurricane accelerated northeastward, and weakened to a 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h) Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane on September 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Steady weakening occurred as the hurricane moved into an area of increasing wind shear, drier air, and progressively cooler waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian43_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian43-15">[15]</a></sup> On September 8, while located 680\xc2\xa0miles (1095\xc2\xa0km) east-northeast of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Race" title="Cape Race">Cape Race</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>, Fabian transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical storm</a>, with no deep convection remaining near the center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabian48_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabian48-16">[16]</a></sup> The extratropical remnant turned to the north on September 9, and on September 10 it merged with another extratropical storm while located between southern <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fabian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Several days prior to Fabian striking <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_prediction_model" title="Tropical cyclone prediction model">computer models</a> forecast a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_pressure_area" title="High pressure area">ridge of high pressure</a> forcing the hurricane to the west of the island by 200\xc2\xa0miles (320\xc2\xa0km).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> Not expected to be a direct threat, a meteorologist at the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Weather_Service" title="Bermuda Weather Service">Bermuda Weather Service</a> expected gusty winds and potentially heavy rainfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg1_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-rg1-18">[18]</a></sup> Each successive advisory brought the hurricane steadily closer to Bermuda,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> and 35\xc2\xa0hours before Fabian made its closest approach, the Bermuda Weather Service issued a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Watch" title="Hurricane Watch">hurricane watch</a> for the island. When a track near Bermuda became more certain, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches#Hurricane_Warning" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane warning</a> was issued for the island, approximately 29\xc2\xa0hours before Fabian made a direct hit on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Electric_Light_Company_Limited" title="Bermuda Electric Light Company Limited">Bermuda Electric Light Company Limited</a> recommended Bermuda residents to buy hurricane supplies such as candles, batteries, and non-perishable foods, to fill bathtubs and extra containers with water, and fill gasoline tanks for automobiles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg2_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-rg2-19">[19]</a></sup> In preparation, residents formed long lines at gas stations, banks,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap1-20">[20]</a></sup> and supermarkets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters1_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuters1-21">[21]</a></sup> All government offices and many businesses closed on the day prior to the hurricane hitting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap1-20">[20]</a></sup> All schools were closed, while all flights in and out of the island were canceled. Officials opened <a href="/wiki/Emergency_shelter" title="Emergency shelter">emergency shelters</a>, and recommended 2,000 low-lying residents to evacuate;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters1_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-reuters1-21">[21]</a></sup> a hotel on the south shore of the island was evacuated as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgcauseway_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-rgcauseway-22">[22]</a></sup> Several cruise ships expected to remain on the island departed early to avoid the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters1_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-reuters1-21">[21]</a></sup> Local insurance companies on Bermuda experienced a great increase in business, as residents renewed lapsed policies or signed up for new policies for homes or businesses, though marine policies were stopped several days before the hurricane struck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg3_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-rg3-23">[23]</a></sup> The arrival of Fabian forced the cancellation or delay of several sports events, including a <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> match, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> game, and a <a href="/wiki/Dinghy" title="Dinghy">dinghy</a> race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg4_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-rg4-24">[24]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fabian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="wikitable" style="float:right; clear:right; margin:0.5em 0 0 0.5em;">\n<caption>Storm deaths by region</caption>\n<tr>\n<th>Region</th>\n<th>Direct deaths</th>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a></td>\n<td>4</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Banks" title="Grand Banks">Grand Banks</a></td>\n<td>3</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a></td>\n<td>1</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Total</th>\n<th>8<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></th>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p>Throughout its path, Fabian killed 8\xc2\xa0people and caused US<span class="sortkey" style="display:none">7008300000000000000\xe2\x99\xa0</span>$300\xc2\xa0million in damages, primarily in <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>.</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean">Caribbean</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fabian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Caribbean">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The hurricane produced storm surge damage in <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbuda</a>, where some boats were lightly damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMO_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-WMO-25">[25]</a></sup> Strong swells and high tides produced large waves on the north coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, washing out beaches in various locations. Waves knocked out a 10\xc2\xa0ft (3\xc2\xa0m) portion of a construction site in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocean_Park,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ocean Park, Puerto Rico">Ocean Park</a>, resulting in US<span class="sortkey" style="display:none">7004300000000000000\xe2\x99\xa0</span>$30\xc2\xa0thousand in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-NCDC-26">[26]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, the hurricane produced waves of up to 8\xc2\xa0ft (2.4\xc2\xa0m) in height. Due to the waves and gusty winds, boats were advised to stay at port. Several families had to be evacuated in <a href="/wiki/Nagua" title="Nagua">Nagua</a> when rough seas flooded their homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMO_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-WMO-25">[25]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bermuda">Bermuda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fabian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bermuda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Fabian.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="529" data-file-width="720" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Hurricane_Fabian.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Fabian.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Hurricane_Fabian.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Fabian.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Hurricane_Fabian.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Fabian.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Fabian.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Fabian over Bermuda late on September 5</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Fabian struck Bermuda on Friday, the 5 September, 2003, with sustained winds having reached 39\xc2\xa0mph (63\xc2\xa0km/h) by 0800, 74\xc2\xa0mph (119\xc2\xa0km/h) by 1400, and 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h) by 1755. The eye did not pass directly over the archipelago, instead passing to the west (placing Bermuda in the North-East quarter where the winds were particularly powerful) with the eye wall dragging over Bermuda for three hours. This prolonged the damaging winds the island was subjected to.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The storm produced a 10-minute average wind speed of 120\xc2\xa0mph (195\xc2\xa0km/h), while a peak wind gust of 164\xc2\xa0mph (264\xc2\xa0km/h) occurred at Bermuda Harbour Radio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The strongest of the winds lasted approximately three to four hours,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> and while the eastern portion of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyewall" title="Eyewall">eyewall</a> moved over the island, winds decreased to 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Large waves battered the southern portion of the island for several days, reaching heights of 25 to 35\xc2\xa0feet (7 to 10\xc2\xa0m) at the worst of the hurricane, and upon passing the island, the hurricane produced a <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> exceeding 11\xc2\xa0feet (3\xc2\xa0m) in height. Due to its fast motion, rainfall totals rose to only 1.82\xc2\xa0inches (46.2\xc2\xa0mm). There were several unofficial reports of tornadoes as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> Strong <a href="/wiki/Rip_current" title="Rip current">rip currents</a> from the hurricane persisted for several days prior to Fabian passing the island; two swimmers were caught in the currents and relied on <a href="/wiki/Lifeguard" title="Lifeguard">lifeguard</a> assistance to return to shore. As a result, <a href="/wiki/Rip_current" title="Rip current">rip tide</a> warnings were posted for the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg5_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-rg5-28">[28]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:06_Sept_03_John_Smith-s_Bay_01.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="321" data-file-width="480" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/06_Sept_03_John_Smith-s_Bay_01.jpg/220px-06_Sept_03_John_Smith-s_Bay_01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/06_Sept_03_John_Smith-s_Bay_01.jpg/330px-06_Sept_03_John_Smith-s_Bay_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/06_Sept_03_John_Smith-s_Bay_01.jpg/440px-06_Sept_03_John_Smith-s_Bay_01.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:06_Sept_03_John_Smith-s_Bay_01.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nA house at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smith%27s_Parish,_Bermuda" title="Smith\'s Parish, Bermuda">John Smith\'s Bay</a> during the approach of the storm</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Strong waves caused extensive damage to the coastline, especially on the southern portion of Bermuda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-4"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> The strong waves broke a boat from its moorings at <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Point,_Bermuda" title="Spanish Point, Bermuda">Spanish Point</a>. Not wanting to lose the ship, the owner, accompanied with two people, tried to save the vessel. One fell overboard before climbing aboard the boat. The three ventured the vessel through tornadoes and 20-foot (6\xc2\xa0m) waves, which dropped several feet of water in the ship; however, ultimately they safely arrived at <a href="/wiki/Hamilton_Harbour,_Bermuda" title="Hamilton Harbour, Bermuda">Hamilton Harbour</a>. Five charter boats capsized from the waves, while several others crashed against reefs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgboat_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-rgboat-29">[29]</a></sup> Strong waves collapsed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_wall" title="Sea wall">sea wall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Bermuda" title="Hamilton, Bermuda">Hamilton</a>, causing traffic jams for one day until it was fixed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yearend_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-yearend-30">[30]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The winds downed numerous power lines, causing 78% of the island\'s 32,031 power customers to experience <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">power outages</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-5"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup>[The strong winds damaged or destroyed the roofs of numerous buildings on Bermuda. One of the areas lightest hit was around a hotel outside of Hamilton, which experienced no power outages or blown out windows,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shorts_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-shorts-31">[31]</a></sup> while one of the harder hit areas was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warwick_Parish,_Bermuda" title="Warwick Parish, Bermuda">Warwick</a>. There, one resident noted, "Too many homes have lost roofs to count". One house was entirely destroyed on Rec View Hill, while an unconfirmed tornado destroyed much of the roof of a house in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Devonshire_Parish,_Bermuda" title="Devonshire Parish, Bermuda">Devonshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-building_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-building-32">[32]</a></sup> The hurricane destroyed a restaurant in <a href="/wiki/Southampton_Parish,_Bermuda" title="Southampton Parish, Bermuda">Southampton</a> and also damaged stands and roofs at sports facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shorts_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-shorts-31">[31]</a></sup> Strong winds from the hurricane damaged several landmark buildings, including the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Assembly_of_Bermuda" title="House of Assembly of Bermuda">House of Assembly</a> and the City Hall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgcauseway_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-rgcauseway-22">[22]</a></sup> both in Hamilton, and the former military headquarters in St. George\'s.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fabian4.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" The runway escaped major damage, however, and the airport re-opened the following day for emergency relief flights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Confronting the damages in the storm\'s aftermath, Airport General Manager <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=James_Howes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="James Howes (page does not exist)">James Howes</a> was quoted by the news media as saying, "My heart sank when I first saw the Airport that morning. One hotel that remained open experienced damage to 25% of its rooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hotel_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-hotel-37">[37]</a></sup> The strong winds uprooted hundreds of trees along the island\'s <a href="/wiki/Golf_course" title="Golf course">golf courses</a>, though little damage was reported at most courses. One course experienced significant damage at its club house, temporarily closing it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-samstevens_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-samstevens-38">[38]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Government Conservation Officer Dr. <a href="/wiki/David_B._Wingate" title="David B. Wingate">David B. Wingate</a> reported the damage to the South Shore of Bermuda was the worst in a thousand years. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The strong winds blew down hundreds of trees, caused severe damage to vegetation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-6"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> and destroyed many of the island\'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna_in_Bermuda" title="Flora and fauna in Bermuda">indigenous plants</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plants_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-plants-40">[40]</a></sup> The hurricane washed away large sections of the nesting island for the <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a> <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_petrel" title="Bermuda petrel">Bermuda petrel</a> and destroyed 10 of the 70\xc2\xa0active nests. The endangered birds were not on the island, though local residents quickly gathered to restore their habitat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berpetrel_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-berpetrel-41">[41]</a></sup> Strong waves resulted in severe <a href="/wiki/Coastal_erosion" title="Coastal erosion">coastal erosion</a>, and at one beach, the lack of sand destroyed a natural cove. The Natural Arches, a set of eroded stones resembling an arch that was popular in photographs, was destroyed by the waves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormcarib_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-stormcarib-42">[42]</a></sup> The powerful winds knocked coconuts off several trees and scattered them across the ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-building_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-building-32">[32]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The storm surge from the hurricane stranded one vehicle with three police officers and another with a resident on <a href="/wiki/The_Causeway,_Bermuda" title="The Causeway, Bermuda">the causeway</a> between <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Parish,_Bermuda" title="St. George\'s Parish, Bermuda">St. George\'s Parish</a> and <a href="/wiki/St._David%27s_Island,_Bermuda" title="St. David\'s Island, Bermuda">St. David\'s Island</a>. After a fire truck failed in its attempt to rescue them, powerful waves washed the vehicles into <a href="/wiki/Castle_Harbour,_Bermuda" title="Castle Harbour, Bermuda">Castle Harbour</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> and Bermuda police divers mounted a full-scale search for the missing people during the worst of the storm. The strong winds and emotional issues of searching for colleagues made the search difficult.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgsearching_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-rgsearching-43">[43]</a></sup> Ultimately, the vehicles<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgcauseway_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-rgcauseway-22">[22]</a></sup> and one dead body were recovered,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-7"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> with the others remaining missing, presumably dead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgcauseway_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-rgcauseway-22">[22]</a></sup> Another vehicle was on the causeway as the two cars became stranded, though the driver was able to safely cross.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgsearching_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-rgsearching-43">[43]</a></sup> High winds and surf tore off the causeway\'s side walls and greatly damaged the structure,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgcauseway_22-4"><a href="#cite_note-rgcauseway-22">[22]</a></sup> temporarily closing it to automobile traffic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yearend_30-2"><a href="#cite_note-yearend-30">[30]</a></sup></p>\n<p>As most people were well-prepared, these were the only four deaths on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-8"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> In addition, nine people sought medical attention for minor injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabnp_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-fabnp-44">[44]</a></sup> Damage on Bermuda totaled to US<span class="sortkey" style="display:none">7008300000000000000\xe2\x99\xa0</span>$300\xc2\xa0million, reportedly the worst to affect the area since 1926.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-14"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_coast">Atlantic coast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fabian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Atlantic coast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Swells from the hurricane produced rip currents and heavy surf along the eastern <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> coastline. One man drowned near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras,_North_Carolina" title="Cape Hatteras, North Carolina">Cape Hatteras</a> from the rip currents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcnc_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcnc-45">[45]</a></sup> Fabian produced moderate surfing conditions along the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>, primarily from <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> to North Carolina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surfline_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-surfline-46">[46]</a></sup> Three deaths were reported in the north Atlantic when a ship, <i>The Pacific Attitude</i>, sank south of Newfoundland in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Banks" title="Grand Banks">Grand Banks</a> due to strong waves of over 60\xc2\xa0feet (20\xc2\xa0m) in height.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-15"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-envcanada_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-envcanada-47">[47]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fabian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:BDA_Fabian_damage3.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/BDA_Fabian_damage3.jpg/220px-BDA_Fabian_damage3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/BDA_Fabian_damage3.jpg/330px-BDA_Fabian_damage3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/BDA_Fabian_damage3.jpg/440px-BDA_Fabian_damage3.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:BDA_Fabian_damage3.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nRoad washout at Bermuda International Airport</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Blocked roads initially caused delays to crews from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bermuda_Electric_Light_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bermuda Electric Light Company (page does not exist)">Bermuda Electric Light Company</a> (BELCO), which were attempting to restore power to the island. Of high priority among the places to be restored were the hospital, as well as hotels to accommodate tourists who remained on the island through the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgcauseway_22-5"><a href="#cite_note-rgcauseway-22">[22]</a></sup> By the second day after the storm passed the island, power was restored to 11,000\xc2\xa0residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-body_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-body-48">[48]</a></sup> Power on Bermuda was restored to all customers within three weeks of the hurricane, but the electricity supply distribution system was greatly weakened. This resulted in a rehabilitation plan implemented in October 2003. Days after the hurricane passed, moisture from <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Henri_(2003)" title="Tropical Storm Henri (2003)">Tropical Storm Henri</a> resulted in thunderstorms and heavy rainfall; this hindered recovery efforts, but did not cause any reported damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2003wmobr_17-9"><a href="#cite_note-2003wmobr-17">[17]</a></sup> Lack of power caused interruptions to communications. While an emergency broadcast station was installed and tested prior the hurricane, the hurricane caused a problem to the system\'s back-up generator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rginfo_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-rginfo-49">[49]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Causeway,_Bermuda" title="The Causeway, Bermuda">The causeway</a> in Bermuda remained closed for several days after the hurricane as road crews made temporary repairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yearend_30-3"><a href="#cite_note-yearend-30">[30]</a></sup> Upon being opened three days after the storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-body_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-body-48">[48]</a></sup> traffic was limited to one lane of the original two. However, the causeway had to be closed in the event of rain, winds exceeding 30\xc2\xa0mph (48\xc2\xa0km/h), and at night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormcarib_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-stormcarib-42">[42]</a></sup> The bridge was fully repaired by early November 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yearend_30-4"><a href="#cite_note-yearend-30">[30]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In the days after the storm, people provided assistance for each other, especially for the elderly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shorts_31-2"><a href="#cite_note-shorts-31">[31]</a></sup> Three days after the storm, for example, a radio station DJ announced a name for an elderly person in need, and within minutes someone would call to provide for their needs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormcarib_42-2"><a href="#cite_note-stormcarib-42">[42]</a></sup> Residents cleared smaller roads from debris with <a href="/wiki/Chainsaw" title="Chainsaw">chainsaws</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shorts_31-3"><a href="#cite_note-shorts-31">[31]</a></sup> which in turn helped the power companies to make repairs quickly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rgcauseway_22-6"><a href="#cite_note-rgcauseway-22">[22]</a></sup> As a result of damage on their homes, dozens of people stayed in five shelters or undamaged hotels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fabnp_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-fabnp-44">[44]</a></sup> Residents purchased large quantities of gasoline in the days after Fabian, some of whom bought over US$500\xc2\xa0worth. Officials assured there was no shortage, but requested drivers to conserve gas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shoppers_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-shoppers-50">[50]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/XL_Capital_Ltd." title="XL Capital Ltd.">XL Capital Ltd.</a> shipped 250 <a href="/wiki/Tarpaulin" title="Tarpaulin">tarpaulins</a>, 10 <a href="/wiki/Electric_generator" title="Electric generator">electric generators</a>, and rope supply to the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-body_48-2"><a href="#cite_note-body-48">[48]</a></sup> The UK government offered help to the island with two <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> ships with supplies such as tents, dried foods, and blankets. <a href="/wiki/List_of_Premiers_of_Bermuda" title="List of Premiers of Bermuda">Premier</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Alexander_Scott" title="William Alexander Scott">Alex Scott</a> declined the offer, believing the island could withstand on its own. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Vision" title="World Vision">World Vision</a> also offered assistance with blankets, tents, and other supplies, while two United States companies offered to send generators. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bermuda_Electric_Light_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bermuda Electric Light Company (page does not exist)">Bermuda Electric Light Company</a> received aid from the Caribbean Electric Organization, which sent over 20 electricians to repair power lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aid_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-aid-51">[51]</a></sup></p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fabiandamage.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="375" data-file-width="500" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Fabiandamage.jpg/220px-Fabiandamage.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Fabiandamage.jpg/330px-Fabiandamage.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Fabiandamage.jpg/440px-Fabiandamage.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fabiandamage.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane damage in Bermuda</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Initially, the time for the destroyed vegetation on Bermuda to regrow was estimated to take decades. To help, the <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> Maritime Heritage Foundation, with support from a <a href="/wiki/Boy_Scouting_(Boy_Scouts_of_America)" title="Boy Scouting (Boy Scouts of America)">Boy Scout troop</a> and nearby nurseries, delivered 1,000 <a href="/wiki/Buxus" title="Buxus">boxwood</a> plants to the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plants_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-plants-40">[40]</a></sup> Following the damage to the Bermuda petrel\'s habitat, Bermuda\'s Department of Conservation carried out a translocation program, which involved moving the habitat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonsuch_Island" title="Nonsuch Island">Nonsuch Island</a>. That island, a long-standing nature preserve, was much higher and safer for the birds, and by two years after the hurricane the population numbers were higher than before the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-birdlife_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-birdlife-52">[52]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Shortly after the hurricane, the American home improvement franchise <i><a href="/wiki/This_Old_House" title="This Old House">This Old House</a></i>, unaware of the limited extent of damage, decided to do a hurricane repair story. Upon realising that there would be little to nothing available for the <i>Ask This Old House</i> to repair, it was decided instead to do a renovation of an 1805 home in <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s,_Bermuda" title="St. George\'s, Bermuda">St. George\'s</a>. It was only the second time that the franchise had worked outside of the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThisOldHouse_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-ThisOldHouse-53">[53]</a></sup></p>\n<p>A memorial to the four Bermudians whose lives were claimed by Hurricane Fabian was subsequently erected near the Causeway\'s east end by the airport, where the 10th anniversary of their deaths was remembered by government officials in September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> The onslaught of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Gonzalo" title="Hurricane Gonzalo">Hurricane Gonzalo</a> in October 2014 prompted a catastrophe modeller to revisit Fabian\'s destruction, concluding that had it struck in 2014, it would have caused around $650 million in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-air_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-air-55">[55]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fabian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p>Due to the damage and deaths on <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, the name Fabian was retired in the spring of 2004 and will never again be used for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a>. As a result, the Bermuda Weather Service allowed residents to suggest a replacement name, with the only rule being the name had to be a male name beginning with the letter "F", able to be easily pronounced, and not currently in use. The service received a list of over 30 names, including Forrest and Frodo, after <a href="/wiki/Frodo_Baggins" title="Frodo Baggins">the character</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings" title="The Lord of the Rings">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-retire-56">[56]</a></sup> The Bermuda Weather Service sent three names to the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>; Fred, Ford, and Flynn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo2_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmo2-57">[57]</a></sup> The World Meteorological Organization ultimately replaced Fabian with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Fred_(disambiguation)" title="Tropical Storm Fred (disambiguation)">Fred</a></i> to be on the list for the <a href="/wiki/2009_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2009 Atlantic hurricane season">2009 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMO_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-WMO-25">[25]</a></sup> Fabian is one of only two tropical cyclones to have its name retired without making landfall anywhere, with the other being <i><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Klaus" title="Hurricane Klaus">Klaus</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/1990_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1990 Atlantic hurricane season">1990</a>.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
