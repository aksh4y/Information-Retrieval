border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2005 Atlantic hurricane season">2005 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Tropical Storm Franklin</b> was a strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">tropical storm</a> over the western Atlantic Ocean during July of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2005 Atlantic hurricane season">2005 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. It was the sixth named storm of the season and twice approached hurricane strength. The storm formed over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> on July 21 then moved north erratically, approaching <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on July 26. Franklin eventually became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical" title="Extratropical">extratropical</a> near <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a> on July 30, before being absorbed by a larger system. The <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> struggled to predict Tropical Storm Franklin mainly due to difficulties in predicting the effects of wind shear. There were only minor effects on land from Tropical Storm Franklin and no damages were caused. The name Franklin was used for the first time because of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Floyd" title="Hurricane Floyd">Hurricane Floyd</a> which was retired in the <a href="/wiki/1999_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1999 Atlantic hurricane season">1999 season</a>.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Franklin_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Franklin_2005_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="740" data-file-width="1024" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Franklin_2005_track.png/220px-Franklin_2005_track.png" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Franklin_2005_track.png/440px-Franklin_2005_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Franklin_2005_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A tropical wave emerged from the African coast late on July 10. The wave entered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> on July 21 and organized into Tropical Depression Six while 70\xc2\xa0miles (110\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Eleuthera" title="Eleuthera">Eleuthera</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FranklinTCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FranklinTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Initially the storm was predicted to execute a clockwise loop and drift to the west in response to a high pressure system. Several models indicated the possibility of the storm to drift westward into central <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHC-disc1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-disc1-2">[2]</a></sup> Soon after the depression formed, it strengthened into Tropical Storm Franklin.</p>\n<p>Tropical Storm Franklin suffered high levels of wind shear associated with the development of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Gert_(2005)" title="Tropical Storm Gert (2005)">Tropical Storm Gert</a>, which led the forecasters at the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> to say that Franklin could be torn apart in the next few days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHC-disc7_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-disc7-3">[3]</a></sup> However the shear abated as Franklin moved to the northeast allowing the storm to strengthen. The forecasters then stated that Franklin could "attain and maintain hurricane strength" and make a close approach to <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHC-disc8_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-disc8-4">[4]</a></sup> Shortly after on July 23, Tropical Storm Franklin reached its peak strength with 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FranklinTCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FranklinTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Franklin moved erratically to the east weakening as shear increased again. The NHC predicted that would dissipate, but the weakening trend stopped on July 25 with Franklin a minimal tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHC-disc13_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-disc13-5">[5]</a></sup> The storm passed to 200\xc2\xa0miles (325\xc2\xa0km) to the west of Bermuda on July 26 and moved slowly northwards into the warmer waters of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>. The shear also reduced once again allowing Franklin to re-strengthen somewhat, with winds reaching 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h) on July 28. Franklin began to accelerate to the northeast, becoming <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical" title="Extratropical">extratropical</a> on July 30 to the south of Newfoundland. The extratropical storm passed just south of the <a href="/wiki/Avalon_Peninsula" title="Avalon Peninsula">Avalon Peninsula</a> later that day and was absorbed by a larger system on July 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FranklinTCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FranklinTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Franklin_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As Tropical Storm Franklin was forming, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm warning</a> was issued for the northwest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, but it was canceled as Franklin moved north and away from the islands. A tropical storm watch was issued for <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on July 25 but was canceled a day later when Franklin turned away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FranklinTCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-FranklinTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Tropical Storm Franklin developed very close to land in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> and passed near <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, but there were no reports of tropical storm force winds overland, with the strongest gust recorded on Bermuda being 37\xc2\xa0mph (60\xc2\xa0km/h). After Franklin became extratropical, it brushed southeastern Newfoundland, bringing about 1\xc2\xa0inch (25\xc2\xa0mm) of rain to the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC-bulletin_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CHC-bulletin-6">[6]</a></sup> There were no damages or fatalities as a result of Tropical Storm Franklin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FranklinTCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-FranklinTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
