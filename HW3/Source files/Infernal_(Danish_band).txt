(Photo: Lars Schmidt)</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p><b>Infernal</b> (occasionally stylized as <b><a href="/wiki/Metal_umlaut" title="Metal umlaut">Inf\xc3\xabrnal</a></b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a>/<a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> group from <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, consisting of members <a href="/wiki/Lina_Rafn" title="Lina Rafn">Lina Rafn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paw_Lagermann" title="Paw Lagermann">Paw Lagermann</a>. They made their Danish debut in 1997 with the release of the track "Sorti de L\'enfer", and have gone on to international chart success in recent years. Their most successful single to date has been "<a href="/wiki/From_Paris_to_Berlin" title="From Paris to Berlin">From Paris to Berlin</a>", which charted well in many <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> countries throughout 2006 and 2007. In addition to the original single, an alternate version was released in the UK titled "From London to Berlin", supporting <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_Football_World_Cup" title="2006 Football World Cup">2006 Football World Cup</a>.</p>\n<p>Infernal have released four studio albums, with the 1998 debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Infernal_Affairs_(album)" title="Infernal Affairs (album)">Infernal Affairs</a></i> reaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Double_platinum" title="Double platinum">double platinum</a> sales in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Their mainstream breakthrough came with <i><a href="/wiki/From_Paris_to_Berlin_(album)" title="From Paris to Berlin (album)">From Paris to Berlin</a></i> from 2004, and the success continued with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_Cabaret_(album)" title="Electric Cabaret (album)">Electric Cabaret</a></i> in 2008, certified with double platinum and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platinum_certification" title="Platinum certification">platinum</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Paw_Lagermann" title="Paw Lagermann">Paw Lagermann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lina_Rafn" title="Lina Rafn">Lina Rafn</a> have made a comeback in class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span id="Musical_beginnings_and_early_success_(1997\xe2\x80\x932003)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_beginnings_and_early_success_.281997.E2.80.932003.29">Musical beginnings and early success (1997\xe2\x80\x932003)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Infernal_(Danish_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical beginnings and early success (1997\xe2\x80\x932003)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Infernal started out as a three-piece band in 1997. Lina, Paw and the third being S\xc3\xb8ren Haahr, but S\xc3\xb8ren left the group in 2000. Initially they were known for their unconventional style of singing as well as their experimental blend of different genres of music. In recent years they have turned towards more songwriting and their sound have gone from a subgenre club-sound to dance-oriented pop-sound aimed at the charts. An example of their early musical experiments is their track "<a href="/wiki/Kalinka_(song)" title="Kalinka (song)">Kalinka</a>" which is based on the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> Drobushki <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a> melody. From 1998 to 2004, the band released four albums in their native <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, three studio albums, one of them being a re-issue and one remix/live compilation album. All of them spawned Top 10 hits in Denmark, for example "Sunrise", "Muzaik", "Kiss The Sky", "Kalinka" and "You Receive Me", the latter being the theme song for a Danish season of the TV show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_Brother_(Denmark)" title="Big Brother (Denmark)">Big Brother</a></i>.</p>\n<h3><span id="International_breakthrough_(2004\xe2\x80\x932007)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="International_breakthrough_.282004.E2.80.932007.29">International breakthrough (2004\xe2\x80\x932007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Infernal_(Danish_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International breakthrough (2004\xe2\x80\x932007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2004, their third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/From_Paris_to_Berlin_(album)" title="From Paris to Berlin (album)">From Paris to Berlin</a></i> was released. With the release of the title track, "<a href="/wiki/From_Paris_to_Berlin" title="From Paris to Berlin">From Paris to Berlin</a>", they went on to major international chart-success. "From Paris to Berlin" became the only song released by a Danish act nominated at the Nordic Music Awards 2005. Ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">World Cup 2006</a>, "From Paris to Berlin" was re-recorded for the English market with lyrics celebrating the English football squad, titled "From London to Berlin". However, the song was withdrawn from radio airplay and TV following England\'s knockout from the World Cup In July 2006, "From Paris to Berlin" climbed to #2 in the UK charts and remained in the top five for seven weeks, becoming the 6th best selling single in the UK in 2006. The following single released in the UK was titled "<a href="/wiki/Self_Control_(Raf_song)" title="Self Control (Raf song)">Self Control</a>". It was a cover of a 1984 song by Italian pop singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raffaele_Riefoli" title="Raffaele Riefoli">Raf</a>, which was also covered by <a href="/wiki/Laura_Branigan" title="Laura Branigan">Laura Branigan</a> that same year. The Infernal version stalled at #18 in the UK charts, and #14 in Ireland. The single wasn\'t released in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, due to a similar version being released by Spanish singer <a href="/wiki/Soraya_Arnelas" title="Soraya Arnelas">Soraya Arnelas</a>. Instead, they released "<a href="/wiki/Ten_Miles" title="Ten Miles">Ten Miles</a>" in Spain, which topped the Spanish singles charts in November 2006. "<a href="/wiki/I_Won%27t_Be_Crying" title="I Won\'t Be Crying">I Won\'t Be Crying</a>" served as the third single in Europe but failed to build momentum and gain significant airplay in the UK. It still went on to become a moderate hit in Europe and became the band\'s most watched video on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, passing 3,000,000 views. On May 28, 2007, the band released a new version of their <i>From Paris to Berlin</i> album, this time in Europe and Asia. The new album contained two new songs and a bonus DVD on the Japanese edition. Counting sales of both first and second versions, <i>From Paris to Berlin</i> has sold more than one million copies worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><br/>\nAfterwards, "Ten Miles", was released in Europe. The song became an unexpected hit for the duo, surpassing the peak of "I Won\'t Be Crying" in Europe and reaching Top 5 in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.</p>\n<h3><span id="Electric_Cabaret_and_Fall_from_Grace_(2008\xe2\x80\x932010)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Electric_Cabaret_and_Fall_from_Grace_.282008.E2.80.932010.29"><i>Electric Cabaret</i> and <i>Fall from Grace</i> (2008\xe2\x80\x932010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Infernal_(Danish_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Electric Cabaret and Fall from Grace (2008\xe2\x80\x932010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<blockquote class="toccolours" style="text-align:left; width:27%; float:right; margin: 10px 10px 5px 5px; padding:10px 10px 10px 10px; display:table;">\n<p>"<i>We are experimenting with the sound and the direction of the album. The only thing we know is that we want it to be a little less feathers&amp;sequins and a bit more jeans&amp;T-shirt\xe2\x80\x99ish. I don\xe2\x80\x99t know how that sounds. We only know what feeling we are trying to create.</i>"</p>\n<p>\xe2\x80\x94 <a href="/wiki/Lina_Rafn" title="Lina Rafn">Lina Rafn</a> on the creation of the forthcoming 2010 album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010_album_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-2010_album-5">[5]</a></sup></p>\n</blockquote>\n<p>The band announced in February 2008 that their next album, titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_Cabaret_(album)" title="Electric Cabaret (album)">Electric Cabaret</a></i>, would be released worldwide on 11 August 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The lead single, "<a href="/wiki/Downtown_Boys_(song)" title="Downtown Boys (song)">Downtown Boys</a>", was premiered at Boogie Prisen, a Danish award show on national TV. The single was released on 14 April 2008 and peaked at number #2 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danish_Singles_Chart" title="Danish Singles Chart">Danish Singles Chart</a>, surpassing the peaks of "<a href="/wiki/Self_Control_(Raf_song)" title="Self Control (Raf song)">Self Control</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Won%27t_Be_Crying" title="I Won\'t Be Crying">I Won\'t Be Crying</a>", and #1 for four weeks on the Danish Airplay Chart, being their first single to top this chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 13 Jun, 2008, the full track list and album cover were released. The album met positive reviews, stating that stand-out tracks from the album include "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Punk_Disco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Punk Disco (page does not exist)">Punk Disco</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Redefinition_(Infernal_song)" title="Redefinition (Infernal song)">Redefinition</a>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <i>Electric Cabaret</i> was Infernal\'s first album to be released digitally through the band\'s official website, allowing fans from countries where the band is not signed to buy their music legally.</p>\n<p>On 26 June 2009 Lina Rafn announced that Infernal had started working on new material for their forthcoming fifth studio album, which was at that time scheduled to be released "sometime in 2010."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup> The overall sound of the album was described as, "less feathers&amp;sequins and a bit more jeans&amp;T-shirt\xe2\x80\x99ish."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010_album_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010_album-5">[5]</a></sup> In November 2009, Paw Lagermann said that they had written three songs with <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Troelsen" title="Thomas Troelsen">Thomas Troelsen</a>, who had previously co-written "Dead or Alive" from <i>Electric Cabaret</i>, including one outtake from the previous album, which had been reworked. He also revealed that they recorded 16-18 potential songs for the album, some of which would remain instrumental tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup> On 4 April 2010, Lagermann revealed that the album titled <i>Fall from Grace</i> would be released in late September 2010. He also said that the lead single off the album, "Love Is All...", would be released in early May 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 10 July 2010, the entire <i>Fall from Grace</i> track list was posted by Infernal themselves on their official website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><br/></p>\n<p>On 15 September 2010, Infernal released the second single off <i>Fall from Grace</i>, titled "Alone, Together". The single was released in Scandinavian iTunes Stores and their own Music Store that utilizes the music downloading service Klicktrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In the first two weeks after its release, <i>Fall from Grace</i> sold only 1,850 copies in Denmark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span id="New_Music_(2017-Present)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Music_.282017-Present.29"><i>New Music</i> (2017-Present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Infernal_(Danish_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New Music (2017-Present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In early 2017 Infernal released their first promotional single "Hurricane" from their forthcoming album which they plan to release later this year. The official Hurricane Remix reached #2 on the Danish Dance Chart in February 2017 and spent 14 weeks in the Dance Chart top 10.</p>\n<p>Since then two more promotional singles have been released
