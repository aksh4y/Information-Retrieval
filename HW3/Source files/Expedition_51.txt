51st expedition to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>, which began upon the departure of Soyuz MS-02 on April 10, 2017 and concluded upon the departure of Soyuz MS-03 on June 2, 2017. <a href="/wiki/Peggy_Whitson" title="Peggy Whitson">Peggy Whitson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Novitskiy" title="Oleg Novitskiy">Oleg Novitskiy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Pesquet" title="Thomas Pesquet">Thomas Pesquet</a> were transferred from <a href="/wiki/Expedition_50" title="Expedition 50">Expedition 50</a>, with Peggy Whitson taking the commander role. She is the first woman to command two expeditions to the ISS, having previously commanded <a href="/wiki/Expedition_16" title="Expedition 16">Expedition 16</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to a decision to cut down the number of participating Russian cosmonauts in 2017, only two cosmonauts were launched on <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_MS-04" title="Soyuz MS-04">Soyuz MS-04</a> on April 20, 2017 - bringing the total crew number to five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Transfer of Command from Expedition 51 to <a href="/wiki/Expedition_52" title="Expedition 52">Expedition 52</a> was done on June 1, 2017. Expedition 51 officially ended on June 2, 2017 10:47 UTC, with the undocking of <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_MS-03" title="Soyuz MS-03">Soyuz MS-03</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-end_3-0"><a
