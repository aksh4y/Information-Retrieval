<b>F4-134 Hurricane</b> was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inline-4" title="Inline-4">inline-4</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piston_engine" title="Piston engine">piston engine</a> and powered the famous <a href="/wiki/Jeep_CJ" title="Jeep CJ">Jeep CJ</a> in the CJ-3B, CJ-5, and CJ-6 models. It was also used in the Willys 473 and 475 pickups, wagons, and sedan deliveries. It replaced the <a href="/wiki/Willys_Go_Devil_engine" title="Willys Go Devil engine">Willys Go Devil engine</a> that was used in the <a href="/wiki/Willys_MB" title="Willys MB">MB Jeep</a> and other early Jeep-based models like the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeep_Jeepster" title="Jeep Jeepster">Jeepster</a>. This engine was also built by <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi" title="Mitsubishi">Mitsubishi</a> for their license-built Jeep, as well as other applications.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Design"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Design</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#F134"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">F134</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#F161"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">F161</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Super_Hurricane"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Super Hurricane</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#Mitsubishi_versions"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">Mitsubishi versions</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n<p></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Hurricane_engine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Hurricane was based on the earlier Go-Devil <a href="/wiki/Flathead_engine" title="Flathead engine">flathead engine</a>. To get more power from the engine, the induction system was changed from the Go-Devil\'s side-valve configuration to an <a href="/wiki/IOE_engine" title="IOE engine">inlet-over-exhaust configuration, or "F-head"</a>. This allowed the valves to be larger and the combustion chamber to be smaller, improving flow and increasing the <a href="/wiki/Compression_ratio" title="Compression ratio">compression ratio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown80_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brown80-2">[2]</a></sup> The compression ratio rose from 6.5:1 in the Go-Devil engine to 7.5:1 in the Hurricane engine, although a version of the Hurricane engine was made with a 6.9:1 compression ratio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown236_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brown236-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="F134">F134</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Hurricane_engine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: F134">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Willys_Go_Devil_engine" title="Willys Go Devil engine">L134 Go Devil</a> was updated with the F-head to become the <b>F134</b> in 1950.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown74_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brown74-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeep4x4PerformanceP193_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jeep4x4PerformanceP193-5">[5]</a></sup> This engine produced a gross output of 75\xc2\xa0hp (56\xc2\xa0kW; 76\xc2\xa0PS) at 4000\xc2\xa0rpm and 114\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (155\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) of torque at 2000\xc2\xa0rpm with a 7.5:1 compression ratio. The gross power and torque outputs fell to 72\xc2\xa0hp (54\xc2\xa0kW; 73\xc2\xa0PS) and 112\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (152\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) respectively when the engine had a 6.9:1 compression ratio. Bore and stroke dimensions were the same as the L-head engine at <span class="nowrap">\xe2\x80\x8a</span><span class="frac nowrap">3<span class="visualhide">\xc2\xa0</span><sup>1</sup>\xe2\x81\x84<sub>8</sub></span>" x <span class="nowrap">\xe2\x80\x8a</span><span class="frac nowrap">4<span class="visualhide">\xc2\xa0</span><sup>3</sup>\xe2\x81\x84<sub>8</sub></span>" giving 134.2\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0in (2,199\xc2\xa0cc).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown236_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Brown236-3">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The F4-134 was introduced in 1950 in the <a href="/wiki/Willys_Jeep_Truck" title="Willys Jeep Truck">Jeep Truck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown74_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Brown74-4">[4]</a></sup> Vehicles with this engine were given the model designation 4-73.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen215_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allen215-6">[6]</a></sup> This engine was not placed in a CJ until the introduction of the CJ-3B in 1953, which had a distinctive high hood to accommodate the much taller engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown80_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Brown80-2">[2]</a></sup> The engine remained in production until 1971, after <a href="/wiki/American_Motors" title="American Motors">American Motors Corporation</a> (AMC) purchased Kaiser Jeep.</p>\n<p>Applications:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>1950\xe2\x80\x931961 <a href="/wiki/Willys_Jeep_Truck" title="Willys Jeep Truck">Willys Jeep Truck</a></li>\n<li>1950\xe2\x80\x931961 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Willys_Jeep_Wagon" title="Willys Jeep Wagon">Willys Jeep Wagon</a></li>\n<li>1950 <a href="/wiki/Willys-Overland_Jeepster" title="Willys-Overland Jeepster">Willys-Overland Jeepster</a></li>\n<li>1952\xe2\x80\x931971 <a href="/wiki/Willys_M38A1" title="Willys M38A1">Willys M38A1</a></li>\n<li>1953 Willys 475A Lark</li>\n<li>1953\xe2\x80\x931968 <a href="/wiki/Jeep_CJ#CJ-3B" title="Jeep CJ">Willys CJ-3B</a></li>\n<li>1955\xe2\x80\x931971 <a href="/wiki/Jeep_CJ#CJ-5" title="Jeep Commando</a></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="F161">F161</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Hurricane_engine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: F161">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The F6-161 Hurricane is an F-head version of the L6-161 Lightning flathead straight six. It was used in the Model 685 Station Wagon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<p><b>BF-161</b> The BF-161 has a <span class="nowrap">\xe2\x80\x8a</span><span class="frac nowrap">3<span class="visualhide">\xc2\xa0</span><sup>1</sup>\xe2\x81\x84<sub>8</sub></span> inch bore and a <span class="nowrap">\xe2\x80\x8a</span><span class="frac nowrap">3<span class="visualhide">\xc2\xa0</span><sup>1</sup>\xe2\x81\x84<sub>2</sub></span> inch stroke, a one-barrel <a href="/wiki/Carburetor" title="Carburetor">carburetor</a>, and an output of 90\xc2\xa0hp (67\xc2\xa0kW; 91\xc2\xa0PS) at 4400\xc2\xa0rpm and 135\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (183\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) of torque at 2000\xc2\xa0rpm. Its 161.1\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0in (2,640\xc2\xa0cc) displacement features a compression ratio of 7.6:1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruralwillys_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ruralwillys-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p><b>2600</b> The 2600 was essentially the same BF-161 engine made in Brazil by Willys-Overland\'s subsidiary, but it had two one-barrel carburetors (simultaneously opened) and had an output of 130\xc2\xa0hp (97\xc2\xa0kW; 132\xc2\xa0PS) at 4400\xc2\xa0rpm and 140\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (190\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) of torque at 2000\xc2\xa0rpm. The compression ratio remained 7.6:1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruralwillys_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-ruralwillys-8">[8]</a></sup></p>\n<p><b>3000</b> The 3-liter version is almost identical to the BF-161 engine with the stroke increased to 4 inches (100\xc2\xa0mm) giving it a displacement of 3,016\xc2\xa0cc (184.0\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruralwillys_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-ruralwillys-8">[8]</a></sup> With a 2-barrel carburetor it produced 140\xc2\xa0hp (104\xc2\xa0kW; 142\xc2\xa0PS) at 4400\xc2\xa0rpm and 161\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (218\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) of torque at 2000\xc2\xa0rpm. It also had a slightly higher compression ratio of 8:1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruralwillys_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-ruralwillys-8">[8]</a></sup> It initially used a different head with removable intake manifold. Later, after Ford acquired Willys-Overland do Brasil, they reverted the engine to its former head design with integral intake manifold, improved cooling between cylinders 5 and 6 and installed a side-mounted oil filter, instead of the front-mounted, hose connected arrangement used by Willys.</p>\n<p>Applications:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>1961\xe2\x80\x931962 <a href="/wiki/Willys_Aero" title="Willys Aero">Willys Aero</a></li>\n<li>1963\xe2\x80\x931971 <a href="/wiki/Willys_Aero" title="Willys Aero">Willys Aero</a> 2600</li>\n<li>1960-1974 Willys-Overland <a href="/wiki/Jeep" title="Jeep">Jeep</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rural_Willys&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rural Willys (page does not exist)">Rural Willys</a></li>\n<li>1968\xe2\x80\x931971 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Itamaraty&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Willys Itamaraty (page does not exist)">Willys Itamaraty</a> 3000</li>\n<li>1972-1974 <a href="/wiki/Ford_Maverick" title="Ford Maverick">Ford Maverick</a> six-cylinder version in Brazil</li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Super_Hurricane">Super Hurricane</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span Willys Utility Wagon</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The 6-226 "Super Hurricane"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> was a L-head 6-cylinder<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeep101_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jeep101-11">[11]</a></sup> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Continental_straight_six_engine" title="Continental straight six engine">Continental</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeep101_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jeep101-11">[11]</a></sup> with a bore of <span class="nowrap">\xe2\x80\x8a</span><span class="frac nowrap">3<span class="visualhide">\xc2\xa0</span><sup>5</sup>\xe2\x81\x84<sub>16</sub></span>\xc2\xa0inches and stroke of <span class="nowrap">\xe2\x80\x8a</span><span class="frac nowrap">4<span class="visualhide">\xc2\xa0</span><sup>3</sup>\xe2\x81\x84<sub>8</sub></span>\xc2\xa0inches, giving a displacement of 226.2\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0in (3,707\xc2\xa0cc).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSMar1954_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-PSMar1954-12">[12]</a></sup> Horsepower rating is 105\xc2\xa0hp (78\xc2\xa0kW; 106\xc2\xa0PS) at 3600\xc2\xa0rpm or 115\xc2\xa0hp (86\xc2\xa0kW; 117\xc2\xa0PS) at 3650\xc2\xa0rpm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSMar1954_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-PSMar1954-12">[12]</a></sup> as well as a torque rating of 190\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (258\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) at 1400\xc2\xa0rpm or at 1800\xc2\xa0rpm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSMar1954_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-PSMar1954-12">[12]</a></sup> depending on the year of production.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mitsubishi_versions">Mitsubishi versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Hurricane_engine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mitsubishi versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Mitsubishi built a version of the Hurricane from 1954 as the JH4 (69 hp), mostly for use in their license-built version of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Jeep" title="Mitsubishi Jeep">Jeep</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> They later developed a 61\xc2\xa0PS (45\xc2\xa0kW) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overhead-valve_engine" title="Overhead-valve engine">overhead-valve</a> diesel version of the same, called <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mitsubishi_Fuso_engines#KE_series" title="List of Mitsubishi Fuso engines">KE31</a>. This was also turned into a 3.3 liter six-cylinder version with the same internal dimensions, producing 85\xc2\xa0PS (63\xc2\xa0kW), which was named KE36. These diesel engines were used in the Jeep, but also in a number of light to medium-weight trucks and buses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CamionFuso_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CamionFuso-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Hurricane_engine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-baeta-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-baeta_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-baeta_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a class="external text" href="http://www.baeta.org/opie/engine.htm#R" rel="nofollow">"Engine Specs - Jeep Engines - Hurricane F-Head 134 I4"</a>.
