colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Emily</b> was the earliest forming <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5 Atlantic hurricane</a> on record in a season and the most intense to form before August. A powerful, early <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="Atlantic hurricane season">season</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde-type_hurricane" title="Cape Verde-type hurricane">Cape Verde-type</a> <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> that caused significant damage across the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, the storm formed on July\xc2\xa010, 2005, in the central Atlantic Ocean before passing through the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a> on July\xc2\xa014. Tracking generally towards the west-northwest, the storm gradually intensified as it traversed the Caribbean, peaking as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_5" title="SSHS">Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane</a> on July\xc2\xa016, marking the earliest date for a storm to do so during the course of a given year. The system subsequently made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</a> as a Category\xc2\xa04. Quickly crossing the peninsula, Emily emerged into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> and reorganized. On July\xc2\xa020, the storm struck <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_3" title="SSHS">major hurricane</a> and rapidly dissipated within 24\xc2\xa0hours. The storm caused significant damage along its path, with up to $1.01 billion (2005 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USD" title="USD">USD</a>) in damages recorded, as well as causing 17 fatalities.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Meteorological_history"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Emily_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Emily_2005_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Emily_2005_track.png/220px-Emily_2005_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Emily_2005_track.png/330px-Emily_2005_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Emily_2005_track.png/440px-Emily_2005_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Emily_2005_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nMap plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>On July 6, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Moving westward, the disturbance gradually organized until July\xc2\xa010, at which point convection became more concentrated, and it is estimated Tropical Depression Five formed in the central tropical Atlantic that evening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Late on July\xc2\xa011, it strengthened and was named Tropical Storm Emily. Initially forecast to strengthen rapidly and move west-northwest through the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a>, Emily instead moved almost due west toward the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a>, remaining a moderate tropical storm. The storm languished while moving quickly west, and struggled with maintaining its form. Contrary to the normal occurrence that hurricanes leave a cold wake behind, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Dennis" title="Hurricane Dennis">Hurricane Dennis</a> had made portions of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> warmer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Emily_Discussion_8_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Emily_Discussion_8-2">[2]</a></sup> and therefore more favorable for tropical cyclone development. Late on July\xc2\xa013, Emily strengthened rapidly and reached hurricane strength while passing <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">Tobago</a> and entering the eastern Caribbean. On July\xc2\xa014, Emily made landfall in northern <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a> with 90\xc2\xa0mph (145\xc2\xa0km/h) winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The intensification trend picked up again the next day with a fairly rapid drop in the storm\'s <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">central pressure</a> as it entered the southeastern Caribbean Sea, a region typically unfavorable for intensification. Hurricane Emily\'s winds increased in reaction, briefly bringing the storm to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_4" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 4</a> strength early on July\xc2\xa015. During the day, the storm\'s strength fluctuated greatly, dropping to a Category\xc2\xa02 storm and then rebuilding to Category\xc2\xa04. On July\xc2\xa016, Emily strengthened considerably, making it the strongest hurricane ever on record to form in the month of July with peak winds of 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h), the earliest known <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_5" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 5</a> in the Atlantic basin. Initially at this point Emily was thought to have peaked as a Category\xc2\xa04 storm, but the post-storm analysis showed it was indeed a Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The storm weakened slightly as it continued westward, and remained a Category\xc2\xa04 while passing south of <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> and, on July\xc2\xa017, the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>. Hurricane Emily continued on its nearly straight track to the west-northwest, weakening somewhat but remaining at Category\xc2\xa04 until striking <a href="/wiki/Cozumel" title="Cozumel">Cozumel</a> just before mainland landfall at <a href="/wiki/Playa_del_Carmen" title="Playa del Carmen">Playa del Carmen</a> at 06:30\xc2\xa0UTC on July\xc2\xa018. Sustained winds were 135\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h), and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyewall" title="Eyewall">eyewall</a> passed directly over Cozumel.</p>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Emily_stormfloater_ir_0718_0645UTC.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="720" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Emily_stormfloater_ir_0718_0645UTC.jpg/220px-Emily_stormfloater_ir_0718_0645UTC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Emily_stormfloater_ir_0718_0645UTC.jpg/330px-Emily_stormfloater_ir_0718_0645UTC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Emily_stormfloater_ir_0718_0645UTC.jpg/440px-Emily_stormfloater_ir_0718_0645UTC.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Emily_stormfloater_ir_0718_0645UTC.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Emily making landfall on July\xc2\xa018, 2005</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>The center of circulation emerged over the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> later that morning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Passage over land disrupted the hurricane\'s center of circulation, and it had weakened to a minimal hurricane with wind speeds of 75\xc2\xa0mph (120\xc2\xa0km/h). However, several hours over the warm waters of the western Gulf provided the energy needed for Emily to regenerate, and by midnight wind speeds were increasing. The increase in wind speed stalled, but the storm continued to become better organized. Emily started to show very symmetrical <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>, but the hurricane\'s strongest winds were being found at three different distances from the center. However, the outer wind radii subsided in the end, and the inner core prevailed. The result was a rapid strengthening of the inner core on the evening of July\xc2\xa019. The pressure dropped about 30\xc2\xa0millibars and the winds went from 90\xc2\xa0mph (145\xc2\xa0km/h) to more than 125\xc2\xa0mph (200\xc2\xa0km/h), all within a few hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Further strengthening was expected by forecasters, but did not come.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The storm\'s motion slowed, and the center began wobbling erratically toward the coast. The storm made landfall around 11:00\xc2\xa0UTC on July\xc2\xa020 near <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando,_Tamaulipas" title="San Fernando, Tamaulipas">San Fernando</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>. The storm had sustained winds of 125\xc2\xa0mph (200\xc2\xa0km/h), Category\xc2\xa03 on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale. After heading inland over northeast Mexico, it dissipated over the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Oriental" title="Sierra Madre Oriental">Sierra Madre Oriental</a> on July\xc2\xa021.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Emily_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Emily_on_Brownsville_NEXRAD_at_0307_UTC.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="620" data-file-width="620" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Hurricane_Emily_on_Brownsville_NEXRAD_at_0307_UTC.png/200px-Hurricane_Emily_on_Brownsville_NEXRAD_at_0307_UTC.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Hurricane_Emily_on_Brownsville_NEXRAD_at_0307_UTC.png/300px-Hurricane_Emily_on_Brownsville_NEXRAD_at_0307_UTC.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Hurricane_Emily_on_Brownsville_NEXRAD_at_0307_UTC.png/400px-Hurricane_Emily_on_Brownsville_NEXRAD_at_0307_UTC.png 2x" width="200"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Emily_on_Brownsville_NEXRAD_at_0307_UTC.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nHurricane Emily, as seen by the US <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/NEXRAD" title="NEXRAD">NEXRAD</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">Brownsville, Texas</a> at 03:07\xc2\xa0UTC July\xc2\xa020. The storm\'s eye is clearly visible, surrounded by the strong storms of the <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eyewall</a>. At imaging time, Emily was a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane with 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km) winds, was moving west-northwest at 7\xc2\xa0mph (11\xc2\xa0km/h), and was roughly 100\xc2\xa0miles (160\xc2\xa0km) away from the location of landfall.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In Mexico, tens of thousands of tourists and residents were evacuated from the beach resorts in and around <a href="/wiki/Canc%C3%BAn" title="Canc\xc3\xban">Canc\xc3\xban</a>, the Riviera Maya, and <a href="/wiki/Cozumel" title="Cozumel">Cozumel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Hotel guests were evacuated on Saturday afternoon, and staff on Sunday afternoon. Though some hotel guests in second floor rooms and above were given the option to be bussed into the center of Cancun to safe houses and shelters or to stay and wait out the storm in only the modern built hotels and resorts. Most of the remaining guests were restricted to their rooms and were not allowed out on the beach areas. No alcohol was sold in Cancun for 36\xc2\xa0hours prior to the arrival of the storm, in an attempt to avoid drunken tourists being injured during the night. Two <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> pilots were killed when their aircraft crashed while evacuating offshore <a href="/wiki/Oil_platform" title="Oil platform">oil platforms</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/Pemex" title="Pemex">Pemex</a>. A German resident was electrocuted while on his roof in <a href="/wiki/Playa_del_Carmen" title="Playa del Carmen">Playa del Carmen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TerraDaily1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TerraDaily1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Emily_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean">Caribbean</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Emily_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Caribbean">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On July 14, Emily hit <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a> which was still recovering from the impact of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">Hurricane Ivan</a> less than a year earlier. Emily resulted in one reported fatality and significant damage in the northern part of the country, including <a href="/wiki/Carriacou" title="Carriacou">Carriacou</a> which had been spared the worst effects of Ivan. 16\xc2\xa0houses were destroyed and well over 200 more were damaged, and two of the main hospitals were flooded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdnn_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdnn-5">[5]</a></sup> Damages in Grenada amounted to $110.4\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CDERA2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CDERA2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Landslides were reported in eastern <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, triggered by heavy rain as the storm passed south of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdnn_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdnn-5">[5]</a></sup> Five people were killed due to an accident caused by flash flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CDERA1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CDERA1-7">[7]</a></sup> Damages in Jamaica were estimated at $65\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CDERA2_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-CDERA2-6">[6]</a></sup> Damage was also reported in <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>, where landslides and flooding damaged several homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdnn_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-cdnn-5">[5]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, a man drowned in a river swollen by rains from Emily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TerraDaily1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-TerraDaily1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span id="Yucat.C3.A1n_Peninsula">Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Emily_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Striking the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula on July\xc2\xa018 as a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane, widespread damage was expected from 135\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h) winds; however, these winds were confined to a small area around Emily\'s center. Areas in Playa del Carmen, Tulum and Cozumel sustained the most severe impact. In a few instances, concrete <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Utility_poles" title="Utility poles">utility poles</a> were snapped in half by powerful wind gusts. Due to the relatively fast movement of Emily, rainfall was fairly light, peaking at 4.9\xc2\xa0in (120\xc2\xa0mm). Few meteorological reports exist during Emily\'s passage of the Yucat\xc3\xa1n, though an unofficial station recorded a <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> of 15\xc2\xa0ft (4.6\xc2\xa0m) in San Miguel, Cozumel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Along the mainland, surge heights were generally less than 4\xc2\xa0ft (1\xc2\xa0m). Additionally, waves reached 13\xc2\xa0ft (4\xc2\xa0m), resulting in some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">beach erosion</a> and damage to dunes and <a href="/wiki/Coral_reef" title="Coral reef">coral reefs</a>. Additionally, 0.7\xc2\xa0mi (1.1\xc2\xa0km) of retaining walls sustained damage, leaving areas vulnerable to flooding from future storms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MexicoReport3_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-MexicoReport3-17">[17]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The high winds produced by Emily caused considerable impact in <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>, especially in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Solidaridad,_Quintana_Roo" title="Solidaridad, Quintana Roo">Solidaridad</a>, leaving nearly 200,000 residences without power. In terms of structural damage, 851 homes were impacted in varying degrees. Roughly 13,345\xc2\xa0acres (5,400\xc2\xa0hectares) of forests and agricultural land was affected by the storm; some swathes of trees experienced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defoliation" title="Defoliation">defoliation</a>. The most substantial losses associated with the hurricane stemmed from the tourism industry, with hotels experiencing 947\xc2\xa0million pesos ($88.7\xc2\xa0million) worth of damage. More than 12,500 rooms, nearly one-fifth of the state\'s available hotel infrastructure, sustained damage. Overall, damage in the state reached 1.11\xc2\xa0billion pesos ($104.3\xc2\xa0million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MexicoReport3_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-MexicoReport3-17">[17]</a></sup></p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Northeastern_Mexico">Northeastern Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Emily_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Northeastern Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Emily\'s second landfall as a strong Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane brought significant damage to the northeast coast of Mexico. In the fishing community of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Laguna_Madre,_Tamaulipas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Laguna Madre, Tamaulipas (page does not exist)">Laguna Madre</a>, over 80% of the buildings were destroyed as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Several communities on the remote coast of <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> were isolated after the storm, and major coastal flooding was reported along with heavy wind damage, with numerous homes destroyed. Inland flooding was also reported in <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guycarp_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-guycarp-18">[18]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Communication to the Riviera Maya area was difficult after the storm; not all cellular phones were receiving coverage to the area, land lines were down, and electricity was out. About 18,000\xc2\xa0people in 20 low-lying communities in the state of <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>, just south of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S.-Mexican_border" title="U.S.-Mexican border">U.S.-Mexican border</a>, were evacuated. Including losses sustained by the oil industry, damage in Mexico amounted to 8.87\xc2\xa0billion pesos ($834.3\xc2\xa0million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MexicoReport2_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-MexicoReport2-14">[14]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Texas">Texas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Emily_(2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Texas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Emily_2005_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="910" data-file-width="1055" height="190" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Emily_2005_rainfall.gif/220px-Emily_2005_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Emily_2005_rainfall.gif/330px-Emily_2005_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Emily_2005_rainfall.gif/440px-Emily_2005_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>\n<div class="thumbcaption">\n<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Emily_2005_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nTotal rainfall from Emily in the United States</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>In southern <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, damage was relatively minor despite the proximity of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday1_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday1-19">[19]</a></sup> Portions of the state experienced tropical storm force winds and gusts as high as 65\xc2\xa0mph (100\xc2\xa0km/h), resulting in scattered roof damage. Along the coast, a storm surge of 4.26\xc2\xa0ft (1.30\xc2\xa0m) flooded portions of <a href="/wiki/Texas_State_Highway_100" title="Texas State Highway 100">Texas State Highway 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EmilyTCR_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-EmilyTCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> No significant structural damage was reported, although some trees were down and over 30,000 customers lost electricity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday1_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday1-19">[19]</a></sup> Rainfall from the storm peaked at 5.2\xc2\xa0inches in <a href="/wiki/Mercedes,_Texas" title="Mercedes, Texas">Mercedes, Texas</a>. Additionally, eight tornadoes touched down in Texas as a result of Emily, damaging or destroying several homes. Some positive effects were noted; the remains of Emily passed farther west into Texas and delivered some badly needed rainfall, helping relieve a drought.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guycarp_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-guycarp-18">[18]</a></sup> Agricultural losses in Texas amounted to $4.7\xc2\xa0million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USDA1_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-USDA1-16">[16]</a></sup> while property losses reached $225,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-NCDC-15">[15]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span
