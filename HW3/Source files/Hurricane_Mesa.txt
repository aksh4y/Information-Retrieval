text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Hurricane_Mesa&amp;params=37_14_25_N_113_12_41_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">37\xc2\xb014\xe2\x80\xb225\xe2\x80\xb3N</span> <span class="longitude">113\xc2\xb012\xe2\x80\xb241\xe2\x80\xb3W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">\xef\xbb\xbf / \xef\xbb\xbf</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">37.24028\xc2\xb0N 113.21139\xc2\xb0W</span><span style="display:none">\xef\xbb\xbf / <span class="geo">37.24028; -113.21139</span></span></span></a></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a> landform near <a href="/wiki/Hurricane,_Utah" title="Hurricane, Utah">Hurricane, Utah</a>, used for <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> tests of rocket ejection seats for supersonic aircraft at the <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Supersonic_Research_Site" title="Hurricane Supersonic Research Site">Hurricane Supersonic Research Site</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UHQ_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-UHQ-2">[2]</a></sup> The mesa is "flat bedrock of faultless <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shinarump&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shinarump (page does not exist)">Shinarump</a> conglomerate" 1,500\xc2\xa0ft (460\xc2\xa0m) above the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_River" title="Virgin River">Virgin River</a> valley, which allowed clearance for assessment of a longer flight trajectory up from the mesa and over the cliff for the test object (e.g., the anthropoid simulator--dummy--named "Hurricane Sam").</p>\n<p>Currently the facility is still used to test military ejection seats for the US and Foreign Govt\'s.</p>\n<h2><span
