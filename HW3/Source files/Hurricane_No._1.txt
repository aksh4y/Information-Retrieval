The band were formed by former <a href="/wiki/Ride_(band)" title="Ride (band)">Ride</a> <a href="/wiki/Guitarist" title="Guitarist">guitarist</a> <a href="/wiki/Andy_Bell_(musician)" title="Andy Bell (musician)">Andy Bell</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">vocalist</a> / guitarist Alex Lowe, <a href="/wiki/Bassist" title="Bassist">bassist</a> Will Pepper and <a href="/wiki/Drummer" title="Drummer">drummer</a> Gareth "Gaz" Farmer. After releasing two albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_No._1_(album)" title="Hurricane No. 1 (album)">Hurricane #1</a></i> (1997) and <i><a href="/wiki/Only_the_Strongest_Will_Survive" title="Only the Strongest Will Survive">Only the Strongest Will Survive</a></i> (1999), the band broke-up. Bell went to join <a href="/wiki/Gay_Dad" title="Gay Dad">Gay Dad</a> and then eventually <a href="/wiki/Oasis_(band)" title="Oasis (band)">Oasis</a>, while Lowe ventured into a solo career. Fronted by Lowe, the band reformed in 2014 and released their third album, <i>Find What You Love and Let It Kill You</i>, in November 2015.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_No._1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span id="Formation,_Hurricane_No._1_and_Only_the_Strongest_Will_Survive_(1996\xe2\x80\x9399)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation.2C_Hurricane_No._1_and_Only_the_Strongest_Will_Survive_.281996.E2.80.9399.29">Formation, <i>Hurricane No. 1</i> and <i>Only the Strongest Will Survive</i> (1996\xe2\x80\x9399)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_No._1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation, Hurricane No. 1 and Only the Strongest Will Survive (1996\xe2\x80\x9399)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Wishing to perform the later-day sound of his former band <a href="/wiki/Ride_(band)" title="Ride (band)">Ride</a>, vocalist/guitarist <a href="/wiki/Andy_Bell_(musician)" title="Andy Bell (musician)">Andy Bell</a> formed Hurricane No. 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMJJan98_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CMJJan98-1">[1]</a></sup> Signed to <a href="/wiki/Creation_Records" title="Creation Records">Creation Records</a> (along with fellow bands such as <a href="/wiki/Oasis_(band)" title="Oasis (band)">Oasis</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Jesus_and_Mary_Chain" title="The Jesus and Mary Chain">The Jesus and Mary Chain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Primal_Scream" title="Primal Scream">Primal Scream</a>) they released their debut <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a> in 1997, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_No._1_(album)" title="Hurricane No. 1 (album)">Hurricane #1</a></i>. This included their first <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Step_into_My_World" title="Step into My World">Step into My World</a>", which reached number 29 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> (and a <a href="/wiki/Paul_Oakenfold" title="Paul Oakenfold">Paul Oakenfold</a> <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> of which reached number 19 in 1997),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-2">[2]</a></sup> and other less successful singles "<a href="/wiki/Just_Another_Illusion" title="Just Another Illusion">Just Another Illusion</a>" and "Chain Reaction".</p>\n<p>In 1999, follow-up album <i><a href="/wiki/Only_the_Strongest_Will_Survive" title="Only the Strongest Will Survive">Only the Strongest Will Survive</a></i> spawned the band\'s other highest charting single, also called "Only the Strongest Will Survive". The song reached number 19 in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-2">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span id="Break_up_(1999\xe2\x80\x932014)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Break_up_.281999.E2.80.932014.29">Break up (1999\xe2\x80\x932014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_No._1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Break up (1999\xe2\x80\x932014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1999, Bell parted ways with Hurricane No. 1, joining <a href="/wiki/Gay_Dad" title="Gay Dad">Gay Dad</a> on guitar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NME-3">[3]</a></sup> Shortly after joining Gay Dad, Bell split from the band to become the <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass</a> player for Oasis. In 2004, a <a href="/wiki/Double_album" title="Double album">double</a> <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">disc</a> <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation</a> of their <a href="/wiki/Song" title="Song">songs</a> was released, entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Step_into_My_World_(Anthology)" title="Step into My World (Anthology)">Step into My World</a></i>.</p>\n<p>Former Hurricane No. 1 vocalist / guitarist, Lowe has pursued a <a href="/wiki/Solo_(music)" title="Solo (music)">solo</a> career, and has released three albums to date. In 2007, Lowe announced a new project called Garage Flowers, and a single, "Where To Begin", was released via <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>.</p>\n<h3><span id="Reformation_(2014\xe2\x80\x93present)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Reformation_.282014.E2.80.93present.29">Reformation (2014\xe2\x80\x93present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_No._1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reformation (2014\xe2\x80\x93present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In November 2014, it was announced that Hurricane No. 1 had reunited without Bell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reunion_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-reunion-4">[4]</a></sup> The line-up, fronted by Lowe,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reunion_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-reunion-4">[4]</a></sup> featured Carlo Mariani on guitar, Lucas Mariani has now been replaced by Chris Mullin on bass and Chris Campbell on drums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The band\'s third album, <i>Find What You Love and Let It Kill You</i>, was released on 26 November 2015 through German-based label <a href="/wiki/Tapete_Records" title="Tapete Records">Tapete</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xsnoize_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-xsnoize-6">[6]</a></sup> Bell features "Think of the Sunshine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stereogum_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stereogum-7">[7]</a></sup> Lowe wrote the album while he was undergoing treatment for cancer in hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stereogum_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stereogum-7">[7]</a></sup> Lowe explained that "When you are wired up to chemo and radio therapy, the last thing you want to do is wallow in it and feel sorry for yourself so I had the idea that the album should be happy and not too dark."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stereogum_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stereogum-7">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<p><i>Melodic Rainbows</i>, the band\'s fourth album, was released on 12 October 2016<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> in Japan. The album will be released in the UK in November 2017. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The band are set to play Beano on the Sea festival in September<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and the Shiine On Weekender in November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The band are set to embark on a tour early 2018 and will also releasing another album.. For now the band are releasing a single via download everyone month from September 2017 until some time in 2018... They are back and sounding bigger than ever.</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discography">Discography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_No._1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Discography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_No._1_discography" title="Hurricane No. 1 discography">Hurricane No. 1 discography</a></div>\n<dl>\n<dt>Studio albums</dt>\n</dl>\n<ul>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_No._1_(album)" title="Hurricane No. 1 (album)">Hurricane #1</a></i> (1997)</li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Only_the_Strongest_Will_Survive" title="Only the Strongest Will Survive">Only the Strongest Will Survive</a></i> (1999)</li>\n<li><i>Find What You Love and Let It Kill You</i> (2015)</li>\n<li><i>Melodic Rainbows</i> (2016)</li>\n<li><i>Delusions of Grandeur</i> (2017)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>\n</ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_No._1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<dl>\n<dt>Citations</dt>\n</dl>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
