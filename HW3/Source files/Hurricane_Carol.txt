border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">Part of the <b><a href="/wiki/1954_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1954 Atlantic hurricane season">1954 Atlantic hurricane season</a></b></td>\n</tr>\n<tr style="display:none">\n<td colspan="2"></td>\n</tr>\n</table>\n<p><b>Hurricane Carol</b> was among the worst <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">tropical cyclones</a> on record to affect the states of <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It developed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> on August 25, 1954, and slowly strengthened as it moved northwestward. On August 27, Carol intensified to reach winds of 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h), but weakened as its motion turned to a northwest drift. A strong <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> of low pressure turned the hurricane northeastward, and Carol later intensified into a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">major hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[nb 1]</a></sup> While paralleling the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_states" title="Mid-Atlantic states">Mid-Atlantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southeastern United States</a>, the storm produced strong winds and rough seas that caused minor coastal flooding and slight damage to houses in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>. The well-organized hurricane accelerated north-northeastward and made landfall on eastern <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, and then over eastern <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> on August\xc2\xa031 with sustained winds estimated at 110-mph and a barometric pressure near 956 mb. Carol transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> over <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a> later on August 31, 1954.</p>\n<p>In New York, strong winds on Long Island damaged about 1,000 houses, left 275,000\xc2\xa0people without electricity, downed many trees, and resulted in heavy crop losses. Storm surge flooded <a href="/wiki/LaGuardia_Airport" title="LaGuardia Airport">LaGuardia Airport</a> and inundated the <a href="/wiki/Montauk_Highway" title="Montauk Highway">Montauk Highway</a>, which left the eastern portion of Long Island isolated. Carol also brought strong winds and rough seas to coastal Connecticut, Rhode Island, and southeastern Massachusetts. Throughout the region, about 150,000\xc2\xa0people were left without electricity and telephone service. 1,545\xc2\xa0houses were destroyed and another 9,720\xc2\xa0were damaged. Approximately 3,500\xc2\xa0cars and 3,000\xc2\xa0boats were destroyed. There were 65\xc2\xa0deaths and 1,000\xc2\xa0injuries in New England. The storm caused an additional $1\xc2\xa0million in damage in Canada as well as two deaths. Overall, Carol caused 72\xc2\xa0fatalities and damage totaled $462\xc2\xa0million (1954\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[nb 2]</a></sup> making it the costliest hurricane in the history of the United States, at the time. Following the storm, Carol was <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">retired</a>, becoming the first name to be removed from the naming lists in the Atlantic basin.</p>\n<p></p>\n<div class="toc" id="toc">\n<div class="toctitle">\n<h2>Contents</h2>\n</div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a class="thumb tleft">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Carol_1954_track.png"><img alt="Track of Hurricane Carol, beginning near the Bahamas and ending over Canada" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2082" data-file-width="2700" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Carol_1954_track.png/220px-Carol_1954_track.png" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale</a></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> spawned a tropical depression over the northeastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> on August\xc2\xa025.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> It moved to the north-northwest and intensified into a tropical storm early on August\xc2\xa026.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[3]</a></sup> Receiving the name Carol, the storm gradually turned to the north, and strengthened under generally favorable conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> On August\xc2\xa026, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> reported an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>, 23 miles (37\xc2\xa0km) in diameter despite Carol being a tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-6">[4]</a></sup> The next day, Carol strengthened to attain hurricane status while located about 345\xc2\xa0miles (545\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Canaveral" title="Cape Canaveral">Cape Canaveral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> With a large <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a> persisting across the southeastern United States,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wc1954_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-wc1954-7">[5]</a></sup> the motion of Carol turned to a northwest drift. The hurricane continued to strengthen,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-6">[4]</a></sup> and Carol reached an initial peak intensity of 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h) on August\xc2\xa028.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[3]</a></sup> By that time, it was a small hurricane, and the <a href="/wiki/Radius_of_maximum_wind" title="Radius of maximum wind">radius of maximum winds</a> was smaller than normal for its latitude and central pressure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-6">[4]</a></sup> After maintaining peak intensity for 30\xc2\xa0hours and moving a distance of about 75\xc2\xa0miles (120\xc2\xa0km), Carol weakened slightly off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup></p>\n<p>An eastward moving deep-wave <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> intensified as it moved through the eastern <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. This caused Carol to accelerate as it turned to the north and north-northeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wc1954_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-wc1954-7">[5]</a></sup> On August\xc2\xa030, the hurricane again strengthened to reach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 2</a> status while located 180\xc2\xa0miles (290\xc2\xa0km) east of <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a>. Early on August\xc2\xa031, Carol passed very near <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">Cape Hatteras</a>, North Carolina with reconnaissance aircraft intensity estimates from 75\xe2\x80\x93125\xc2\xa0mph (120\xe2\x80\x93200\xc2\xa0km/h). The hurricane continued north-northeastward with a forward motion of up to 39\xc2\xa0mph (63\xc2\xa0km/h),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> and Carol intensified further to make landfall on eastern Long Island a Category 3 hurricane with peak winds of 111\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[3]</a></sup> After quickly crossing <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Sound" title="Long Island Sound">Long Island Sound</a>, the hurricane made landfall again near <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton, Connecticut</a>,.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[3]</a></sup> Carol had maintained its small structure and well-defined eye, and observers on both eastern Long Island and Groton, Connecticut reported blue skies and light winds as the eye passed overhead, followed by strong hurricane force winds. The landfall intensity was based on a pressure of 957\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (28.3\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>) recorded in <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton, Connecticut</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reanal_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-reanal-6">[4]</a></sup> Ninety percent of homes in the Groton Long Point area were destroyed by the storm surge and winds in Hurricane Carol.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cobb_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-cobb-8">[6]</a></sup> Carol quickly lost tropical characteristics while crossing into inland eastern <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, and became extratropical over southwestern <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a> late on August\xc2\xa031. The powerful extratropical storm continued northward, and after entering <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> lost its identity over southern <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-5">[3]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a Island</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Before affecting North Carolina, the threat of Carol prompted a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane warning</a> from <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">Wilmington</a> to <a href="/wiki/Manteo,_North_Carolina" title="Manteo, North Carolina">Manteo</a>. Storm warnings were issued southward to <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston</a>. Residents evacuated north of Wilmington along the ocean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theday_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-theday-9">[7]</a></sup> While passing by the state, the strongest winds remained to the east of Hurricane Carol, though winds of 90 to 100\xc2\xa0mph (145 to 160\xc2\xa0km/h) were reported at Cape Hatteras. Further inland, the hurricane produced a wind gust of 55\xc2\xa0mph (90\xc2\xa0km/h) in Wilmington and 65\xc2\xa0mph (105\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cherry_Point,_North_Carolina" title="Cherry Point, North Carolina">Cherry Point</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> The winds resulted in agricultural damage to the corn and soy bean crop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdam_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdam-10">[8]</a></sup> High winds caused minor damage to roofs and houses,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdam_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncdam-10">[8]</a></sup> and also downed some trees and power lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> Near the coast, waves from the storm damaged fishing piers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdam_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-ncdam-10">[8]</a></sup> and flooding was reported in <a href="/wiki/New_Bern,_North_Carolina" title="New Bern, North Carolina">New Bern</a>. High waves also damaged coastal roadways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> Damage in the state totaled to around $228,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> Carol passed 100\xc2\xa0miles (160\xc2\xa0km) to the east of Virginia, and produced 40\xc2\xa0mph (65\xc2\xa0km/h) winds in <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach</a>. The hurricane produced 4\xc2\xa0inches (100\xc2\xa0mm) of rain in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk</a>. Further to the northwest, rainfall from the system alleviated drought conditions in the Washington, D.C. area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-virhis_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-virhis-12">[10]</a></sup> Damage was minor from Virginia to Delaware, where light rains fell. Precipitation also extended into Pennsylvania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>In New Jersey, high winds downed power lines,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prescott_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-prescott-13">[11]</a></sup> which killed two people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> Along the coast, high waves damaged boardwalks and caused flooding. In neighboring Pennsylvania, Carol caused a tractor to crash into a train, resulting in two deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> Damage in the state was estimated at $250,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>On eastern Long Island near where Carol made landfall, a pressure of 960\xc2\xa0mbar (28\xc2\xa0inHg) was recorded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> Winds on the island gusted to 125\xc2\xa0mph (200\xc2\xa0km/h),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdata1954_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdata1954-16">[14]</a></sup> leaving thousands of homes without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redc_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-redc-17">[15]</a></sup> The winds downed many trees,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redc_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-redc-17">[15]</a></sup> and left heavy crop damage to various fruits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> High winds damaged widespread homes, boats, and cars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> About 1,000\xc2\xa0houses were damaged on the island,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redc_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-redc-17">[15]</a></sup> and 275,000\xc2\xa0people lost power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> The hurricane\'s storm surge covered the <a href="/wiki/Montauk_Highway" title="Montauk Highway">Montauk Highway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montauk,_New_York" title="Montauk, New York">Montauk</a>, effectively isolating eastern Long Island for a period of time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[16]</a></sup> Flooding also affected <a href="/wiki/LaGuardia_Airport" title="LaGuardia Airport">LaGuardia Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> Due to the compact nature of the storm, areas west of <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island" title="Fire Island">Fire Island</a> were largely unaffected by the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[17]</a></sup> There were power outages in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, but little damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> Damage was estimated at $5\xc2\xa0million in the state,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-6"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> and one death was reported, after thousands of people evacuated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-4"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_England_and_Canada">New England and Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New England and Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright">\n<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Carol_in_Rhode_Island.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>\nEdgewood Yacht Club withstands the storm surge from Carol in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edgewood,_Rhode_Island" title="Edgewood, Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>.</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p>Hurricane Carol produced hurricane and gale force winds across New England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-7"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> Strong winds from Hurricane Carol destroyed nearly 40% of the apple, corn, peach, and tomato crops from eastern Connecticut to Cape Cod.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> Overall crop damage was estimated at $22.25\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-8"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> The hurricane destroyed several thousand homes in New England, many of which were destroyed from the waters or the powerful winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> Overall, 11,785\xc2\xa0families were directly impacted by the hurricane, including 9,720\xc2\xa0houses that were damaged and 1,545\xc2\xa0that were destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redc_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-redc-17">[15]</a></sup> High winds left over 150,000\xc2\xa0people without power in New England,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> potentially as many as one-third of all of New England,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miami_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-miami-21">[19]</a></sup> and many residents also lost phone service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-9"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> The hurricane also destroyed 3,500\xc2\xa0cars and 3,000\xc2\xa0boats in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> Heavy rainfall from the storm caused traffic accidents, but only minor flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-10"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Throughout the United States, Hurricane Carol caused $461\xc2\xa0million in damage,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[20]</a></sup> mostly in New England,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-9"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> making it the costliest Atlantic hurricane at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[21]</a></sup> There were 65\xc2\xa0deaths in New England,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> and about 1,000\xc2\xa0injured people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-11"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> Despite the high damage, advance warning allowed for many fewer deaths than the total of 488\xc2\xa0fatalities during the <a href="/wiki/1938_New_England_hurricane" title="1938 New England hurricane">1938 New England hurricane</a>, which affected the same area with similar strength.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> However, some areas did not receive advance warning, due to power outages preventing people from receiving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Weather_Bureau" title="United States Weather Bureau">Weather Bureau</a> warnings ahead of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miami_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-miami-21">[19]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Hurricane Carol struck <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> shortly after <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">high tide</a>, and its combination with 10 to 15\xc2\xa0feet (3 to 4.5\xc2\xa0m) <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surges</a> from <a href="/wiki/New_London,_Connecticut" title="New London, Connecticut">New London</a> eastward produced widespread tidal flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-5"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> About 2,000\xc2\xa0people were stranded when a rail line between <a href="/wiki/New_Haven,_Connecticut" title="New Haven, Connecticut">New Haven</a> and Rhode Island was flooded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-3"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> The heaviest rainfall associated with the passage of the storm occurred in New London, where up to 6\xc2\xa0inches (150\xc2\xa0mm) fell<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-6"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> and wind blew off a portion of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_hall" title="City hall">city hall</a> roof.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcquaid_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcquaid-24">[22]</a></sup> These strong winds left much of the eastern portion of the state without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-7"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> Near the coast, the combination of strong winds and the storm surge damaged or destroyed thousands of buildings, including 100\xc2\xa0destroyed houses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redc_17-4"><a href="#cite_note-redc-17">[15]</a></sup> Many other homes in Eastern Connecticut were damaged by falling trees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-8"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> Thousands had evacuated before the storm reached the area, and one person died in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-5"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> Due to the compact nature of the storm, western Connecticut experienced little effects from Carol.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[23]</a></sup> Overall damage in the state was estimated at $50\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-12"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The hurricane produced a record-high wind gust of 135\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Block_Island" title="Block Island">Block Island</a>, while on mainland <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, sustained winds peaked at 90\xc2\xa0mph (145\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Warwick,_Rhode_Island" title="Warwick, Rhode Island">Warwick</a> with gusts to 105\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h). Upon making landfall around high tide, Carol produced a storm surge of up to 14.4\xc2\xa0feet (4.4\xc2\xa0m) in <a href="/wiki/Narragansett_Bay" title="Narragansett Bay">Narragansett Bay</a>, surpassing that of the New England Hurricane of 1938. The resulting storm surge flooded downtown <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence</a> with 12\xc2\xa0feet (3.7\xc2\xa0m) of water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-9"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> News reports indicated that the floods covered the area with 4 feet (1.2\xc2\xa0m) in about an hour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miami_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-miami-21">[19]</a></sup> The winds downed two broadcasting towers in the city. <a href="/wiki/Westerly,_Rhode_Island" title="Westerly, Rhode Island">Westerly</a> was also flooded,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-6"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> where 200\xc2\xa0homes were washed away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-4"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> There was also heavy damage in <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-7"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> where the <a href="/wiki/Newport_Casino" title="Newport Casino">Newport Casino</a> was damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-5"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> Some entire coastal communities were nearly destroyed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-10"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> and 620\xc2\xa0houses and 83\xc2\xa0other buildings were destroyed in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redc_17-5"><a href="#cite_note-redc-17">[15]</a></sup> The winds destroyed the roofs of hundreds of buildings, forcing many to evacuate to shelters during the passage of the storm. The powerful winds also downed thousands of trees and power lines, <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">blacking out</a> the entire state and interrupting 95 percent of phone service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daleo_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-daleo-26">[24]</a></sup> Damage in the state totaled about $200\xc2\xa0million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-13"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> and there were at least 17\xc2\xa0deaths in Rhode Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-8"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<div 180\xc2\xa0km/h) across much of the eastern part of the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-11"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> Gusts reached 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) at lines;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-14"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> much of eastern Massachusetts lost power during the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-12"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> Carol left about $15\xc2\xa0million in crop damage in the state. The winds destroyed much of the corn crop, about half of the peach crop, and about 1.5\xc2\xa0million <a href="/wiki/Bushel" title="Bushel">bushels</a> of apples.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-6"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> High winds destroyed the spire of the <a href="/wiki/Old_North_Church" title="Old North Church">Old North Church</a> in Boston, which was the location of where the lanterns were hung during <a href="/wiki/Paul_Revere" title="Paul Revere">Paul Revere</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Paul_Revere%27s_Ride" title="Paul Revere\'s Ride">ride</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prescott_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-prescott-13">[11]</a></sup> It was a replacement spire, after a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snow_Hurricane_of_1804" title="Snow Hurricane of 1804">hurricane in 1804</a> destroyed the original.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-7"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> Heavy damage was reported throughout Boston,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-11"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> and in <a href="/wiki/Wareham,_Massachusetts" title="Wareham, Massachusetts">Wareham</a>, about 1,500\xc2\xa0people were left homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miami_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-miami-21">[19]</a></sup> Statewide, 3,350\xc2\xa0homes were damaged to some degree, and another 800\xc2\xa0were destroyed. The hurricane destroyed another 213\xc2\xa0buildings and severely damaged 530.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redc_17-6"><a href="#cite_note-redc-17">[15]</a></sup> Near the coast, strong storm surges were reported,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_15-13"><a href="#cite_note-nws-15">[13]</a></sup> and a 20 feet (6.1\xc2\xa0m) storm tide was reported at <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford,_Massachusetts" title="New Bedford, Massachusetts">New Bedford</a>, setting a record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miami_21-4"><a href="#cite_note-miami-21">[19]</a></sup> At least 15\xc2\xa0deaths were reported in the state,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-12"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> and damage was estimated at $175\xc2\xa0million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-15"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Carol maintained its intensity as it moved inland,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carolmaine_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-carolmaine-27">[25]</a></sup> and its winds were strong enough to knock down trees and power lines in New Hampshire. One tree fell onto a car, killing a person, and there were three deaths overall in the state,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-13"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> along with $3\xc2\xa0million in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-16"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> There was also a death in neighboring Vermont.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-14"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> Carol produced winds of up to 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Maine" title="Augusta, Maine">Augusta, Maine</a>. Throughout the state, the winds downed hundreds of trees, some of which damaged houses, wrecked cars, destroyed one building, or fell onto power lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carolmaine_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-carolmaine-27">[25]</a></sup> Fallen trees blocked highways,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-15"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> and one person was injured by a falling tree limb. Downed power lines left several counties without power or telephone services. The winds flattened hundreds of acres of corn in <a href="/wiki/Livermore,_Maine" title="Livermore, Maine">North Livermore</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carolmaine_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-carolmaine-27">[25]</a></sup> and throughout the state, there was heavy damage to the apple crop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-8"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> Damage to the apple crop amounted to $1.7\xc2\xa0million. While moving west of Maine, the hurricane dropped heavy rainfall, including a report of 2.15\xc2\xa0inches (63\xc2\xa0mm) in 12\xc2\xa0hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carolmaine_27-3"><a href="#cite_note-carolmaine-27">[25]</a></sup> Along the coast, high waves damaged boats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-16"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> In Maine, the hurricane killed three people, injured at least eight, and caused $10\xc2\xa0million in damage, the costliest natural disaster in the state\'s history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carolmaine_27-4"><a href="#cite_note-carolmaine-27">[25]</a></sup> Carol lost this distinction 10\xc2\xa0days later when <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Edna" title="Hurricane Edna">Hurricane Edna</a> caused $15\xc2\xa0million in damage in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ednamaine_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-ednamaine-28">[26]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Rainfall in Canada peaked at 4.27\xc2\xa0inches (108\xc2\xa0mm) in Quebec. In <a href="/wiki/Yarmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Yarmouth, Nova Scotia">Yarmouth, Nova Scotia</a>, wind gusts peaked at 47\xc2\xa0mph (75\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ec_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-ec-29">[27]</a></sup> In Quebec, the extratropical remnants of Carol downed trees and power lines in Montreal from wind gusts as high as 55\xc2\xa0mph (89\xc2\xa0km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-10"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> Widespread power outages were reported in New Brunswick and Quebec, and downed trees struck three cars in <a href="/wiki/Saint_John,_New_Brunswick" title="Saint John, New Brunswick">Saint John, New Brunswick</a>. The storm caused flights to be canceled, and subways were flooded in Montreal. Damage there totaled about $1\xc2\xa0million (1954\xc2\xa0<a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">CAD</a>, $980,000 USD)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[nb 3]</a></sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ec_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-ec-29">[27]</a></sup> Two people were killed, one of whom due to drowning on a sunken barge in <a href="/wiki/Quebec_City" title="Quebec City">Quebec City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-17"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>\n<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_of_Rhode_Island" title="Governor of Rhode Island">Governor of Rhode Island</a> <a href="/wiki/Dennis_J._Roberts" title="Dennis J. Roberts">Dennis J. Roberts</a> declared <a href="/wiki/Martial_law" title="Martial law">martial law</a> for the state after Carol caused heavy damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spok_14-18"><a href="#cite_note-spok-14">[12]</a></sup> In Massachusetts, the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_National_Guard" title="Massachusetts National Guard">National Guard</a> were deployed to six towns to prevent looting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-9"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> National Guardsmen flew a plane of <a href="/wiki/Dry_ice" title="Dry ice">dry ice</a> from <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a> to Boston to assist the widespread areas without power and refrigeration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[31]</a></sup> Widespread areas were without power for days, and in some areas for up to a week, until crews could repair downed lines. Spoiled food due to lack of refrigeration resulted in about $1\xc2\xa0million in losses. Power crews from elsewhere in the United States arrived to assist in the restoration. Workers quickly removed trees from highways. Damaged factories in Rhode Island prevented employees from working for three weeks after the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clima_11-17"><a href="#cite_note-clima-11">[9]</a></sup> The steeple of the Old North Church in Boston was rebuilt in 1955, after residents throughout the country provided $150,000 in donations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Maine" title="Governor of Maine">Governor of Maine</a> <a href="/wiki/Burton_M._Cross" title="Burton M. Cross">Burton M. Cross</a> declared a state of emergency for the state. The Small Business Administration declared six counties in Maine as disaster areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carolmaine_27-5"><a href="#cite_note-carolmaine-27">[25]</a></sup> In the days after the storm, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dwight_Eisenhower" title="Dwight Eisenhower">Dwight Eisenhower</a> declared Massachusetts and Rhode Island as federal disaster areas. The federal government provided financial aid, amounting to $1.5\xc2\xa0million in Massachusetts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mi94_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-mi94-36">[33]</a></sup> The president ordered for troops to assist in the aftermath. The <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> quickly deployed teams to the most affected areas,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[34]</a></sup> feeding hundreds of families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-milw_20-10"><a href="#cite_note-milw-20">[18]</a></sup> About 12\xc2\xa0days after Carol struck New England, Hurricane Edna struck eastern Massachusetts, causing an additional $40\xc2\xa0million in damage and 20\xc2\xa0deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_4-11"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-4">[2]</a></sup> More disaster aid was provided after the second hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[35]</a></sup></p>\n<p>The high damage caused by Hurricane Carol and other hurricanes in 1954 prompted the United States government to devote research to set up the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Research_Project" title="National Hurricane Research Project">National Hurricane Research Project</a>. Hurricane Hunters and the Weather Bureau collected data on subsequent hurricanes to determine their structure, as well as attempted to weaken storms with <a href="/wiki/Silver_iodide" title="Silver iodide">silver iodide</a> via <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">Project Stormfury</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[36]</a></sup></p>\n<p>Due to the high damage, the name Carol was removed from the naming list for 10\xc2\xa0years. The name was reused in the <a href="/wiki/1965_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1965 Atlantic hurricane season">1965 season</a>, but was retroactively retired, and it will never again be used for an Atlantic hurricane. Carol was the first Atlantic hurricane name in history to be retired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[37]</a></sup></p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
