href="#p-search">search</a>\n</div>\n<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Little Egg Inlet</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Inlet" title="Inlet">inlet</a> connecting the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Bay_(New_Jersey)" title="Great Bay (New Jersey)">Great Bay</a> along the southeastern coast of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, United States. Little Egg Inlet forms a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritime_border" title="Maritime border">maritime border</a> separating <a href="/wiki/Little_Egg_Harbor_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Little Egg Harbor Township, New Jersey">Little Egg Harbor Township</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Ocean_County,_New_Jersey" title="Ocean County, New Jersey">Ocean County</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galloway_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Galloway Township, New Jersey">Galloway Township</a> in northeastern <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_County,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic County, New Jersey">Atlantic County</a>. On March 3, 2017 the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> has temporarily closed the inlet due to serve <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shoaling" title="Shoaling">shoaling</a> and had removed six out of seven <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Navigation_Buoys&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Navigation Buoys (page does not exist)">Navigation Buoys</a>, they had reported that their ship couldn\'t reach the seventh buoy and had left it there. They have no plan date to put the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Navigation_Buoys&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Navigation Buoys (page does not exist)">Navigation Buoys</a> back. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> has reported to use the inlet "at your own risk"</p>\n<p>On the morning of August 28, 2011, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irene" title="Hurricane Irene">Tropical Storm Irene</a> made its second U.S. landfall at <a href="/wiki/Brigantine,_New_Jersey" title="Brigantine, New Jersey">Brigantine</a>, though initial reports placed it at Little Egg Inlet.
