the middle of march it is characterized by dry{1] and dusty northeasterly trade wind of the same name which blows from the sahara desert over west africa into the gulf of guinea{2] the name comes from or is related to ga haramata{3] it is cold{1] in most places but can be hot{4] in some places depending on circumstances{5]the harmattan blows during the dry season which occurs during the lowest-sun months when the subtropical ridge of high pressure stays over the central sahara desert and when the low-pressure intertropical convergence zone (itcz) stays over the gulf of guinea on its passage
