silver iodide is also used as an antiseptic and in cloud seedingcontents1 structure2 preparation and properties3 cloud seeding4 safety5 referencesstructure{edit]the structure adopted by silver iodide is temperature dependent:{2]below 420xc2xa0k the xcexb2-phase of agi with the wurtzite structure is most stable this phase is encountered in nature as the mineral iodargyriteabove 420xc2xa0k the xcexb1-phase becomes more stable this motif is a body-centered cubic structure which has the silver centers distributed randomly between 6 octahedral 12 tetrahedral and 24 trigonal sites{3] at this temperature ag+ ions can move rapidly through the solid allowing fast ion conduction the transition between the xcexb2 and xcexb1 forms represents the melting of the silver (cation) sublattice the entropy of fusion for xcexb1-agi is approximately half that for sodium chloride (a typical ionic solid) this can be rationalized by considering the agi crystalline lattice to have already "partly melted" in the transition between xcexb1 and xcexb2 polymorphsa metastable xcexb3-phase also exists below 420xc2xa0k with the zinc blende structurethe golden-yellow crystals on this mineral sample are iodargyrite a naturally occurring form of xcexb2-agipreparation and properties{edit]silver iodide is prepared by reaction of an iodide solution (eg potassium iodide) with a solution of silver ions (eg silver nitrate) a yellowish solid quickly precipitates the solid is a mixture of the two principal phases dissolution of the agi in hydroiodic acid followed by dilution with water precipitates xcexb2-agi alternatively dissolution of agi in a solution of concentrated silver nitrate followed by dilution affords xcexb1-agi{4] if the preparation is not conducted in the absence of sunlight the
